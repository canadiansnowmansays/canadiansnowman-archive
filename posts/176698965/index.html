#8: Black Women "Lack Brain Processing Power" - Part II: The Challenge

November 12, 2025

Charlie Kirk claimed Black women leaders lack “brain processing power.” Let’s test this with their actual achievements, scientific consensus, and Kirk’s own standards of merit.

The Educational Reality Check

The educational contrast alone destroys Kirk’s presumption to judge these women’s intellectual capacity.

Contemporary Excellence: Jasmine Crockett

Representative Jasmine Crockett (D-TX-30), serving in 2025, provides a contemporary refutation of Kirk’s claims:

Educational Achievement

  • Rhodes College, B.A. in Business Administration

  • University of Houston Law Center, J.D.

  • First African American graduate in the law school’s 75-year history to serve in Congress

  • Licensed to practice in Texas, Arkansas, and federal courts

Legislative Excellence

  • Filed more bills than any other freshman in the Texas Legislature

  • Elected Freshman Leadership Representative in Congress

  • Appointed Vice Ranking Member, House Oversight Committee

  • Serves as Ranking Member, House Judiciary Subcommittee

Breaking Barriers

  • First Black female Democrat to play in the Congressional Baseball Game (breaking a 114-year tradition)

  • National co-chair of the Harris-Walz 2024 campaign

  • Key surrogate who traveled to all seven swing states

This is the kind of achievement Kirk dismissed as lacking “brain processing power”—breaking century-old barriers while excelling at every level of American democracy.

The Science: Intelligence Has No Color

The scientific consensus completely destroys Kirk’s racial claims:

The Ugly History: Kirk’s “brain processing power” claim descends directly from debunked racist pseudosciences:

  • Phrenology claimed skull shape determined intelligence (used to justify slavery)

  • Eugenics led to forced sterilizations of 60,000+ Americans deemed “inferior”

  • IQ tests were weaponized to exclude immigrants and segregate schools

Modern Consensus:

  • American Psychological Association & American Anthropological Association (2024): Intelligence variation within racial groups equals variation between groups

  • No genetic basis links race to intelligence

  • Environmental factors (education access, socioeconomic status, stereotype threat) explain achievement gaps

  • Race itself is a social construct, not a biological reality determining cognitive ability

The Intersection: Race and Gender

Kirk didn’t just attack Black people—he specifically targeted Black women, compounding racism with sexism. This intersectional prejudice means:

  • Facing doubled barriers to achievement

  • Receiving half the credit for twice the work

  • Navigating stereotypes from multiple directions

This echoes Sojourner Truth’s “Ain’t I a Woman?” (1851), Ida B. Wells being dismissed as “emotional,” and Shirley Chisholm’s observation about facing “double discrimination.” Black women must be twice as good to get half as far—then face men like Kirk claiming they’re stealing opportunities.

Projection & Logical Failures

Kirk exemplified the Dunning-Kruger effect: those with limited competence overestimate their abilities. A college dropout presuming to judge Harvard Law graduates’ intelligence is peak irony. As James Baldwin wrote, Kirk was “measuring [his] intelligence by the white ticket in [his] pocket.”

His zero-sum thinking ignored how expanding opportunity benefits everyone. Pre-2023 data showed diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones. Kirk’s selective scrutiny—questioning only Black achievement—revealed the racism underlying his “merit” concerns.

Failed by His Own Standards

Kirk demanded others “prove me wrong.” Let’s apply his own standards:

  1. “Prove Me Wrong”: The achievements and scientific evidence definitively refute his claims

  2. Merit-Based Evaluation: These women’s credentials and accomplishments far exceed his own

  3. Evidence Over Emotion: Empirical data on intelligence shows no racial differences

  4. Christian Values: “God shows no partiality” (Acts 10:34); “You shall not bear false witness” (Exodus 20:16); “From one man he made all the nations” (Acts 17:26)

The Theological Challenge

Kirk claimed Christian faith, yet his words violated core biblical principles:

  • Imago Dei: All humans bear God’s image equally (Genesis 1:27)

  • Unity in Christ: “There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus” (Galatians 3:28)

  • Against Favoritism: “My brothers and sisters, believers in our glorious Lord Jesus Christ must not show favoritism” (James 2:1)

Scripture offers no support for racial hierarchies of intelligence—quite the opposite.

The Real Intelligence Test

True intelligence manifests in positive impact:

  • Michelle Obama: Transformed childhood health and education access for millions

  • Ketanji Brown Jackson: Shapes constitutional interpretation for generations

  • Sheila Jackson Lee: Delivered justice reforms and recognized Juneteenth nationally

  • Joy Reid: Amplifies marginalized voices to millions daily

Charlie Kirk: Amplified Prejudice and division

Modern Excellence Continues

Black women’s excellence extends far beyond Kirk’s targets:

  • Rosalind Brewer & Ursula Burns: Fortune 500 CEOs

  • Dr. Kizzmekia Corbett: COVID vaccine developer

  • Vice President Harris & Justice Jackson: Reshaping American governance

  • Dr. Ruth Simmons: First Black Ivy League president

The Ultimate Irony

Kirk demanded intellectual diversity on campuses while denying Black women possess the intelligence to deserve their positions. He couldn’t see that true diversity includes diversity of thought that comes from different lived experiences—exactly what these accomplished women bring to their roles.

The violence of Kirk’s words—telling accomplished women their minds are inferior—represents more than individual prejudice. It’s an attempt to maintain systems that exclude and diminish, wrapped in the language of merit while practicing its opposite.

The Rhetoric Lives On

As this debate publishes— a few days after Donald Trump’s latest Fox News meltdown—Charlie Kirk’s toxic legacy proves immortal. On Laura Ingraham’s show Monday, Trump mangled the English language into oblivion, declaring the White House’s East Wing had been “disbanded” under Biden (a word he slurred into the viral neologism “despanded,” equal parts gaffe and synaptic short-circuit). He pivoted straight to his favorite slur: calling Rep. Jasmine Crockett a “low, very low IQ person” who “couldn’t pass a basic cognitive test.” The irony? Trump—the man who once bragged about acing those same “very hard” dementia screeners (you know, the ones where you name animals and memorize five words)—just handed us Exhibit A for who really lacks brain processing power.

This isn’t isolated; it’s the sequel Kirk scripted. Throughout 2025, Trump’s hammered Crockett with “low IQ” jabs: March’s “lowlife” rant after her “Hot Wheels” takedown of Gov. Abbott; August’s CNBC sneer (”She’s a very low IQ person—gotta be kidding if she’s a leader”); October’s Air Force One demand for her to “pass the exams I took at Walter Reed.” All while admitting those tests stumped him. Kirk claimed Black women like Crockett default to “emotional reasoning” over intellect. Trump? He can’t even “despand” a coherent insult without broadcasting his own cognitive fireworks.

And who is this “low IQ” paragon he’s targeting? The trailblazing Rep. Jasmine Crockett:

  • First Black woman from Rhodes College Law School to serve in Congress

  • Filed more bills as a Texas freshman than any peer in a decade

  • Vice Ranking Member of the House Oversight Committee, grilling corruption like it’s her cardio

  • Shattered a 114-year barrier as the first woman of color in the Congressional Baseball Game

Crockett fired back with surgical shade: “I don’t know what Black woman hurt him in his past, honey, but it’s really taking him through it. He’s never been known as an Einstein—but for that silver spoon, he wouldn’t have cracked any institution’s door. Unlike me.” Boom. She doesn’t just clap back; she excavates the rot.

The thread from Kirk to Trump is barbed wire-tight. One dropout peddles pseudoscience about Black women’s “processing deficits.” The other, flailing with neologisms on national TV, echoes it verbatim to prop up his fragile empire. It’s no accident. When white men with megaphones and fragile egos target Black women’s intellect, it’s never analysis—it’s the American ritual rebooted: Dismantle her excellence to soothe their insecurity. Kirk dared us to “prove him wrong.” Trump’s “despanded” disaster? It proves the peril ain’t in our brains—it’s in theirs. Every barrier Crockett (or Michelle, Ketanji, Joy) shatters gets slapped with “affirmative action” or “low IQ.” But mediocrity’s shouts fade fast when excellence roars.

Conclusion: Excellence Needs No Permission

Charlie Kirk updated centuries-old racist pseudoscience for modern audiences, but neither his words nor the eugenics they echo can diminish actual achievement. When Jasmine Crockett introduced the FREE SPEECH Act in 2025, she didn’t need Kirk’s validation of her “brain processing power”—her excellence spoke for itself.

Kirk asked us to prove him wrong. The evidence—from neuroscience to theology, from Harvard Law to the halls of Congress—proves him definitively, completely, and irrevocably wrong. These women’s brilliance needs no defense, but Kirk’s ignorance demanded this response.

Black women will continue to excel, lead, and transform our nation. They don’t need Charlie Kirk’s permission or approval. They never did.
