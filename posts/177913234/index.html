Trump Discovers the E-Word

A Children’s Guide to Constitutional Arson

SCOTUS Meets the Boy Who Cried Emergency

November 3, 2025

On Wednesday, the Supreme Court will hear Donald Trump explain why Canadian lumber is plotting against America.

No, really.

See, Trump has discovered a magic word that makes Congress disappear: “Emergency.” It’s like “Abracadabra,” but for tariffs. Got a trade deficit? Emergency! Don’t like French wine? Emergency! Carney said something mean about your hair? DEFCON 1 emergency, 500% maple syrup tax, effective immediately.

The case—two boring corporate lawsuits smooshed together—asks whether the President can use something called IEEPA to tax whatever he wants. IEEPA was created in 1977 to freeze terrorist bank accounts. Trump thinks it’s a gift card to Best Buy.

“Look,” Trump told reporters last week, “China has been laughing at us. I heard them. Through the fortune cookies.”

His lawyers will argue that “regulate” means whatever Trump wants it to mean. Like when he said he “won” the 2020 election. Or when he claimed windmills kill birds with “sonic death rays.” Words are just suggestions in Trumpland.

The beautiful part? Trump keeps insisting foreigners pay these tariffs. “Mexico will pay for the wall” has evolved into “Belgium will pay for your beer.” Every economist in America has explained that’s not how tariffs work. Trump’s response: “Fake news. I know tariffs. I invented tariffs. The Chinese told me, ‘Sir, your tariffs are the most beautiful tariffs we’ve ever paid.’”

(The Chinese did not say this.)

Here’s what’s actually happening: Trump wants to tax your iPhone, call it patriotism, and blame Tim Cook when you can’t afford one. It’s genius if you squint hard enough and forget how money works.

Justice Kagan will likely ask the obvious question: “Is there anything that ISN’T an emergency?”

Trump’s team: “Emergencies are like art, Your Honor. You know them when you see them.”

Translation: No.

The White House has already declared 47 emergencies this term, including “The Great Brie Betrayal of 2025” and something about German cars having “suspicious” turn signals. At this rate, Trump will declare Tuesday an emergency because it comes after Monday, which was also an emergency.

What makes this perfect Trump theater is the circular logic. Create chaos with tariffs → chaos becomes emergency → emergency justifies more tariffs → repeat until everyone’s broke or bored.

If he wins, expect emergency declarations on everything. Italian suits? Emergency (they make him look fat). Japanese toilets? Emergency (too many buttons). British accents? Double emergency (they’re clearly mocking us).

If he loses? That’s the biggest emergency of all. “The Supreme Court has been infiltrated by foreign robe manufacturers,” he’ll tweet from his golden toilet. “Many people are saying Kavanaugh drinks IMPORTED beer!!!”

The hearings start Wednesday at 10 a.m., though Trump has already declared 9:45 an emergency because his McMuffin was cold.

Your move, Constitution.
