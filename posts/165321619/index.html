The Trump Grift

Part 4 The $400 Million Flying Palace: Second-Term Schemes Go Supersonic

June 27, 2025

CanadianSnowmanSays
June 27, 2025

The Gloves Come Off

If Trump's first presidency was a masterclass in conflicts of interest, his second term is a PhD program in weaponizing government power for personal profit. This time, he's not even pretending to maintain ethical guardrails.

Eight years later, as Trump prepared to return to the White House, he made no promises to divest from any of his financial interests, which have now soared to include a cryptocurrency business and a stake valued at $3.76 billion in a social media company, in addition to his family firm's growing number of foreign deals.

The bankruptcy king had evolved into something unprecedented: a sitting president openly monetizing the office while his family's business empire expanded globally. What we're witnessing isn't just corruption—it's the systematic dismantling of the barriers between public service and private profit.

Chapter 1: The $400 Million "Gift" That Isn't Actually Free

Perhaps the most audacious scheme of Trump's second term is the Qatari jet deal—a masterpiece of misdirection that perfectly embodies the Trump family's approach to wealth extraction.

The Setup: When a "Gift" Isn't a Gift

In May 2025, reports surfaced that Trump planned to accept a Boeing 747-8 from Qatar to use as Air Force One. Trump called it a generous "gift" from the Qatari royal family, worth approximately $400 million.

"So the fact that the Defense Department is getting a GIFT, FREE OF CHARGE, of a 747 aircraft to replace the 40 year old Air Force One, temporarily, in a very public and transparent transaction, so bothers the Crooked Democrats," Trump posted on Truth Social.

But CNN's investigation revealed the truth: The Trump administration first approached Qatar to inquire about acquiring a Boeing 747 that could be used as Air Force One, four sources familiar with the discussions told CNN. That's contrary to the narrative from the president that Qatar reached out and offered the jet as a "gift" to him.

So let's be clear: Trump asked Qatar for a plane, Qatar said yes, and now Trump is calling it a generous "gift."

The Scam: Taxpayers Pay, Trump Keeps It

Here's how the scheme works:

Step 1: Qatar "gifts" the $400 million plane to the U.S. government
Step 2: Taxpayers pay $50-150 million to convert it for presidential use
Step 3: After Trump leaves office, the plane goes to Trump's presidential library—meaning it becomes his personal property

Converting the luxury jet into Air Force One could cost hundreds of millions of dollars and take up to two years, according to current and former officials. The retrofit would involve stripping the aircraft down to its frame and rebuilding it with classified communications equipment and defensive capabilities.

The plane is currently sitting at San Antonio airport, awaiting its taxpayer-funded conversion into Trump's personal flying palace.

The Pushback: Even Republicans Are Uncomfortable

The scheme was so brazen that even Trump allies expressed concern. Sen. Ted Cruz said the plane "poses significant espionage and surveillance problems" and noted Qatar's "disturbing pattern of funding theocratic lunatics who want to murder us, funding Hamas and Hezbollah."

Rep. Jamie Raskin launched an investigation, claiming the Qatari plane "was no gift at all, but instead a coerced offering after a months-long pressure campaign" involving senior administration officials.

But Trump defended it by saying he "would never be one to turn down that kind of an offer. I mean, I could be a stupid person say, 'No, we don't want a free, very expensive airplane.'"

Free? When taxpayers are paying for the conversion and Trump gets to keep it afterward?

Chapter 2: The Meme Coin Carnival - Crypto Grift Goes Presidential

If the jet deal was audacious, Trump's cryptocurrency ventures are absolutely unprecedented. In January 2025, just days before his inauguration, Trump launched something that had never existed before: a presidential meme coin.

The $TRUMP Token: A New Form of Influence Peddling

The $TRUMP meme coin generated $350 million in fees for the Trump family, with Donald, Eric, and Don Jr. all promoting sales. But the real innovation was turning cryptocurrency into a direct access-selling mechanism.

The coin's most brazen feature was a contest: the top buyers would win dinner with the president. The top 220 holders, who spent a combined $148 million, got invited to the black-tie gala at Trump's Virginia golf club. The top 25 got even more exclusive access, including a VIP White House tour.

Think about that: people could literally buy access to the president by purchasing his cryptocurrency.

The Victims: 764,000 Wallets Lose Money

While Trump and his allies profited handsomely, the typical buyers got crushed. About 764,000 wallets that purchased the $TRUMP meme coin lost money, according to Chainalysis. The typical buyer spent $100 and lost $62.

Conservative radio host Rebecca Davis epitomizes the victims: she knew nothing about cryptocurrency but believed in Trump's business savvy. Her $32 investment is now worth $11—a 66% loss.

Meanwhile, 58 wallets made more than $10 million each, totaling roughly $1.1 billion in gains. The anonymous wallets included "SUN" (later revealed to be crypto entrepreneur Justin Sun, who faces SEC fraud charges) with an $18.5 million investment.

World Liberty Financial: The Crypto Empire

The meme coin was just the flashy front for a much larger operation. Trump's family also controls World Liberty Financial, with the Trump Organization owning 60% and entitled to 75% of all revenue.

The company attracted some interesting foreign investors:

  • UAE-based DWF Labs: $25 million purchase

  • MGX (Emirati-backed firm): $2 billion investment in Binance using Trump's cryptocurrency

  • Justin Sun: $75 million total investment while facing federal fraud charges

This isn't just a business—it's a new form of international influence peddling where foreign governments and individuals can funnel money directly to the president's family through cryptocurrency purchases.

Chapter 3: Truth Social - Monetizing Grievance Into Billions

But Trump's most successful wealth extraction vehicle might be the one hiding in plain sight: Truth Social. What started as Trump's response to being banned from Twitter has become a masterclass in how to monetize political persecution while leaving your own supporters holding the bag.

The T1 Phone: Pricing Patriotism at Presidential Numbers

Even more brazen than Truth Social is Trump's latest venture: the Trump Mobile "T1 Phone," available for pre-order at trumpmobile.com. The monthly plan is called "The 47 Plan" and costs exactly $47.45—a direct reference to Trump being the 45th president in his first term and the 47th president now. He's literally pricing his phone service as a tribute to his presidential numbers.

But here's the grift: the plan is significantly overpriced compared to competitors. Visible by Verizon offers unlimited talk, text, data and hotspot for $25 per month, while Mint Mobile's 12-month unlimited plan costs $30 monthly. Trump is charging his supporters nearly double what they could pay elsewhere for the same service—with the premium justified purely by presidential numerology.

The $499 phone is marketed as "MADE IN THE USA" and features golden "Make America Great Again" branding. The company promises extras like telehealth services and free international calling to military bases, but these add-ons don't justify charging supporters $17-22 more per month than established carriers offer.

The phone's launch during his presidency shows how completely Trump has abandoned any separation between political office and business ventures. Previous presidents avoided even the appearance of profiting from their position—Trump turned the presidency into a product development platform that charges supporters premium prices for basic services.

The T1 Phone represents the ultimate fusion of political identity and consumer exploitation. Supporters aren't just buying a phone service; they're paying a monthly premium specifically calculated to honor Trump's presidential legacy. It's political tribalism transformed into recurring revenue, where loyalty is measured in monthly overpayments of exactly $47.45.

The Presidential Product Line Expands

The phone plan isn't an isolated scheme—it's part of a systematic product line built around Trump's presidential numbers. In June 2025, Trump launched "Victory 45-47" fragrances, featuring cologne and perfume bottles shaped like golden trophies with "45-47" prominently displayed.

"Trump Fragrances are here. They're called 'Victory 45-47' because they're all about Winning, Strength, and Success," Trump posted on Truth Social, directing followers to gettrumpfragrances.com.

The fragrance launch reveals the broader strategy: Trump has turned his presidential sequence into a comprehensive brand identity that can be applied to any consumer product. Whether it's $47.45 monthly phone bills or "Victory 45-47" cologne, the formula is identical—use presidential numerology to justify premium pricing while creating emotional attachment through political identity.

This isn't just product licensing—it's the systematic conversion of political office into a lifestyle brand where supporters express loyalty through purchasing decisions. Every product launch while serving as president normalizes the complete merger of public service and private profit.

The Setup: Building a Platform on Grievance

After being banned from major social media platforms following January 6th, Trump announced the creation of Truth Social in October 2021. The platform was positioned as a "free speech" alternative to Big Tech censorship, perfectly calibrated to appeal to his base's sense of victimhood.

Trump Media & Technology Group went public through a SPAC (Special Purpose Acquisition Company) merger, creating a publicly traded company where Trump held a dominant stake. Despite Truth Social having minimal revenue and a tiny user base compared to established platforms, the stock initially soared based purely on Trump's brand and political prospects.

The Valuation Scam: $5.7 Billion for a Money-Losing Platform

By 2025, Trump Media was valued at $5.7 billion despite generating minimal actual revenue. The company's value had virtually nothing to do with its business fundamentals and everything to do with Trump's political fortunes and his supporters' willingness to buy the stock as a form of political donation.

Truth Social's user base and advertising revenue remained tiny compared to its valuation. The platform was hemorrhaging money, but that didn't matter—Trump had found a way to convert political loyalty into personal wealth through the stock market.

The Insider Trading Windfall

Here's where the scam gets sophisticated: As president, Trump's every action affects Truth Social's stock price. When he posts on the platform, grants interviews, or makes policy announcements that boost his popularity, the stock price moves. And Trump owns the dominant stake.

In 2025, Trump netted $300 million via insider trading in Truth Social stock, with Eric Trump promoting stock purchases on X. Think about that: the President of the United States is making hundreds of millions by trading stock in a company whose value depends entirely on his political actions.

Every time Trump posts on Truth Social as president, he's essentially creating taxpayer-funded advertising for his own stock. Every policy announcement, every controversy, every media appearance drives traffic to his platform and potentially increases his net worth.

The Supporters Get Crushed

While Trump extracted hundreds of millions, his supporters—the retail investors who bought Truth Social stock—got crushed. The stock has dropped 30% during his first month in office, wiping out billions in value held mostly by small investors who believed in the platform and Trump's promises.

These weren't sophisticated Wall Street traders—these were Trump supporters who bought the stock as both an investment and a way to support their political champion. They believed Trump when he promoted the platform and suggested it would compete with Big Tech giants.

The Truth Social story perfectly embodies the Trump template: Trump gets rich, his supporters pay the price.

The Platform as Presidential Megaphone

Now that Trump is president again, Truth Social has become something unprecedented: a privately-owned social media platform that doubles as an official presidential communication channel. When Trump makes policy announcements on Truth Social, he's simultaneously:

  • Conducting official presidential business

  • Driving traffic to his private platform

  • Increasing the value of his personal stock holdings

  • Generating revenue for his private company

It's the ultimate fusion of public office and private profit. Previous presidents used the White House briefing room or official government platforms for major announcements. Trump uses his private, for-profit social media company.

Truth+ and the Streaming Expansion

Not content with just the social media platform, Trump launched Truth+, a streaming service that charges $9.99/month and has generated $50 million. Trump promotes subscriptions at campaign rallies and presidential events, using his official platform to drive revenue to his private entertainment business.

The streaming service perfectly illustrates how Trump has weaponized the presidency: every time he appears at a government event or gives a presidential speech, he can mention or promote his private business ventures to a guaranteed audience of millions.

Chapter 4: Middle East Business Empire Triples

While crypto provided one avenue for enrichment, the Trump Organization's Middle East expansion has been breathtaking in scope and audacity.

The Numbers: From Millions to Billions

The Trump family's business ties to the Middle East have more than tripled since the president's first term, CNN reported, including a slew of new projects announced since he reclaimed the White House.

The scale of current deals dwarfs anything from the first term:

  • Qatar: $5.5 billion Trump International Golf Club project

  • Saudi Arabia: Multiple towers worth over $1 billion total

  • UAE: $1 billion Dubai tower accepting Bitcoin payments

  • Oman: $500 million luxury resort

  • Vietnam: $1.5 billion golf and residential development

Eric's Middle East Tour: Business Development as Foreign Policy

Eric Trump has been the point man for this expansion, traveling to the region while his father sets foreign policy toward those same countries.

In April 2025, Eric was in Dubai speaking at cryptocurrency conferences and announcing new tower deals. The timing wasn't coincidental—it happened during his father's first foreign trip as president to the same region.

The Vietnam deal was particularly brazen. Eric held a groundbreaking ceremony with Vietnam's prime minister for the $1.5 billion project while Vietnam was negotiating to avoid 46% U.S. tariffs. Vietnamese officials offered concessions including favorable conditions for Elon Musk's Starlink in exchange for the trade considerations.

The Government Partnership Problem

Many of these deals involve direct partnerships with foreign governments, contradicting the Trump Organization's stated ethics policy of avoiding government partnerships.

The Qatar golf club project involves Qatari Diar, a company owned by the Qatari government. The Oman resort is developed with that government's tourism arm. Even the Vietnam project received expedited government approval just one week before Eric's groundbreaking ceremony.

When asked about the apparent contradiction, the Trump Organization claimed its agreements were solely with private companies. But Eric Trump had publicly thanked both private and government partners in press releases.

Chapter 5: The Executive Branch Club - Selling Access for $500K

Donald Trump Jr.'s innovation was the most direct: just sell access to government officials outright.

The Executive Branch club charges $500,000 for membership and promises access to White House officials and Cabinet secretaries. Launched at a White House Correspondents' Dinner after-party, the Georgetown-based club drew immediate criticism as "unbelievable corruption."

But the club represents something new: instead of hiding conflicts of interest or maintaining plausible deniability, Don Jr. is openly selling government access as a luxury service.

The club's membership likely includes foreign nationals who couldn't directly donate to U.S. political campaigns but can pay half a million dollars for access to policymakers. It's influence peddling transformed into a membership service.

Chapter 6: The Regulatory Capture Campaign

What makes Trump's second-term schemes particularly dangerous is how systematically he's weakened the institutions designed to provide oversight.

Crypto Regulation Goes Dark

Since Trump took office, the SEC has been rolling back crypto enforcement and taking a more industry-friendly approach. SEC Commissioner Hester Peirce essentially told people they're on their own with meme coins like Trump's: "You should not be looking to the SEC for protection in this area."

The timing is remarkable: Trump launches cryptocurrency ventures while simultaneously dismantling the agencies that might regulate them.

DOJ and Congressional Oversight Neutered

The Supreme Court ruled that Trump is immune from prosecution for official acts, while congressional Democrats lack the power to investigate Trump's business dealings as they did in his first term.

"We have very little potential outside oversight because I don't think there are any functional systems of accountability in place," said Jeff Hauser, executive director of the Revolving Door Project.

The DOGE Connection

Trump's Department of Government Efficiency, led by Elon Musk, has become another avenue for rewarding allies and punishing critics:

  • 400 FAA employees fired after the agency fined Musk's SpaceX

  • Department of Labor oversight gutted while investigating Tesla

  • $500 million in contracts awarded to Trump allies

It's not government efficiency—it's a protection racket operated from federal agencies.

Chapter 7: The First 100 Days Corruption Speedrun

Congressional Democrats documented the scale of corruption in Trump's first 100 days. Rep. Gerald Connolly released a list of 100 conflicts of interest, noting that "selling access and influence is the defining feature of the Trump White House."

Some examples:

  • Apple CEO donates $1M to inauguration → Trump lifts electronic device tariffs

  • Crypto.com investigation paused → SEC ends probe in March 2025

  • TikTok ban delayed after lobbying by Trump ally Brian Ballard

The pattern is clear: pay Trump or his allies, get favorable treatment. Cross them, face regulatory retaliation.

Chapter 8: The International Implications

What makes Trump's second-term schemes particularly dangerous is their international scope. Foreign governments have learned they can influence U.S. policy by:

  • Investing in Trump family cryptocurrencies

  • Partnering on Trump Organization real estate projects

  • Joining the Executive Branch club

  • Providing "gifts" like $400 million jets

Ben Freeman of the Quincy Institute warns that Gulf nations "may seek to sway U.S. policy" through these deals, posing "a real risk" to national interests.

We're witnessing the transformation of American foreign policy into a business development opportunity for the Trump family.

The New Normal: Corruption as a Service

Trump's second term represents the complete abandonment of any pretense of separating public service from private profit. The family has created a comprehensive corruption-as-a-service platform that includes:

  • Cryptocurrency: Direct payments for access

  • Real estate: Foreign government partnerships

  • Clubs: Membership-based influence peddling

  • Travel: Taxpayer-funded advertising for Trump properties

  • Regulatory capture: Agencies serve Trump business interests

This isn't just unprecedented in American history—it's a fundamental transformation of how government operates. The Trump family has turned the presidency into the ultimate licensing deal, where every aspect of government power can be monetized.

The template Fred Trump created in Brooklyn has reached its logical conclusion: the complete capture of the federal government for private wealth extraction.
