The Masculinity Con

Part 6: The Business Model of Exploitation: Following the Money

December 19, 2025

Over eight years, the Tate brothers earned at least £21 million (nearly $28 million) from their webcam pornography operation, according to British courts that found they had evaded taxes on this income. Romanian prosecutors documented how they generated these profits: by coercing women into sexual content through romance, psychological manipulation, surveillance, and violence.

But that £21 million was just the foundation. The brothers built an integrated operation that monetized exploitation at multiple levels: first, directly exploiting women for webcam revenue; second, teaching others to replicate the model through paid courses; third, leveraging their notoriety into a massive social media following; and finally, converting that following into political protection when they needed it.

This wasn’t about “defending masculinity.” It was about building a sophisticated exploitation machine that converted women’s bodies and young men’s grievances into profit, then using culture war rhetoric and political connections to avoid accountability.

Follow the money, and the “masculinity movement” reveals itself as an organized criminal enterprise with excellent marketing and diplomatic protection.

The Original Business Model: Direct Exploitation

Andrew Tate has never hidden the fundamentals of his business - he just reframed them. His 2019 explanation is remarkably candid:

“It’s not just about picking up girls. It’s about converting them into really loving you enough to moving in with you and working for you and giving you all the money.”

Let’s break down the economics:

The Recruitment Phase:

The Romanian indictment alleges the brothers targeted attractive women and created the illusion of genuine romantic relationships. Women believed they were dating someone who loved them and saw a future with them.

Cost: Minimal. Time investment, some dates, gifts. Standard courtship expenses.

The Transition Phase:

Once emotionally invested, women were introduced to webcam work. The relationship provided leverage: this is temporary, we’re building our future together, this money will let us start our real life.

Cost: Housing (apartments in Britain, later Romania), webcam equipment, internet infrastructure.

The Production Phase:

Locations documented:

  • By 2015: Women in apartments and hotel room in Britain

  • Operations expanded to Romania as home base

  • Multiple women in separate controlled locations

  • Surveillance systems installed

Platforms evolved with technology:

  • Traditional webcam sites initially

  • Expanded to TikTok, OnlyFans, and other platforms by 2022

  • Diversified revenue streams as new platforms emerged

The Control Mechanisms:

From the Romanian indictment:

  • Surveillance of women’s activities and movements

  • Restricted movements (couldn’t leave without permission)

  • Rule systems with financial penalties

  • Pay docked for “infractions” like crying on camera

  • Psychological manipulation maintaining relationship fiction

  • Violence or threats when other controls failed

Cost: Surveillance equipment, enforcers (the “PIMPS” chat shows Tristan and associates managing this).

The Revenue Split:

Andrew’s description: the brothers “took most of the profits.” Women received reduced portions, further decreased for rule violations.

Classic extraction model: Controllers provide minimal infrastructure, take majority of earnings, use relationship manipulation and control to maintain compliance.

The Scale:

Romanian evidence shows:

  • 7 women in original indictment

  • 30+ additional women coerced since 2014

  • Operations across multiple countries

  • £21 million documented over 8 years (likely more undocumented)

  • Tax evasion on these earnings

If £21 million is documented across 8 years from 30+ women, that’s roughly £700,000 per woman over the period. Even if women received 30-40% (generous assumption), the brothers extracted hundreds of thousands per woman while maintaining psychological and physical control.

The Violence Economics:

Violence served business purposes, as the “PIMPS” chat reveals:

Tristan: “both of you go, get her out of the house”
Associate: “Ok”
Tristan: “hit her if you have to, I don’t care / don’t let her take her things”

This wasn’t aberrant - it was enforcement. When a woman wouldn’t work, violence expelled her without payment and served as warning to others. The economics are clear: maintaining control through violence is cheaper than losing revenue from non-compliant workers.

The 15-year-old who told investigators Andrew beat her with a belt and choked her until she passed out, then tried to bribe her to stay quiet - that’s cost-benefit analysis. The violence maintained control. The bribery attempted to avoid legal costs.

The Business Expansion: Monetizing the Model

The brothers didn’t just run the operation - they taught others to replicate it, creating a second revenue stream:

The “PHD” Course:

“Pimping Hoes Degree” - they weren’t hiding what they were teaching. The course promised to show young men how to:

  • “Convert girls” into workers

  • Use romance as recruitment tool

  • Establish control and compliance

  • Structure revenue extraction

  • Build their own webcam operations

This is trafficking-as-a-service. Not only exploiting women directly, but teaching thousands of men to do the same.

Hustler’s University / The Real World:

Broader courses on:

  • Wealth building and entrepreneurship

  • “Escaping the matrix” (evading regulation?)

  • Fitness and mindset

  • Managing relationships and “employees”

  • Online business models

Price points ranged from monthly subscriptions ($49/month) to more expensive master classes. With tens of thousands of students, this generated millions annually.

The Affiliate Model:

Students who recruited others earned commissions. This created:

  • Distributed marketing (students promoting to recruit)

  • Passive income for top promoters

  • Viral growth through incentivized sharing

  • Army of defenders (financial stake in Tates’ success)

The Network Effect:

Justin Waller, the “third brother,” exemplifies the structure. He helped run the courses, developed independent following, and maintained political connections (including to Barron Trump). About Barron: “He’s not a bad ally to have — let’s be frank.”

The expansion: From direct exploitation (webcam) to teaching exploitation (courses) to building political networks (influence operation).

The Social Media Monetization

The criminal charges became brand-building opportunities:

The Persecution Narrative:

Getting kicked off platforms in 2022 made them famous. Each restriction increased visibility. Being “cancelled” became their selling point. The arrest in December 2022 supercharged their notoriety.

Tucker Carlson Interview:

112 million views. This wasn’t just reputation laundering - it was audience building. Each view represented potential course customers, affiliate marketers, political supporters.

The Revenue Streams:

  • Ad revenue from massive followings

  • Platform subscriptions (Rumble deals, etc.)

  • Sponsored content despite controversies

  • Merchandise lines

  • Personal appearances commanding fees

  • Licensing and partnerships

The Integration:

Each component reinforced others:

  • Notoriety from charges → social media following

  • Following → course sales

  • Courses → army of promoters

  • Promoters → more following

  • Following → political connections

  • Political connections → protection

  • Protection → operation continues → more revenue

The Victim Economics

The financial analysis reveals exploitation’s mathematics:

30+ Women Over 10+ Years:

If £21 million documented, and women received minority shares:

  • Each woman generated hundreds of thousands in revenue

  • Brothers extracted majority of this value

  • Women faced surveillance, control, violence, psychological trauma

  • Some were minors (15, 17 years old)

  • Exit barriers: financial dependence, physical threats, psychological bonds, immigration status

The Cost-Benefit to Controllers:

Initial investment per woman:

  • Romance phase: dates, gifts (thousands)

  • Setup: housing, equipment share (thousands)

  • Control: surveillance, enforcers (shared costs)

Revenue per woman: Hundreds of thousands over months/years

Return on investment: Astronomical, especially when scaled across 30+ women

Legal risk: Managed through jurisdiction shopping (Romania over Britain)

The Barrier to Exit:

Women faced:

  • Financial dependence (money controlled by brothers)

  • Physical threats and violence

  • Psychological manipulation (”this is temporary,” “we’re building our future”)

  • Immigration complications (some foreign nationals)

  • Surveillance making escape planning difficult

  • Shame of admitting relationship was exploitation

Each barrier was cost-effective for controllers: easier to maintain barriers than constantly recruit replacements.

The Teaching Operation: Scaling the Model

The most disturbing aspect: industrializing exploitation by teaching replication.

The Course Content:

While full content isn’t public, marketing revealed:

  • “How to attract and convert women”

  • Building “online business” (webcam operations)

  • “Escaping the matrix” (evading regulation and taxes?)

  • Managing relationships and “employees”

  • Revenue maximization strategies

The Scaling Problem:

If thousands of men take courses, and even 5% attempt to replicate the model, that’s potentially hundreds of new exploitation operations. Romanian prosecutors documented one operation (30+ women). How many more exist undocumented because students followed the blueprint?

The Ideological Cover:

Framed as:

  • “Entrepreneurship” not exploitation

  • “Men helping men” not teaching trafficking

  • “Defending masculinity” not monetizing coercion

  • Critics dismissed as “woke” or “feminist”

This reframing makes recruitment easier: young men think they’re learning business and masculinity, not trafficking techniques.

The Distributed Model:

Traditional trafficking requires:

  • Central organization

  • Physical control of victims

  • Identifiable operations

  • Concentrated risk

The Tates’ teaching model creates:

  • Distributed operations

  • Independent operators

  • Harder to trace and prosecute

  • Risk diffused across thousands

This is trafficking-as-franchise: providing the blueprint, taking a cut through course sales, letting others take enforcement risks.

The Political Protection as Business Investment

The cultivation of Trump world connections was business strategy:

The Cost:

  • Years of relationship building (2017-2024)

  • Supporting Trump publicly (free)

  • Access to their young male following (valuable but cost them nothing)

  • Course access for allies (minimal marginal cost)

The Return:

  • Diplomatic intervention in Romania

  • Prosecutors overruled from above

  • Travel restrictions lifted despite opposition

  • Freedom to continue operations internationally

  • Ongoing protection from U.S. prosecution?

Compare cost to return: Essentially free political cultivation yielded diplomatic intervention worth potentially millions (avoiding Romanian prison, continuing business, maintaining brand).

This is politics as insurance policy. The Tates delivered young male voters. Trump world delivered diplomatic pressure on foreign prosecutors. Both sides profited.

Justin Waller’s Role:

Maintaining connections to Barron Trump while running Tates’ courses shows the integration: political access and business operations aren’t separate - they’re mutually reinforcing. Barron’s connection provides political protection. Waller’s business operations provide wealth. Each enables the other.

The Continuing Operation

Evidence suggests the business never stopped:

During Romanian Restrictions:

Brothers confined but not imprisoned (after initial 3 months). Operations could continue:

  • Digital nature enables remote management

  • Network of associates handling day-to-day

  • Courses continue selling throughout

  • Social media presence maintains following

After U.S. Release:

Andrew’s April 2025 video: “I’m in Dubai, I’m still rich, all I do is win” - driving off in Bugatti.

Travel pattern post-release:

  • Romania (required check-ins)

  • Turkey

  • UAE (Dubai)

  • Multiple international locations

  • Maintaining lavish lifestyle

The message: Business continues, wealth intact, restrictions minimal.

Ongoing Activities:

  • Social media posting never stopped

  • Course sales continue (websites remain active)

  • New assault allegation suggests pattern continues (Beverly Hills, March 2025)

  • Lawsuits against accusers (intimidation, costs victims)

The operation adapted but didn’t stop. Romanian restrictions were worked around. U.S. arrival brought diplomatic intervention. The business model proved resilient.

The Accountability Gap: Why It Persists

Jurisdictional Arbitrage:

  • Operating across multiple countries

  • Moving when scrutiny increases (Britain → Romania)

  • Choosing countries with weaker enforcement

  • Andrew’s explicit statement: Romania where “you can get away with shit”

Cost of moving: Minimal relative to avoiding prosecution

The Digital Dimension:

  • Online operations harder to police

  • Multiple platforms provide redundancy

  • Can rebuild following after bans

  • International nature complicates prosecution

  • Cryptocurrency enables hidden transactions

The business is platform-agnostic and jurisdiction-fluid.

The Political Protection:

  • U.S. administration intervention in Romania

  • Allies in positions of power

  • Conservative media providing cover

  • Young male base creates political value

Political protection isn’t free, but return on investment is massive: diplomatic intervention worth millions, cost mostly just political positioning.

The Revenue Flow:

Money keeps coming in, which enables:

  • Top legal teams (Joseph McBride defended Jan 6 defendants)

  • Political connections (contributions, access)

  • Media management (PR, content creation)

  • Self-reinforcing protection cycle

Each revenue stream funds the protection that enables continuing revenue.

Conclusion: Who’s Going to Make Them Pay It Back?

The Tate brothers earned at least £21 million by systematically exploiting women through a combination of romance fraud, psychological manipulation, surveillance, and violence. When British authorities started investigating, they moved to Romania where, in Andrew’s words, “you can get away with shit you can’t get away with in the West.”

When Romanian authorities charged them, they cultivated political connections that led to diplomatic intervention overriding prosecutors. They’re now teaching thousands of young men to replicate their model through paid courses, having successfully rebranded criminal exploitation as “defending masculinity.”

They found a political movement willing to defend them in exchange for access to their young male following. Conservative media figures misrepresented charges to 112 million people. Trump officials intervened diplomatically despite allegations from 30+ women across multiple countries.

The business model was exploitation. The marketing was masculinity. And the protection was political.

Every element had a price and a return:

  • Women’s bodies → £21M+ in webcam revenue

  • Teaching exploitation → Millions in course sales

  • Controversy → Massive social media following

  • Following → Political value

  • Political value → Diplomatic intervention

  • Intervention → Freedom to continue

  • Freedom → Ongoing revenue

The cycle is self-reinforcing and, from a business perspective, highly successful.

The question isn’t whether there’s evidence. The question isn’t whether the pattern is clear. The question isn’t even whether the system worked - it demonstrably did.

The question is: Who’s going to break the cycle?

Romanian prosecutors were overruled. British charges are pending. American investigation continues but faces political headwinds. The brothers travel internationally, maintain their wealth, grow their following, and teach others to replicate their model.

The Tates made £21 million from alleged exploitation of 30+ women. They spent years building political connections. They delivered young male voters to Trump world. In exchange, they got diplomatic intervention when they needed it.

The transaction was explicit. The timeline is documented. The results are undeniable.

But here’s the larger question: If this model works - if exploitation can be monetized, rebranded as politics, protected through diplomatic intervention, and continued indefinitely - what’s to stop the next operation?

The Tates aren’t just alleged criminals who evaded accountability. They’re proof of concept that the system works. They documented their model, taught it to thousands, and showed that political protection is available for the right price.

And that brings us back to where this series began.

Gloria Steinem fought for women’s liberation from a position of structural disadvantage without exploiting men. The suffragettes didn’t need to traffic or coerce anyone to win the vote. Betty Friedan didn’t build her movement on the backs of male victims. Real liberation movements seek equality, not domination. They fought from disadvantage without creating victims.

The Tates claim to defend masculinity - already the structurally advantaged position - while allegedly building a £21 million operation by systematically exploiting women. Then they taught thousands of men to replicate it. They operated from advantage by monetizing exploitation and calling it masculinity.

That comparison reveals everything about the con.

Prostitution may be the world’s oldest profession, but the Tates found something to sell that’s even more valuable: Trump’s accountability evasion system itself. Not just the exploitation - that’s ancient. The innovation was packaging it as a teachable model, complete with political protection for the right price.

The system works, and it’s for sale. The courses are still online. The blueprint is documented. The political protection has been demonstrated. And thousands of young men have already enrolled.

They made £21 million. The question isn’t just who’s going to make them pay it back.

The question is: How many more operations are already following their blueprint?

How many young men who took “Pimping Hoes Degree” are now running their own operations?

How many have learned that if you wrap exploitation in “defending masculinity” rhetoric, deliver a useful voting demographic, and cultivate the right political connections, accountability becomes optional?

The Tates didn’t just evade consequences - they franchised the evasion system and proved it works internationally. That’s not a masculinity movement. That’s an organized criminal enterprise with political protection, excellent marketing, and a proven business model.

And unlike the suffragettes who fought for rights without requiring victims, the Tates built an empire that requires victims by design - and taught others to do the same.
