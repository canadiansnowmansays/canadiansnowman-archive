The Guardian: Khamenei's Iran

Khamenei’s Iran

Part 11: The Guardian's Account

April 16, 2026

Editor’s Note

This essay was written in January 2026 as a prospective reckoning — holding a living man accountable to the standards he set for himself over 37 years. By the time you read it, the subject is dead. Killed on February 28, 2026, by the same external enemies he spent his entire rule invoking to justify every domestic failure.

I have not changed a word of the analysis. A posthumous indictment is still an indictment. The verdict does not expire because the defendant is no longer present to contest it. If anything, death has closed the record: there will be no final speech, no comeback, no reframing. The evidence is what it is.

On March 9, 2026, the Assembly of Experts — under pressure from the IRGC — named Mojtaba Khamenei, the Supreme Leader's son, as his successor. The revolution that overthrew a monarchy just completed a dynastic transfer of power. The Guardian's final act was to become the Shah.

The seven revolutionary promises examined in this essay. His own words. His own standards. You can judge for yourself whether the Guardian guarded anything worth keeping.

— Stephen, April 2026

Final Reckoning by Revolutionary Standards

Iranians crowd the streets in jubilation after Shah Pahlavi was overthrown

On February 11, 1979, the Islamic Revolution overthrew Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi. After 2,500 years of monarchy, Iran became an Islamic Republic.

The revolution made promises. Seven specific commitments that justified overthrowing the existing order and establishing a theocratic state.

For 37 years, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has ruled as Guardian of that revolution. His title—Rahbar, meaning Guide or Leader—explicitly charges him with protecting and advancing the revolution’s principles.

This isn’t Western liberal democratic standards being imposed on Iran. This is Iran’s own revolutionary framework, established by Iranians, articulated in their constitution, and proclaimed in thousands of speeches.

Khamenei set the standards. Let’s measure him against them.

Not what critics wanted. Not what might have been. But what he himself promised and what the revolution he guards claimed to deliver.

By those standards—the only standards that matter when judging a revolutionary guardian—here is the account.

I. THE SEVEN PROMISES

When Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini led the revolution in 1979, these commitments formed the moral foundation:

Promise #1: Economic Justice

“Islam is the religion of those who struggle for justice, the religion of those who seek independence... Islam is the religion of those who desire freedom and independence. It is the school of those who fight against colonialism.” —Khomeini, 1979

What this meant:

  • End corruption and cronyism of Shah’s regime

  • Equitable distribution of oil wealth

  • Economic independence from foreign powers

  • Priority to the mostazafin (oppressed/dispossessed)

  • Islamic economics prioritizing social welfare over individual enrichment

Promise #2: Political Freedom

“We have always supported freedom of expression in the framework of Islam... The people must be free to elect and to be elected.” —Khomeini, 1979

What this meant:

  • Democracy within Islamic framework

  • Popular sovereignty through elections

  • Freedom of speech and assembly (within Islamic bounds)

  • End to SAVAK (Shah’s secret police) brutality

  • Government accountable to people

Promise #3: National Sovereignty

“Neither East, nor West—Islamic Republic!” —Revolutionary slogan

What this meant:

  • Independence from both US (West) and USSR (East)

  • No foreign military bases

  • Control over national resources (oil)

  • Self-determination in foreign policy

  • End to great power interference

Promise #4: Islamic Justice

“In Islam, the legislative power and competence to establish laws belong exclusively to God Almighty.” —Khomeini, Constitution preamble

What this meant:

  • Sharia law as foundation

  • Justice (adalah) as central principle

  • Protection of oppressed (mustazafin)

  • Moral society based on Quranic principles

  • Rule of law (Islamic law) over rule of men

Promise #5: Social Dignity

“The government is not alien to the people, it does not lord over them, and it is not based on the interests of a certain group or class.” —Khomeini, 1979

What this meant:

  • End to class divisions of Shah’s era

  • Dignity for all Iranians regardless of background

  • Social mobility through merit and piety

  • Government serving all people, not elite

  • Respect for Islamic values in public life

Promise #6: Anti-Imperialism

“Export the revolution!” —Revolutionary doctrine

What this meant:

  • Support for oppressed Muslims globally

  • Resistance to Western/US dominance

  • Challenge to Sunni monarchies allied with West

  • Leadership of Islamic world

  • Model for other nations to follow

Promise #7: Guard the Revolution

“The system of the Islamic Republic is a system that directly and indirectly assumes the perpetual responsibility of defending the rights of the oppressed.” —Iranian Constitution, Article 154

What this meant:

  • Preserve revolutionary values across generations

  • Prevent counter-revolution or restoration

  • Maintain Islamic character of government

  • Ensure revolution’s gains endure

  • Pass revolutionary legacy to future intact

These weren’t aspirational hopes. They were explicit commitments, written into the constitution, proclaimed in speeches, taught in schools, and used to justify everything from laws to wars.

For 37 years, Khamenei claimed to guard these promises.

Let’s see how he did.

II. ECONOMIC JUSTICE: THE POVERTY RECORD

The Promise: Economic justice, equitable distribution of oil wealth, priority to the oppressed.

The Reality, January 2026:

50%+ of Iranians live below poverty line (official government statistics, likely understated)

60% of Iranian children suffer malnutrition (UNICEF data)

48% annual inflation rate (2024 official figures)

Currency collapse: Iranian rial fell to 1.4 million per USD (December 2025)—down from 70 to 1 USD in 1979

Unemployment: Official rate ~14%, but youth unemployment (ages 15-29) exceeds 25%

Where Did the Money Go?

Iran is one of world’s largest oil producers. Even with sanctions, oil revenue has been substantial. In the decade 2010-2020 alone, Iran earned approximately $500+ billion from oil exports.

So where is it?

IRGC Economic Empire:

The Revolutionary Guards control approximately 40% of Iran’s economy through:

  • Bonyads (ostensibly charitable foundations) that control billions in assets

  • Front companies in construction, telecommunications, smuggling

  • Control of major ports and airports (smuggling access)

  • Black market operations circumventing sanctions

The IRGC enriched itself while claiming to serve the mostazafin.

Clerical Foundations:

Major religious foundations (bonyads) like Astan Quds Razavi (controlling Imam Reza shrine in Mashhad) manage assets worth tens of billions, operate tax-free, face no accountability, and report only to Supreme Leader.

Foreign Proxy Spending:

While Iranians starve, the regime spent billions supporting:

  • Hezbollah in Lebanon ($700 million annually, according to Hezbollah officials)

  • Assad regime in Syria ($15-20 billion total over decade, per some estimates)

  • Houthis in Yemen (hundreds of millions)

  • Hamas and Palestinian Islamic Jihad (hundreds of millions)

  • Shia militias in Iraq (substantial sums)

Corruption at Every Level:

Transparency International’s Corruption Perceptions Index consistently ranks Iran in bottom third globally. Systematic bribery, nepotism, and embezzlement define Iranian governance.

The Verdict on Economic Justice

By the revolution’s own standard—prioritizing the mostazafin—the system is catastrophic failure.

The poor are poorer than under the Shah when adjusted for inflation and purchasing power. The oppressed are more oppressed. The wealth flows upward to IRGC generals, clerical foundations, and connected elites.

This isn’t economic justice. It’s economic predation dressed in revolutionary rhetoric.

Khamenei’s Response to Economic Protests:

When Iranians protested economic conditions in 2017-19, chanting “Leave Syria, think about us!” and “Death to the dictator,” Khamenei:

  • Blamed foreign enemies

  • Ordered violent crackdown

  • Killed 1,500+ protesters (November 2019 alone)

  • Arrested thousands

  • Changed nothing about economic policy

When Tehran’s Grand Bazaar merchants—traditionally conservative, often regime supporters—closed shops in protest (December 28, 2025), security forces responded with bullets.

The Guardian of economic justice kills those demanding economic justice.

Promise #1: Failed

III. POLITICAL FREEDOM: THE REPRESSION RECORD

The Promise: Democracy within Islamic framework, freedom of expression, elected government accountable to people.

The Reality:

Election Rigging

Guardian Council vetting: Unelected 12-member body (6 appointed directly by Khamenei, 6 by his appointed judiciary chief) approves all candidates for every election.

2024 parliamentary elections: Of 15,200 candidates, Guardian Council disqualified ~12,000 (approximately 80%). Only regime loyalists allowed.

2021 presidential election: All serious reformist and moderate candidates disqualified. Only hardliners permitted to run. Turnout: 48%—lowest in Islamic Republic history, signaling public rejection.

2009 Green Movement: When millions protested election fraud (Ahmadinejad vs. Mousavi), Khamenei:

  • Declared Ahmadinejad winner before vote counting completed

  • Ordered violent crackdown

  • Killed dozens (official count), likely hundreds

  • Imprisoned thousands

  • Placed Mousavi and Karroubi under house arrest (where they remain 17 years later)

Imprisoned Journalists, Activists, and Dissidents

Iran consistently ranks among world’s worst jailers of journalists (Reporters Without Borders).

Narges Mohammadi: Nobel Peace Prize winner (2023), imprisoned for advocating women’s rights and opposing death penalty. Currently serving multiple sentences totaling decades. Denied medical care. Husband and children in exile.

Political prisoners: Estimated 10,000+ imprisoned for political activism, journalism, or dissent.

Torture widespread: UN Special Rapporteur on Iran consistently documents systematic torture in Iranian prisons.

Execution Record

Iran has one of world’s highest execution rates per capita.

2023 alone: 853 executions (Iran Human Rights documentation), second only to China globally.

Many executions political: Protesters from 2022 “Woman, Life, Freedom” movement executed after rushed trials. Charges often include moharebeh (enmity against God)—death penalty offense applied to political dissent.

1988 Prison Massacres: Estimated 5,000+ political prisoners executed in summer 1988, mostly MEK (Mujahedin-e-Khalq) members but also leftists. Mass graves still concealed. Perpetrators never held accountable. Some hold high government positions today.

Internet Censorship

Iran operates one of world’s most sophisticated internet censorship systems:

  • Social media blocked (Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube)

  • VPNs criminalized (though widely used)

  • Internet shutdowns during protests (2019, 2022, 2025)

  • Regime operates “halal internet” with government-controlled content

Media Control

All broadcast media state-controlled. Print media requires licenses (easily revoked). Independent journalism effectively criminalized.

Freedom of Assembly

All public gatherings require government permission. Protests = automatic violent crackdown.

January 2026: Nationwide protests met with live ammunition, heavy machine guns, deliberate targeting of heads and vital organs.

The Verdict on Political Freedom

This isn’t democracy within Islamic framework. This is dictatorship using Islam as justification.

Elections are theater. Media is propaganda. Dissent is criminalized. Protest is death sentence.

The revolution promised freedom from SAVAK brutality. It delivered IRGC brutality plus religious justification.

Khamenei’s Own Words:

“Democracy is for the West. We have an Islamic system.” —Khamenei, 2009 (after Green Movement)

Translation: The promise of democratic participation was lie.

Promise #2: Failed

IV. NATIONAL SOVEREIGNTY: THE SUBORDINATION RECORD

The Promise: Independence from great powers, “Neither East nor West,” national self-determination.

The Reality:

Economic Dependence on China

China purchases approximately 80% of Iran’s oil exports. Iran has no leverage—it sells at discounted prices under sanctions, accepts yuan instead of dollars, and tolerates Chinese economic exploitation.

Belt and Road Initiative investments come with Chinese control of ports, infrastructure, and strategic assets. This isn’t partnership—it’s dependency.

Military Dependence on Russia

Iran receives military technology, weapons systems, and satellite intelligence from Russia. When Russia needs support (Ukraine war), Iran provides drones. This isn’t sovereignty—it’s junior partnership in anti-Western axis.

Regional Proxies Collapse

Syria: Assad regime—propped up by tens of billions in Iranian support, thousands of IRGC and proxy fighters, and massive casualties—fled to Moscow in December 2024. Iran’s 13-year investment produced nothing but dead Iranians and lost treasure.

Lebanon: Hezbollah severely weakened after Israel eliminated much of its leadership and destroyed significant military infrastructure (2024). Iran’s most capable proxy crippled.

Yemen: Houthis remain active but haven’t delivered Iran strategic advantage beyond harassing shipping (which brings international retaliation).

Iraq: Shia militias create some influence but can’t prevent Iraq from maintaining relations with US, Saudi Arabia, and other actors Iran opposes.

Palestine: Hamas launched October 7, 2023 attack without informing Iran—demonstrating Iran doesn’t control even its own proxies. Subsequent Israeli response devastated Gaza without advancing Iranian interests.

The June 2025 Humiliation

When US and Israel struck Iranian nuclear facilities and military infrastructure:

  • Iran couldn’t defend its airspace

  • Air defense systems failed

  • Retaliation was ineffective

  • No ally came to Iran’s defense

  • Regime admitted strategic defeat

This was the culmination of 37 years of “Neither East nor West” policy: Neither Russia nor China defended Iran militarily. The supposed independence revealed as isolation.

The Verdict on National Sovereignty

Iran is:

  • Economically dependent on China

  • Militarily dependent on Russia

  • Regionally isolated (no Arab allies)

  • Internationally sanctioned

  • Strategically defeated (June 2025)

This isn’t sovereignty. This is weakness masquerading as resistance.

Promise #3: Failed

V. ISLAMIC JUSTICE: THE HYPOCRISY RECORD

The Promise: Sharia law, Islamic justice, protection of the oppressed.

The Reality:

Corruption Contradicts Islamic Law

Islam explicitly prohibits:

  • Bribery (rishwa)—pervasive in Iranian government

  • Theft (sariqah)—IRGC steals national wealth

  • Oppression (zulm)—systematic torture violates Islamic prohibition

  • Injustice (jawr)—rigged elections and political prisons violate Islamic justice principles

The system violates Islamic law daily while claiming to enforce it.

Selective Application

Strict enforcement for ordinary Iranians:

  • Women arrested for hijab violations

  • Youth imprisoned for Western music

  • Alcohol consumption = flogging

  • Adultery = stoning (though rarely implemented)

No enforcement for elite:

  • IRGC generals live lavishly (Islamic modesty ignored)

  • Clerical families accumulate wealth (Islamic redistribution ignored)

  • Officials’ children party in Dubai (Islamic morality ignored)

Killing Contradicts Islamic Principles

Quran 5:32: “Whoever kills a soul unless for a soul or for corruption in the land—it is as if he had slain mankind entirely.”

January 2026: Thousands of Iranian protesters killed for demanding economic justice and political freedom.

By Islamic law, this is zulm—oppression and injustice. The Guardian commits the very crimes Islam prohibits.

The Mahdi Claim

Khamenei and regime propagandists claim to be preparing for return of Mahdi (Hidden Imam in Shia theology). But Mahdi is prophesied to bring justice, end oppression, establish righteous government.

Current system produces opposite: injustice, oppression, corrupt government.

Claiming to prepare for Mahdi while creating exactly the conditions Mahdi is supposed to eliminate isn’t piety—it’s blasphemy.

The Verdict on Islamic Justice

This isn’t Islamic justice. This is dictatorship using Islam as costume.

The Guardian violates Islamic principles of justice, mercy, honesty, and protection of the weak—all while claiming to enforce Islamic law.

Promise #4: Failed

VI. SOCIAL DIGNITY: THE HUMILIATION RECORD

The Promise: Government serves all people, dignity for every Iranian, end to class divisions.

The Reality:

Morality Police

Iranian women beaten, arrested, and imprisoned for hijab “violations.” Mahsa Amini died in custody September 2022 after morality police arrest for “improper hijab”—sparking nationwide protests.

This isn’t dignity. This is state-sanctioned humiliation of women.

Generational Divide

Youth (60% of population under 30) face:

  • No economic opportunity

  • No political voice

  • No cultural freedom

  • No hope for future

Regime treats them as threats, not citizens. The revolution that claimed to serve the people now fears the people.

Brain Drain

Educated Iranians flee. Doctors, engineers, scientists, entrepreneurs—anyone with skills and resources emigrates.

World Bank estimates: Iran loses 150,000-180,000 educated professionals annually to brain drain. That’s the population rejecting the system with their feet.

Daily Blackouts

Despite being major oil producer, Iran can’t keep electricity running consistently. Daily blackouts humiliate population and demonstrate government incompetence.

Water Shortages

Lake Urmia (once one of world’s largest salt lakes) has shrunk 90%. Rivers run dry. Cities face water rationing. Government mismanagement plus climate change = disaster.

Farmers who protested water shortages (2021 Isfahan protests): shot by security forces.

The Verdict on Social Dignity

The government doesn’t serve the people—it survives off them. Every interaction between state and citizen reinforces hierarchy, humiliation, and helplessness.

Promise #5: Failed

VII. ANTI-IMPERIALISM: THE STRATEGIC FAILURE RECORD

The Promise: Export revolution, lead Islamic world, resist Western imperialism.

The Reality:

“Axis of Resistance” Collapsed

The regional network Iran spent decades and billions building:

  • Syria: Assad fled, Iran’s investment wasted

  • Lebanon: Hezbollah weakened severely

  • Yemen: Houthis can’t deliver strategic advantage

  • Gaza: Hamas devastated, Palestinian cause not advanced

  • Iraq: Shia militias don’t translate to Iranian control

Sunni World Rejects Iran

Iran claimed to lead Islamic world. Result:

  • Saudi Arabia: Regional rival, recently reconciled somewhat but fundamentally opposed

  • UAE: Anti-Iran, allied with Israel

  • Egypt: Opposes Iranian influence

  • Jordan: Firmly in Western camp

  • Turkey: Rival for regional influence

  • Pakistan: Limited cooperation, fundamentally suspicious

Sunni-majority Islamic world (85% of Muslims) views Shia Iran with suspicion at best, hostility at worst.

Normalization Deals Isolate Iran

Abraham Accords (UAE, Bahrain, Morocco, Sudan normalizing with Israel) demonstrated: Arab states prioritize their own interests over Palestinian cause or Iranian “resistance axis.”

Iran’s revolutionary anti-Zionism failed to prevent Arab-Israeli normalization. The resistance axis couldn’t resist.

The Verdict on Anti-Imperialism

Iran didn’t defeat imperialism—it created opening for it.

By destabilizing region (Iraq, Syria, Yemen, Lebanon), Iran gave US and Israel justification for intervention. By threatening neighbors, Iran drove Arab states toward Israel and US.

The “resistance” produced strategic disaster.

Promise #6: Failed

VIII. GUARD THE REVOLUTION: THE BETRAYAL RECORD

The Promise: Preserve revolutionary values, prevent counter-revolution, maintain Islamic character.

The Reality:

January 2026: Protesters across all 31 provinces chant:

  • “Death to Khamenei!”

  • “This country won’t be fixed until we bury the mullahs!”

  • “This year is the year of blood, Khamenei will be overthrown!”

Even regime strongholds—Qom (religious center), Tehran’s Grand Bazaar (traditional supporters)—witness protests.

The revolution’s own base rejects the revolution.

What the Guardian Guarded

Not revolutionary values—but his personal power.

Not Islamic justice—but systematic oppression.

Not the people’s interests—but elite enrichment.

Not Iran’s sovereignty—but regional ambitions that bankrupted the country.

The Revolutionary Betrayal

The 1979 revolution promised:

  • Justice—delivered poverty

  • Freedom—delivered repression

  • Sovereignty—delivered dependence

  • Dignity—delivered humiliation

  • Islamic government—delivered corruption dressed in religious language

Khamenei didn’t guard the revolution. He buried it beneath 37 years of lies, bullets, and brutality.

The Verdict on Guarding the Revolution

When the people reject the revolution, when protesters burn the Supreme Leader’s image, when traditional supporters close shops in protest, when slogans call for overthrowing the entire system—the Guardian has failed absolutely.

Promise #7: Failed

IX. THE FINAL ACCOUNT

Seven promises. Seven failures.

This isn’t close call. This isn’t mixed record. This is comprehensive, systematic, total failure measured by the revolution’s own standards.

Economic justice? 50% poverty while IRGC generals grow rich.

Political freedom? Elections rigged, dissent crushed, thousands imprisoned.

National sovereignty? Dependent on China and Russia, regionally isolated, militarily humiliated.

Islamic justice? Corruption, torture, killing in the name of Islam.

Social dignity? Daily humiliation, generational despair, mass emigration.

Anti-imperialism? Strategic disaster, Arab-Israeli normalization, resistance axis collapsed.

Guard the revolution? The people want to overthrow it.

The Historical Parallel

Khamenei resembles no one so much as the man he helped overthrow: Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi.

The Shah promised: Modernization, prosperity, strength.

The Shah delivered: Corruption, repression, dependence on US.

Result: Revolution overthrew him.

Khamenei promised: Islamic justice, resistance, dignity.

Khamenei delivered: Corruption, repression, dependence on Russia/China.

Result: The people want revolution again.

The Guardian became the thing he claimed to guard against.

By His Own Standards

This isn’t Western criticism. This is Iranian criticism using Iranian standards established by Iranian revolutionaries.

The Islamic Republic set the terms. Khamenei accepted the responsibility. The results speak.

The Bunker’s Meaning

In June 2025, Khamenei spent twelve days underground while his country was bombed. That image captures his entire legacy:

Hidden from reality

Protected by concrete and lies

Isolated from the people he claimed to serve

Defended by those with guns, not legitimacy

Surviving, not leading

The revolution promised heaven on earth under Islamic rule. Khamenei delivered a bunker underground.

And soon, it won’t be enough.
