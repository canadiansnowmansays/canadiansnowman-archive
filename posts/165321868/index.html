The Trump Grift

Part 6 The Reckoning: When the Marks Realize They're Not the House

July 11, 2025

CanadianSnowmanSays
July 11, 2025

The End of the American Experiment?

BOTTOM LINE UP FRONT: We began this series with a scene from January 2025: the President of the United States launching a $TRUMP cryptocurrency three days before his inauguration. Conservative radio host Rebecca Davis, who invested $32 believing in Trump's business acumen, watched her investment shrink to $11 as 764,000 people lost money on his meme coin.

"There was definitely a lot of influential people that had posted online about it that got me hyped up," she said. "Then when it tanked, I was like, 'Whoa, what the hell?'"

Rebecca's story isn't just about a bad investment—it's about the moment when the marks realize they're not the house. And when that realization spreads beyond cryptocurrency to encompass the entire American political system, we're looking at something much more dangerous than financial fraud.

We're looking at the collapse of faith in democratic institutions themselves.

The Evolution of a Grift

Over five parts, we've traced the evolution of the Trump family's wealth extraction system across three generations:

🏗️ The Foundation (1940s)

Fred Trump created the template in Brooklyn: access other people's money, extract maximum value, externalize all risks, let others absorb the losses.

🎰 The Perfection (1980s-2010s)

Donald Trump perfected it through six bankruptcies, failed licensing deals, and criminal tax fraud convictions, proving you could profit spectacularly from failure as long as you structured the deals correctly.

🏛️ The Systematization (2017-2021)

The Trump family systematized it during the first presidency, with each member finding their own angle to monetize government access—Chinese trademarks, Saudi investments, NFT portraits, and half-million-dollar access clubs.

⚡ The Weaponization (2025-Present)

The second presidency weaponized it completely, abandoning any pretense of separation between public service and private profit through:

  • $TRUMP cryptocurrency launched 3 days before inauguration

  • $400 million Qatar jet accepted as "gift"

  • Trump Mobile phone service at $47.45/month

  • $249 "Victory" fragrances promising to smell like success

  • $26+ million in taxpayer-funded golf trips to Trump properties

Chapter 1: The Brand Extension Explosion

Trump's second presidency hasn't just continued the grift—it has systematized and scaled it beyond anything previously imagined. The traditional boundaries between presidential duties and personal branding have been obliterated.

📱 The Mobile Phone Empire

June 2025: The Trump Organization launched "Trump Mobile," offering "The 47 Plan" at $47.45 per month—a reference to Trump being the 45th and 47th president.

Key Features:

  • No contracts or credit checks required

  • Promises "same coverage as the 3 nationwide phone service carriers"

  • $499 "T1 Phone" in gold with Trump styling

  • 256GB storage, "proudly designed and built in the United States"

The Fine Print: Like other Trump ventures, this is primarily a licensing deal. The company disclaims that products "are not designed, developed, manufactured, distributed or sold by The Trump Organization," yet profits flow to Trump's business empire through licensing fees.

🌸 The Fragrance of Victory

July 2025: Trump launched $249 "Victory 45-47" fragrances for men and women, marketed as being "all about Winning, Strength, and Success."

The Products:

  • Bottles topped with golden Trump statuettes

  • Marketed as a "rallying cry in a bottle"

  • Sold through same infrastructure as Trump sneakers

  • Trump made $2.5 million from sneakers and fragrances in 2024 alone

The Timing: The fragrance launch came just as Senate Republicans passed massive cuts to SNAP and Medicaid that could deprive more than 10 million Americans of health insurance.

Chapter 2: The Mounting Victims

The human cost of the Trump grift is staggering, and it's accelerating:

🎓 The Students: Trump University's $25 Million Lesson

Bob Guillo paid $35,000 for Trump's "real estate secrets" and got nothing but recycled internet content and worthless seminars.

"The more and more I got involved in Trump University, the more and more I found out that I had truly been scammed," he told NPR.

The Damage: 7,000 students lost money before Trump agreed to a $25 million settlement—paid not by Trump, but by his business partner.

💰 The Crypto Marks: 764,000 Wallets and Counting

The meme coin carnage continues unfolding:

  • 764,000 people lost money buying $TRUMP cryptocurrency

  • Current price: $9.58—down 87% from its all-time high of $74.27

  • Trump's holdings were valued at more than $20 billion at the peak

  • Rebecca Davis represents millions of Trump supporters who believed and got fleeced

🏌️ The Taxpayers: Over $26 Million for Golf

American taxpayers are funding the world's most expensive advertising campaign for Trump properties:

  • By March 2025: Golf trips cost $26,127,531

  • 18 of his first 69 days in office spent at golf courses

  • On pace to exceed the $151.5 million spent during his first term

  • Equivalent cost: Could provide Medicaid for thousands or pay hundreds of federal workers for years

👥 The Workers: Federal Employees Cut

Trump's DOGE cuts eliminated jobs while protecting his allies:

  • 10,000 federal workers fired in February 2025

  • 4,000 more cut in March

  • Pattern clear: Those who enrich Trump get protected, those who cross him get fired

Chapter 3: The Institutional Destruction

What makes Trump's second-term grift particularly dangerous is how systematically he's destroyed the guardrails designed to prevent exactly this kind of corruption.

⚖️ The Captured Courts

The Supreme Court handed Trump a constitutional get-out-of-jail-free card that fundamentally altered the presidency itself.

The immunity ruling means Trump can:

  • ✅ Accept the Qatar jet → "Official act" securing transportation

  • ✅ Promote $TRUMP cryptocurrency → "Official communication"

  • ✅ Take golf trips → "Official meetings" and "working vacations"

  • ✅ Family business deals → "Economic diplomacy"

🏛️ The Neutered Congress

  • Democrats: Lack power for meaningful oversight

  • Republicans: Defend Trump regardless of evidence

  • Result: Legislative branch's investigative powers neutralized

🔒 The Regulatory Protection Racket

Trump weaponized federal agencies to serve his business interests:

  • SEC enforcement rolled back during crypto launches

  • FAA investigators fired after targeting Trump allies

  • Labor Department oversight gutted

  • DOGE contracts worth $500 million awarded to supporters

📱 The Propaganda Platform

Truth Social serves as both official presidential communication and private money-maker:

  • Policy announcements drive traffic to his company

  • Government business increases his stock value

  • Revenue generation for his family

  • Boundary between government and profit completely erased

Chapter 4: International Implications

The Trump grift has transformed American foreign policy into a business development opportunity with devastating implications for national security.

🛩️ The Qatar Model: $400 Million "Gift"

The Deal:

  • Most valuable foreign gift ever offered to U.S. government

  • Despite Trump's claims, his administration approached Qatar first

  • Will require $400 million to $1 billion in taxpayer upgrades

  • After serving as Air Force One, transfers to Trump Presidential Library

The Conflicts:

  • Attorney General Pam Bondi previously lobbied for Qatar ($115,000/month)

  • Trump Organization investing $5.5 billion in Qatar golf resort

  • Creates constitutional crisis over foreign emoluments

🌍 Foreign Governments Learn the Game

Countries can now influence U.S. policy by:

  • 💰 Investing in Trump cryptocurrencies through anonymous wallets

  • 🏗️ Partnering on Trump Organization projects

  • ✈️ Providing "gifts" like luxury jets

  • 📈 Buying Truth Social stock or advertising

Result: America's foreign policy influenced by whoever can figure out how to enrich the Trump family.

🎯 Security Vulnerabilities

Foreign intelligence services now have multiple attack vectors:

  • Manipulating crypto markets to affect Trump's wealth

  • Targeting Truth Social algorithms and content

  • Exploiting business relationships with Trump properties

  • Using anonymous channels to reward or punish Trump

Chapter 5: The Normalization Crisis

Perhaps the most dangerous aspect is how Trump's corruption is becoming normalized through systematic exhaustion of public outrage.

😴 The Exhaustion Strategy

  • Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics: Documented 3,403 conflicts in first term

  • That's nearly 3 per day

  • When everything is a scandal, nothing is a scandal

🛡️ The Partisan Shield

Trump supporters conditioned to see corruption as partisan attacks:

  • Meme coin victims blame "hype," not fraud

  • Golf trip costs seen as "Trump's weekend routine"

  • Corruption viewed as evidence of business sophistication

📺 The Media Complicity

Mainstream coverage treats systematic corruption as personality quirks rather than threats to democracy.

Chapter 6: Global Democratic Backsliding

The Trump grift follows a predictable international pattern of democratic breakdown:

📊 The Erosion Sequence

  1. Normal corruption (campaign finance, lobbying)

  2. Institutional capture (courts, agencies)

  3. Open monetization (direct profit from office) ← We are here

  4. Systematic extraction (government serves private interests)

  5. Authoritarian consolidation (democracy becomes facade)

🌐 The International Examples

Hungary's Orbán, Turkey's Erdoğan, Venezuela's Maduro all followed similar paths:

  • Use democratic elections to gain power

  • Systematically dismantle democratic institutions

  • Enrich themselves and families

  • Trump's system is more sophisticated than most

Chapter 7: Resistance Points

Despite the systematic nature of corruption, pressure points for accountability remain:

🏛️ State-Level Action

  • State prosecutors can investigate Trump businesses

  • Can't be fired by Trump

  • Represent last line of legal accountability

📝 Congressional Documentation

  • Democrats documenting corruption scope

  • Crucial for future investigations and reforms

  • Historical record being preserved

👥 Grassroots Pressure

  • FOIA requests for transparency

  • Support for oversight organizations

  • Media pressure for aggressive coverage

🗳️ Electoral Consequences

  • 2026 midterms and 2028 presidential election

  • Ultimate test: Do Americans prefer democracy or oligarchy?

Chapter 8: What We've Learned

This six-part series revealed crucial truths about the Trump family and American politics:

🧬 The Template is Generational

Three-generation wealth extraction system that evolved from Brooklyn tax schemes to global corruption networks.

📈 Corruption Scales

What worked for 1940s real estate deals now works for international diplomacy.

🏛️ Institutions Are Fragile

American democracy wasn't designed for systematic family enterprises dedicated to wealth extraction.

⚖️ Legal Loopholes Enable Everything

Sophisticated lawyers find ways to extract wealth while maintaining technical legality.

🎯 The Base Enables It

Trump's supporters often become his biggest victims, yet continue supporting him.

Chapter 9: The Choice Before Us

America faces a fundamental choice about what kind of country we want to be:

🛤️ Option 1: Accept the New Normal

Accept that presidents will use office to enrich themselves and families.

↳ This path leads to oligarchy—political power exists primarily to generate wealth for those in charge.

⚡ Option 2: Demand Accountability

Insist that public office serves public interest, not private profit.

This requires:

  • Legal accountability: Prosecuting violations of law

  • Political accountability: Electoral consequences for corruption

  • Institutional accountability: Strengthening ethics rules

  • Economic accountability: Cutting off profit streams

⏰ The Window Is Closing

The longer corruption continues without consequences, the more normal it becomes. We're approaching a tipping point where corruption becomes irreversible.

Chapter 10: The Final Reckoning

We're not dealing with ordinary political corruption. The Trump family has created something unprecedented—a systematic wealth extraction machine operating from the highest levels of government.

🎰 The House Always Wins

The Trump family turned American government into their casino, where they always win and everyone else always loses.

The marks funding the operation:

  • Trump University students

  • Casino investors

  • Meme coin buyers

  • Truth Social stockholders

  • American taxpayers

💡 The Moment of Recognition

Rebecca Davis's reaction—"Whoa, what the hell?"—represents the crucial moment when marks realize they're suckers.

That recognition is spreading as Americans realize:

  • ❌ Their tax dollars fund Trump's golf advertising

  • ❌ Their investments lose money while he profits

  • ❌ Their support benefits him more than them

  • ❌ Their government serves his interests, not theirs

❓ The Ultimate Question

Is America a country where public office serves the public interest, or is it a business opportunity for those clever enough to capture political power?

The answer determines whether we remain a democracy or become an oligarchy with democratic window dressing.

🏛️ The Legacy

Three generations of wealth extraction philosophy applied to the presidency:

  • Fred Trump: Taught that wealth flows to those clever enough to extract it

  • Donald Trump: Proved any system can become a wealth extraction mechanism

  • The Trump Children: Now systematizing presidential monetization

The result: cryptocurrency launched days before inauguration, $400 million foreign jets accepted as "gifts," taxpayer-funded golf trips generating presidential revenue, gold-plated smartphones sold to "real Americans," and $249 perfumes promising to smell like "Winning, Strength, and Success."

They turned patriotism into a product, democracy into a casino, and citizenship into a subscription service.

The Choice

The American people face the same choice Rebecca Davis faced after losing money on Trump's cryptocurrency: Do you keep playing a rigged game, or do you walk away?

For Rebecca, it was a $32 lesson.
For America, it's the future of democracy itself.

The house always wins—but only as long as people keep playing. The moment enough Americans understand that they're not the house but the customers, the game changes fundamentally.

Rebecca Davis lost $32 and learned her lesson.

The question is: What will it take for America to learn its lesson?

Because in the end, the Trump grift only works as long as we let it. The house is counting on your apathy.

Don't give them that satisfaction.
