The $400 Million Hour

Part 41: How the President’s Truth Social Account Became the Most Profitable Trading Desk in History — and Why Nobody Is Investigating It

April 28, 2026

Truth Social: social media platform or the most profitable trading desk in presidential history

The Trump Grift series (a companion investigation) documented how the Trump family extracted $385 million from the $TRUMP meme coin — a financial instrument whose only real purpose, as Anthony Scaramucci put it, was to let anyone in the world "deposit money into the bank account of the President of the USA with a couple of clicks." That series also documented the White House ballroom donors, the Saudi state dinner trades, and the Truth Predict betting platform where Americans wager on outcomes the president directly controls.

Those were the warmup acts.

On the morning of March 23, 2026, someone made between $300 and $400 million off a single Truth Social post. In approximately one hour. On a war whose fictional resolution they apparently knew about before the markets did.

What makes this story different from everything documented in this series so far is not just the scale. It is that Monday’s trade is the fifth documented instance of the same pattern — and Anthony Scaramucci, who worked inside the Trump White House, says there are at least a dozen more.

Let us go through them in order.

Incident One: The $TRUMP Meme Coin — January 17-18, 2025

Three days before his inauguration, Trump launched the $TRUMP meme coin via CIC Digital LLC, an affiliate of the Trump Organization. The launch came without public announcement — appearing first as a stealth offering before Trump promoted it on Truth Social and X.

Analysis confirmed that leaked information about the launch allowed certain traders to buy before the public announcement. Trump-affiliated companies held 80 percent of the 1 billion token supply — giving insiders overwhelming control over the market. The coin surged more than 300 percent overnight, briefly reaching a $13 billion market cap. Within two weeks, it had generated over $100 million in trading fees, captured almost entirely by insiders while retail investors — the marks — bought into a peak they could not see coming.

The Trump family ultimately extracted $385 million from the venture, per Financial Times analysis. Retail investors who bought during the surge lost an average of 62 percent of their investment. The coin has since fallen 86 percent from its high.

Scaramucci’s verdict at the time: “Idi Amin level corruption. Now anyone in world can essentially deposit money into bank account of President of USA with a couple clicks.”

Incident Two: The $MELANIA Coin — January 19, 2025

One day after $TRUMP launched and was still climbing, Melania Trump released her own meme coin — with no public warning. $TRUMP immediately crashed 50 percent as liquidity drained from one coin to the other. Traders with advance knowledge of the dual launch could short $TRUMP into $MELANIA’s announcement and profit on both sides of the move.

The fake BARRON memecoin, unassociated with Trump’s youngest son but capitalizing on the moment, accumulated a $460 million market cap before crashing 95 percent — leaving retail investors holding the rubble while insiders had already exited.

Two coins. Two days. One family. Retail investors left holding losses on both.

Incident Three: The $TRUMP Dinner Announcement — April 23, 2025

Three months after launch, Trump announced that the top 220 holders of $TRUMP coin would be invited to a black-tie gala dinner at his golf course outside Washington, with the top 25 receiving a VIP White House tour.

Analysis confirmed that leaked information about the dinner announcement allowed certain traders to accumulate coins before it went public. When the announcement hit, the coin surged 60 percent as investors rushed to buy enough to qualify for a dinner seat.

The results of who qualified deserve careful reading: a Bloomberg analysis found that 19 of the top 25 winners were likely foreign nationals. The top spot was held by Justin Sun — a Chinese billionaire and blockchain founder facing an active SEC lawsuit alleging fraudulent market manipulation. Sun owned more than $18 million in $TRUMP coin when the contest ended.

The SEC lawsuit against Sun was subsequently paused by the Trump administration.

That sequence — foreign national buys into presidential access vehicle, wins top seat, faces federal prosecution, prosecution paused — is the $TRUMP coin grift reduced to its purest form. The coin is not a meme. It is a mechanism for purchasing favorable treatment from the federal government, denominated in a currency the president’s family controls.

Incident Four: Liberation Day / The Tariff Reversal — April 2-9, 2025

On April 2, 2025, Trump announced sweeping tariffs on imports from most of the world. Markets collapsed. The S&P 500 fell sharply over the following days as investors absorbed what appeared to be a permanent restructuring of global trade.

Then, on the morning of April 9, Trump posted on Truth Social: “BE COOL! Everything is going to work out well. The USA will be bigger and better than ever before!” and “THIS IS A GREAT TIME TO BUY!!!”

Less than four hours later, he announced a 90-day pause on tariffs for most countries. The Nasdaq soared 12.1 percent — its largest single-day gain since 2021. The Dow jumped 7.8 percent — its biggest one-day increase in five years.

Anyone who bought at the open, following Trump’s morning post, made extraordinary returns by the close. Anyone who had bought the day before — with advance knowledge that the pause was coming — made even more.

The trading evidence is documented. A “flurry of call options buying” in S&P 500 and Nasdaq ETFs was detected before the announcement — positions that would profit precisely from the reversal that followed. ProPublica documented more than a dozen US officials who sold stocks before the tariff crash that preceded the reversal — trades that, if timed with knowledge of what was coming, would have been extraordinarily profitable.

Senator Adam Schiff and Senator Ruben Gallego wrote to Trump’s chief of staff demanding an investigation. Senator Elizabeth Warren called it “corruption in plain sight” on the Senate floor. Representative Steven Horsford, questioning Trump’s trade representative as the announcement landed — in real time, during his congressional testimony — asked directly: “If it was a plan, if it was always the plan, how is it not market manipulation?”

The SEC is led by a Trump appointee. No investigation was opened.

And there is one more detail that connects Liberation Day to March 23, 2026: the 10-year Treasury yield. Before Trump’s tariff reversal in April, the yield was approaching 4.40 percent — the level Trump himself had referenced as the threshold that made the bond market “yippy.” Before Trump’s Iran announcement Monday, the yield hit 4.40 percent again. In both cases, the announcement came before the yield crossed the threshold. The 10-year yield is not just a market indicator. It appears to function as the trigger mechanism — the moment the trade becomes maximally profitable, and the announcement materializes.

Incident Five: The Iran TACO Announcement — March 23, 2026

At 7:17 AM Eastern, as Brent crude traded above $114 and the 10-year yield approached 4.40 percent, Trump posted on Truth Social that the United States and Iran had held “VERY GOOD AND PRODUCTIVE CONVERSATIONS REGARDING A COMPLETE AND TOTAL RESOLUTION OF OUR HOSTILITIES IN THE MIDDLE EAST” and that he was postponing strikes on Iranian power plants for five days.

Approximately one hour before that post, someone had placed the following bets: $1.5 billion in notional S&P E-Mini futures — long — and $192 million in crude oil futures — short. The volume was four to six times normal market activity for that window.

Brent fell from $114 to below $96 within minutes of the announcement. S&P futures swung from negative to positive 1.5 percent. The 10-year yield retreated from 4.40 to 4.35 percent. The trade produced between $300 and $400 million in profits.

These figures are not from a Democratic senator’s press release. They come from Anthony Scaramucci — founder of SkyBridge Capital, former White House Communications Director, speaking on Don Lemon’s show Monday morning with the precision of someone who tracks these markets professionally.

Iran denied the talks had taken place. Its foreign ministry called Trump’s announcement “an effort to reduce energy prices and to buy time for implementing his military plans.” A senior Iranian security official said Trump “backed down after Iran’s military threats became credible” and that there were no negotiations. The only confirmed contact was a preliminary phone call between Iran’s foreign minister and US Special Envoy Witkoff — described by three Iranian officials as preliminary discussions, explicitly not negotiations.

Israel launched a new wave of strikes on Tehran’s IRGC headquarters during the announcement of productive peace talks.

The war continued. The markets moved. Someone collected.

The Pattern

Five incidents. Four involving cryptocurrency markets directly controlled by the Trump family. One involving equity and futures markets that moved on a presidential announcement Iran said was fabricated. Scaramucci’s documented count: at least 12 to 15 irregular trading activity reports tied to Trump announcements and reversals across the full period.

“They’re laughing at you and they are laughing at me while they’re doing it,” Scaramucci said Monday. “And the kids have said to people before: this is over, we’re going to be the richest family in the world.”

He then said something that should be quoted in full, because no paraphrase does it justice: “If they don’t come up with a strategy that enriches Donald Trump to get him out, I don’t think he’s going to de-escalate. Someone’s going to go to him and say, ‘Hey, here’s your cut on this real estate deal, on that real estate deal, and let’s de-escalate.’”

Anthony Scaramucci, the founder of SkyBridge Capital

A former White House Communications Director. On the record. On a live podcast. Saying that the most likely path to ending an active war that has killed 13 Americans and 3,000+ Iranians is to find a financial mechanism that personally enriches the president.

The BBC Investigation: Independent Confirmation

On April 20, 2026 — one month after the Iran TACO trade — the BBC published an independent investigation examining trade volume data across several financial markets matched against Trump’s most significant market-moving statements. Their findings confirmed what Scaramucci had been saying and what the five incidents above documented: a consistent pattern of trading spikes hours or sometimes minutes before presidential announcements.

The BBC added critical new evidence across three areas.

First: a previously undocumented oil trade. On March 9, nine days into the Iran war, Trump told CBS News in a phone interview that the conflict was “very complete, pretty much.” Oil plunged 25 percent within minutes of the interview becoming public. But market data shows a massive surge of bets on oil prices falling at 18:29 GMT — a full 47 minutes before the reporter posted about the interview on X at 19:16 GMT. The traders who placed those bets made millions.

This was two weeks before the March 23 TACO trade. The same market. The same instrument. The same pattern: someone betting on oil falling before a Trump statement moved oil prices down. If March 23 was Incident Five, March 9 was its dress rehearsal.

Second: the prediction markets. The BBC documented a trail of suspicious accounts on Polymarket, the blockchain-powered betting platform. One account — username Burdensome-Mix — was created in December 2025, placed $32,500 betting on Venezuela’s President Maduro being ousted by the end of January 2026, won $436,000 when US special forces seized Maduro on January 3, then changed its username and never placed another bet.

Six more accounts were created on Polymarket in February 2026. All wagered on a US strike on Iran by February 28. When Trump confirmed the attacks in the early hours of that day, the six accounts earned $1.2 million between them. Five of those six users have placed no bets since. The sixth subsequently made $163,000 by correctly betting on a US-Iran ceasefire by April 7 — which was announced on that day.

New accounts. Single bets. Correct calls on outcomes the president controls. Cash out. Disappear.

Third — and this is where the institutional architecture becomes visible — the BBC reported that Donald Trump Jr. is an investor in Polymarket and sits on its advisory board. He also acts as a strategic advisor to Kalshi, the other major prediction market platform. The president’s son has a financial stake in the platforms where anonymous accounts are placing winning bets on outcomes his father controls.

That is not circumstantial. That is structural. The same family that launches meme coins, announces tariff reversals, starts and pauses wars, and posts market-moving statements on Truth Social has a financial interest in the prediction markets where those events are being wagered on — by anonymous accounts that appear, win, and vanish.

The BBC also surfaced institutional responses that constitute admissions the problem exists. The White House sent an internal email to staff warning them not to use insider information to place bets on prediction markets. Both Polymarket and Kalshi adopted new anti-insider-trading rules in March 2026. The CFTC chair told a congressional committee his organization had “zero tolerance” for fraud and insider trading — while declining to comment on any of the specific incidents.

Zero tolerance. Zero investigations. Zero consequences.

None of the US financial authorities contacted by the BBC acknowledged any of the allegations. The White House did not respond to a request for comment. The SEC declined to comment on whether it had investigated the trading patterns documented by multiple US senators and now by the BBC itself.

The pattern is no longer a pattern. It is a business model — documented independently by a former White House Communications Director, by Bloomberg terminal data, by blockchain forensic analysts, by US senators in real time, and now by one of the world’s most credible news organizations.

The Pelosi Projection

Three weeks before Monday’s trade, Trump called out Nancy Pelosi by name at the State of the Union for insider trading, to bipartisan applause. “Let’s also ensure that members of Congress cannot corruptly profit from using insider information. Pass the Stop Insider Trading Act without delay.”

Pelosi’s response: “The president has his own exposure, so he’s always projecting.”

The numbers are worth placing side by side.

Pelosi ranked 28th among congressional traders in 2025 with a 20 percent return — barely beating the S&P 500. Seven of the top ten congressional traders were Republicans. Her husband Paul Pelosi’s trades are publicly disclosed under the STOCK Act, filed within 45 days, and scrutinized by the entire internet precisely because they are transparent. There has never been a documented instance of a $1.5 billion leveraged position placed in the hour before a Pelosi announcement.

Against that record, set the five incidents documented above — now independently confirmed and extended by the BBC. Set the $385 million meme coin extraction. Set the SEC lawsuit against Justin Sun — paused after Sun bought $18 million into Trump’s access vehicle. Set the 12-15 irregular trading reports Scaramucci has tracked. Set $300-400 million extracted from a single Truth Social post on a war Iran says was based on fictional diplomacy. Set anonymous Polymarket accounts that bet on the seizure of a head of state and the bombing of another country, collected, and vanished — on a platform where the president’s son sits on the advisory board.

When Trump called out Pelosi at the State of the Union, he then turned around and actively killed Senator Hawley’s bill when it threatened to include the president and his family in the trading ban. He called Hawley — a Republican — a “pawn” and a “second-tier senator.” He holds individual stocks himself. He launched a prediction market platform, Truth Predict, where Americans bet on tariff decisions, interest rate changes, and federal policy outcomes that he directly controls.

He is not against insider trading. He is against other people’s insider trading.

The Accountability Vacuum

Who investigates this?

The SEC monitors equities and derivatives. The CFTC monitors futures — exactly the E-Mini contracts at the center of Monday’s trade. The DOJ’s Market Integrity and Major Frauds unit investigates market manipulation. All three report, directly or indirectly, to the Trump administration.

The CFTC also has jurisdiction over prediction markets — exactly the Polymarket and Kalshi platforms where anonymous accounts are placing winning bets on presidential decisions. Its chair has declared “zero tolerance” for insider trading. The BBC asked whether the CFTC had investigated any of the documented incidents. No response.

“If you had a watchdog organization,” Scaramucci said, “because of the electronics now, this is very easy to track who’s doing this. Even if you have an anonymous account you can track it. But we’re not doing that because we’re letting them get away with it.”

The SEC was confirmed under Trump nominee Paul Atkins on the same day Trump posted “THIS IS A GREAT TIME TO BUY” before the tariff reversal. In February, Trump signed an executive order claiming more power over independent regulatory agencies including the SEC. Senate Democrats demanded investigations after incidents one, three, and four. Not one has been opened.

The Oxford Law Blog — not a partisan outlet — analyzed the legal exposure after the tariff reversal and concluded that under European Market Abuse Regulation, the conduct “would likely” constitute market manipulation. Under US law, prosecution would be difficult — not because the conduct is legal, but because the president controls the investigators.

The house doesn’t need to hide the game when the regulators work for the house.

The Final Accounting

The Trump Grift series documented $1.408 billion extracted from the presidency in Trump’s first year — which the New York Times noted was a minimum, not a complete tally.

Add to the ledger the five incidents above — now independently confirmed by the BBC’s analysis of Bloomberg trade volume data across multiple markets. Add the March 9 oil trade the BBC documented: someone betting millions on oil falling 47 minutes before Trump told CBS the war was nearly over. Add the Polymarket ghost accounts: Burdensome-Mix winning $436,000 on Maduro’s seizure, six accounts splitting $1.2 million on Iran strikes, one account adding $163,000 on a ceasefire — all on a platform where the president’s son is an investor and advisor. Add the $385 million from the meme coin alone. Add $300-400 million from a single Monday morning post made while Americans paid $3.96 per gallon for gas funding the war whose fictional resolution generated the trade. Add the 10-14 additional incidents Scaramucci has documented but which remain uninvestigated.

Fred Trump extracted $4 million from the federal government through housing fraud over decades. His grandson may have extracted that amount in minutes — repeatedly — through a Truth Social account.

The template is identical. The mechanism has been upgraded. The zeros have changed beyond recognition.

Create leverage. Extract payment. Transfer costs. Claim credit.

The leverage: control over wars, peace announcements, tariff reversals, cryptocurrency markets, prediction markets where your son sits on the advisory board, a Truth Social account with 100 million followers.

The payment: documented across five incidents and now independently confirmed across multiple additional trades by the BBC, totaling hundreds of millions of dollars, with 10-14 more uninvestigated.

The costs: transferred to retail crypto investors who lost billions, to American consumers paying $4 gas, to service members who died in a war whose “resolution” was announced to move markets, to a democracy whose market regulators answer to the man they are supposed to investigate.

The credit: “We are very intent on making a deal with Iran.” — Donald Trump, CNBC, March 23, 2026.

The clock is running — not against the investigators, but against the investigated. Criminal charges for insider trading carry a six-year statute of limitations. Civil enforcement actions carry five years. But disgorgement — the legal mechanism for clawing back ill-gotten profits — carries ten years for intentional fraud under amendments Congress buried in the 2021 National Defense Authorization Act. The $300-400 million extracted from the Iran TACO trade on March 23, 2026 remains within reach of a federal prosecutor until approximately March 2036. The Liberation Day trades of April 2025 remain within disgorgement reach until approximately April 2035. The $TRUMP meme coin extraction of January 2025 remains actionable until January 2035.

The trading data is electronic and permanent. Scaramucci has stated publicly that it is traceable even through anonymous accounts. The BBC has independently confirmed the trading patterns are visible in Bloomberg terminal data. The blockchain analysis firm Bubblemaps has traced the Polymarket accounts. The congressional record — Warren, Schiff, Gallego, Horsford — has preserved contemporaneous demands for investigation that were ignored in real time. What is missing is not evidence, legal authority, or documented harm. What is missing is a Securities and Exchange Commission that does not report to the man being investigated.

That changes in January 2029 at the latest. The statute of limitations will not have expired. The receipts will still exist. And the house, for the first time, may find the game is no longer rigged in its favor.

There is, however, one final irony. Across forty-one documented accountability failures, we have traced the most systematic, brazen, and financially successful corruption in American presidential history — a grift spanning three generations, perfected across five decades, and culminating in the extraction of billions from financial markets, a war, a cryptocurrency empire, and the presidency itself.

By the time any post-2029 investigation is meaningfully underway, the man at the center of it all may not be aware it is happening.

The ultimate accountability — the moment the marks finally stop playing and the house is called to account — may arrive to find the dealer has already left the table. Not fled. Not escaped. Simply gone, in the way that Fred Trump went before his own reckoning could arrive, leaving the template and the damage and the debts to everyone else.

The house always wins.

Even at the end, it wins by not being present for the loss.
