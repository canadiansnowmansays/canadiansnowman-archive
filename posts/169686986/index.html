The Great Energy Challenge

Meeting the AI Crisis Through a Renaissance of Clean Power Technologies

Addendum: When Political Ostrich Economics Meets Energy Reality

August 6, 2025

Stephen Lawton - August 6, 2025

President Donald Trump signed his massive spending bill during the White House's Fourth of July celebrations.

When Predictions Become Nightmares

When I began writing this series in May, I posed a critical question: "The question isn't whether the clean energy transition will happen—market forces and international competition are driving it forward. The question is whether America will lead this transition or cede technological and economic dominance to competitors."

That question has been answered by Donald Trump.

On July 4th, President Trump signed the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" - legislation that doesn't just ignore the AI energy crisis I documented throughout this series, but actively sabotages America's ability to compete in the clean energy race that will define the next century.

The bill "eliminates subsidies for unreliable 'green' energy sources like wind and solar" and "repeals much of the Inflation Reduction Act," representing "a law not of creation but destruction."

The Ostrich Administration's Energy Suicide

Every scenario I outlined in this series just became exponentially harder to achieve. While I documented how urgent the AI energy challenge is—with data centers potentially consuming 21% of global electricity by 2030—the Trump administration responded by kneecapping the very technologies that could meet this demand.

Solar Devastation: The bill "rolls back tax credits that helped to make wind, solar and other clean power cheaper" and will "drive up electricity bills across the U.S." Just as I showed solar becoming the world's cheapest electricity source, Trump made it artificially expensive again.

Wind Industry Collapse: The legislation has "thrown America's wind and solar industries into turmoil at a time when electricity demand is expected to increase significantly for the first time in decades." This is economic vandalism disguised as energy policy.

Manufacturing Exodus: The law threatens to reverse the "manufacturing boom" where "firms unveiled plans to invest more than $100 billion to build solar-panel and EV and battery factories that would create an estimated 115,000 jobs."

The AI Energy Crisis Gets Worse, Not Better

Remember the core challenge I identified: AI's explosive energy appetite happening precisely when we need to decarbonize. The "One Big Beautiful Bill" makes both problems worse simultaneously:

Higher Energy Costs: According to energy analysis, "when the clean energy tax credits phase out at the end of 2025, that will bump up electricity costs for ordinary Americans" because "higher costs for clean energy development will slow the pace of new supply, just as U.S. energy demand hits record highs."

Supply Shortages: New power generation "is expected to fall by about a third over the next decade, mainly due to wind and solar projects that won't come online." Exactly when AI needs massive new electricity capacity, Trump just guaranteed we'll have less.

Regional Devastation: In Republican states like Oklahoma, "homeowners, renters and businesses are likely to see electricity rates shoot up by between 60% and 350% over the next decade" with similar increases expected in Kentucky (48%), Missouri (39%), and Kansas (30%).

The Head-in-Sand Ideology Behind the Destruction

Trump's executive order reveals the ideological blindness: "For too long, the Federal Government has forced American taxpayers to subsidize expensive and unreliable energy sources like wind and solar. The proliferation of these projects displaces affordable, reliable, dispatchable domestic energy sources, compromises our electric grid, and denigrates the beauty of our Nation's natural landscape."

This is the same thinking that drove Trump's decade-long legal battle against wind turbines near his Scottish golf course—personal aesthetic preferences now masquerading as national energy policy.

The administration calls clean energy "unreliable" while my series documented how wind generation now exceeds coal generation for months at a time, and solar provides the cheapest electricity in human history. This isn't policy disagreement—it's reality denial.

China Wins While America Retreats

While Trump destroys American clean energy competitiveness, China accelerates:

Chinese Solar Domination: My series showed China deploying solar at nearly 100 TWh/month—an exponential growth curve that seemed impossible just years ago. Trump's response? Make American solar more expensive and less competitive.

Manufacturing Supremacy: As I documented, China "produces enough batteries to cater to the entire global demand" while the U.S. just dismantled policies that were creating domestic manufacturing.

AI Infrastructure Advantage: Chinese tech companies will have access to cheap, abundant clean electricity while American AI companies face higher energy costs and supply constraints. This is how technological leadership gets handed to competitors.

The Fossil Fuel Delusion

The bill gives fossil fuels "another $150 million each year in direct subsidies" while eliminating support for technologies that represent "vital 21st century technology." This is exactly backwards from what the AI energy crisis demands.

As I showed throughout this series, data centers need massive amounts of reliable electricity. Fossil fuel plants can't scale fast enough, clean enough, or cheap enough to meet AI's exponential energy growth. By doubling down on yesterday's energy sources, Trump guarantees America will fall behind in tomorrow's economy.

The Policy Malpractice

The Congressional Budget Office projects this legislation will "increase the deficit by $3.4 trillion over 10 years" while simultaneously making America less competitive in the industries that will define the next century.

This isn't conservative fiscal policy—it's economic self-sabotage. We're borrowing trillions to subsidize fossil fuels while China invests in the clean energy technologies that will power AI infrastructure.

Let me update each pathway I analyzed based on this legislative disaster:

Solar + Storage Surge: SABOTAGED

  • Domestic solar manufacturing investments canceled

  • Project development costs increase 30-50%

  • U.S. falls further behind China's deployment pace

  • Battery storage expansion slowed by higher costs

Wind + Transmission Revolution: CRIPPLED

  • Offshore wind projects face increased financing costs

  • Transmission infrastructure investments delayed

  • Supply chain advantages handed to competitors

  • Technology leadership ceded to Europe and China

Nuclear Renaissance: UNCERTAIN

  • SMR development may continue due to bipartisan support for nuclear

  • But higher electricity costs make nuclear relatively more attractive

  • Uranium supply chain investments still needed but no policy support

Fusion Breakthrough: DELAYED

  • Private investment continues but federal support reduced

  • China's $1.5 billion annual fusion investment gains advantage

  • American fusion companies may relocate to countries with supportive policy

Ocean Energy: STALLED

  • Early-stage technologies lose government development support

  • European and Asian competitors gain first-mover advantages

  • Coastal installation opportunities wasted

The Competitive Catastrophe

Every warning I made about America ceding technological leadership is now coming true in real time:

Economic Competitiveness: While other countries invest in "the future," the U.S. "has hitched itself to fossil fuels, to combustion, to literally ancient forms of energy that more forward-thinking countries will be leaving behind."

Energy Security: My series showed how clean energy creates true energy independence. Instead, we're doubling down on volatile fossil fuel markets and geopolitical dependencies.

Climate Reality: The law will cause "billions more tons of carbon dioxide" to "escape into the atmosphere, needlessly, trapping more and more heat under the lid of a planet that is already boiling over."

The Technology Companies' Dilemma

The tech giants driving AI development now face a terrible choice: accept higher energy costs and constrained supply in the U.S., or move operations to countries with abundant clean electricity.

As energy analysts note, "clean energy projects are crucial to help power new data centers and factories, because the plants can be constructed quickly and produce electricity that is relatively cheap." Trump just made those projects more expensive and harder to build.

Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and others will increasingly look to Canada, Europe, and other regions where clean energy policy supports rather than sabotages their infrastructure needs.

The Red State Reality Check

The cruelest irony: Republican-led states will see "some of the sharpest increases" in electricity costs, with places like Oklahoma facing rate increases of "between 60% and 350% over the next decade."

The same constituents who voted for "cheap energy" will pay the highest prices because their representatives destroyed the policies that were making energy actually cheap.

The Path Forward: Damage Control

The damage is done, but not permanent. The clean energy revolution continues globally, driven by economics and necessity. American companies will adapt by:

Geographic Arbitrage: Moving energy-intensive operations to regions with supportive clean energy policy and lower electricity costs.

Corporate Procurement: Signing direct contracts with clean energy developers, bypassing hostile government policy through market mechanisms.

Technology Investment: Doubling down on energy efficiency and storage technologies to reduce dependence on grid electricity.

Political Engagement: The tech industry's massive economic influence will create counterpressure for rational energy policy.

Global Competition: The Rest of the World Moves On

While America retreats, the rest of the world accelerates:

Europe: Continuing massive grid infrastructure investments and offshore wind deployment
China: Scaling solar and battery manufacturing while advancing fusion research
Canada: Positioning as North America's clean energy supplier and AI infrastructure hub
Australia: Leveraging abundant renewable resources for energy-intensive industries

Conclusion: From Leadership to Laggard

The AI energy crisis documented throughout this series demands urgent, coordinated action to deploy clean energy technologies at unprecedented scale. Instead, we got legislation that actively undermines every solution identified in Parts 1-7.

This isn’t just environmental policy—it’s economic competitiveness. As I wrote in Part 2, “For reasons economical as much as ecological, the future will be dictated by clean energy.” Countries that understand this will prosper. Countries that don’t will fall behind.

The Trump administration has made its choice clear.

My series showed that abundant clean energy is achievable through solar, wind, nuclear, and fusion technologies. That future remains technically and economically viable—just not under current American leadership.

But there’s another possibility I haven’t yet explored.

What if the solution to AI’s energy demands doesn’t require reforming American energy policy at all? What if the answer lies not in deploying more solar panels in Arizona or wind turbines in Texas, but in going where energy is already abundant and free?

What if we built the data centers in space?

The next decade will see unprecedented divergence between countries that embrace energy abundance and those that cling to energy scarcity. America, despite every technological and resource advantage, has chosen scarcity for its terrestrial infrastructure.

But the age of abundance may still arrive—240,000 miles away, where the sun never sets and cooling is free.

December 22, 2025 Update: The Collapse Is Complete

When I wrote this Addendum in August predicting Trump’s policies would collapse America’s wind industry, I hoped events would prove me wrong.

They didn’t.

On December 22, 2025—one day after I published Part 9 exploring space-based alternatives to Earth’s energy policy chaos—the Trump administration completed the demolition I predicted.

The Interior Department halted five offshore wind projects:

  • Vineyard Wind 1 (Massachusetts) - Half of 62 turbines already operational, sending power to 400,000 homes. Halted.

  • Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind - Backed by Republican Governor Glenn Youngkin until Democrat Abigail Spanberger won the governor’s race in November. Halted.

  • Empire Wind (New York) - $5 billion project that survived April’s shutdown order after negotiations with Governor Hochul. Now dead.

  • Sunrise Wind (New York) - Halted.

  • Revolution Wind (Rhode Island/Connecticut) - Halted.

Parts for the construction of Revolution Wind, one of five projects the Trump administration paused today .Credit...Brian Snyder/Reuters

What remains of America’s offshore wind industry: Two projects. One small installation off Rhode Island from 2016. One larger project off New York from 2023. That’s it.

The administration cited unspecified “national security concerns” involving “adversary technologies” and “vulnerabilities created by large-scale offshore wind projects.” No details provided. No classified briefing to Congress. Just vague assertions that a federal judge already ruled “arbitrary and capricious” when Trump tried similar tactics earlier this month.

The Validation of Every Warning

In Part 3 (June 2025), I documented that offshore wind could meet global electricity demand 18 times over. In Part 8 (July 2025), I showed how the “Wind + Transmission Revolution” required $720 billion in grid investment but offered massive clean electricity for AI data centers. In this August Addendum, I warned that Trump’s policies would “collapse” the industry and hand China competitive advantages.

December 22 validated everything:

1. Economic Competitiveness Destroyed

$5+ billion in private investment vaporized by executive order. What energy company will invest in U.S. infrastructure when operational projects can be canceled for “unspecified national security concerns”? The signal to global capital markets is clear: America’s energy sector is too politically volatile for major investment.

2. China’s Advantage Widens

While we halt operational wind farms that were already generating electricity, China continues offshore wind deployment at scale. As I documented in this Addendum, Chinese companies have “access to cheap, abundant clean electricity while American AI companies face higher energy costs and supply constraints.” December 22 accelerated that divergence.

3. The Space Solution Becomes Necessary

When I concluded this Addendum asking “What if we built the data centers in space?”, I was exploring theoretical alternatives to political obstruction. December 22 proves that wasn’t academic speculation—it’s operational necessity.

You can’t halt orbital infrastructure with a vaguely-worded Interior Department memo. SpaceX’s satellites aren’t subject to arbitrary executive decisions about “compromising electric grids” or “denigrating natural landscape beauty.” The administration’s December 22 decision makes the case for space-based computing infrastructure far stronger than any technical argument could.

4. Red State Energy Crisis Intensifies

My August warning that Republican states would face “the sharpest increases in electricity costs” just became more certain. You can’t meet AI’s exponential energy demands by destroying projects that would generate clean electricity. Oklahoma’s projected 60-350% rate increases? They just got more likely.

A federal judge called it “arbitrary and capricious.” The administration calls it “protecting the American people.”

Meanwhile, data centers still need massive electricity. Wind projects that could provide it are being killed. And America’s tech giants are increasingly looking at Canada, Europe, and—as Part 9 documents—space.

What December 22 Proves

Every scenario I analyzed in Part 8 assumed some baseline level of rationality in energy policy. December 22 destroys that assumption.

The “Wind + Transmission Revolution” scenario? CRIPPLED became DESTROYED.

The “Hybrid Ecosystem” that assumed different technologies would complement each other? Can’t work when entire technology categories are eliminated by executive fiat.

The hope that “market forces from tech companies would constrain political damage”? I was wrong. Billions in stranded investment apparently doesn’t matter when personal aesthetic preferences drive policy.

January 5, 2026: China's Answer

Fourteen days after Trump destroyed America's offshore wind industry, China provided a response.

On January 5, 2026, Beijing Linyi Yunchuan Energy Technology successfully tested the S2000—the world's first megawatt-class airborne wind turbine. The helium-lifted system rose to 6,560 feet above Sichuan Province, generated 385 kilowatt-hours in 30 minutes, and became the first airborne wind system to successfully feed power into a commercial grid.

The S2000 represents a fundamentally different approach to wind energy:

Technical Specifications:
- 3 megawatt maximum capacity
- 197 feet long, 131 feet wide, 131 feet tall
- 12 turbines in ducted airflow design
- Reaches 6,560 feet altitude where winds are 3× stronger than ground level
- Can be deployed in 4-5 hours from shipping containers

The Ironic Solution to Trump's "Problem":

Remember the Scottish golf course windmills that triggered Trump's decade-long legal battle and shaped America's current energy policy? Those turbines stood visible on the horizon from his Aberdeen course.

The S2000 operates at 6,560 feet—well above commercial aircraft cruising altitude. From a golf course, these turbines would be completely invisible, appearing as nothing more than a small dot in the sky, if visible at all.

China just solved Trump's aesthetic objection. Too bad we destroyed our wind industry before anyone could build them here.

Why High-Altitude Wind Matters:

Wind energy increases with the cube of wind speed. At 6,560 feet, where the S2000 operates, winds are roughly three times faster than ground level—translating to 27 times more energy potential per turbine.

This isn't vaporware. China's progression has been systematic:
- October 2024: S500 (50 kW at 1,640 feet)
- January 2025: S1000 (100 kW at 3,281 feet)
- September 2025: S1500 (1 MW at 4,921 feet)
- January 2026: S2000 (3 MW at 6,560 feet, grid-connected)

Mass production scheduled for 2026. First commercial grid connections planned for 2027.

The Contrast Couldn't Be Starker:

While the U.S. destroys operational offshore wind projects generating power for 400,000 homes based on "unspecified national security concerns," China is innovating with airborne systems that:
- Use 40% less materials than traditional turbines
- Cost 30% less per kilowatt-hour
- Deploy in hours, not years
- Access wind resources completely unavailable to ground-based turbines
- Can serve remote locations, emergency power, or supplement existing grids
- Won't ruin anyone's golf course view

The company's CEO projects eventual deployment to 32,808 feet, where wind is up to 200 times stronger than ground level. At that altitude, you'd need a telescope to see them from the ground.

Two Paths Diverging:

America, December 22: "These wind farms pose national security risks" [destroys $5 billion in operational infrastructure]

China, January 5: "Here's a 3-megawatt flying wind turbine that feeds the grid from 6,560 feet" [successfully demonstrates next-generation technology]

This is how technological leadership changes hands. Not through superior innovation being impossible in America—we have the engineers, the capital, the technology base. But through policy decisions that actively destroy domestic capabilities while competitors advance.

The Trump administration didn't just halt American offshore wind. They handed China a 14-day news cycle demonstrating exactly why countries that enable clean energy innovation will dominate the AI economy, while countries that destroy it will fall behind.

And the cruelest irony? If Trump had actually wanted wind power that didn't interfere with golf course views, airborne turbines at 6,560 feet would have been invisible from his Scottish property. But by the time China developed them, we'd already destroyed our wind industry based on his two-decade grudge.
