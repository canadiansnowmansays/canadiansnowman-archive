Why Congress Must Stop RFK Jr. From Undermining America's Strategic Advantage

A critical analysis of how cutting mRNA vaccine research threatens national security

August 6, 2025

Robert F. Kennedy Jr. speaks at the Department of Health and Human Services in D.C. on July 29. (Saul Loeb/AFP/Getty Images)

I am not a doctor or scientist, but I am someone who has been closely following the implications of recent U.S. policy decisions. What I've observed should concern everyone who values global health security and technological innovation, regardless of nationality.

Last week, HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. terminated $500 million in mRNA vaccine research contracts, affecting 22 projects led by major pharmaceutical companies including Pfizer, Moderna, Sanofi, and others. While this decision is being framed as a science-based policy correction, the reality is far more troubling: the U.S. is voluntarily surrendering a critical technological advantage to strategic competitors, particularly China.

The immediate real-world consequences are already becoming apparent. Since Kennedy took office, measles cases have been rising across the United States - a direct result of his systematic undermining of vaccination programs. This provides a preview of what happens when vaccine skepticism becomes official policy.

This requires immediate Congressional intervention.

The Measles Warning: Policy Consequences in Real Time

The measles outbreak currently unfolding across America provides a stark preview of Kennedy's impact on public health. Since taking office, he has:

  • Fired the entire 17-member vaccine advisory committee and replaced them with vaccine skeptics

  • Removed COVID-19 vaccines from CDC's recommended immunization schedule for children and pregnant women

  • Ordered studies on the long-debunked vaccine-autism link

  • Banned thimerosal, a safe vaccine preservative, based on anti-vaccine activism rather than science

The result? Measles cases are climbing in a country that had virtually eliminated the disease. This isn't theoretical harm - it's happening now, to real children, because of Kennedy's ideology.

The mRNA decision follows the same pattern: reject proven science, embrace discredited theories, and leave Americans vulnerable to preventable diseases.

Let me share a relevant personal experience that illustrates why this technology matters. When my partner contracted COVID-19, we continued sharing the same living space and bed. By every measure, I should have become infected.

I did not.

This outcome was not luck—it was the direct result of mRNA vaccination, which I have received twice yearly since 2021. This represents exactly the kind of real-world effectiveness that demonstrates American technological superiority in action.

My experience is not unique. It reflects the documented performance of mRNA vaccines in preventing transmission even under the highest-risk exposure conditions. This represents innovation that has protected people worldwide—American technology that serves global health security.

The Strategic Competition Reality

While the United States dismantles its mRNA research infrastructure, China is dramatically expanding its biotechnology investments. Chinese pharmaceutical companies are actively recruiting scientists globally, building advanced manufacturing facilities, and positioning themselves to dominate the global vaccine market that American innovation created.

This represents a fundamental strategic miscalculation with global implications. The U.S. has consistently used national security arguments to protect critical industries from foreign competition—implementing tariffs, restricting technology transfers, and prioritizing domestic manufacturing. The decision to abandon mRNA research directly contradicts these principles and affects global health security.

Strategic vulnerabilities created by this decision:

  • Dependence on foreign nations for pandemic response capabilities

  • Loss of technological superiority in biological defense

  • Reduced deterrent effect against biological warfare

  • Weakened ability to protect military personnel and critical infrastructure

Direct Benefits for American Citizens: Beyond national security, restoring mRNA funding directly benefits every American family. This technology enables faster response to future pandemics, reduces healthcare costs through prevention rather than treatment, maintains America's leading position in high-paying biotech jobs, and ensures continued access to cutting-edge medical innovations.

Economic Impact on American Industry: The biotech sector directly employs over 1.8 million Americans with average salaries exceeding $100,000 annually. Kennedy's decision threatens this economic engine - companies are already announcing research layoffs, venture capital is shifting to European and Asian competitors, and top scientists are considering relocating abroad. The ripple effects extend beyond biotech to pharmaceutical manufacturing, medical device companies, and university research programs across the country.

Personal experiences like mine - staying healthy despite maximum exposure to COVID - represent the tangible protection this technology provides to millions of American families.

Consider the strategic implications:

  • Force multiplication: One bioweapon could cause more damage than conventional military attacks

  • Economic disruption: Biological crises can shut down entire economies, as COVID-19 demonstrated

  • Social destabilization: Pandemics create civil unrest and political instability

  • Alliance reliability: Allied nations depend on U.S. technological capabilities for their own defense

Personal experiences with vaccine effectiveness - like remaining healthy despite intimate exposure to infectious diseases - demonstrate that this technology works. When citizens are protected by innovation, nations maintain the social and economic stability that underpins global leadership.

National Security Implications

mRNA vaccine technology extends far beyond pandemic response. This capability represents critical infrastructure for:

Military Readiness: Rapid vaccine development protects forces from biological threats, whether naturally occurring or weaponized. The ability to deploy countermeasures in weeks rather than years could determine the outcome of future conflicts. Traditional vaccine development timelines are simply unacceptable in military scenarios.

Biodefense Against Engineered Threats: The same mRNA technology that protected people from COVID-19 could be rapidly adapted to counter:

  • Engineered bioweapons targeting specific genetic markers

  • Enhanced pathogens designed to evade traditional vaccines

  • Novel biological agents released by terrorist organizations

  • Weaponized diseases with accelerated transmission rates

Economic Security: Pandemics can be weaponized to destabilize economies and supply chains. Nations with rapid response capabilities maintain operational continuity while competitors face disruption. America's ability to rapidly deploy medical countermeasures protects against economic warfare tactics used by hostile nations.

Critical Infrastructure Protection: Domestic mRNA production capabilities reduce dependence on foreign pharmaceutical manufacturers, ensuring critical medical supplies remain under democratic control during crises. Strategic stockpiles can be rapidly replenished using domestic technology.

Technological Supremacy: The country that leads in mRNA technology will dominate biotechnology markets worth hundreds of billions annually. Ceding this advantage undermines America's competitive position across multiple sectors.

Alliance Leadership: The U.S.'s ability to provide cutting-edge medical countermeasures to allies strengthens international cooperation and coalition-building capabilities. Countries like Canada have benefited enormously from American biotechnology leadership during the pandemic.

Intelligence assessments consistently identify biological threats as among the top risks to national security. Dismantling America's leading-edge vaccine development capability during a period of heightened global tensions represents a strategic blunder of the highest order.

Expert Consensus vs. Ideological Policy

The scientific community's response to Kennedy's decision has been swift and damning. This represents a continuation of Kennedy's long-standing anti-vaccine ideology - even Anthony Fauci previously characterized Kennedy's approach as fundamentally flawed after a meeting where Kennedy showed slides claiming "vaccinations are responsible for the following diseases" and listed "every disease in the world." Fauci's response: "Bobby I'm sorry, but we don't really agree with you... I don't know what's going on in his head but it's not good."

Current expert opposition includes:

Michael Osterholm, University of Minnesota infectious disease expert: "This may be the most dangerous public health judgment that I've seen in my 50 years in this business. It is baseless, and we will pay a tremendous price in terms of illnesses and deaths."

Rick Bright, former BARDA director: "This decision signals a dangerous complacency. Disinvesting from mRNA strips us of one of the fastest tools we have to contain the next pandemic, natural or deliberate."

Chris Meekins, former Trump administration biodefense official: "I think that it endangers the national security of the United States. It could put the U.S. at a strategic national security disadvantage."

Peter Hotez, Baylor College of Medicine: "This is a proven technology for emerging respiratory viruses or respiratory virus pandemics. It is extremely safe and has been incredibly effective."

The 2023 Nobel Prize in physiology or medicine was awarded to scientists for developing mRNA vaccines - the very technology Kennedy is now abandoning. These experts represent the scientific consensus that Kennedy has consistently rejected throughout his career.

Debunking Kennedy's Claims

Kennedy's justifications for this decision crumble under scientific scrutiny:

Kennedy's Claim: "mRNA vaccines don't perform well against viruses that infect the upper respiratory tract" and "fail to protect effectively against upper respiratory infections like COVID and flu."

Scientific Reality: mRNA vaccines demonstrated 90-95% efficacy in clinical trials and continued protecting against severe disease across multiple COVID variants. The technology earned its creators the 2023 Nobel Prize in Medicine for contributing to "the unprecedented rate of vaccine development during one of the greatest threats to human health in modern times."

Kennedy's Claim: Single mutations render mRNA vaccines "ineffective" and can "encourage new mutations and can actually prolong pandemics."

Scientific Reality: This fundamentally misunderstands mRNA technology's key advantage—rapid adaptability. Unlike traditional vaccines requiring months or years to update, mRNA platforms can address new variants within weeks. Viruses mutate regardless of vaccination status; unvaccinated populations actually serve as mutation reservoirs.

Kennedy's Claim: mRNA technology "poses more risks than benefits for these respiratory viruses."

Scientific Reality: Extensive safety monitoring across billions of doses has identified rare, manageable side effects vastly outweighed by documented benefits in preventing severe disease and death.

Addressing Legitimate Concerns: While some have raised questions about rare side effects like myocarditis, these occur at rates of 1-5 cases per 100,000 doses and are typically mild and treatable. This compares to COVID-19's risk of severe myocarditis, hospitalization, and death - risks that are orders of magnitude higher and precisely what mRNA vaccines prevent. The risk-benefit analysis overwhelmingly favors vaccination, which is why every major medical organization globally continues to recommend these vaccines.

Congressional Authority and Options

Congress possesses clear constitutional authority to address this policy reversal through multiple mechanisms:

Appropriations Power (Article I, Section 8): Direct funding restoration through emergency legislation, framed as critical defense spending rather than healthcare expenditure. Congress can mandate specific language requiring continued vaccine development programs and oversight of HHS spending priorities.

Legislative Override: Passage of laws requiring HHS to maintain mRNA research programs, with creation of dedicated funding streams protected from executive discretion and establishment of pandemic preparedness requirements.

Historical Precedent: Congress has repeatedly intervened when executive decisions threatened national security interests, from overriding arms control decisions to mandating defense spending priorities. The Supreme Court has consistently upheld Congressional authority to direct national security spending, even when it conflicts with executive preferences.

Administrative Procedure Act Challenges: The decision may be subject to judicial review as arbitrary and capricious, given the lack of substantial scientific evidence supporting Kennedy's risk-benefit analysis and failure to follow proper procedural requirements.

Congressional Oversight Authority:

  • Subpoena power to examine the scientific basis for the decision

  • Hearings to question HHS officials and independent experts

  • Investigation of potential conflicts of interest

  • GAO investigation of the decision-making process

The legal precedent is clear—Congress regularly intervenes when executive decisions threaten national security interests.

The Economic and Strategic Stakes

This decision occurs within a broader context of technological competition between the United States and China that affects the entire global community. While U.S. policy retreats from biotechnology leadership, Chinese companies are positioning themselves to capture:

  • A global mRNA vaccine market projected to exceed $30 billion annually

  • Critical intellectual property developed with significant public investment

  • Manufacturing capabilities that could control global supply chains during future pandemics

  • The geopolitical influence that comes with providing life-saving medical technology worldwide

International Allies Are Taking Notice: Canada's health ministry has already expressed concerns about U.S. policy shifts affecting joint research initiatives. European Union officials are reportedly accelerating their own mRNA research programs to reduce dependence on U.S. innovation. These allied responses demonstrate the global implications and create potential diplomatic pressure for Congressional intervention.

The timing couldn't be worse. As the news reports make clear, COVID is currently rising again in the U.S., children are returning to school, and experts warn about potential H5N1 bird flu pandemic risks. Kennedy is dismantling pandemic preparedness precisely when it's most needed.

The implications extend far beyond U.S. borders. Countries worldwide—including Canada—have relied on American biotechnology leadership for pandemic response. This decision potentially shifts that leadership to nations with very different priorities and values.

A Call for Strategic Consistency

The current administration has demonstrated clear priorities: protecting key industries, maintaining technological advantages, and ensuring security. The decision to terminate mRNA research contradicts all of these objectives and has implications that extend far beyond U.S. borders.

Congressional intervention would apply the same logic used for other strategic decisions:

  • If tariffs protect critical manufacturing, funding should protect critical biotechnology

  • If restrictions safeguard technological advantages, research investment should safeguard health security leadership

  • If defense spending ensures military capabilities, vaccine development ensures biological preparedness

This transcends partisan politics. Economic competitiveness, technological leadership, and national security are bipartisan concerns. The question isn't whether to support vaccines - it's whether America will lead in 21st-century biotechnology or cede that leadership to China.

From a global perspective, U.S. leadership in biotechnology serves as a public good for democratic nations worldwide. When America retreats from innovation, authoritarian competitors fill the vacuum - not just in markets, but in influence and global health governance.

What Congress Must Do

Immediate Actions:

  1. National Security Emergency Appropriations - Frame as critical defense spending, restore the $500 million as "Biological Defense Investment," add funding for "Strategic Vaccine Stockpile" capabilities, include language preventing future executive termination without Congressional approval on national security grounds

  2. Biological Defense Preparedness Act - Mandate minimum funding levels for vaccine research as national security requirement, establish Defense Department coordination with HHS on biological threats, create oversight mechanisms treating vaccine capabilities as critical defense infrastructure

  3. Congressional Hearings - Call HHS Secretary Kennedy to testify under oath, invite independent scientific experts to provide testimony, examine the scientific basis for the policy change

Overcoming Implementation Challenges: While political resistance is expected, the national security framing provides a path forward. Defense spending traditionally receives bipartisan support, and framing mRNA research as critical defense infrastructure rather than healthcare policy could help overcome partisan divisions. Additionally, pressure from defense contractors, allied nations, and business communities dependent on biotech innovation could provide leverage for Congressional action.

Expected Timeline for Results: Emergency appropriations could restore funding within 60-90 days of Congressional action. Research programs could resume within 6 months, with initial new vaccine candidates potentially available within 12-18 months for emerging threats. Economic benefits would begin immediately with the restoration of research positions and renewed investor confidence in American biotechnology leadership.

Long-term Measures:

  1. Scientific Integrity Protection - Legislation requiring scientific peer review for major health policy changes, protection for government scientists from political interference, mandatory consultation with external scientific advisory boards

  2. Innovation Ecosystem Protection - Beyond immediate health security, establish broader protections for biotechnology research that drives economic growth and maintains America's competitive edge across multiple sectors

  3. International Coordination Framework - Create mechanisms for Congress to consider allied nations' input on decisions affecting global health security, recognizing that health threats are inherently transnational

  4. GAO Investigation - Request Government Accountability Office review of the decision-making process, assess compliance with scientific advisory procedures, evaluate impact on pandemic preparedness

  5. Establish mRNA research as protected defense infrastructure and create mechanisms preventing future politically-motivated cuts to critical technologies

Conclusion

This is not ultimately about domestic vaccine policy—it is about global leadership in a critical technology sector. Personal experiences like mine, where innovative technology demonstrably protected lives across borders, illustrate the real-world value of maintaining these capabilities.

China will not abandon biotechnology development because the U.S. chooses to do so. They will accelerate their investments while America retreats, gaining advantages that could prove decisive in future global health crises.

Congressional action could prevent this strategic misstep. The question is whether U.S. legislators will act before technological leadership becomes irretrievably lost—not just for America, but for the global community that has benefited from American innovation in health technologies.

The stakes transcend national borders and partisan politics. This is about ensuring the world's leading democratic power remains at the forefront of technologies that protect human lives globally and maintain the kind of international leadership that serves both American interests and global health security.

The measles outbreak currently spreading across America provides a real-time preview of what happens when vaccine skepticism becomes policy. Rising case numbers in a country that had virtually eliminated the disease demonstrate the immediate human cost of Kennedy's ideology. The mRNA decision follows the same dangerous pattern.

Public mobilization is already building. Parents seeing measles return to their communities, biotech workers facing layoffs, and healthcare professionals witnessing the dismantling of pandemic preparedness are contacting their representatives. This grassroots pressure, combined with concerns from allied nations and defense contractors, creates a political environment where Congressional action becomes not just possible but necessary.

Congressional action is needed now, not just for American interests, but for the broader cause of maintaining democratic leadership in global health innovation. The choice is clear: restore funding and maintain technological leadership, or watch China dominate the biotechnology of the future.
