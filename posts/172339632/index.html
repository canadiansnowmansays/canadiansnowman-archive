Presidential Complicity

When Trump's 'Go Wild' Promise Became a License to Kill

August 31, 2025

Part of the RFK Files series documenting the systematic dismantling of America's public health infrastructure

Bottom Line Up Front: Trump's enthusiastic endorsement of RFK Jr.'s anti-vaccine agenda has transformed what could have been rogue Cabinet misconduct into presidential complicity in policies that are killing American children. When two children died from preventable measles in West Texas, Trump didn't course-correct—he doubled down on celebrating the man whose policies created the conditions for their deaths.

From Campaign Rhetoric to Children's Graves

At Madison Square Garden in October 2024, Donald Trump made a promise that would echo in pediatric wards across America: "I'm going to let him go wild on health. I'm going to let him go wild on the food. I'm going to let him go wild on medicines."

The possessive pride was even more revealing in private. "I have a Kennedy," Trump bragged to donors earlier this year, treating America's public health as his personal trophy collection while children died from preventable diseases.

Trump wasn't speaking hypothetically. He was promising to unleash Robert F. Kennedy Jr.—America's most prominent vaccine skeptic—on the nation's public health infrastructure. As the Washington Post reported this week, "Trump pledged that he'd empower Robert F. Kennedy Jr. to 'go wild' on health care. The president has kept his promise — to the alarm of public health leaders and even some Republicans."

The results have been catastrophic and predictable: pediatric deaths from diseases America had effectively eliminated, while the president who promised to unleash Kennedy celebrates the destruction as a policy success. As the Post confirmed, Kennedy "has worked to limit access to vaccines and promoted debunked health theories despite near-daily condemnation from public health leaders."

The Moral Transformation: From Oversight to Enablement

Presidential responsibility for Cabinet officials extends beyond mere appointment—it includes ongoing oversight and the moral obligation to intervene when policies threaten American lives. Trump has not merely failed this test; he has actively inverted it.

When measles cases reached their highest levels in 30 years, Trump didn't express concern. When two children in West Texas died from preventable measles—children doctors confirmed had no underlying health conditions—Trump didn't demand policy changes. Instead, he reinforced his support for the architect of these tragedies.

This represents a fundamental abandonment of presidential responsibility. The commander-in-chief's primary duty is to protect American lives. When preventable childhood deaths occur and the president responds by promising the responsible official even more authority, we face a constitutional crisis of the highest order.

The "Go Wild" Doctrine: Abdicating Constitutional Authority

The scope of Trump's abdication becomes clear through Kennedy's own interpretation. CNN reported Kennedy's interpretation of Trump's promise: Kennedy said Trump had promised to give him "control" of several public health agencies, including the US Department of Health and Human Services, CDC, FDA, NIH "and a few others."

But the Post reveals something more troubling: Trump's personal satisfaction with this arrangement. As one senior White House official put it, "Secretary Kennedy has been a crown jewel of this administration." This isn't reluctant tolerance of a problematic appointment—it's active celebration of policies killing children.

This represents an unprecedented abdication of presidential responsibility for federal agencies charged with protecting American lives. Trump's Madison Square Garden pledge has created a constitutional vacuum where:

  • Cabinet officials systematically violate Senate confirmation commitments with presidential blessing

  • Scientific institutions are dismantled without oversight

  • International alliances are damaged through presidential endorsement of anti-science policies

  • American children die from preventable diseases while the president celebrates the policies causing their deaths

Constitutional scholars would recognize this as a textbook case of presidential enabling of Cabinet misconduct—exactly the scenario the founders designed congressional oversight to address.

Expert Warnings Ignored: Even Trump's Own Officials Sound Alarms

The moral bankruptcy of Trump's position becomes stark when even his own appointees warn against the policies he celebrates. Dr. Jerome Adams, who served as surgeon general under Trump, warned that letting Kennedy "go wild on health" would be "a very bad idea," noting that Trump's campaign had been warned by donors about "impacts on the party and the country of coming across as anti-science, and of having to manage and own a deluge of measles and polio outbreaks."

Dr. Adams specifically warned that Kennedy could "spread misinformation and take us back to the dark ages in regards to vaccine-preventable diseases." When Trump's own appointed health officials sound such alarms, his continued support represents willful ignorance of expert warnings about threats to American lives.

This isn't partisan opposition—it's Trump's own team desperately trying to prevent the predictable consequences of his campaign promise. Their warnings have proven prophetic, yet Trump continues celebrating the very policies they warned would endanger Americans.

The Body Count: Preventable Deaths as Policy Success

The human cost of Trump's enablement is already visible and growing:

Measles Resurgence: The highest number of U.S. measles cases in 30 years, directly linked to Kennedy's systematic undermining of vaccination programs

Child Deaths: Two children in West Texas died from measles—entirely preventable deaths that occurred because Kennedy removed COVID vaccines from recommended schedules and undermined public trust in vaccination

Healthcare Worker Violence: The August shooting at CDC headquarters by a gunman "angry about Covid vaccines" killed a police officer and traumatized employees, directly stemming from the anti-vaccine rhetoric Trump celebrates

Institutional Collapse: CDC described as "gone" by public health experts after Kennedy forced out the director and top officials, leaving America vulnerable to biological threats

Each of these tragedies traces directly to policies Trump promised Kennedy could implement at Madison Square Garden. The president didn't just enable these outcomes—he actively promised them.

The Emergency Powers Paradox: Claiming Emergency Authority While Ignoring Child Deaths

The constitutional absurdity reaches its peak in Trump's selective use of emergency powers. Throughout his presidency, Trump has claimed emergency authority for:

  • Border walls

  • Trade disputes

  • Immigration restrictions

  • Defense spending redirections

Yet when faced with an actual public health emergency—American children dying from preventable diseases—Trump treats it as cause for celebration rather than immediate action.

As one analysis noted, "Trump's joining forces with RFK Jr., perhaps the country's best-known disparager of vaccines... is a promise to keep inflicting harm upon the public's health." The emergency isn't some external threat requiring unprecedented presidential authority. The emergency is Trump's own Cabinet appointment systematically dismantling America's disease prevention capabilities while the president cheers him on.

Constitutional Crisis: When Presidential Judgment Catastrophically Fails

As experts warn, "The country's health care apparatus will soon be in the hands of unqualified people who have pushed disputed, false or dangerous notions about public health." When presidential judgment so catastrophically fails that children die from preventable diseases, Congress faces both constitutional authority and moral imperative to intervene.

The founders designed congressional oversight precisely for situations where executive officials—with or without presidential support—threaten American lives through systematic policy failures. Kennedy's violations of confirmation commitments, combined with Trump's active endorsement of those violations, create textbook grounds for legislative action.

Congressional authority becomes even more critical when the president who should serve as the ultimate check on rogue Cabinet officials instead becomes their primary enabler.

The Acceleration Effect: Why Presidential Support Makes Action More Urgent

Trump's enthusiastic backing doesn't mitigate the case for Kennedy's removal—it makes intervention more urgent. The Post reports that Kennedy is now "advancing some of his most controversial ideas, including by pushing an investigation that resurrects the discredited claim of a link between vaccines and autism." With 200 days in office and Trump's continued support, Kennedy has only grown bolder:

  • No Self-Correction: Presidential support proves Kennedy will never moderate his positions or reverse destructive policies

  • Accelerated Damage: The Post confirms Kennedy "is advancing some of his most controversial ideas," emboldened by presidential endorsement

  • Constitutional Precedent: Allowing presidential enablement of Cabinet violations without congressional response sets dangerous precedent for future administrations

  • Moral Urgency: Every day of continued presidential celebration represents more potential preventable deaths, as Kennedy continues his systematic dismantling of vaccine protections

The International Humiliation: America's Moral Authority in Ruins

America's allies watch in horror as the president of the world's most powerful democracy actively promotes policies that contradict decades of scientific evidence. Trump's "go wild" promise isn't just domestic policy—it's a signal to the world that America has abandoned evidence-based governance.

Trump's celebration of Kennedy's anti-vaccine crusade while children suffer preventable deaths represents perhaps the most damaging blow to American international credibility since World War II. When preventable pediatric deaths occur in the world's wealthiest nation while the president celebrates the policies that caused them, it represents a complete abdication of the moral leadership America has provided for generations.

The reputational damage extends far beyond health policy. When America abandons evidence-based governance in favor of presidential whims that result in preventable childhood deaths, it undermines every aspect of American international leadership.

The Maxine Waters Question: Presidential Removal vs. Cabinet Removal

Representative Maxine Waters has called for Trump's removal based on his enablement of Kennedy's destructive policies. While the constitutional bar for removing presidents is higher than firing Cabinet officials, the moral case is compelling: when a president actively celebrates policies that kill children, fundamental questions about fitness for office naturally arise.

However, the more immediate and achievable remedy remains Kennedy's removal. Congressional action to remove Cabinet officials enjoys clearer precedent and lower procedural hurdles than presidential impeachment. Kennedy's systematic violation of confirmation commitments provides concrete grounds for legislative intervention that don't require the supermajority thresholds of impeachment.

The strategic priority should be stopping the immediate harm through Kennedy's removal while documenting presidential complicity for future accountability measures.

Congressional Moral Imperative: When Presidential Enablement Demands Action

Congress faces a choice that will define American governance: either legislative oversight serves as a meaningful check on executive power, or presidential enablement of Cabinet misconduct becomes the new normal.

When children die from preventable diseases and the president responds by promising the responsible official even more authority to "go wild," Congress confronts both constitutional crisis and moral emergency.

The founders never envisioned a scenario where presidential judgment would so completely fail that the commander-in-chief would celebrate policies killing American children. But they provided congressional authority precisely for situations where executive power threatens the general welfare.

Conclusion: The Stakes Have Never Been Clearer

Trump's transformation from passive observer to active cheerleader of Kennedy's deadly policies eliminates any possibility that the situation will self-correct. The president who should serve as the ultimate check on rogue Cabinet officials has instead become their most powerful enabler.

The moral equation is stark: preventable pediatric deaths continue, and the president's response is to celebrate the policies that caused them while promising their architect even more authority to transform American health policy.

Congress faces a constitutional and moral imperative that transcends partisan politics. Either legislative oversight serves as a meaningful check when presidential judgment catastrophically fails, or American children will continue dying from preventable diseases while their president treats their deaths as policy victories.

The choice before Congress is binary: act to remove Kennedy despite presidential support, or accept responsibility for every child who dies from preventable diseases while Trump continues celebrating the policies that killed them.

The stakes have never been clearer. American children's lives hang in the balance, and their president has chosen to enable the policies threatening those lives rather than protect them.

History will judge not just Trump's moral failure, but Congress's response to it.
