Trump's Crisis-to-Credit Playbook

The TikTok Edition

How the Don Scripts His Own Sabotage, from Tariff Tempests to App-Saving Shakedowns

September 26, 2025

Oh, TikTok—bless its algorithm-fueled soul. Yesterday, September 25, 2025, Donald J. Trump, the self-anointed savior of short-form video, signed an executive order “rescuing” the app from the grave he himself shoveled for it back in 2020.

Remember? That time he thundered about Chinese spies lurking in your For You Page, slapping a ban threat that had creators sweating and influencers packing their bags? Fast-forward five years, and here he is, greenlighting a $14 billion fire sale of its U.S. operations to Oracle cronies and Abu Dhabi sheikhs, pocketing a multibillion-dollar “security fee” for the Treasury like some mob boss shaking down a corner store.

“YOUR FAVORITE PRESIDENT SAVED TIKTOK!” blared the White House X post today, as if the whole saga wasn’t his personal fanfic of faux-nationalism. It’s peak Trump: Ignite the dumpster fire, fan the flames with fiery tweets, then hose it down with a half-baked “deal” and demand a standing ovation. Classic. Audacious. And, let’s be real, predictable.

But TikTok’s just the viral remix of a greatest-hits album that’s been looping since January 2025. Trump’s not solving problems; he’s directing them, a one-man chaos factory where every crisis is custom-built for his ego, every resolution a remix of “You’re welcome, America.”

Family Separations: The Cruelest Sequel

Let’s connect the dots between Trump’s first-term family separation “pilot program” and today’s mass deportation machine ripping apart American families.

First Term: The Zero Tolerance Experiment (2018)

Trump’s “zero tolerance” policy separated 5,000+ families at the border. Toddlers in tents, chain-link “cages,” leaked audio of crying children that broke the internet. International outrage forced his hand.

June 20, 2018: Executive order—”Families together!” (with fine print exceptions). “Fixed those Dem loopholes!” he claimed, while 1,000+ kids remained in foster purgatory for years. Crisis manufactured, solution theater performed, credit claimed.

Second Term: ICE Stormtroopers and American Orphans (2025)

Now it’s American citizens in the crosshairs. CNN found 100+ US citizen children left stranded after ICE detained or deported their parents. Six-year-old Febe and nine-year-old Angelo don’t understand why mommy was “taken away by ICE.” An autistic 11-year-old in foster care after dad’s detention. A 10-month-old left with family friends during workplace raids.

In just five months, this administration has taken approximately 500 migrant children from their homes into government custody. Tom Homan’s response? “If you’re in the country illegally and you choose to have a US citizen child, that’s on you.”

It’s the same playbook: Create family trauma, claim it’s necessary for “deterrence,” then take credit for “securing the border.” Except now the victims are American kids, and the trauma is permanent.

Government Shutdown Theater: The Sequel We’re Living Through

As we write this, Trump is directing his latest shutdown crisis from the same playbook. September 30 deadline looms, and guess who’s manufacturing the emergency?

First Term Déjà Vu (2018-19)

Back then: 35 days of shutdown hell for $5.7 billion wall funding. 800K furloughed, $11 billion GDP hit, national parks trashed. “Proud to shut it down!” Trump preened on TV, then caved with no wall money. Next day? Emergency declaration to raid military funds. Crisis created, “solution” imposed, credit stolen.

Current Crisis: Same Script, Higher Stakes (September 2025)

Trump cancels meetings with Democratic leaders, calls their demands “unserious and ridiculous.” The real issue? Democrats want to extend healthcare subsidies; Trump wants to cut Medicaid and score political points.

Wednesday night bombshell: Office of Management and Budget instructs agencies to plan for mass layoffs—not just furloughs, but permanent firings—if shutdown occurs. It’s not about fiscal responsibility; it’s about weaponizing government against American workers.

The pattern’s identical: Reject compromise, blame Democrats, then claim any eventual deal as Trump’s “victory.” Funding runs out September 30. Federal workers face layoffs. But Trump gets his crisis theater while real families suffer real consequences.

Federal Troops in Cities: Military Theater in Blue America

Beyond shutdown threats, Trump’s deployed actual troops in American cities. It’s the ultimate crisis-to-credit performance—manufacture urban warfare, deploy the cavalry, claim victory over your own invention.

Los Angeles: Immigration Raids to Military Occupation

June 2025: ICE raids spark protests in LA. Normal Tuesday, right? Wrong. Trump deploys 4,100 National Guard troops plus 700 Marines because federal agents felt “threatened.” The real threat? Democratic mayors who wouldn’t bow to his immigration theater.

August: Mission accomplished! Troops reduced to 250 while Trump claims “victory and restored public safety.” The DOJ charged just 26 people—and dropped 8 cases because immigration officials lied. A federal judge later ruled the deployment violated the Posse Comitatus Act. Details, details.

Washington DC: Federal Takeover of the Capital

August 11, 2025: Trump declares a “crime emergency” in DC, federalizes the Metropolitan Police, deploys 800 National Guard troops. “Liberation Day!” he beams, claiming to rescue the capital from “crime, bloodshed, bedlam and squalor.”

The crisis? Crime was actually dropping—homicides down 32% in 2024, carjackings down 50%. But Trump needed visuals: armored vehicles at Union Station, troops patrolling the National Mall, the works. When asked about using military for homeless clearance, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said soldiers would “assist local law enforcement.” Nothing dystopian here.

The pattern’s predictable: Declare emergency where none exists, flood with federal force, bask in the authoritarian aesthetics. Chicago’s next on the list, then New York. Because nothing says “law and order” like manufacturing the disorder you claim to solve.

Border Emergency Theater: The Military Deployment Spectacle

Day One, January 20, 2025: Trump declares a “national emergency” at the southern border, deploying 1,600 active-duty troops because “America’s sovereignty is under attack.” The “invasion” rhetoric flies as military police hit the desert and Marines build barriers.

The crisis? Border crossings were already declining. The emergency? Recycled talking points from 2018. But the theater was perfect: combat fatigues, chain-link symbolism, and prime-time invasion footage. Within weeks, Trump’s bragging about a “96% reduction” in crossings—as if his deployment caused the drop rather than seasonal patterns and ongoing enforcement.

Classic Trump: Manufacture urgency, deploy the props, claim the victory. The military becomes his border wall backup dancers while he takes bows for trends already in motion.

Tariff Theater 2.0: The Escalation Extravaganza

If border emergencies are Trump’s opening act, tariffs are his main event—and 2025’s been a masterclass in manufactured economic chaos.

The Fentanyl Tariff Frenzy: China Round Two

It’s worth remembering Trump’s first-term China playbook before diving into 2025’s escalation. Back then: Started with $50 billion in tech tariffs for “IP theft,” ballooned to $250 billion. Beijing retaliated, soybean exports cratered 74%, Trump bailed out farmers with $28 billion. “Phase One” deal promised $200 billion in Chinese purchases (they delivered 60%). Classic crisis-to-credit theater.

Now for the sequel, with higher stakes and lower subtlety:

February 1, 2025: “Fentanyl emergency!” Trump slaps 10% tariffs on China, 25% on Canada and Mexico. Markets panic, allies scramble, households brace for price hikes averaging $1,300 yearly.

March 4: Not dramatic enough—bumps China to 20% “fentanyl tariffs” while claiming Beijing hasn’t done enough about precursor chemicals.

April: Still not satisfied—escalates to 125% “reciprocal tariffs” on China, stacked to 145% total when combined with existing duties. Supply chains convulse, economists scream, Republicans privately panic.

May: Plot twist! Claims “breakthrough” with China, reduces to 30% for 90 days. “I negotiated the greatest deal!” he beams, as if he didn’t create the crisis that required negotiating. Same playbook, bigger numbers, identical outcome.

The Universal Tariff Tease

April 2: Announces 10% universal tariffs on all countries because “economic emergency.”

April 10: Suspends most for 90 days to allow “negotiations” with the 75+ countries that “reached out.”

The pattern? Light the economic house on fire, then sell himself as the fire chief. Each escalation creates new leverage points, each “suspension” becomes a victory lap.

COVID-19: From “Hoax” to Warp Speed to Pandemic Surrender

Perhaps no crisis better illustrates Trump’s failure-to-credit pipeline than COVID-19—both his catastrophic first-term response and his systematic 2025 dismantling of pandemic defenses.

First Term: The Deadliest Denial (2020)

February 2020: “It’s going to disappear. One day—it’s like a miracle—it will disappear.” March: “Hoax!” becomes 15 cases becomes 1 million American deaths. Trump downplayed, delayed, and denied while PPE shortages killed healthcare workers and testing failures blinded the nation.

Then the pivot: Warp Speed! $10 billion for vaccine development, shots by December. “I saved 2.2 million lives!” he touted, airbrushing the months of deadly denial that made rescue necessary. Hero on a heap of his own catastrophic mismanagement.

Second Term: Dismantling Defense While Bird Flu Looms (2025)

You’d think a president who lived through COVID would strengthen pandemic preparedness. Instead, Trump’s systematically destroying it:

  • Gutted the White House pandemic office: OPPR, created by Congress after COVID, now operates with zero staff and no director

  • Withdrew from WHO pandemic reforms: Rejected 2024 International Health Regulations designed to prevent the next COVID

  • Canceled mRNA flu vaccine contracts: Axed $766 million for bird flu vaccine development while H5N1 spreads through farms

  • Slashed CDC global health security: From 50 staff supporting outbreak response to just 6, with more cuts coming

The pattern’s identical: Ignore the threat (bird flu spreading), dismantle the defenses (pandemic office), then claim any future response as Trump’s genius. We’re watching him manufacture the next pandemic crisis in real-time.

National Emergency Spree: Crisis Manufacturing at Scale

Beyond pandemic destruction, Trump’s declared everything an emergency. His first 100 days featured eight national emergencies—more than any modern president. Each one unlocks sweeping powers that bypass Congress, turning Trump into both the problem and the solution.

Energy “Emergency”: Fossil Fuel Theater

January 21: Trump declares a “national energy emergency” because America has “precariously inadequate and intermittent energy supply.” The crisis? Biden’s policies allegedly created an “unreliable grid” and high prices.

The reality? The US is the world’s largest oil producer. The grid’s actually more reliable than ever. But emergency powers let Trump fast-track drilling permits, bypass environmental reviews, and claim he’s “making America energy dominant again.” Crisis invented, solution provided, credit claimed.

Foreign Aid Freeze: Humanitarian Crisis as Leverage

Day One: Trump freezes virtually all US foreign aid—$50+ billion—for a “90-day review” to ensure programs align with his agenda. Stop-work orders shut down HIV treatment for 20 million people, malaria prevention, childhood vaccination programs.

The justification? Foreign aid is “antithetical to American values” and “destabilizes world peace.” Translation: Countries better fall in line or lose life-saving aid. It’s hostage diplomacy disguised as fiscal responsibility.

Federal judges repeatedly ordered the funding restored, but Trump keeps appealing. Meanwhile, children die from preventable diseases while he plays budget theater with congressional appropriations.

Paris Agreement Redux: Climate Denial as Policy

January 20: Trump withdraws from the Paris Climate Agreement—again—claiming it’s “unfair” and “one-sided.” Same script as 2017, same manufactured crisis about economic burden.

The timing? After the hottest year on record, during LA wildfires, months after devastating hurricanes. But climate emergencies are real; trade deficits are Trump emergencies. Priorities!

Each withdrawal weakens US credibility while China fills the leadership vacuum Trump creates. But hey, at least we’re not bound by those pesky emission targets.

Back to TikTok: The Encore Performance

And so we loop to TikTok, September 25, 2025—the newest flavor in Trump’s crisis-to-credit factory. That 2020 ban? His Rubio-fueled fever dream of data doomsaying, nuking a platform 150 million Americans adored.

Yesterday’s “save”? A $14 billion Oracle handover with a Treasury “security fee” that smells like a shakedown. Evidence of real Chinese threats? Citizen Lab’s 2021 analysis found nothing but standard data collection. But who needs facts when you’ve got theater?

“I saved your favorite app!”—as if he didn’t try to delete it into oblivion first. It’s the same playbook that gave us border emergencies from declining crossings, tariff wars that required tariff peace, and auto industry “rescues” from self-inflicted wounds.

The Pattern Perfected: Crisis as Governing Strategy

Here’s what you’re witnessing: governance by manufactured emergency. Trump doesn’t solve problems—he is the problem, then sells himself as the solution. Each crisis follows the same playbook:

Step 1: Declare an emergency (border, energy, trade, crime, health, climate)
Step 2: Deploy maximum force/pressure (troops, tariffs, freezes, withdrawals)
Step 3: Let chaos build until it becomes politically costly
Step 4: Offer “solutions” that restore status quo or create new problems
Step 5: Claim total victory for fixing the crisis you created

Federal troops occupying American cities. Tariff wars with allies. Foreign aid hostage-taking. Climate denial during climate disasters. Each manufactured crisis becomes Trump’s heroic intervention story.

It’s not incompetence—it’s strategy. Trump thrives on chaos because chaos requires a strongman. Every emergency declaration expands presidential power. Every manufactured crisis justifies more Trump.

The question isn’t whether Trump will engineer his next disaster to solve—it’s whether we’ll keep buying tickets to this exhausting show while real families suffer the consequences. Government workers face layoffs this weekend. American kids are stranded without parents. Pandemic defenses crumble while bird flu spreads.

The reviews are in: The sequel’s bombing, democracy’s burning, and we’re the ones paying the price. Time to change the channel before the theater burns down.
