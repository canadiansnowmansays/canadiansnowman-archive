The Baseline Problem

When the Dam Breaks

Part 6: From Quantico to Death Threats in Seven Weeks

November 20, 2025

November 20, 2025

Seven weeks ago, I documented Governor JB Pritzker becoming the first sitting governor in American history to publicly demand invocation of the 25th Amendment. Trump had just delivered a 70-minute address to 800 generals and admirals at Quantico, proposing to use American cities as military “training grounds” against what he called “the enemy within.”

Retired four-star General Barry McCaffrey called that speech “incoherent” and “stupid.” The military brass sat in complete silence.

Today, we have our answer to what happens when cognitive decline meets public humiliation: the President of the United States is threatening to execute members of Congress for reciting military law.

This morning, Trump posted on Truth Social: “SEDITIOUS BEHAVIOR, punishable by DEATH!” He then amplified a supporter’s call to “HANG THEM GEORGE WASHINGTON WOULD!!”

His target? Six Democratic veterans—including Senator Mark Kelly, a retired Navy captain and astronaut, and Senator Elissa Slotkin, a former CIA officer—who posted a video reminding troops that they must refuse illegal orders. Not radical activism. Basic JAG school training that every service member learns.

The acceleration I’ve been documenting since June has reached a critical threshold. We’re no longer tracking concerning symptoms. We’re witnessing a president with documented cognitive decline threatening mass execution of political opponents—and doing so within 24 hours of being publicly humiliated by Congress over the Epstein files.

The Epstein Trigger

To understand this morning’s explosion, you need to understand what happened Tuesday.

The House passed the Epstein Files Transparency Act 427-1, ordering the Justice Department to release records related to Jeffrey Epstein. This came after months of Trump’s resistance—his allies openly complained he was dragging his feet, and the pressure from survivors and Congress became overwhelming.

Trump signed the bill. Then he ran to Truth Social for a late-night “victory lap,” claiming he “ASKED Speaker of the House Mike Johnson and Senate Majority Leader John Thune to pass this Bill” and that “Because of this request, the votes were almost unanimous.”

The cognitive distortion is remarkable. He’s claiming credit for something Congress passed over his resistance, with a vote so lopsided it barely required his signature. But more telling is what he didn’t mention: the victims, the survivors, the substance. Just his ego.

Within 24 hours of this forced capitulation, six Democratic veterans posted a 60-second video. They looked into the camera and said: “You can refuse illegal orders. You must refuse illegal orders.”

Trump’s response was to threaten them with death.

The October Acceleration

The Epstein meltdown didn’t emerge from nowhere. Since my last update in early October, the documented symptoms have accelerated precisely as experts predicted.

October 10-27: The Walter Reed Mystery

Trump made a second visit to Walter Reed in six months—unusual for someone who had a “yearly physical” in April. On October 27, he revealed he’d undergone an MRI but couldn’t explain why: “I have no idea what they analyze. But whatever they analyze, they analyzed it well.”

When pressed whether it was for his brain or heart, he said he didn’t know. The White House has provided no clarification.

More revealing was his description of the cognitive assessment. Trump called it a “very hard aptitude test,” claiming Representatives AOC and Jasmine Crockett couldn’t pass it. The MoCA—Montreal Cognitive Assessment—is a screening tool designed to detect cognitive impairment, not measure intelligence. Many questions are answerable by toddlers. The fact that Trump doesn’t understand what the test measures is itself a data point.

October 16: The Iran-India Confusion

Speaking to reporters at the White House, Trump claimed tariffs helped him prevent a “nuclear war” between Pakistan and Iran.

He meant India. In May 2025, there was indeed a brief armed conflict between Pakistan and India. Trump confused two countries starting with “I.”

This wasn’t his first such error. In recent months, he’s claimed three times to have reconciled Azerbaijan with Albania—apparently meaning Armenia. He’s confused Cambodia with Armenia. The pattern isn’t occasional misstatement; it’s systematic geographic and temporal confusion.

As Dr. John Gartner noted: “It’s one thing to get a name wrong, maybe even to reverse it. But he’s actually confusing the countries themselves.”

October 28: Wandering in Japan

During a welcoming ceremony in Tokyo, Trump appeared to forget where he was going as he walked through a room filled with dignitaries and military band members. At one point, he left Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi behind entirely. She had to extend her hand to guide him back to the platform.

Stephen Colbert compared it to “taking grandpa to the mall.” The Daily Show ran commentary treating him like a confused show dog wandering from his handler.

The same day, aboard the USS George Washington, Trump delivered a rambling speech urging the Navy to abandon electronic catapults for steam: “I love the sight of that beautiful steam pouring off the deck. With the electric you don’t have that.” He announced he would sign an executive order to reverse decades of naval engineering.

Late October: The Forgotten Name

Perhaps most telling: Trump forgot Hakeem Jeffries’s name—one day after meeting with him to discuss the government shutdown.

Describing the talks to reporters, he said he’d met with “Chuck Schumer, who was here yesterday, along with... uhh, the, a very nice gentleman who I didn’t really know.”

Jeffries is the House Minority Leader. They’d just met face-to-face on the most pressing domestic issue in the country.

Dr. Gartner’s assessment: “That’s clearly demented memory loss.”

Expert Consensus: The Acceleration Is Real

The clinical experts who’ve been tracking Trump’s decline now speak with increasing urgency.

Dr. John Gartner, former Johns Hopkins psychiatry faculty, stated unequivocally: “There is absolutely no doubt Trump has dementia.” He described “major deterioration in functioning in language and thinking and psychomotor performance and impulse control,” predicting Trump will “fall off the cognitive cliff” before his term ends.

Gartner has documented specific clinical markers: phonemic paraphasia—where words are left incomplete and finished with nonsense endings—which he says is “something very specific that is linked to dementia and organic cognitive decline.”

On Trump’s Quantico speech, Gartner described “a stone skipping along the water. He’s going from one association to another, but it doesn’t make any linear sense.”

Harry Segal of Cornell and Weill Cornell Medicine has echoed the concern: “What’s alarming is how the rate of Trump’s bizarre speech and political decisions have been increasing.”

The term both emphasize is “accelerating.” Not declining. Accelerating.

This Morning: The Dam Breaks

Which brings us to today.

Six Democratic veterans posted a video reminding troops of their legal obligation to refuse illegal orders. They cited the threats to the Constitution “from right here at home.” They encouraged vigilance. They said: “We have your back.”

Trump’s response escalated through the morning:

First: “Each one of these traitors to our Country should be ARRESTED AND PUT ON TRIAL.”

Then: “SEDITIOUS BEHAVIOR FROM TRAITORS!!! LOCK THEM UP???”

Then: “SEDITIOUS BEHAVIOR, punishable by DEATH!”

Then he amplified a call to “HANG THEM GEORGE WASHINGTON WOULD!!”

House Democratic leaders have contacted the House Sergeant at Arms and Capitol Police to ensure the safety of the targeted members and their families. They called Trump’s posts “disgusting and dangerous death threats” and demanded Republicans “forcefully” condemn them.

Governor Gavin Newsom called Trump “sick in the head.”

No Republican leader has defended Trump’s cognitive fitness. They’ve attacked Democratic tone while avoiding the substance entirely.

The silence speaks volumes.

The Clinical Picture

What we’re witnessing fits the pattern experts have been documenting for months.

Cognitive decline doesn’t just affect memory and speech. It amplifies existing personality traits while eroding impulse control. Trump’s grandiosity becomes more pronounced. His paranoia intensifies. His ability to regulate emotional responses diminishes.

Dr. Gartner has specifically warned that cognitive decline is “propping up his grandiosity and paranoia,” and that with nuclear weapons in Trump’s possession, “it really would be impossible to overstate the gravity of this risk.”

The Epstein files represented something Trump couldn’t control—a public humiliation forced upon him by a 427-1 vote. His administration had resisted for months. Survivors and Congress prevailed. He had to sign.

Within 24 hours, he was threatening to execute members of Congress for stating military law.

This isn’t proportionate political theater. This is what happens when impaired impulse control meets wounded ego in someone with access to the powers of the presidency.

The Video They’re Dying For

Let’s be clear about what the six Democrats actually said.

They reminded troops that their oath is to the Constitution, not to any individual. They stated that illegal orders must be refused—which is literally what JAG officers teach and what military law requires. They said they have the troops’ backs.

This is sedition?

The National Lawyers Guild Military Law Task Force publishes guidance on refusing illegal orders. The JAG school trains officers to recognize patently illegal orders that “must be disobeyed, or the service member risks criminal liability.” After Nuremberg, the United States explicitly rejected “I was just following orders” as a defense.

Trump isn’t upset because these Democrats violated the law. He’s upset because they reminded the military that it exists—that service members aren’t obligated to follow him into illegality.

That’s why this terrifies him. Not sedition. Accountability.

The 25th Amendment Problem

In Part 5, I noted the constitutional gap: the 25th Amendment assumes cabinet members can recognize presidential incapacity and act. But when the cabinet is selected for loyalty rather than competence, constitutional safeguards fail.

We’re now watching that failure in real time.

A sitting president is threatening to hang members of Congress. He’s amplifying explicit calls for their execution. He’s doing this in response to a video reminding troops to follow the law.

Where is the intervention?

Governor Pritzker called for the 25th Amendment seven weeks ago after Quantico. Nothing happened. The symptoms have accelerated. The threats have become explicit. Still nothing.

The experts who warned in 2017 that cognitive decline “could only go in one direction” have been proven correct. The governors who recognized the pattern have spoken out. Half of Americans now perceive decline in polling.

And still, the constitutional mechanisms designed for this moment remain unused.

Someone Take His Phone Away

Remember 2009? When Barack Obama was elected president, there was a national debate about whether he could keep his Blackberry. Security experts worried about hacking. Legal scholars fretted about presidential records. The Secret Service had serious concerns.

Obama was eventually allowed a highly secured, limited device - but only after his personal Blackberry was confiscated and replaced with something the intelligence community could monitor and protect.

The worry was about what messages Obama might receive.

Fast forward sixteen years. We now have a president using an unsecured smartphone to threaten members of Congress with execution. In real-time. On a public platform. While the world watches.

“SEDITIOUS BEHAVIOR, punishable by DEATH!”

“HANG THEM GEORGE WASHINGTON WOULD!!”

The presidency has come a long way. The wrong way.

If responsible Barack wasn’t allowed his Blackberry because of security protocols, surely irresponsible Donald shouldn’t have unlimited access to social media.

This isn’t a joke. Every “truth” Trump posts creates security risks, diplomatic incidents, and now—explicit threats against elected officials that require Capitol Police intervention.

The Secret Service took Obama’s phone to protect national security.

Who’s going to take Trump’s phone to protect members of Congress?

Lock Him Up

For nine years, “Lock Her Up” has been the rallying cry of Trump’s movement—directed at Hillary Clinton for using a private email server, directed at anyone who crosses him.

Today, Trump threatened to execute members of Congress for reciting military law.

Perhaps it’s time the chant reversed.

Not as partisan theater. As accountability.

When a president threatens to hang elected officials for reminding troops to follow the Constitution, that’s not political disagreement. That’s incitement. When he amplifies explicit calls for their execution, that’s not free speech. That’s stochastic terrorism.

The difference between “Lock Her Up” and “Lock Him Up” is evidence. Clinton’s emails were investigated exhaustively. No charges. Trump is posting death threats against Congress in real time, on the public record, for the world to see.

If threatening to execute political opponents for stating the law isn’t grounds for accountability, what is?

The Bottom Line

Seven weeks ago, I asked how many flashes of insanity we should tolerate from a president. O’Donnell suggested zero.

We’re well past zero now.

The trajectory I’ve documented—from concerning speech patterns to institutional destruction to explicit death threats—represents the acceleration that experts predicted. Trump turns 80 in June 2026. At that age, one in six Americans has dementia. His father had Alzheimer’s. The chronic venous insufficiency indicates cardiovascular issues affecting cerebral blood flow.

The Epstein files humiliation appears to have been a breaking point. Forced to sign legislation over his own resistance, he responded by threatening to execute members of Congress within 24 hours.

This is what cognitive decline looks like when it reaches the powers of the presidency. Not confusion alone. Not forgetfulness alone. But impaired impulse control meeting wounded grandiosity with access to the mechanisms of state violence.

The dam has broken. The question is no longer whether we’re witnessing decline.

The question is whether any institution will act before the consequences become irreversible.
