Meeting the AI Crisis Through a Renaissance of Clean Power Technologies

Part 4: Ocean Power Awakens - Wave and Tidal Energy's Commercial Dawn

July 2, 2025

The Ocean's Energy Just Waiting to Be Harvested

While solar and wind grab headlines, the ocean holds energy resources that dwarf both combined. India's Ministry of New and Renewable Energy's recognition of ocean energy as a promising renewable resource, with an estimated 40,000 MW of untapped potential along the country's coastline. And that's just one country.

For decades, wave and tidal energy have been the "technologies of tomorrow"—always promising but never quite arriving. That's changing fast. Wave and tidal energy market size is projected to reach USD 1823.1 million by 2031 from USD 880.2 million in 2024, exhibiting a CAGR of 10.96%.

More importantly, these technologies offer something solar and wind can't: predictability. The tides follow the moon, and waves follow the wind patterns. This makes ocean energy potentially perfect for AI data centers that need constant, reliable power.

Tidal Energy: As Reliable as Gravity

Tidal energy might be the most underrated clean energy technology. One of the advantages of tidal energy is its reliability. Unlike some other forms of renewable energy like wind or solar power, tidal energy is predictable. Tides follow a regular and consistent pattern based on lunar cycles, allowing for accurate forecasting of energy production.

The Market Is Heating Up: The tidal energy segment is expected to value more than USD 2.8 billion by 2034 on account of higher energy density than many other renewable sources, making them capable of generating substantial amounts of power even from relatively small tidal currents.

Real Projects, Real Power: In October 2023, Orbital and its project partners were selected by the European Commission for its EURO-TIDES project as part of its Horizon Europe Programme. The project aims to deliver 9.6 MW of tidal energy.

The UK is leading the charge. In December 2024, Inyanga Marine Energy Group announced its 20MW HydroWing tidal project at Morlais, Wales, featuring Tocardo T3 turbines. Securing the UK's largest tidal energy award in AR6, the modular design aims to cut costs by 60% while maximizing energy yield.

Wave Energy: Harnessing the Ocean's Endless Motion

Wave energy faces bigger technical challenges than tidal, but the resource is enormous. Among these scenarios, one stood out: harnessing 27GW of wave energy capacity. This capacity is deemed essential to meet the burgeoning electricity demands projected to triple by 2050 while ensuring the lowest cost for a net zero energy system.

Technology Breakthroughs: CorPower Ocean's breakthrough, marked by the successful deployment of its C4 machine off the coast of northern Portugal, addresses historical challenges faced by wave energy technologies, including survivability in storm conditions and efficient power generation in normal sea states.

This is huge. Wave energy's biggest problem has always been building devices that can survive ocean storms while efficiently capturing energy in normal conditions. If CorPower has cracked this, it changes everything.

Global Deployment Is Accelerating

The most exciting development is how quickly projects are moving from concept to reality:

India: In February 2025, Eco Wave Power signed a non-binding memorandum of understanding with Bharat Petroleum Corp. Ltd. (BPCL), a government-owned oil and gas company, to jointly develop wave energy projects offshore India, beginning with a feasibility study.

Portugal: In January 2025, Eco Wave Power Global engaged Portuguese engineering firm MOQ Engineering to finalize design and load calculations for its wave energy project in Porto, Portugal. The project will feature an underwater wave energy museum and support Portugal's renewable energy goals.

Taiwan: In October 2024, Eco Wave Power Global signed an agreement with I-Ke International Ocean Energy Co. for the sale of its first wave energy generation unit in Taiwan. Under the agreement, I-Ke will fully finance a 100KW pilot project, with plans to expand up to 400MW.

The Economics Are Improving Fast

Growing companies' investments in developing next-generation wave and tidal energy converters with enhanced durability and performance, reducing maintenance costs, and improving scalability will promote the business outlook.

Major Funding Rounds: In February 2025, CorPower Ocean has secured USD 18.2 million in funding from the European Innovation Council Accelerator program, including a USD 2.6 million grant and a USD 15.6 million equity investment.

This level of investment indicates that serious money believes ocean energy is ready for commercial scale-up.

Regional Leadership Patterns

Europe Dominates: The region has played a pioneering role in wave and tidal energy development, with countries such as the UK, Germany, and Italy investing heavily in research and development, pilot projects, and commercial-scale deployments.

Asia-Pacific Rising: The wave and tidal energy industry in Asia Pacific is expected to register the fastest growth, with a projected CAGR of 11.04% over the forecast period.

Perfect for AI Data Centers

Ocean energy has unique characteristics that make it ideal for AI infrastructure:

Predictable Output: Tidal energy systems offer predictable and consistent power generation due to the reliable nature of tidal cycles. This is exactly what 24/7 data centers need.

High Energy Density: Tidal energy boasts a high energy density, meaning it can create significant amounts of power from relatively small installations. This makes it perfect for space-constrained coastal data center locations.

Coastal Advantage: Most major data centers are located near population centers, which are often coastal. Ocean energy can deliver power directly where it's needed without long transmission lines.

The Challenges Remain Real

Ocean energy still faces significant hurdles:

Harsh Environment: Wave energy devices may experience downtime for maintenance and repairs, particularly in harsh marine environments. Salt water, storms, and marine growth all challenge equipment durability.

Environmental Concerns: Construction activities associated with tidal energy infrastructure, such as barrages or turbine installations, may disrupt local ecosystems and habitats.

Scale-Up Challenges: Moving from successful pilot projects to gigawatt-scale deployment requires massive manufacturing and installation capabilities that don't yet exist.

The Breakthrough Moment

Ocean energy appears to be reaching an inflection point. Anders Jansson, Head of Business Development at CorPower Ocean, hailed the recent progress as an inflection point for wave energy, signaling its readiness for widescale adoption.

The combination of improved technology, falling costs, and desperate need for reliable clean power is creating the perfect conditions for ocean energy to finally fulfill its potential.

For the AI energy crisis, ocean power offers something unique: the possibility of large-scale, predictable, renewable electricity generation. While solar and wind will provide the bulk of clean electricity, ocean energy could provide the reliable baseload that keeps data centers running when the sun isn't shining and the wind isn't blowing.

UPDATE - July 8, 2025

Just days after publishing this piece, major news broke that perfectly validates our analysis. A tidal turbine off Scotland's coast has achieved a remarkable milestone: over 6.5 years of continuous operation without needing unplanned maintenance.

This breakthrough at Scotland's MeyGen project isn't just about one turbine—it's proof that ocean energy has solved the durability challenge that skeptics demanded. The MeyGen site, with its four 1.5 MW turbines powering 7,000 homes, plans to expand to 20 turbines by 2030 and potentially 130 turbines long-term. This is exactly the scaling from demonstration to deployment that we identified as crucial for ocean energy's commercial dawn.

As Andrea Copping from the University of Washington noted: "Skeptics said, 'How on Earth are you going to operate these things for any length of time in this very tough environment?' And that's what I think they proved."

The future we wrote about yesterday is happening today.
