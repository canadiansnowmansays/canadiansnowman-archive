Sketches from Elsinore

I have launched a new series !

April 6, 2026

Something I should mention, since you have been kind enough to read the daily work here for the past year.

On Sunday mornings, I have been doing something quieter.

Sketches of Elsinore is a new weekly series — published separately, at Sketches of Elsinore on Substack — about a fictional city of thirty-three thousand people on the Avon River in southwestern Ontario. The city is named after Hamlet’s castle. It has a Swan Warden, a volunteer coordinator named Gerald who moves at his own pace and has copies of everything, and something that is always, gently, rotten.

The series is written in the tradition of Stephen Leacock — Canada’s Mark Twain, though Leacock would have had views about the comparison — and each sketch finds in a small local matter a mirror of something larger happening to the south. The mirror is not always flattering to either reflection.

The Introduction — the town, the river, the Festival Theatre marquee, Gerald already on the bank — published this Sunday. The people follow next week. The sketches proper begin the Sunday after.

In the weeks ahead: an Easter egg hunt with a butter tart coupon and a gold egg problem to the south. A grant application for King Lear that requires measurable cultural outcomes. A proposed Justin Bieber statue and two swans who have opinions about the site. A paddle boat excursion on the Avon that has since been shared three hundred times in Seoul. A municipal election in which the phrase “unelected influence” is used as punctuation — and then, to the south, written in capital letters with federal agents. A question about who owns the swans, which turns out to be a larger question than anyone at the Tourism and Culture Committee anticipated.

If you have been reading CanadianSnowmanSays and occasionally want a Sunday morning that is still paying attention to the world but doing it more quietly — this is the right moment to arrive.
