Episode 10: Scenario Planning, Decision Framework & Series Conclusion

December 4, 2025

CanadianSnowmanSays November 27, 2025

Episode 9 established three distinct investment strategies (conservative, moderate, aggressive) and compared alternative approaches—Waymo’s patient partnership model versus Tesla’s aggressive vertical integration bet.

But strategies need probability assessment: What could actually happen? What are the realistic scenarios and their odds? How should sophisticated short-seller perspectives inform risk management?

This final episode provides scenario planning with updated probabilities, explores what Michael Burry and other sophisticated shorts see, offers a practical decision framework, and concludes the series with synthesis of the entire autonomous vehicle investment landscape.

Updated Investment Thesis: Four Distinct Scenarios (Plus Macro Overlay)

Critical Note: All scenarios below assume relatively stable macro conditions. If AI bubble fears materialize (per Burry’s thesis), subtract 30-50% from all valuation outcomes regardless of Tesla’s execution.

Scenario 1: Tesla Executes Manufacturing Moonshot + Macro Holds (15-20% probability)

What happens:

  • April 2026 Cybercab production starts on time

  • FSD v14.3 achieves unsupervised approval Q1-Q2 2026

  • 2027 production reaches 4M run rate with 750K robotaxis

  • 2028 production reaches 5M run rate with 1.5-2M robotaxis

  • Terra fab operational 2027-2028 with AI5 at <10% competitor costs

  • Unit economics achieve $0.30-1.20 margin per mile

  • AND AI sector multiples remain elevated / no bubble burst

Valuation outcome:

  • 2028: $3-5 trillion enterprise value

  • 2030: $5-7 trillion enterprise value

  • Current investors: 3-5x returns

Who wins: Aggressive growth investors who stomached volatility, maintained conviction through execution period, AND correctly bet macro stays supportive

Investment strategy: This scenario rewards concentrated Tesla positions with long time horizons and strong conviction

Note: Probability lowered from 25% to 15-20% due to macro risk overlay requiring both execution AND macro cooperation

Scenario 2: Partial Execution with Delays + Stable Macro (40-45% probability)

What happens:

  • Cybercab production delayed to late 2026 (not catastrophic, just slower)

  • FSD unsupervised approval comes Q3-Q4 2026

  • 2027 production reaches 3.5M run rate (vs. 4M target) with 500K robotaxis

  • 2028 production reaches 4.5M run rate (vs. 5M target) with 1M robotaxis

  • Terra fab timeline extends to 2029, partners fill gap temporarily

  • Unit economics achieve target but deployment slower than hoped

  • Macro remains relatively stable with normal tech valuations

Valuation outcome:

  • 2028: $1.5-2.5 trillion enterprise value

  • 2030: $2.5-4 trillion enterprise value

  • Current investors: 0-80% returns (depending on entry point and exit timing)

Who wins: Moderate growth investors who balanced exposure, managed risk through execution phase, and correctly assessed macro wouldn’t crater

Investment strategy: This scenario rewards diversified AV exposure with dynamic rebalancing

Note: This is now the “best realistic case” given elevated macro risks and typical execution challenges in aggressive manufacturing ramps

Scenario 3: Manufacturing Execution Failure OR Macro Collapse (35-40% probability)

Two distinct paths lead here:

Path A - Execution Failure:

  • Cybercab production delayed to 2027

  • FSD unsupervised approval pushed to late 2026 or 2027

  • 2027 production reaches 3M run rate (vs. 4M target) with 250K robotaxis

  • 2028 production reaches 3.5M run rate (vs. 5M target) with 500K robotaxis

  • Terra fab delayed to 2029-2030, chip cost advantage postponed

  • Unit economics challenged by slower scale ramp

Path B - AI Bubble Burst (Despite Reasonable Execution):

  • Tesla executes reasonably well (scenario 2 level performance)

  • BUT AI sector multiples compress from 100-200x to 30-50x

  • Tesla’s 172x forward P/E compresses to 40-60x regardless of robotaxi progress

  • Nasdaq bear market creates selling pressure across all tech

  • Correlation goes to 1.0, Tesla can’t differentiate

Valuation outcome:

  • 2028: $400-700 billion enterprise value (execution failure)

  • 2028: $500-900 billion enterprise value (bubble burst despite execution)

  • Current investors: 50-70% losses

Who wins: Conservative investors who waited for proof points, took positions in Alphabet/Baidu instead, or hedged macro exposure

Investment strategy: This scenario rewards patience, diversification, and macro awareness

Note: Probability increased from 25% to 35-40% due to current Nasdaq weakness, AI bubble fears, and typical challenges in aggressive execution timelines

Scenario 4: Execution Failure PLUS Macro Collapse (5-10% probability - disaster scenario)

The perfect storm:

  • Tesla misses manufacturing targets significantly

  • FSD unsupervised approval delayed to 2027 or beyond

  • Capital raise required, diluting shareholders

  • Competitive losses to Waymo or Chinese players

  • SIMULTANEOUSLY: AI bubble bursts

  • Multiple compression + business disappointment compound

  • Nasdaq enters deep bear market (30-50% decline)

Valuation outcome:

  • 2028: $200-400 billion enterprise value

  • Current investors: 70-85% losses

Who wins: Cash holders, short sellers, or those who avoided AV exposure entirely

Investment strategy: This scenario is unwinnable for long-only investors. Only hedged positions or cash survive.

Note: This is the “Michael Burry is right about AI bubble AND Tesla execution fails” scenario. While low probability, the severity justifies consideration in risk management.

The Burry Factor: What Sophisticated Shorts See

When sophisticated investors like Michael Burry take billion-dollar short positions against AI sector companies like Palantir, retail investors should pause and consider what they might be seeing.

Burry’s Implicit Thesis (based on past positions and public statements):

  • AI companies trade at nosebleed valuations based on future promises

  • Monetization timelines keep extending while capital requirements grow

  • Winner-take-most assumptions may not materialize (multiple winners possible)

  • Revenue generation lags investment by years

  • Multiple compression inevitable when reality disappoints vs. expectations

How This Applies to Tesla:

Burry isn’t shorting Tesla specifically—but his thesis applies to any company trading at AI multiples based on future promises rather than current profitability.

Questions Tesla bulls must answer:

  • Monetization timeline: Tesla promises 2-2.5M robotaxis by 2028. What if it’s 2030-2032?

  • Capital requirements: What if terra fab costs $20-25B instead of $10-15B?

  • Competitive dynamics: What if Chinese competitors capture international markets?

  • Regulatory timing: What if approval takes 3-5 years instead of 1-2 years?

The Uncomfortable Truth:

You can be completely right about Tesla’s execution—and still lose money if Burry is right about AI sector multiples.

  • Perfect execution + multiple compression = 0-20% returns

  • Imperfect execution + multiple compression = 50-70% losses

  • Failed execution + multiple compression = 70-85% losses

Investor Implication:

Any Tesla investment requires conviction in TWO independent theses:

  1. Micro thesis: Tesla will execute manufacturing moonshot successfully

  2. Macro thesis: AI sector multiples will hold or expand

Lacking conviction in either one threatens the entire investment.

Practical Decision Framework: Should You Invest?

Use this decision tree to determine appropriate strategy:

Question 1: Do you believe AI sector multiples will hold through 2026-2028?

  • No → Avoid Tesla entirely or take very small position (<2%) with tight stops

  • Uncertain → Conservative or moderate approach with macro hedges

  • Yes → Proceed to Question 2

Question 2: Do you believe Tesla will execute manufacturing moonshot by 2028?

  • No → Invest in Alphabet (Waymo) or Baidu instead

  • Uncertain → Moderate approach with diversification

  • Yes → Proceed to Question 3

Question 3: What’s your risk tolerance for potential 50-80% drawdowns?

  • Low → Conservative approach, wait for proof points

  • Medium → Moderate approach with dynamic rebalancing

  • High → Aggressive approach with concentrated position

Question 4: What’s your time horizon?

  • < 2 years → Avoid entirely, timeline too short for thesis to play out

  • 2-3 years → Moderate approach, align with critical execution period

  • 3-5+ years → Any approach works depending on risk tolerance

Question 5: Can you monitor actively or prefer passive?

  • Passive → Conservative approach with diversification (Alphabet/Baidu)

  • Active → Moderate or aggressive with dynamic rebalancing

Question 6: What’s your conviction level?

  • Low → Don’t invest, conviction required for volatility

  • Medium → Moderate approach, diversification reduces conviction requirement

  • High → Aggressive approach appropriate if risk tolerance matches

Key Principles for All Investors

Regardless of strategy chosen, follow these core principles:

1. Respect the Macro Override: Markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent. Macro conditions can overwhelm even perfect company execution. Don’t fight the Fed, don’t fight Nasdaq trends, don’t ignore AI bubble warnings.

2. Demand Proof Over Promises: The $25 billion AV graveyard is filled with promising technology that failed execution. Wait for validation milestones before increasing exposure. Musk’s credibility on timelines is mixed—demand evidence.

3. Diversify Across Approaches: Don’t bet everything on vertical integration vs. partnerships. Alphabet offers Waymo exposure with diversification. Baidu offers proven profitability. Component suppliers win regardless of platform victor.

4. Monitor Religiously: This is an 18-36 month binary bet with quarterly milestones. Miss a key development and you’ll be caught on wrong side. Set calendar reminders for earnings, production reports, regulatory filings.

5. Accept Asymmetric Downside: In normal conditions, base case might deliver 1-2x returns with 50% downside risk. In AI bubble burst, base case might deliver 0% returns with 70% downside. Risk/reward has deteriorated post-macro analysis.

6. Respect the Timeline: This isn’t a 10-year “Tesla will eventually win” bet. Success or failure will be clear by 2027-2028. If execution isn’t tracking by end of 2026, reassess immediately.

7. Be Honest About Conviction: If you can’t stomach 40% quarterly swings or if you’ll panic sell at the bottom, avoid concentrated positions. Better to miss upside than take downside you can’t handle psychologically.

8. Learn from Failed Investors: Cruise, Argo AI, Apple—all had sophisticated teams and billions in capital. They still failed. What makes you think you can identify the winner when they couldn’t? Humility is essential.

Conclusion: The Manufacturing Moonshot Requires Three Simultaneous Bets

The autonomous vehicle investment opportunity has evolved from speculative technology betting to strategic business analysis. But Tesla’s November 6 announcements created something even more challenging: A manufacturing moonshot with 18-36 month binary execution timeline during a period of heightened AI bubble concerns and tech sector weakness.

After years of speculation and $25 billion in failures, Tesla investors face not one but three simultaneous bets:

  1. Manufacturing Execution: Can Tesla ramp from 1.8M to 5M vehicles, build terra fab, achieve regulatory approval, and deploy 2-2.5M robotaxis?

  2. Technology Validation: Does FSD v14.3 achieve unsupervised capability? Do unit economics work at scale?

  3. Macro Cooperation: Does the AI sector avoid bubble burst? Do multiples hold through execution period?

All three must succeed for bull case returns.

The Investment Paradox:

Bear case is stronger:

  • Execution risk across manufacturing, technology, regulatory is extreme

  • Historical precedent suggests aggressive timelines slip

  • Current Nasdaq weakness and AI bubble fears add macro risk

  • Michael Burry-style shorts suggest sophisticated investors expect multiple compression

  • Even perfect execution may not prevent 50-70% decline if AI bubble bursts

Bull case is bigger but requires everything working:

  • If Tesla executes AND macro remains supportive, vertical integration creates winner-take-most dynamics

  • $3-7T valuations possible by 2028-2030

  • But requires: Manufacturing excellence + Technology validation + Regulatory approval + Continued AI sector strength

  • Four independent success factors must all align—historically low probability

Investment Strategy Summary:

Conservative investors: Wait for proof points (April 2026 Cybercab, Q2-Q3 2026 validation), favor Alphabet/Baidu diversification, monitor macro closely, use small Tesla position with hedges

Moderate investors: Diversified portfolio (40% Alphabet, 30% Tesla, 20% Baidu, 10% suppliers), dynamic rebalancing based on execution and macro, options collars for downside protection

Aggressive investors: Concentrated Tesla position with explicit acceptance of binary outcomes and macro risk, long-dated call options for defined risk, macro hedges (Nasdaq puts), intensive monitoring, clear exit triggers

The Macro Lesson:

When sophisticated investors like Michael Burry take billion-dollar shorts against AI sector, and when Nasdaq shows weakness across tech, retail investors must respect that macro can override even correct fundamental analysis.

You can be right about Tesla’s execution and still lose money if AI multiples compress sector-wide. That’s not a failure of analysis—it’s the reality of investing during potential bubble periods.

Final Assessment:

The survivors of the $25 billion shakeout proved they can outlast their competitors. Now Tesla is attempting something no survivor has tried: Manufacturing-scale deployment that either validates vertical integration as the winning strategy—or proves that even the best-capitalized focused bet can fail on ambitious timelines.

But they’re attempting this during a period when:

  • Nasdaq is selling off

  • AI bubble fears are rising

  • Prominent investors are shorting the sector

  • Multiple compression threatens even successful businesses

For investors willing to stomach extreme volatility, accept macro risk on top of execution risk, and maintain conviction through inevitable setbacks, the post-November 6 landscape offers a binary bet with triple threat dynamics:

Generational wealth creation if manufacturing moonshot succeeds AND AI sector multiples hold, or 70-90% losses if either execution falters OR macro turns against AI regardless of Tesla’s success.

The autonomous vehicle revolution is happening—just slower, more expensive, and with fewer winners than anyone predicted. Tesla’s manufacturing moonshot will determine whether they’re the biggest winner of all, or another cautionary tale about execution risk and macro timing defeating even the most compelling strategy.

The macro lesson always applies: Markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent, and they can punish even correct fundamental analysis with sector-wide multiple compression. Tesla investors in late 2025 must respect both microeconomic execution risk and macroeconomic sentiment risk—because both can kill the investment thesis independently.

Series Conclusion

We’ve traced the autonomous vehicle industry from Tesla’s strategic Dojo pivot through FSD’s real-world validation, dissected the $25 billion in spectacular failures, mapped the surviving global competitors, analyzed the brutal economics that killed most participants, examined the strategic implications of consolidation, and provided comprehensive risk assessment and investment frameworks for evaluating the manufacturing moonshot.

The final verdict: The autonomous vehicle revolution is happening—but Tesla’s November 6 announcements transformed the investment question from “will autonomy work?” to “can anyone execute manufacturing at this scale, with this timeline, during a period when AI bubble fears threaten to compress multiples regardless of business success?”

The companies that learned from their competitors’ failures now control the strategic high ground. Whether Tesla can maintain that advantage through the most aggressive manufacturing ramp in automotive history—while macro conditions cooperate—remains the defining investment question of the 2026-2028 period.

Three simultaneous bets must work. Most investors should diversify across approaches. The bold might concentrate on vertical integration. The wise will monitor both execution and macro religiously.

And everyone should remember: The $25 billion graveyard of failed AV investments isn’t a cautionary tale about bad technology. It’s a reminder that execution, timing, and capital discipline matter more than vision.
