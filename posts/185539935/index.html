The Emperor Has No Clothes

When the Greengrocer Refused to Display the Sign

January 25, 2026

CLASSIFIED - FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

U.S. Department of State
Office of Protocol and Ceremonial Affairs
Second Trump Administration

OPENING: TWO FAIRY TALES, ONE TRUTH

The Danish Tale (1837)

Hans Christian Andersen wrote “The Emperor’s New Clothes.” An emperor parades naked while subjects pretend to see magnificent robes. A child finally says: “But he has nothing on!” The empire doesn’t collapse when he’s naked—it collapses when someone says it aloud.

The Czech Tale (1978)

Václav Havel wrote “The Power of the Powerless.” A greengrocer places a sign in his window: “Workers of the world, unite!” He doesn’t believe it. No one believes it. But everyone displays the sign to avoid trouble, to signal compliance, to get along. The system persists not through truth, but through collective performance of a fiction everyone knows is false.

The Canadian Diagnosis (January 2026)

Prime Minister Mark Carney stood before the global elite at Davos and combined both frameworks:

“A greengrocer places a sign in his window. He doesn’t believe it, no one does, but he places the sign anyway to avoid trouble, to signal compliance. And because every shopkeeper does the same, the system persists.”

Then the pivot:

“Friends, it is time for companies and countries to take their signs down.”

Protocol Assessment: The Emperor has been parading naked for years. The greengrocers have been displaying signs they don’t believe for decades. What changed in January 2026 is that someone finally said it aloud—and others stopped pretending they didn’t see it too.

PART I: THE GREENLAND DEMAND - When the Child Spoke

Protocol 1.1: The Imperial Fantasy

December 2024 - January 2026: President Trump repeatedly demands “ownership and control of Greenland” as “an absolute necessity.”

The Framing:

  • Uses “sphere of responsibility” language from NSS

  • Frames Greenland as essential to American security

  • Cannot comprehend refusal

  • Threatens economic consequences

Protocol 1.2: Denmark Plays the Child

Denmark’s Prime Minister: “Greenland is not for sale.”

Greenland’s Premier: “We are not for sale and will never be for sale.”

European Union: Immediate, unified backing.

The Emperor Has No Clothes - Moment #1:

A mid-sized European nation and an autonomous territory with 57,000 people said “no” and there was nothing he could do about it. The magnificent robes of American imperial power only work when everyone agrees to see them. Denmark refused to see them.

PART II: LIVING WITHIN THE LIE

Protocol 2.1: Why Anyone Displayed the Sign

Carney explained what middle powers always knew:

“For decades, countries like Canada prospered under what we called the rules-based international order. We knew the story was partially false. That the strongest would exempt themselves when convenient. That trade rules were enforced asymmetrically. We knew international law applied with varying rigour depending on the identity of the accused or the victim.”

“But this fiction was useful, and American hegemony helped provide public goods: open sea lanes, a stable financial system, collective security. So we placed the sign in the window. We participated in the rituals and largely avoided calling out the gaps between rhetoric and reality.”

Like Havel’s greengrocer: they didn’t believe the sign, but displayed it anyway because the system, for all its flaws, was preferable to alternatives.

Protocol 2.2: The Rupture

Then Carney identified when the system broke:

“Let me be direct. We are in the midst of a rupture, not a transition.”

“Great powers have begun using economic integration as weapons, tariffs as leverage, financial infrastructure as coercion, supply chains as vulnerabilities to be exploited.”

The killing line:

“You cannot live within the lie of mutual benefit through integration when integration becomes the source of your subordination.”

Translation: You can’t keep displaying the sign when the system has stopped even pretending to serve any purpose beyond naked coercion.

The Emperor Has No Clothes - Moment #2:

The 2025 NSS officially designated Canada as existing “within the United States’ strategic sphere of responsibility”—using literal Kremlin language for vassal states. At that point, continuing to display the sign isn’t diplomacy. It’s self-subordination.

PART III: THE PERFORMANCE OF SOVEREIGNTY

Protocol 3.1: The Devastating Diagnosis

Carney’s most brutal assessment:

“When we only negotiate bilaterally with a hegemon, we negotiate from weakness. We accept what’s offered. We compete with each other to be the most accommodating. This is not sovereignty. It’s the performance of sovereignty while accepting subordination.”

Just as the greengrocer performs belief by placing the sign in the window, countries perform sovereignty by engaging in bilateral negotiations—even as they accept dictated terms.

Protocol 3.2: Variable Geometry - The Alternative

Different coalitions for different issues based on common values and interests:

  • Ukraine: “Core member of the coalition of the willing”

  • Arctic: “We stand firmly with Greenland and Denmark” (explicit rejection of Trump’s claims)

  • Trade: Building bridge between Trans-Pacific Partnership and EU (1.5 billion people)

  • Defense: Sweden (Gripen fighters), Germany (industrial integration)

In six months: 12 trade and security deals on four continents.

This isn’t abstract philosophy. This is systematic reduction of vulnerability to coercion.

Protocol 3.3: Living the Truth

Carney’s instruction manual for taking down the sign:

“Stop invoking the rules-based international order as though it still functions as advertised. Call it what it is.”

“Reduce the leverage that enables coercion. Building a strong domestic economy should be every government’s immediate priority. Diversification internationally is not just economic prudence—it’s the material foundation for honest foreign policy.”

The conclusion:

“We are taking the sign out of the window.”

PART IV: THE TABLE AND THE MENU

Protocol 4.1: Carney’s Framework

“Middle powers must act together because if we’re not at the table, we’re on the menu.”

This was the bargain. For decades, the calculation made sense: sit at the table (display the sign, perform the rituals) or be consumed.

Protocol 4.2: When the Table Became the Menu

But what happens when sitting at the table doesn’t protect you from being on the menu?

The 2025 National Security Strategy provided the answer:

  • Canada designated as within U.S. “sphere of responsibility”

  • Canadian sovereignty framed as threat requiring U.S. management

  • Greenland demanded as U.S. territory

  • European demographics labeled “civilizational erasure”

These aren’t table participants being consulted. These are menu items being selected.

The Board of Peace isn’t a negotiating table. It’s a tasting menu.

Protocol 4.3: The Disinvitation

After Carney’s Davos speech, Trump’s response was swift: Canada was disinvited from the Board of Peace.

The Emperor’s Intent: “You’re off my table. Now you’re on the menu.”

The Emperor’s Assumption: Being excluded from U.S.-led initiatives = punishment.

But Carney already diagnosed this:

“When we only negotiate bilaterally with a hegemon, we negotiate from weakness. This is not sovereignty. It’s the performance of sovereignty while accepting subordination.”

Bilateral negotiation = sitting at the Emperor’s table = being on the Emperor’s menu.

The disinvitation isn’t punishment. It’s liberation.

Protocol 4.4: Building Other Tables

Note Carney’s complete statement: “Middle powers must act together...”

“Act together” = build OTHER tables.

Canada didn’t try to get back to the Emperor’s table. Canada built a restaurant district:

  • The Sweden Table (Gripen fighters, Arctic defense)

  • The Germany Table (defense industrial integration)

  • The EU-Pacific Table (1.5 billion people)

  • The Critical Minerals Table (buyers’ clubs)

  • The Qatar/China Table (strategic partnerships)

At none of these tables is Canada the food.

Protocol 4.5: The Beautiful Reversal

Trump thought: “I’ll remove you from my table. Now you’re on the menu.”

Canada already left: “Your table was a menu. I’m dining elsewhere.”

Canada’s position on Greenland: “We stand firmly with Greenland and Denmark and fully support their unique right to determine Greenland’s future.”

This position is incompatible with a “peace board” where the chairman demands Greenland ownership.

Being disinvited from a table where your fundamental principles make you the main course isn’t punishment—it’s avoiding getting eaten.

Protocol 4.6: The Emperor’s Miscalculation

The Emperor can ban Canada from his table. But Canada is eating at other tables. Better tables. Tables where Canada isn’t the food.

By kicking Canada off his table, Trump pushed Canada to do the exact thing that strengthens Canada’s position.

The Emperor weaponized the exact punishment that makes middle powers stronger.

Protocol 4.7: Who Needs Whom?

The Emperor needs:

  • Greenland (for territorial expansion fantasy)

  • Canadian resources (NSS calls them “shared North American assets”)

  • Allied cooperation (for any actual global initiatives)

  • Coalition members (for legitimacy)

Canada needs:

  • Partners who treat them as equals ✓ (has multiple)

  • Defense cooperation ✓ (Sweden, Germany, NATO partners)

  • Trade diversification ✓ (12 deals, four continents)

  • Technology partnerships ✓ (Sweden, Germany)

Canada got kicked off the table and has everything it needs from other tables.

The Emperor got his restaurant to himself and needs diners.

PART V: THE CASCADING GREENGROCERS

Protocol 5.1: Once Denmark Removed the Sign

If Denmark can refuse Greenland... If Canada can build alternative alliances... If France can declare “Europe prefers respect to bullies”... If Davos participants can force retreat...

Then what happens when all the greengrocers realize the Emperor refusing to shop isn’t punishment?

Protocol 5.2: Who Still Displays the Signs

The domestic feudal system still functions:

  • Congressional Republicans (must display signs to survive primaries)

  • Appointed officials (must display signs to keep jobs)

  • Dependent corporations (must display signs for contracts)

  • FIFA’s Infantino (corruption recognizes corruption)

But internationally, the signs are coming down:

  • Democratic allies with backing (Denmark, Canada, France, Germany)

  • Economic powers with leverage (Davos elite)

  • Military powers who can fight back (Iran, Venezuela)

Protocol 5.3: The Fibonacci Sequence of Imperial Collapse

  1. Emperor demands tribute from weakest → success reinforces belief in power

  2. Emperor demands from stronger subjects → mixed results

  3. Emperor demands from peers → failure, no leverage

  4. Failures embolden previous subjects → cascading resistance

  5. System collapses when enough people stop performing

Current Status: Between steps 3 and 4.

Greenland demand failed. Davos retreat confirmed weakness. Now watching whether step 4 accelerates.

CONCLUSION: THE STREET OF EMPTY RESTAURANTS

The Three Systems Are One

From Andersen (1837): Systems persist when subjects perform belief in fictions

From Havel (1978): The performance continues because the fiction is useful

From Carney (2026): The performance ends when the fiction becomes subordination

The Evolution:

Phase 1 (Old System):

  • One table (U.S.-led order)

  • The sign in the window = your reservation

  • Better to be a junior diner than the main course

Phase 2 (The Rupture):

  • The table itself is how they present the menu

  • “Sphere of responsibility” = you’re the food

  • Board of Peace = tasting menu, not negotiating table

Phase 3 (Variable Geometry):

  • Build other tables

  • Multiple restaurants, different owners

  • Power in diversification

Phase 4 (The Disinvitation):

  • Emperor: “You’re off my table!”

  • Canada: “Your table was a menu. I’m dining elsewhere.”

  • Emperor: Sitting alone with empty chairs

The Discovery:

The Emperor has a board. The Emperor has a menu. The Emperor has a chairman’s gavel.

But the Emperor has no diners.

Because the greengrocers removed their signs, opened other shops, and found other customers.

And the Emperor, walking past shop after shop, table after table, restaurant after restaurant, discovers:

Being banned from a table where you’re the food isn’t punishment.

It’s the Lunch Special escaping before it gets served.

THE FINAL IRONY

Hans Christian Andersen (Danish, 1837): Wrote about emperors without clothes

Denmark (2026): Refused to be the Greenland appetizer

Václav Havel (Czech, 1978): Wrote about greengrocers removing signs

Mark Carney (Canadian, 2026): “If you’re not at the table, you’re on the menu. So we built other tables.”

Donald Trump (American, 2026): Banned the greengrocer from his restaurant

The Discovery:

The greengrocer had other customers.

The menu had no diners.

The Emperor has no clothes, no leverage, no groceries, and an empty restaurant.

Same system. Same performance. Different century. Same ending.

Except this time, the Emperor is naked, hungry, and wondering why the whole street moved to a different neighborhood.
