The Week in Review

August 3, 2025 edition - When Tariff Theater Meets Pentagon Chaos

August 3, 2025

Trump laughing at White House event in April: Mass-fires 62,000 federal workers. August: Fires statistics chief for reporting weak jobs data. The systematic pattern of attacking reality when it conflicts with the narrative

Two patterns define this administration: systematic lies that enable systematic incompetence. Every week, we document how false promises create real failures, how political theater substitutes for governance, and how the combination threatens institutional stability. This week crystallized the ultimate paradox: Trump's most polished political theater—his tariff extortion campaign—operates with systematic competence, while his most critical national security function—the Pentagon under Pete Hegseth—descends into terminal dysfunction. And when economic data doesn't conform to his political narrative, Trump systematically attacks the messengers rather than addressing the message.

THE SYSTEMATIC LIES:

"A letter means a deal" (while issuing unilateral ultimatums, not negotiated agreements)

"We have no inflation" (while inflation runs at 2.4% annually)

"Other countries will pay the tariffs" (while U.S. importers actually pay them)

"Jobs numbers were RIGGED" (without evidence, after weak July report)

"45,000 soldiers in South Korea" (actual number: 26,206)

"Europe gave Ukraine just $100 billion" (they actually committed $298 billion vs. U.S. $139 billion)

THE SYSTEMATIC INCOMPETENCE:

Cannot distinguish between unilateral ultimatums and negotiated trade deals

Fires Bureau of Labor Statistics commissioner hours after weak jobs report with no evidence of wrongdoing

Hegseth operates Pentagon with "skeleton staff" after systematic purge of competent advisors

Announces major policy changes (Ukraine weapons pause) without informing White House or key officials

Claims victory in "trade wars" while systematically isolating America from traditional allies

LIE #1: "A Letter Means a Deal"

THE SYSTEMATIC CONFUSION OF EXTORTION WITH DIPLOMACY

Trump spoke of the letters he sent to various foreign leaders announcing the tariff rates he plans to impose on their countries beginning in August – and said, "I just want you to know - a letter means a deal." That's just not true. Multiple letters the White House revealed on Monday announced tariff rates Trump said he plans to unilaterally place on imports from foreign countries; those letters did not describe negotiated deals.

WHAT TRUMP CLAIMED: His letters to foreign leaders represent successful trade "deals"

WHAT THE LETTERS ACTUALLY CONTAIN: Unilateral tariff ultimatums with August 1 deadlines

THE SYSTEMATIC PATTERN: Trump announced 30% tariffs on goods from the European Union and Mexico that will take effect on Aug. 1... Trump has sent similar letters to 23 other U.S. trading partners this week, including Canada, Japan and Brazil, setting blanket tariff rates ranging from 20% up to 50%.

THE INCOMPETENCE REVEALED: The letters Trump dispatched to more than two dozen countries in recent days were all nearly identical, complete with random capitalizations, typos and other errors. The notification to von der Leyen, for example, referred to the European Union as "a country." The E.U. is a governing bloc that joins 27 separate nations.

Think about this: The President of the United States sent official diplomatic correspondence that couldn't pass a middle school geography test. When systematic incompetence meets international diplomacy, even basic facts get corrupted.

The Mexico Paradox: Punishing Our Helpers

Trump will impose a 30% tariff on Mexico due to fentanyl crossing the border, he said in a letter to Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum. "Mexico has been helping me secure the border, BUT what Mexico has done is not enough."

THE SYSTEMATIC ABSURDITY: Trump simultaneously acknowledges Mexico is "helping" while imposing massive economic punishment. This perfectly captures the lie-incompetence feedback loop: his campaign lies about Mexico being an "enemy" require incompetent policies that punish actual allies.

THE ECONOMIC REALITY: Mexico is one of the countries most vulnerable to the US leader's tariffs, with 80 percent of its exports destined for its northern neighbour. Mexico made up 69% of U.S. vegetable imports and 51% of U.S. fresh fruit imports in recent years, according to U.S. Agriculture Department data. With short shelf lives, produce could see price hikes faster than other goods.

LIE #2: "We Have No Inflation"

DENYING MATHEMATICAL REALITY DURING CABINET MEETINGS

As he has repeatedly, Trump falsely claimed Tuesday, "We have no inflation." The US does have inflation – with Canada achieving just 1.7% inflation in the first quarter while the U.S. struggles with 3.6% inflation over the same period.

THE SYSTEMATIC PATTERN: Trump's inflation lies have escalated to falsely claiming that Democrats are lying when they accurately point out that prices are up and the president's suggestion that not a single price has increased is clearly untrue.

THE CAMPAIGN PROMISE COLLAPSE: Trump made a signature campaign pledge to immediately bring prices down, not to keep prices increasing at a moderate pace. Contrary to his claim on Tuesday, that promised decline hasn't happened.

7 Things That Will Get More EXPENSIVE Under Trump's 'Big Beautiful Bill'" - showing utilities, groceries, prescription drugs, medical care, health insurance, student loans, and grad school all increasing costs for millions of Americans

THE SYSTEMATIC DECEPTION: While claiming "we have no inflation," Trump's own policies systematically increase costs across every category that matters to American families. From gutting SNAP benefits affecting 40 million Americans to cutting healthcare for 17 million people, the "Big Beautiful Bill" delivers the opposite of the price relief he promised.

This demonstrates the exponential lie-incompetence cycle: Campaign lies about bringing prices down enable incompetent policies (tariffs, benefit cuts, healthcare reductions) that raise prices, which require bigger lies about inflation not existing, which demand more systematic denial of basic economic reality.

LIE #4: "Jobs Numbers Were RIGGED"

SHOOTING THE MESSENGER WHEN DATA BECOMES INCONVENIENT

President Donald Trump has fired Dr. Erika McEntarfer, the commissioner of the Bureau of Labor Statistics, whom he accused, without evidence, of manipulating the monthly jobs reports for "political purposes."

WHAT TRUMP CLAIMED: "In my opinion, today's Jobs Numbers were RIGGED in order to make the Republicans, and ME, look bad"

THE ACTUAL DATA: The BLS' monthly labor report Friday showed that the US economy added only 73,000 jobs in July, far below expectations. It also sharply revised down the employment growth that had been previously reported in May and June – by a combined 258,000 jobs.

THE SYSTEMATIC INCOMPETENCE: Rather than address the economic policies causing weak job growth, Trump systematically attacks the independent statistical agency responsible for measuring economic performance. McEntarfer was confirmed by the Senate 86-8 in January 2024 for a term of four years—with overwhelming bipartisan support including from his own Vice President.

The systematic incompetence became even more apparent when the White House struggled to justify the firing to reporters the following day. According to CNBC, administration officials could not provide coherent rationale for dismissing a Senate-confirmed commissioner with overwhelming bipartisan support based solely on accurate data that Trump found politically inconvenient.

The inability to defend the firing exposed the systematic pattern: rather than address why American job growth lags behind Canada's 83,000 jobs with only 73,000 U.S. jobs added in the same period, Trump systematically attacks the institutions measuring his policy failures. When even your own administration can't justify firing the statistician, it proves the problem isn't the data—it's the systematic incompetence the data reveals.

While Trump systematically fires economic data collectors, Democratic Senator Ron Wyden has been systematically exposing the financial networks that actually threaten American institutions. This week, Wyden revealed that the IRS—now headed by Trump appointee Billy Long—never audited Jeffrey Epstein's massive "tax planning" payments despite glaring red flags.

The Epstein Files: Phase 1 cover showing Department of Justice seal, claiming "The Most Transparent Administration in History" while providing minimal actual transparency

THE SYSTEMATIC FAILURE: "Epstein lacked any professional training or certifications in accounting or tax law, yet was chosen by very wealthy people to execute very complex tax-related financial transactions," Wyden wrote to IRS Commissioner Billy Long. "It is unthinkable that transactions amounting to tens of millions of dollars paid to a known criminal for the purpose of helping a mega-wealthy individual dodge billions in taxes were never audited or investigated."

THE RECEIPTS: Wyden's investigation found that billionaire Leon Black paid Epstein $170 million for purported tax advice, with the majority of payments—nearly $100 million—made "on an 'ad hoc' basis without any form of written contract or business services agreement."

THE SYSTEMATIC COMPETENCE: While Trump fires statisticians for doing their jobs accurately, Wyden's team has systematically documented "4,725 wire transfers adding up to nearly $1.1 billion flowing in and out of just ONE of Epstein's bank accounts" and traced connections to Russian banks processing sex trafficking payments.

This represents the ultimate contrast: Trump's systematic incompetence in attacking truth-tellers versus systematic competence in exposing actual financial crimes. While Trump eliminates the people who measure economic problems, Wyden identifies the people who create them.

The Hegseth Brothers: Systematic Incompetence Meets Domestic Militarization

While Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth operates the Pentagon with "skeleton staff" and leaks classified intelligence, his younger brother Phil Hegseth coordinates domestic military operations from inside DHS. A leaked memo obtained by The New Republic reveals the systematic expansion of military deployment on U.S. soil—managed by a family operation spanning both agencies.

The Hegseth Brothers: Pete (left) leaks classified intelligence on Signal while operating Pentagon with 'skeleton staff' after systematic purge; Phil (right) coordinates domestic military deployment 'for years to come' from DHS. Systematic incompetence in national defense enables systematic competence in domestic militarization.

THE SYSTEMATIC FAMILY COORDINATION: Phil Hegseth, DHS senior adviser and liaison to the Pentagon, authored a memo outlining plans for military involvement in domestic operations "for years to come." The memo details a July 21 meeting between the highest levels of both agencies, including his brother Pete Hegseth, to coordinate "defense of the homeland."

THE DOCUMENTED EXPANSION: The memo states "The U.S. military leadership (the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs and NORTHCOM) need to feel — for the first time — the urgency of the homeland defense mission." This comes after Trump already nationalized nearly 5,000 National Guard members and deployed 700 Marines in Los Angeles.

THE EXPERT ALARM: Carrie Lee of the German Marshall Fund called the memo "alarming, because it speaks to the intent to use the military within the United States at a level not seen since Japanese internment." Joseph Nunn of the Brennan Center noted it was "disturbing to see DHS officials pressuring the U.S. military to turn its focus inward even further."

THE SYSTEMATIC INCOMPETENCE: While Pete Hegseth cannot maintain basic Pentagon security or coordinate with the White House on major policy decisions, the brothers systematically coordinate domestic military deployment across agencies. The same incompetence that compromises national security abroad enables systematic militarization at home.

The Gaza Contradiction: When Truth Complicates Alliances

Even in foreign policy, systematic incompetence creates contradictory messaging. Trump acknowledged "real starvation" in Gaza, saying "those children look very hungry" and "you can't fake that"—directly contradicting his close ally Netanyahu, who claimed "there is no starvation in Gaza."

THE SYSTEMATIC CONFUSION: Trump simultaneously promises to set up "food centers" while maintaining unconditional support for the policies creating the starvation. He said "Israel has a lot of responsibility" for limiting aid while continuing to provide weapons for the military operations restricting it.

THE INCOMPETENT COORDINATION: Trump "has seen the images and he does not like them," according to a senior White House official, yet his administration continues the same weapons shipments and diplomatic cover that enable the crisis he claims to want to solve.

This demonstrates how systematic incompetence extends to foreign policy—Trump can identify problems accurately but cannot coordinate coherent responses that address root causes rather than symptoms.

Sen. Cynthia Lummis, R-Wy., said if the data is untrustworthy, the public should find out, but firing the commissioner before knowing whether the numbers are inaccurate is "kind of impetuous."

The Trump-Appointed Predecessor Speaks Out

Former BLS Commissioner William Beach, whom Trump appointed to the position and was confirmed by the Senate in 2019, made a post on X calling McEntarfer's firing "totally groundless," "a dangerous precedent," and undermining "the statistical mission of the Bureau."

When your own previous appointee calls your actions "totally groundless," the systematic incompetence becomes undeniable.

LIE #5: "Other Countries Will Pay the Tariffs"

FUNDAMENTAL ECONOMICS GETS TRUMPIFIED

Trump repeatedly spoke of how his new tariffs mean other countries will have to "pay" the US for the privilege of doing business in the US. Contrary to Trump's frequent assertions, it is the US importers who buy foreign products, not foreign countries themselves, who make the tariff payments to the US government.

THE SYSTEMATIC DECEPTION: Six months into his presidency, Trump continues lying about the basic mechanics of tariffs. This isn't political spin—it's systematic miseducation of the American public about policies that directly affect their wallets.

THE REAL IMPACT: "Higher tariffs going into effect in August also could mean that inflationary effects come through later this year or even into next year, extending the timeline on higher inflation," analysts with Citi wrote.

THE PENTAGON MELTDOWN: When Incompetence Meets National Security

While Trump executes systematic competence in tariff extortion, his Pentagon operates in systematic chaos under Pete Hegseth—proving that even this administration can't sustain competence across multiple fronts simultaneously.

The Signal Scandal Escalates

The controversy, which has dogged the former Fox News personality for much of his tenure at the Pentagon, erupted in March when an editor from the Atlantic magazine was accidentally added to a Signal group chat in which Hegseth and other top Trump administration officials coordinated a forthcoming U.S. bombing campaign in Yemen.

THE CLASSIFIED INTELLIGENCE LEAK: The Post that revealed the inspector general's team was in receipt of evidence indicating the messages disseminated through Hegseth's account on the unclassified chat app originated from a classified email sent to senior Pentagon officials by the top officer overseeing U.S. operations in the Middle East, Gen. Michael "Erik" Kurilla... The email was labeled "SECRET/NOFORN," a classification level at which unauthorized disclosure could be expected to cause serious damage to U.S. national security.

The Ukraine Weapons Pause Debacle

Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth did not inform the White House before he authorized a pause on weapons shipments to Ukraine last week, according to five sources familiar with the matter, setting off a scramble inside the administration to understand why the halt was implemented.

THE SYSTEMATIC BREAKDOWN: The US special envoy to Ukraine, Ret. Gen. Keith Kellogg, and Secretary of State Marco Rubio, who is also Trump's national security adviser, were also not told about the pause beforehand and learned about it from press reports.

THE MANAGEMENT VACUUM: Two of the sources attributed Hegseth's not informing the White House to the fact that he has no chief of staff or trusted advisers around him that might urge him to coordinate major policy decisions better with the interagency partners.

The Staff Purge Continues

Another senior aide to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth is leaving the Pentagon, officials said Saturday, marking at least the sixth departure of a top adviser in Hegseth's first six months in office.

THE SYSTEMATIC PATTERN: The Secretary's inner circle of hand-picked advisers have nearly all resigned or been fired. His chief of staff was dismissed amid allegations of incompetence and unsettling personal behavior.

THE BIPARTISAN REVOLT: "I think he's gonna get it together," Trump said of Hegseth... Meanwhile, one former senior staffer, Colin Carroll, who served as chief of staff to Deputy Defense Secretary Steve Feinberg, said... "We have not had a major challenge at this point... So I don't know how the department would function if we had like the fall of Kabul."

The Inspector General Investigation

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth's team on Tuesday denounced the Pentagon's internal review of his actions in the "Signalgate" affair, calling the independent inquiry "clearly a political witch hunt" and asserting without evidence that details of the nonpartisan review were leaked to the news media by "Biden administration holdovers."

LIE #6: Military Statistics and Basic Geography

CABINET MEETING FACT-CHECKING IN REAL TIME

Trump again exaggerated the US troop presence in South Korea, falsely saying, "You know, we have 45,000 soldiers in South Korea." Official Defense Department data, published online, says the US had 26,206 military personnel in South Korea as of March 31, 2025, with 22,844 on active duty.

According to the Kiel Institute for the World Economy, a German think tank that closely tracks international aid to Ukraine, the US had committed about $139 billion in military, financial and humanitarian aid to Ukraine from late January 2022, just prior to Russia's full-scale invasion, through April 2025 – well short of about $298 billion committed by European countries and the European Union.

THE SYSTEMATIC PATTERN: Trump cannot accurately cite basic military deployment numbers or foreign aid figures during formal government meetings. This isn't political disagreement—it's systematic inability to process factual information during official proceedings.

On a Human Note

While Washington descends into systematic dysfunction, there's something worth noting about competence versus incompetence in practice. This week, as Trump's administration fired statisticians for accurate reporting and confused basic geography in diplomatic letters, thousands of federal employees quietly did their jobs with systematic competence.

Air traffic controllers guided flights safely. Food inspectors protected public health. Weather forecasters saved lives with accurate warnings. Border agents processed legitimate immigration cases. These aren't political appointees playing games—they're professionals maintaining the basic functions that keep society operational.

The contrast couldn't be starker. While political appointees at the Pentagon create "total chaos" and Cabinet meetings become fact-checking marathons, civil servants continue providing systematic competence in essential services. While Defense Secretaries share classified intelligence with journalists by accident, federal employees maintain operational security protocols that protect national interests.

There's something profoundly hopeful about this quiet competence persisting despite systematic political dysfunction. These federal workers aren't making headlines, posting on social media, or conducting investigations into imaginary leakers. They're simply showing up, following established procedures, and delivering results that Americans depend on.

Perhaps most importantly, this systematic competence operates without lies, political theater, or manufactured crises. Just professionals doing complex work with attention to detail, coordination with colleagues, and accountability for outcomes. It's a reminder that governance can function when competence rather than loyalty becomes the primary qualification.

While Pentagon officials hunt for phantom leakers and Cabinet members deny mathematical reality, these federal employees demonstrate what systematic competence actually looks like—and why protecting institutional capacity matters more than partisan theater.

Sometimes the most authentic leadership comes from people whose names we'll never know, working in agencies we rarely consider, solving problems that never make the news. That's not just reassuring—it's essential for democracy's survival when systematic incompetence captures the highest levels of government.
