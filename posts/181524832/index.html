The Charlie Kirk Debates

#13: “Nuremberg Trials for Gender-Affirming Care” - Part I: The Case

December 16, 2025

Charlie Kirk’s “Prove Me Wrong” wasn’t rhetoric—it was a radical call to evidence, a dare to dismantle weak claims with stronger ones. That’s the spirit guiding these debates: Direct engagement with his words, as if he’s here to rebut, because silencing the dead dishonors the discourse he ignited. No gloating over gaps; just the grind of applying his standard back at him, from constitutional ink to scriptural light. If the address feels forward, it’s by design—honoring a provocateur by provoking thought. With that, let’s turn the mirror:

Charlie Kirk, let’s apply your “Prove Me Wrong” standard to your call for “Nuremberg-style trials” for doctors providing gender-affirming care. On April 1, 2024, you declared: “These doctors need to be put in prison quickly. We need to have a Nuremberg-style trial for every gender-affirming clinic doctor.” You compared healthcare providers to Nazi war criminals. Prove me wrong that this isn’t eliminationist rhetoric designed to criminalize medical care and target an already vulnerable community.

Kirk’s Call for Nazi-Style Tribunals

On April 1, 2024, during an episode of “The Charlie Kirk Show,” Kirk escalated his anti-transgender rhetoric to an unprecedented level. He didn’t just oppose gender-affirming care—he called for doctors providing it to face the same tribunals that prosecuted Nazi war criminals.

The Full Statement

Kirk described a scenario where a mother’s daughter “all of a sudden came home and said, I’m a boy”:

“And the whole assembly line, the conveyor belt was started to grind. They wanted to give her cross-sex hormones. They wanted to give her testosterone therapy. They wanted to cut off her breasts. They wanted to do all these sorts of things, and these doctors do this without any thought whatsoever. Yeah. Sure. Here’s the script. Here’s the clinic. Go here. Go for surgery. These doctors need to be put in prison quickly. We need to have a Nuremberg-style trial for every gender-affirming clinic doctor. We need it immediately.”

Kirk invoked the Nuremberg trials—the post-World War II international tribunals that prosecuted Nazi leaders for crimes against humanity including genocide—to describe how America should treat doctors providing evidence-based medical care to transgender patients.

The Nuremberg Comparison

The Nuremberg trials (1945-1946) prosecuted Nazi officials for:

  • The Holocaust (systematic murder of six million Jews)

  • Crimes against humanity

  • War crimes

  • Genocide

Kirk compared doctors providing hormone therapy and gender-affirming surgery—medical treatments endorsed by every major medical organization—to Nazi war criminals who orchestrated mass murder.

The Same Day: Nationwide Ban Proposal

On the same day Kirk called for Nuremberg trials, he also urged Trump to propose a nationwide ban on gender-affirming care for transgender people. Kirk wasn’t just expressing opposition—he was advocating for:

  1. Criminalizing medical care by imprisoning doctors

  2. Show trials modeled on Nazi war crime tribunals

  3. Federal prohibition of evidence-based treatment

  4. Immediate action (”We need it immediately”)

Kirk’s Broader Anti-Transgender Campaign

The Nuremberg call wasn’t isolated—it represented the culmination of Kirk’s systematic campaign to dehumanize and eliminate transgender people from public life.

“A Throbbing Middle Finger to God” (2023): At a megachurch, Kirk said: “The one issue I think that is so against our senses, so against the natural law, and dare I say, a throbbing middle finger to God, is the transgender thing happening in America right now.”

Biblical “Abomination” (2023): Kirk cited Deuteronomy 22:5 to call transgender people an “abomination,” using the same verse to attack swimmer Lia Thomas.

“Take Care of Things the 1950s Way” (Riley Gaines interview): Kirk told Gaines that people should have “just took care of” transgender people “the way we used to take care of things in the 1950s and 60s.”

This reference was to an era when transgender people faced lobotomy, shock therapy, involuntary institutionalization, and police violence. Commissioners described queer people as “a cancer in the community.”

“Groomers” and Child “Mutilation”: Kirk routinely described transgender people as “groomers” and said transgender children were “mutilating” their bodies—language designed to provoke violence.

Physical Confrontation Advocacy: Kirk called for transgender athletes to be physically confronted “for the purported sin of trying to play sports.”

“Social Contagion” Theory: When asked if he would support his children if they came out as transgender, Kirk said he would not, calling trans identity a “social contagion” explained by “the autism spectrum” and “puberty anxiety.”

He claimed: “Young women have always been more prone to social contagions, and transgenderism is a social contagion. A 5,000 percent increase in transgenderism is not normal.”

Transhumanism Conspiracy (February 2022): Kirk said the “transgender movement” aims to “turn human beings into machines” as “an introductory phase to get you to strip yourself of your humanity to mesh with machines.”

Kirk’s Misinformation Campaign

Kirk systematically spread false information about transgender people and gender-affirming care:

Violence Misinformation: Moments before his assassination, Kirk responded to a question about transgender mass shooters by saying “Too many,” despite data showing the vast majority of mass shootings are committed by cisgender men.

Medical Misinformation: Kirk portrayed gender-affirming care as rushed and thoughtless (”Here’s the script. Here’s the clinic. Go here. Go for surgery.”), ignoring the careful, multi-step processes involving mental health assessments, informed consent, and medical evaluation.

Regret Rate Lies: Kirk implied widespread regret, ignoring that gender-affirming surgeries have among the lowest regret rates of any major surgery (0.3-0.6%).

The “Alphabet Mafia” Dehumanization

Kirk routinely called LGBTQ+ people the “alphabet mafia”—language that frames a vulnerable community as organized criminals. Combined with his Nuremberg rhetoric, this created a narrative where transgender people and their healthcare providers were dangerous criminals requiring extraordinary legal measures.

The Pattern: Eliminationist Rhetoric

Kirk’s campaign against transgender people followed a classic pattern of eliminationist rhetoric:

  1. Dehumanization - “groomers,” “alphabet mafia,” “throbbing middle finger to God”

  2. Disease metaphor - “social contagion,” invoking 1950s-60s “cancer” language

  3. Threat framing - corrupting children, destroying society, defying God

  4. Violence advocacy - “take care of things,” physical confrontation, Nuremberg trials

  5. Criminalization - imprison doctors, ban care, show trials

Each step moved from rhetoric toward actual violence and legal persecution.

The Case Established: Kirk called for imprisoning doctors and conducting Nazi-style war crime tribunals for healthcare providers offering gender-affirming care. He provided zero evidence, misrepresented medical processes, and used eliminationist rhetoric designed to criminalize medicine and target vulnerable people.

Now let’s test Kirk’s “Prove Me Wrong” standard against medical consensus, constitutional rights, Holocaust memory, and basic human decency.
