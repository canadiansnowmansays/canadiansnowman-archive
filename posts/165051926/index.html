The $37 Trillion Question

Episode 9: The Senate's $5 Trillion Gamble - America Chooses Fiscal Collapse

July 22, 2025

A weekly series examining how America broke its postwar promise of fiscal responsibility. Each week, we trace the bipartisan policy choices that created today's debt crisis and explore evidence-based solutions that could restore fiscal sanity. This analysis is updated regularly with the latest economic and political developments.

Three weeks ago, America made a historic choice. On July 4th, President Trump signed the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" into law, officially enacting what Republicans themselves acknowledge as "the greatest increase in the debt ceiling ever." The irony of signing this fiscal monstrosity on Independence Day seems lost on a political class that has just mortgaged the nation's future for short-term political gain.

The bill's journey to enactment validates every warning this series has explored about America's bipartisan fiscal dysfunction. The Senate approved the bill 51–50 on July 1, 2025, with Vice President JD Vance casting a tiebreaking vote in support. It passed the House of Representatives, 218–214, on July 3, 2025. The final margin—a single vote in each chamber—reveals how narrowly America chose fiscal collapse over responsibility.

As a Canadian watching this spectacle unfold, the predictable outcome appears both tragic and inevitable. The $37 trillion debt mountain will now grow faster than ever, and the Senate's decision ensures America continues climbing toward fiscal crisis rather than choosing the proven path of progressive taxation and spending discipline.

The Enacted Catastrophe: America's Fiscal Suicide Note

The OBBBA includes hundreds of provisions, is estimated to add roughly $3 trillion to the national debt and is projected to cut approximately $4.46 trillion in tax revenue over a 10-year period. This represents the largest single addition to America's debt burden in peacetime history—a monument to fiscal irresponsibility that will burden generations with the consequences of today's political cowardice.

Major Debt Ceiling Increases by Administration (1981-2025)

The proposed $5.2 trillion debt ceiling increase ranks as the largest in American history, continuing a pattern of escalating fiscal dysfunction. The 2025 increase dwarfs previous expansions, even when adjusted for inflation and economic size.

The Tax Cut Giveaway

The law's core provisions reveal the complete capture of American fiscal policy by wealthy interests. The bill permanently extends the individual tax rates Trump signed into law in 2017, which were set to expire at the end of the year—tax cuts that primarily benefited high earners and already added $1.9 trillion to national debt during their initial implementation.

Most egregiously, the law includes new tax deductions for tips and overtime pay for workers making less than $150,000, capped to $25,000 each. This provision would expire in 2028—revealing the cynical political calculation behind seemingly populist provisions. Workers get temporary relief while wealthy taxpayers receive permanent benefits.

The legislation also increases the cap on the state and local tax deduction to $40,000 for taxpayers making less than $500,000, with the cap reverting to $10,000 after 5 years, at an estimated cost of $142 billion. This provision—demanded by wealthy suburban Republicans—demonstrates how supposed "fiscal conservatives" abandon principle when their own constituents' tax bills are involved.

The Spending Increases Without Revenue

Beyond massive tax cuts, the law includes substantial unfunded spending commitments that compound fiscal irresponsibility. Border security and immigration enforcement provisions cost hundreds of billions while the bill includes a debt ceiling increase to $5 trillion—more than the House originally proposed—to accommodate its own fiscal madness.

The law's agricultural subsidies, rural development programs, and other provisions serve identifiable political constituencies while contributing to the collective fiscal disaster that threatens everyone's long-term interests. Each provision might address legitimate concerns, but their combination creates an unsustainable debt trajectory.

Most remarkably, the legislation enables its own irresponsibility by raising the borrowing limit to prevent the government shutdown that its provisions would otherwise trigger. This self-referential fiscal madness represents the logical endpoint of decades of policy choices that divorce political benefits from fiscal consequences.

The Hidden Cuts That Reveal True Priorities

While extending tax cuts for the wealthy, the law imposes harsh cuts on programs serving America's most vulnerable citizens. The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) estimates the bill's health provisions will result in 11.8 million people losing health coverage by 2034. This stunning figure reveals the true cost of fiscal irresponsibility—the most vulnerable Americans pay the price for tax cuts benefiting the wealthy.

The bill is partially paid for by significant cuts to health care and nutrition programs, like Medicaid and the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, or SNAP. These cuts force state governments to choose between maintaining services for their most vulnerable residents or absorbing costs that the federal government can no longer afford due to self-inflicted revenue reductions.

The law also ends tax credits for new and used electric vehicles, installation of home EV charging equipment and insulation or energy efficient heating and cooling systems—eliminating programs that would reduce long-term energy costs while addressing climate change. This shortsighted approach sacrifices environmental progress and consumer savings to fund tax cuts for wealthy Americans.

Republican Fiscal Fraud: The Complete Abandonment of Conservative Principles

The Republican Party's embrace of history's largest debt increase exposes the complete hollowness of their fiscal conservative rhetoric. The same senators who demanded spending cuts and balanced budgets during Democratic administrations just enacted more debt than any legislation in American history.

The Lonely Voices of Fiscal Sanity

Even within Republican ranks, a few voices recognized the law's fiscal insanity. Republican Senators Susan Collins of Maine and Rand Paul of Kentucky joined all Democratic senators in opposing the resolution. Senator Paul's warning that "There's nothing fiscally conservative about expanding the debt ceiling more than we've ever done it before" proved prophetic as his party chose debt over discipline.

However, these isolated voices of fiscal sanity were overwhelmed by Republican leadership's magical thinking about tax cuts paying for themselves—a claim that four decades of evidence has thoroughly debunked.

The Political Calculation Over Fiscal Reality

Republican support for massive debt increases reflects pure political calculation. Tax cuts provide immediate benefits to wealthy donors and middle-class voters while imposing costs that emerge gradually and fall on diffuse constituencies. The law's enactment early in Trump's term allows Republicans to claim credit for tax relief while pushing fiscal consequences beyond the next electoral cycle.

Corporate interests strongly supported the legislation through campaign contributions and lobbying expenditures that dwarfed public interest advocacy for fiscal responsibility. This concentrated support for irresponsible policies overwhelmed diffuse public interest in sustainable fiscal management—a dynamic that this series has repeatedly identified as central to America's fiscal dysfunction.

Democratic Opposition: Correct but Insufficient

Democratic opposition to the "One Big Beautiful Bill" reflected genuine concern about fiscal irresponsibility, but their criticism carried limited weight given their own history of debt-financed spending programs that contributed to current problems.

The Accurate Criticisms

AHA President and CEO Rick Pollack said, "Today is an extremely disappointing and very difficult day for health care in America. Despite months of clearly demonstrating the implications that these Medicaid proposals will have on the patients and communities we serve, especially the most vulnerable populations, Congress has enacted cuts of nearly a trillion dollars to the Medicaid program."

This criticism accurately captures the law's cruel trade-off: tax cuts for wealthy Americans funded through healthcare cuts for vulnerable populations. Democratic leaders correctly identified the legislation's regressive distribution effects and fiscal madness, providing necessary opposition to legislation that worsens America's debt crisis while serving narrow wealthy interests.

However, Democratic criticism must acknowledge their own contributions to fiscal problems through spending programs inadequately funded by progressive taxation. The pattern is clear: Democrats build worthy programs through deficit financing while Republicans cut taxes for the wealthy through debt accumulation. Both approaches avoid the difficult choices that fiscal responsibility requires.

The Alternative That Wasn't Chosen

Progressive Democrats have proposed alternatives that would fund government operations through progressive taxation rather than continued borrowing. Proposals for wealth taxes, higher rates on capital gains, and corporate minimum taxes offer sustainable revenue sources that could fund public investment while reducing debt accumulation.

But these progressive proposals lack sufficient Democratic support for implementation, revealing internal party divisions between progressives who understand fiscal sustainability and moderates who fear political consequences of higher taxes on wealthy constituents.

The Enacted Catastrophe: America's Fiscal Future

The law's enactment pushes America decisively toward fiscal crisis. Reuters reported that non-partisan analysts believe that the resolution, if enacted as currently written, would add $5.7 trillion to the national debt of the United States over the next 10 years. This massive debt increase will push federal debt toward 150-160% of GDP within a decade—levels that historically coincide with fiscal crises.

Interest payments will consume 30-35% of federal revenue by the early 2030s, effectively paralyzing government capacity to address climate change, infrastructure needs, demographic transition, or economic crises that will inevitably emerge. The law ensures that future American governments will face impossible choices between essential services and debt service obligations.

International Implications of America's Fiscal Surrender

The law's enactment sends unmistakable signals to international observers about America's commitment to fiscal irresponsibility despite obvious debt crisis. NATO allies privately express concern about America's capacity to maintain defense commitments when debt service consumes increasing shares of federal revenue.

Trade partners worry about currency instability that could result from fiscal crisis, leading them to diversify away from dollar-denominated reserves while developing alternative payment systems. Most dangerously, the law's passage threatens the dollar's reserve currency status that has provided enormous economic advantages since World War II.

China's leadership observes American fiscal dysfunction with strategic satisfaction, recognizing that debt crisis constrains American international engagement while providing opportunities for expanded Chinese influence. The law's enactment validates authoritarian arguments that democratic governance provides inferior long-term planning capacity compared to authoritarian systems.

The Path Not Taken: What Responsible Leadership Would Have Done

Rather than enacting history's largest peacetime debt increase, responsible leadership could have addressed legitimate policy goals through sustainable financing:

Progressive Tax Reform Instead of Regressive Tax Cuts

Instead of extending tax cuts for the wealthy, Congress could have implemented progressive tax increases that generate revenue for debt reduction while funding infrastructure, climate action, and social programs that drive long-term growth. The $600 billion annual revenue potential from progressive taxation could have funded worthy priorities while reducing rather than increasing debt accumulation.

Targeted Investment Instead of Blanket Spending

Rather than blanket spending increases across multiple categories, responsible legislation could have identified specific priorities—infrastructure, climate, education—while providing sustainable funding through progressive taxation. This approach would maintain fiscal discipline while addressing genuine national needs.

International Leadership Instead of Fiscal Isolationism

Responsible American leadership could have proposed international coordination on corporate taxation and tax haven abuse that would reduce competitive pressures while ensuring profitable enterprises contribute appropriately to public investments. Such leadership would restore American credibility while addressing global challenges requiring coordinated responses.

The Canadian Perspective: Watching a Neighbor's Preventable Tragedy

As a Canadian observing this fiscal dysfunction, I'm struck by how avoidable America's crisis remains despite the law's enactment. Our experience demonstrates that fiscal responsibility and comprehensive public services can coexist through progressive taxation that asks those who benefit most from economic success to contribute proportionally to its maintenance.

However, the law's passage makes America's fiscal crisis more likely and more severe when it arrives. The $400 billion annual trade relationship between our countries depends on stable economic policies that enable predictable business planning rather than the policy volatility that fiscal crisis would inevitably produce.

Most importantly, global stability requires American fiscal responsibility given the dollar's reserve currency role and America's central position in international financial architecture. The law's enactment increases the probability of American fiscal collapse that would trigger global crisis affecting every nation regardless of their own fiscal management.

Conclusion: America's Choice and Its Consequences

The enactment of the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" represents a defining moment for American democracy and fiscal responsibility. America has chosen to continue down the path of debt accumulation that leads toward fiscal crisis, accelerating the debt trajectory that threatens long-term prosperity and international standing.

The law's passage validates every warning about American fiscal dysfunction while ensuring that interest payments will consume growing shares of federal revenue as essential public investments compete with debt service for increasingly scarce resources. The narrow margins by which the law passed—single votes in both chambers—demonstrate how closely America came to choosing fiscal responsibility over continued magical thinking.

Most tragically, the law's regressive distribution effects ensure that America's most vulnerable citizens will bear the costs of tax cuts benefiting the wealthy. Healthcare cuts, nutrition program reductions, and eliminated clean energy incentives represent the true price of fiscal irresponsibility—a price paid by those least able to afford it.

The evidence was overwhelming, the stakes were enormous, and the choice was clear. American political leaders lacked the courage to choose evidence over ideology, responsibility over political gain, and long-term sustainability over short-term benefits for wealthy donors.

The $37 trillion question has been answered: American democracy no longer possesses the capacity for fiscal sanity when faced with obvious threats to long-term sustainability. The consequences of this choice will unfold over the coming decade as debt service crowds out essential government functions and international confidence in American fiscal management continues to erode.

Next week, in our final episode, we'll examine what hope remains for American fiscal revival after this historic choice for continued irresponsibility, drawing lessons from both American history and international examples to chart a path toward the fiscal sanity that America desperately needs but apparently cannot choose.
