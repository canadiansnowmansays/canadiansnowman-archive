Trump TACO Tuesdays

Episode 3: The China TACO Vindicated

June 17, 2025

From "TOTALLY VIOLATED" to "RELATIONSHIP IS EXCELLENT!" - A Complete TACO Cycle

Welcome to Episode 3 of Trump TACO Tuesdays, where we track the gap between bold presidential promises and inevitable reality checks. Just two weeks after predicting China policy would moderate from confrontation to cooperation, Trump's Wednesday Truth Social post provides perfect real-time vindication of the TACO framework. Our inaugural Double TACO analysis predicted that China policy would shift from Trump's "TOTALLY VIOLATED" accusations to diplomatic accommodation, while steel tariffs would implement as targeted increases rather than broader policy disruption. Both predictions proved accurate as Trump's frustrated outbursts became a "very positive" Xi call, followed by renewed London trade talks.

This week, we witness the complete vindication of the TACO framework through Trump's Wednesday Truth Social announcement. His trade deal with China represents perhaps the most perfect example of the TACO pattern ever documented: bold threats leading to accommodation disguised as victory, complete with the psychological markers our analysis identified. The progression from "HAS TOTALLY VIOLATED ITS AGREEMENT WITH US" to "RELATIONSHIP IS EXCELLENT!" provides a masterclass in how personal psychology shapes policy outcomes when institutional constraints meet diplomatic reality.

Every element of this post reveals classic TACO psychology - from the overenthusiastic caps to the defensive parenthetical admissions. This single tweet documents the complete journey from confrontation to accommodation, wrapped in victory language that masks strategic defeat.

TACO Timeline: The Complete Cycle Documented

January-March 2025: Bold Declaration Phase Campaign promises of immediate, sweeping tariffs escalated to 145% duties on Chinese goods, with threats of complete economic decoupling. Trump positioned this as necessary to counter Chinese "cheating" and promised quick resolution through American strength.

April-May 2025: Reality Check Phase
China retaliated with 125% counter-tariffs and suspended rare earth exports, disrupting global supply chains. Markets plunged, business groups protested, and economic damage mounted. Geneva talks offered temporary relief (30% US/10% China), but tensions quickly re-emerged over implementation.

Late May 2025: Frustrated Blame-Shifting Phase As the Geneva arrangement stalled, Trump's frustration exploded publicly: "China, perhaps not surprisingly to some, HAS TOTALLY VIOLATED ITS AGREEMENT WITH US. So much for being Mr. NICE GUY!" This venting revealed psychological pressure as initial promises proved undeliverable.

June 2025: Accommodation as Victory Phase Following the June 5th Trump-Xi call and London negotiations, the classic TACO capitulation arrived. Trump's Wednesday announcement presented a deal giving China better terms than before the trade war (10% vs. ~20% pre-2025) while spinning it as American triumph: "RELATIONSHIP IS EXCELLENT!"

The Psychology of TACO Language: Deconstructing the Victory Tweet

Trump's Wednesday Truth Social post offers unprecedented insight into TACO psychology. Every element reveals the gap between perception and reality:

"OUR DEAL WITH CHINA IS DONE" - The aggressive certainty masks underlying insecurity. If the deal were genuinely advantageous, such emphatic declarations would be unnecessary.

"SUBJECT TO FINAL APPROVAL WITH PRESIDENT XI AND ME" - Immediate hedging undermines the opening confidence. Classic TACO behavior: bold announcement followed by conditional caveats.

"FULL MAGNETS, AND ANY NECESSARY RARE EARTHS, WILL BE SUPPLIED, UP FRONT, BY CHINA" - Framing China's resumption of profitable exports as an American victory. China needs to sell these materials; treating their export as a concession reveals negotiating weakness.

"INCLUDING CHINESE STUDENTS USING OUR COLLEGES AND UNIVERSITIES (WHICH HAS ALWAYS BEEN GOOD WITH ME!)" - The defensive parenthetical admission that student visa restrictions were never serious policy. Trading away fake threats for real concessions epitomizes TACO deal-making.

"WE ARE GETTING A TOTAL OF 55% TARIFFS, CHINA IS GETTING 10%" - The mathematical deception at the heart of TACO psychology. Presenting a 55%/10% split as victory when the pre-trade war baseline was roughly 20%/20% shows how accommodation gets reframed as strength.

"RELATIONSHIP IS EXCELLENT! THANK YOU FOR YOUR ATTENTION TO THIS MATTER!" - Classic post-TACO relationship repair, complete with gratitude ritual that signals subordinate status in the negotiation.

The Economic Reality: China's Strategic Victory

While Trump celebrates the "excellent relationship," the economic facts reveal China's masterful navigation of the TACO pattern. Beijing's strategy of matching Trump's tariffs while controlling rare earth exports forced exactly the accommodation they sought.

The Numbers Tell the Story:

  • Pre-trade war baseline (2024): ~20% US tariffs, ~20% Chinese tariffs

  • Peak escalation (April 2025): 145% US tariffs, 125% Chinese tariffs

  • "Victory" settlement (June 2025): 55% US tariffs, 10% Chinese tariffs

  • The winner: China now pays half the tariffs they paid before Trump's trade war began

China's Position Fundamentally Improved: The 10% Chinese tariffs represent a dramatic improvement from the ~20% baseline before Trump's trade war began. China endured months of economic pressure to emerge with enhanced terms and demonstrated resistance to American coercion.

Strategic Assets Retained: China maintains control over rare earth processing and strategic mineral exports while gaining assurance that student visa restrictions were performative rather than substantive policy.

International Validation: As Washington Post analysis revealed, Chinese state media are celebrating this as vindication of their "never kneel down" strategy. Jeremy Chan, senior analyst at Eurasia Group, called it "near capitulation by the U.S." Former Global Times editor Hu Xijin posted simply: "This is called, 'victory.'"

The TACO Awareness: Most tellingly, influential Chinese commentators have explicitly seized on the "TACO" acronym - "Trump Always Chickens Out" - suggesting Beijing strategists recognize and can exploit the predictable pattern.

Market Implications: Trading the TACO Pattern Successfully

The China TACO provides valuable lessons for investors and policymakers about distinguishing between Trump's negotiating theater and actual policy outcomes. Markets initially reacted to tariff threats and escalatory rhetoric, but sophisticated traders increasingly recognize the predictable moderation pattern and are profiting from it.

The TACO Trading Strategy: Investment approaches should focus on TACO inflection points rather than initial announcements. When Trump's language shifts from threats to relationship repair ("EXCELLENT!"), it typically signals accommodation disguised as victory - time to buy China-exposed assets. Conversely, when frustration emerges publicly ("TOTALLY VIOLATED"), it often precedes rapid moderation as psychological pressure builds - creating buying opportunities for patient investors.

Supply Chain Planning: For businesses planning international operations, the TACO framework offers predictive value that traditional political analysis misses. Initial threats rarely materialize in full form, while "victories" often represent strategic retreats that favor targeted countries. The China deal exemplifies this: dramatic tariff threats ultimately yielding terms more favorable to China than the original baseline.

Market Efficiency Gains: Goldman Sachs upgraded China growth forecasts following the deal, while major banks shifted investment ratings. Traders who recognized the TACO pattern early positioned for this exact outcome, demonstrating the framework's practical investment value.

Currency and Commodity Impacts: The yuan strengthened and rare earth prices stabilized as markets priced in the predictable TACO resolution. Understanding the psychological cycle provides timing advantages that fundamental analysis alone cannot capture.

The Institutional Constraint Factor

Unlike domestic policy areas where institutional constraints can force TACO moderation, foreign policy TACOs reveal the limits of personal diplomacy when confronting strategic adversaries who understand American psychological vulnerabilities.

China's success in navigating the TACO pattern reflects sophisticated analysis of Trump's decision-making psychology. By maintaining pressure while offering face-saving concessions, Beijing exploited the gap between Trump's public persona and his underlying accommodation preferences when dealing with determined opposition.

Congressional oversight provided minimal constraint on this TACO, unlike the steel tariff and sanctions delay issues where legislative resistance forced moderation. This suggests that foreign policy TACOs may be more susceptible to psychological manipulation than domestic policy areas where institutional checks remain robust.

Comparative TACO Analysis: Patterns Across Policy Areas

The China TACO's completion allows comparison with previous episodes to identify consistent patterns:

Steel Tariffs (Episode 1): Partial TACO with targeted implementation rather than broad retreat. Market-driven constraints forced moderation while preserving face-saving elements.

Putin/Ukraine (Episode 2): Psychological TACO where personal vulnerabilities enabled continued accommodation despite strategic imperatives. "Let them fight for a while" represented complete abandonment of initial promises.

China Trade (Episode 3): Classic full-cycle TACO demonstrating economic constraints, diplomatic pressure, and psychological accommodation converging to produce terms worse than the original baseline while being celebrated as victory.

The progression suggests TACO patterns may be accelerating as adversaries recognize the predictable cycle and develop strategies to exploit it.

What to Watch Next: The Post-TACO Dynamics

With the China TACO essentially complete, several indicators will reveal whether the pattern holds or faces new pressures:

Implementation Challenges: Watch for disputes over rare earth export approvals, technology transfer restrictions, and student visa processing. Classic TACO psychology suggests these details may become sources of new friction as the victory narrative meets operational reality.

Congressional Response: Republican senators' reaction to the China deal will test whether institutional constraints can reassert themselves after TACO completion. Early criticism of the terms could force defensive justifications that reveal the deal's actual weakness.

Allied Coordination: European reactions to Trump's China accommodation will indicate whether alliance relationships can provide alternative constraints on TACO development when direct bilateral pressure fails.

Next TACO Development: The pattern suggests new bold promises may emerge to distract from this accommodation. Watch for escalatory rhetoric toward other targets as the psychological need for strength projection reasserts itself.

The Strategic Cost of TACO Governance

The China TACO reveals the broader implications of governance driven by TACO psychology rather than strategic planning. When policy becomes predictably cyclical - threats, escalation, accommodation, spin - it enables sophisticated adversaries to plan around American decision-making rather than respond to it.

China's patient strategy of enduring temporary pressure while maintaining strategic resistance ultimately yielded better terms than immediate accommodation might have achieved. This lesson will not be lost on other strategic competitors who observe the reliable TACO pattern in American policy-making.

The economic costs of TACO governance extend beyond immediate trade impacts. Business uncertainty, alliance strain, and market volatility all flow from the predictable cycle of bold promises followed by inevitable moderation. When adversaries can anticipate American behavior patterns, they gain strategic advantages that traditional military and economic superiority cannot overcome.

Conclusion: The TACO Pattern Perfected

The China TACO represents the complete validation of our analytical framework. From initial threats through frustrated blame-shifting to final accommodation celebrated as victory, every phase proceeded exactly as the pattern predicted. Trump's own words provide the documentary evidence: "TOTALLY VIOLATED" became "RELATIONSHIP IS EXCELLENT!" in precisely the manner TACO analysis anticipated.

This episode demonstrates that the TACO framework is not mere political commentary but a reliable analytical tool for understanding how personal psychology shapes policy outcomes when institutional constraints prove inadequate. Investors, policymakers, and international observers who recognize the pattern can better anticipate actual outcomes rather than react to theatrical rhetoric.

The strategic implications extend far beyond trade policy. When the world's most powerful nation operates according to predictable psychological patterns that adversaries can exploit, it fundamentally alters the global balance of power. China's successful navigation of the TACO cycle establishes a template that other strategic competitors will study and replicate.

As we continue tracking TACO development across multiple policy areas, the China case provides the baseline for measuring how reliably the pattern holds under different pressures. The psychological drivers appear consistent even when the specific constraints vary, suggesting that TACO governance may be more pervasive than initially recognized.

The international community now has clear evidence that American policy promises, no matter how dramatically presented, follow predictable moderation patterns when confronted with sustained resistance. The frightened psychology behind the bold rhetoric has been exposed, and strategic adversaries have proven they can exploit it systematically.

Next week, we'll examine whether new TACO developments emerge as the psychological need for strength projection reasserts itself, or whether the completion of the China cycle creates space for more conventional policy-making to resume.
