From Threat Matrix to Invasion: The Playbook Executed

January 3, 2026

January 3, 2026

As I documented this morning in Baseline Part 7, Trump’s judgment is demonstrably impaired. Today, he used that impaired judgment to bypass the Constitution and invade a sovereign nation.

At 4:24 AM EST on Saturday, January 3, 2026, President Donald Trump announced on Truth Social that the United States had “successfully carried out a large scale strike against Venezuela” and captured President Nicolás Maduro and his wife, who were “flown out of the Country.”

Every element of the pattern I documented in “The Threat Matrix” essay has now materialized:

✓ Oil as the actual objective
✓ Drug trafficking as the pretext
✓ “Terrorist” designation to justify military force
✓ Regime change admitted openly
✓ Congressional war powers ignored
✓ International law violated
✓ Press conference at Mar-a-Lago, not the White House

The playbook worked exactly as predicted. Except this time, we documented it in advance.

THE CONFESSION TIMELINE

December 10, 2025: I published “The Threat Matrix,” comparing the Iraq WMD narrative to Venezuela’s drug crisis narrative. The thesis: This isn’t about drugs. It’s about oil.

December 16, 2025: Trump posted on Truth Social: “Venezuela is completely surrounded by the largest Armada ever assembled in the History of South America... Until such time as they return to the United States of America all of the Oil, Land, and other Assets that they previously stole from us.”

December 17, 2025: I published Addendum Part I documenting Trump’s confession, Stephen Miller’s imperial logic claiming Venezuelan oil as “American wealth,” and Susie Wiles admitting regime change was the goal.

December 28-29, 2025: ABC News reported Trump had given the green light to capture Maduro “several days ago” and troops were ready to strike on Christmas Day, but were delayed due to military airstrikes in Nigeria against ISIS.

January 3, 2026, 2:00 AM local time: U.S. forces struck multiple sites in Caracas and surrounding states. Witnesses reported explosions at Fuerte Tiuna (Venezuela’s largest military complex), La Carlota Airport, and other military installations.

January 3, 2026, 4:24 AM EST: Trump announced Maduro’s capture via Truth Social.

January 3, 2026, 11:00 AM EST: Trump scheduled press conference at Mar-a-Lago.

The pattern didn’t just hold. It accelerated.

WHAT HAPPENED: THE OPERATION

Sources: Verified videos and photos (strike locations); Venezuela’s communications ministry (states where attacks occurred)

By Agnes Chang, Christiaan Triebert and Pablo Robles/The New York Times

According to NYT reporting and official statements:

The Strikes:

  • Large-scale attacks hit Caracas, Miranda, Aragua, and La Guaira states

  • Explosions reported at Venezuela’s largest military complex

  • Multiple civilian and military casualties (exact toll still being assessed)

  • Venezuelan Vice President Delcy Rodríguez: “We do not know the whereabouts of President Nicolás Maduro and first lady Cilia Flores”

  • Rodríguez demanded “immediate proof of life” from Trump administration

The Buildup:

  • 15,000 U.S. troops deployed to Caribbean (one of largest regional deployments in decades)

  • USS Gerald R. Ford aircraft carrier strike group positioned 100 nautical miles off Venezuelan coast

  • C-17 cargo planes made at least 16 flights to Puerto Rico from U.S. military bases in one week (December)

  • Special operations aircraft moved to Caribbean

  • Navy expeditionary strike group with amphibious warships carrying thousands of Marines

The Legal Framework (Such As It Was):

  • No Congressional authorization

  • No declaration of war

  • No Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF)

  • Trump’s justification: Executing arrest warrant for “narco-terrorism” charges from 2020 indictment

The Casualties:

  • Venezuelan officials report multiple deaths of “officials, soldiers and civilians”

  • U.S. official: “No American casualties” (would not comment on Venezuelan casualties)

  • At least 115 people killed in previous boat strikes since September 2025

The Constitutional Crisis: When NYT asked Trump if he sought Congressional authority before the invasion, Trump responded: “We’ll discuss that. We’re going to have a news conference.”

The call lasted 50 seconds.

THE JUSTIFICATIONS COMPARED

Let me lay out what Trump and his administration have claimed versus what the evidence shows:

OFFICIAL JUSTIFICATION: “Narco-Terrorism”

Trump’s Claims:

  • Maduro runs a “narco-terrorist state”

  • Venezuela is flooding America with drugs

  • Cartel de los Soles is a terrorist organization led by Maduro

The Reality:

  • U.S. intelligence agencies assess Maduro is “actually at odds” with Tren de Aragua gang

  • Analysts say “Cartel de Los Soles does not exist as a concrete organization”

  • Venezuela is not a significant source of fentanyl (the drug killing most Americans)

  • Cocaine from Venezuela flows mostly to Europe, not the U.S.

Attorney General Pam Bondi (today): “They will soon face the full wrath of American justice on American soil in American courts” on charges including “narco-terrorism conspiracy” and “cocaine importation conspiracy.”

The problem: If this were actually about drugs, Trump wouldn’t have pardoned Juan Orlando Hernández, who ran a massive drug operation as president of Honduras from 2014-2022.

ACTUAL OBJECTIVE: Oil

Trump (December 16): “Until such time as they return to the United States of America all of the Oil, Land, and other Assets that they previously stole from us.”

Stephen Miller (December): “American sweat, ingenuity and toil created the oil industry in Venezuela. Its tyrannical expropriation was the largest recorded theft of American wealth and property.”

Venezuelan Government Statement (today): “The objective of this attack is none other than to seize Venezuela’s strategic resources, particularly its oil and minerals, attempting to break the Nation’s political independence by force.”

Context: Venezuela has 303 billion barrels of proven oil reserves—the largest on Earth.

The only significant difference is Trump didn’t bother with Congressional approval.

THE CONSTITUTIONAL VIOLATION

What the Law Requires

Article I, Section 8 of the Constitution: Congress alone has the power to declare war.

War Powers Resolution of 1973:

  1. President must consult Congress BEFORE introducing armed forces into hostilities

  2. Must report to Congress within 48 hours

  3. Must cease hostilities within 60 days without Congressional authorization

What Trump Did

✗ Did NOT consult Congress before invasion
✗ Did NOT seek Congressional authorization
✗ Did NOT obtain declaration of war
✗ Did NOT obtain Authorization for Use of Military Force

Congressional Attempts to Stop Him

Three War Powers Resolutions were introduced to block Trump’s Venezuela campaign:

October 2025: Resolution to block boat strikes
Vote: 48-51 - FAILED

November 2025: Resolution to block land strikes in Venezuela
Vote: 49-51 - FAILED

December 2025: Bipartisan resolution (Castro, McGovern, Massie)
Status: Pending

Voting pattern:

  • Only 2 Republicans voted to restrain Trump: Rand Paul (KY) and Lisa Murkowski (AK)

  • Every other Republican voted to give Trump unlimited war-making authority

  • One Democrat (John Fetterman) initially voted with Republicans, then switched

Trump’s Legal Theory

According to Senator Mike Lee (R-Utah), who spoke with Secretary of State Marco Rubio:

“He informed me that Nicolás Maduro has been arrested by U.S. personnel to stand trial on criminal charges in the United States, and that the kinetic action we saw tonight was deployed to protect and defend those executing the arrest warrant.”

Lee’s assessment: “This action likely falls within the president’s inherent authority under Article II of the Constitution to protect U.S. personnel from an actual or imminent attack.”

The claim: Trump can:

  1. Unilaterally designate a foreign leader as head of “terrorist organization”

  2. Issue arrest warrant through DOJ

  3. Send military to invade sovereign nation and capture that leader

  4. Frame it as “law enforcement” not “war”

  5. Bypass Congressional war powers entirely

Expert Assessment

Senators Tim Kaine (D-VA) and Adam Schiff (D-CA) reviewed the classified legal memo justifying the strikes.

Schiff: “If that opinion were to be adopted, it would not constrain any use of force anywhere in the world. It’s broad enough to authorize just about anything.”

Kaine: The administration claims “the president can use force if he believes it serves U.S. interests, citing both the Constitution and international law. Those claims are unfounded because only Congress can authorize war.”

NYT Editorial Board (today): “The Constitution requires Congress to approve any act of war. Yes, presidents often push the boundaries of this law. But even Mr. Bush sought and received congressional endorsement for his Iraq invasion... Mr. Trump has not even a fig leaf of legal authority for his attacks on Venezuela. Without congressional approval, his actions violate U.S. law.”

International Law Expert Marc Weller (Chatham House): “International law prohibits the use of force as a means of national policy. Force is only available in response to an armed attack or possibly to rescue a population under imminent threat of extermination. Clearly, none of these requirements are fulfilled by the armed operation against Venezuela.”

Even George W. Bush got Congressional authorization for Iraq.

Trump invaded Venezuela, captured its president, killed Venezuelan civilians and military personnel, and is claiming he doesn’t need Congressional approval because it’s “law enforcement.”
