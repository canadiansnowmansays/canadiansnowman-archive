The Noem Files

As the Bodies Pile Up, DHS Refuses Accountability

Twenty-Four Hours After a Resignation Demand, the Violence Escalates and the Lies Continue

January 8, 2026

Police officers blocked off an area near where victims of a shooting involving federal agents were found in Portland, Ore., on Thursday.Credit...Jordan Gale for The New York Times

January 8, 2026

Yesterday, I called for Department of Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem to resign immediately after she lied about the fatal shooting of Renee Nicole Good in Minneapolis. I argued that we must refuse to normalize government lies about state violence, that this was the line that could not be crossed.

Today, we learned what happens when that line is crossed with impunity.

Police officers blocked off an area near where victims of a shooting involving federal agents were found in Portland, Ore., on Thursday.Credit...Jordan Gale for The New York Times

This morning in Portland, Oregon, ICE agents were involved in two more shootings. The details are still emerging, but the pattern is already clear: federal agents operating under Noem’s authority are using lethal force with increasing frequency, and their superiors are defending every instance as justified, regardless of the evidence.

Noem’s response to demands for her resignation? She traveled to New York City for a press conference where she doubled down on her lies about Renee Nicole Good’s killing, attacked critics who dared question the federal narrative, and made clear that the “immigration crackdown” will continue “unabated” no matter how many people die.

This is what normalization looks like. This is what happens when we tolerate lies about state violence. The violence escalates. The lies become more brazen. And accountability becomes impossible.

The Escalation: Not Two Shootings in 24 Hours—The Tenth and Eleventh Since September

Less than 24 hours after Renee Nicole Good was shot and killed in Minneapolis—less than 24 hours after video evidence and forensic analysis proved federal officials lied about the circumstances—ICE agents in Portland, Oregon were involved in two separate shooting incidents.

But here’s what makes this truly terrifying: these weren’t the second and third shootings involving ICE agents. They were the tenth and eleventh since September.

According to reporting, there have been nine ICE shootings since September 2025. And every single one shares the same characteristics: the individuals targeted were in vehicles, and in each case, officials claimed agents fired in self-defense because they feared being struck by the vehicle.

Read that again. Nine shootings. All involving vehicles. All justified with the exact same narrative: the driver tried to run over agents.

Either ICE agents are experiencing an unprecedented epidemic of vehicular attacks—a statistically impossible coincidence—or they have developed a systematic pattern of shooting at vehicles and then using a standardized justification that they know will be accepted without question.

The Minneapolis shooting only gained national attention because multiple witnesses filmed it from different angles, because the New York Times conducted forensic video analysis, because it happened on a residential street where community members immediately challenged the official narrative. But this has been happening for months, case after case, with the same story told every time.

How many of those nine previous shootings were justified? How many involved agents who were actually in danger? How many were, like Minneapolis, situations where video evidence would have contradicted the official narrative—if video evidence existed?

We don’t know. Because in most cases, there was no video. Or if there was video, it wasn’t released. Or if it was released, it wasn’t forensically analyzed. The official narrative was accepted, the shooting was ruled justified, and the pattern continued.

Until Minneapolis. Until Renee Nicole Good. Until video evidence from three angles proved that the standardized justification—”feared being struck by vehicle”—was a lie.

And now, less than 24 hours after that lie was exposed, there are two more shootings in Portland. The eleventh and twelfth since September. Both involving people in vehicles. Both justified—before any investigation, before any video analysis, before any independent review—with the same standardized narrative.

This is not escalation. This is revelation. We’re not watching violence spiral out of control. We’re watching a systematic pattern of violence that has been happening for months finally become visible.

Noem’s Press Conference: Defending the Indefensible

U.S. Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem, flanked by Director of Field Operations (DFO) for Customs and Border Protection’s CBP’s New York Field Office (NYFO), Frank Russo, and Special Agent in Charge at Homeland Security Investigations (HSI), Ricky Patel, attends a press conference to discuss ongoing U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) operations, as part of U.S. President Donald’s Trump’s immigration policy, at One World Trade Center in New York City, January 8, 2026. REUTERS/David ‘Dee’ Delgado

While families in Portland were learning that federal agents had been involved in two more shootings, while Minnesota officials were being shut out of the investigation into Renee Nicole Good’s killing, Kristi Noem stood at a podium in Lower Manhattan and defended the shooting.

Here’s what happened in Minnesota on Thursday: The state’s Bureau of Criminal Apprehension, which had initially been participating in the investigation, withdrew because federal officials denied them access to evidence. Read that again: the state investigating agency was shut out of the investigation into a killing that occurred in their state because federal officials refused to share evidence.

Drew Evans, superintendent of the Bureau of Criminal Apprehension, made the announcement publicly. Governor Walz was blunt about what this meant: “Minnesota must be part of this investigation. I say that only because people in positions of power have already passed judgment.” He noted that federal officials’ statements about the shooting had been “verifiably false.”

Noem’s response to Minnesota being shut out? She claimed at her New York press conference that state officials “don’t have any jurisdiction in this investigation.” This is technically true—federal shootings are investigated by federal agencies—but it reveals everything about how accountability has been systematically eliminated.

The federal government investigates itself. State and local officials who have evidence contradicting the federal narrative are denied access to the investigation. And when those officials speak out about being shut out, they’re told they have “no jurisdiction.”

This is how cover-ups work. Not by hiding evidence, but by controlling who gets to see it. Not by silencing critics, but by excluding them from any formal role in determining what happened.

Meanwhile, federal officials announced they’re deploying 100 more agents to Minnesota—Customs and Border Protection officers from Chicago and New Orleans. The Department of Homeland Security is pausing operations in Chicago to support the Minnesota operation. Governor Walz activated the National Guard “out of an abundance of caution.”

Think about what this means: Less than 48 hours after an ICE agent killed an American citizen under circumstances video evidence shows did not justify lethal force, the federal response is to send more agents, shut state investigators out of the investigation, and prepare for more confrontation.

At that same press conference, Noem defended Jonathan Ross and his use of lethal force.

“I would say that when these individuals use their vehicles to try to ram our law enforcement and put their lives in jeopardy, that law enforcement officer has to make a decision to protect his life and the individuals around him as well,” Noem said.

Note the language: “these individuals.” Not “this woman.” Not “Renee Nicole Good.” Not “an American citizen.” These individuals. Dehumanization as policy.

Noem claimed the officer “followed his training” and was “experienced.” She insisted he acted in self-defense despite video evidence—forensically analyzed by the New York Times from three camera angles—proving that the agent was not in the path of Good’s vehicle when he opened fire.

When pressed about the contradictions between her account and the video evidence, Noem did not acknowledge the discrepancy. She did not express regret. She did not promise a thorough investigation. She simply repeated her narrative and moved on to attacking her critics.

Meet Jonathan Ross: The “Experienced” Officer

We now know the identity of the agent who shot Renee Nicole Good: Jonathan Ross, an ICE officer with a history that makes Noem’s defense of him even more disturbing.

Ross was previously involved in another incident in Minnesota last year where he was dragged by a vehicle during an immigration enforcement operation. This history—far from being evidence of his victimization—raises critical questions about his judgment and use of force.

An officer who has been involved in multiple violent confrontations involving vehicles, who has now escalated to lethal force under circumstances that video evidence shows did not justify it, is precisely the kind of officer who needs additional scrutiny, not blanket defense from his superiors.

But Noem isn’t interested in scrutiny. She characterized Ross as “experienced” and praised him for “following his training.” If this is what ICE training produces—officers who shoot at mothers trying to drive away, officers who then leave the scene of an active crime scene, officers whose superiors immediately lie to protect them—then the training itself is the problem.

And if Ross’s experience includes being dragged by a vehicle in a previous incident, one might ask: does that history make him more likely to see vehicular threats where none exist? More likely to use lethal force preemptively? More likely to shoot first and ask questions never?

These are the questions a responsible Secretary of Homeland Security would be asking. Instead, Noem is defending him unconditionally.

The Standardized Justification: A Cover Story for Systematic Violence

Here’s the pattern that should alarm everyone: Nine ICE shootings since September. All involving people in vehicles. All justified with agents “fearing being struck by vehicle.”

This is not coincidence. This is policy.

When the same justification appears in case after case after case, it stops being a description of what happened and starts being a template for what to say after shooting someone. It’s a standardized cover story that agents know will be accepted, that superiors know how to defend, that insulates them from accountability.

Think about what this means. ICE agents have learned that if they shoot someone in or near a vehicle, all they have to say is “I feared being struck” and the shooting will be ruled justified. No video evidence required. No independent witnesses necessary. No scrutiny of whether the vehicle was actually moving toward them, whether they could have moved out of the way, whether lethal force was the only option.

Just say the magic words—”feared being struck by vehicle”—and you’re protected.

This is exactly what happened in Minneapolis. Jonathan Ross shot Renee Nicole Good and immediately the narrative was deployed: she tried to run over agents, she weaponized her vehicle, she was engaged in domestic terrorism. The standardized justification, ready to go, deployed within hours.

The only problem was the video evidence. Multiple angles. Forensic analysis by the New York Times. Proof that Ross was not in the path of the vehicle when he fired, that Good was turning away from him, that the entire narrative was false.

Without that video evidence, this would have been shooting number nine. Justified. Closed. Pattern continues.

How many of those previous nine shootings were like Minneapolis? How many involved agents who were never actually in danger but knew they could shoot and then claim they feared being struck? How many people have been shot—maybe killed—under circumstances that video evidence would have contradicted, if video evidence existed?

We can’t know. Because in most of those cases, there was no video. Or the video wasn’t released. Or it wasn’t analyzed. The standardized justification was accepted and the case was closed.

But now we know the justification is a lie. We have proof. The New York Times forensically analyzed video from three angles and definitively showed that the circumstances federal officials described did not occur.

Which means every other shooting justified with the same narrative is now suspect. Every claim that an agent “feared being struck by vehicle” must now be independently verified. Every case must be reopened and reexamined with the understanding that federal officials have been systematically lying about these shootings.

Because if they lied about Minneapolis—and they did—why should we believe them about any of the others?

Attacking the Critics: The Mamdani Smear

Noem’s New York press conference was ostensibly about announcing the arrest of 54 people allegedly affiliated with a Dominican street gang. But she couldn’t resist using the platform to attack New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani, who had the temerity to call Renee Nicole Good’s killing what it was: murder.

Mamdani, a Democrat and democratic socialist who took office last week, said after the Minneapolis shooting that New York would “stand with immigrants today, and every day that follows.” This statement—expressing solidarity with immigrant communities being terrorized by federal agents—was apparently intolerable to Noem.

Her response was telling: Mamdani, she said, “chose to stand with illegals instead of those individuals who just want the chance to raise their families in New York City.”

The framing is deliberate and insidious. In Noem’s worldview, you either stand with federal agents—including those who kill American citizens under false pretenses—or you stand with “illegals.” There is no middle ground. There is no space for accountability. There is no possibility that federal agents might be wrong, might use excessive force, might lie to cover their mistakes.

You’re either with us or against us. And if you’re against us—if you dare to question the narrative, if you dare to call a killing a killing—then you’re siding with “illegals” against families who want to live in peace.

This is how authoritarian regimes operate. Criticism becomes disloyalty. Questions become threats. And anyone who demands accountability becomes an enemy of the state.

But Noem wasn’t alone in attacking critics. The entire Trump administration circled wagons on Thursday:

Vice President JD Vance (L) and Renee Nicole Good (R).Credit : Anna Moneymaker/Getty; Knot & Anchor Photography

Vice President JD Vance held a White House press briefing where he called news reporters “agents of propaganda of a radical fringe” for accurately reporting that video evidence showed Good never put the ICE agent in danger before she was shot. Think about that: journalists reporting what forensic video analysis proved are now “agents of propaganda” for contradicting the official lie.

Attorney General Pam Bondi took to social media to warn protesters in Minnesota not to cross a “red line” into “obstructing, impeding or attacking federal law enforcement.” This is the Attorney General of the United States threatening protesters—American citizens exercising their First Amendment rights—in a city where federal agents just killed an American citizen and lied about it.

The message is clear: shut up, accept the official narrative, or face consequences. Don’t protest. Don’t question. Don’t demand accountability. And definitely don’t document what’s actually happening with video cameras, because video evidence is now “propaganda” if it contradicts what the government tells you to believe.

The Pattern: Sanctuary Cities as Targets

Noem’s attack on Mamdani fits a broader pattern that should alarm anyone paying attention. The Trump administration is systematically targeting cities with sanctuary policies—cities that refuse to allow local police to function as immigration agents—with massive federal enforcement operations.

Minneapolis: 2,000 agents deployed, systematic raids, one American citizen dead.

Portland: Federal agents involved in two shootings in a single day.

New York: 54 arrests announced, with Noem using the platform to attack the mayor and warn that enforcement will continue “unabated.”

This is not law enforcement. This is political punishment. Cities that refuse to cooperate with federal immigration enforcement are being subjected to overwhelming shows of force designed to create chaos, terror, and compliance.

And when people die—when American citizens exercising their constitutional rights are shot by federal agents—the response is to lie about the circumstances and attack anyone who questions the official narrative.

This IS the Normalization: How Impunity Breeds Violence

Yesterday, I warned that we must refuse to normalize lies about state violence. I was wrong about one thing: the normalization isn’t something that will happen if we accept these lies. The normalization is happening right now, in real time.

There is a direct line between Noem’s lies about Minneapolis and Thursday’s shootings in Portland. Federal agents across the country watched what happened after Jonathan Ross shot Renee Nicole Good. They watched their superiors immediately defend the shooting. They watched the Secretary and the President lie about the video evidence. They watched critics get attacked and dismissed.

And they learned: you can shoot an American citizen under circumstances that don’t justify lethal force, your leaders will lie to protect you, video evidence doesn’t matter, and there will be no consequences.

Is it any wonder that 24 hours later, there are two more shooting incidents?

When officers see that their colleagues can use lethal force without justification and face no accountability—indeed, receive public defense from the highest levels of government—it changes their calculation about when force is appropriate. If Jonathan Ross can shoot a woman trying to drive away and get defended by the Secretary and the President, why should any other ICE agent hesitate to use lethal force?

The lesson from Minneapolis is clear: shoot first, lie later, let your superiors cover for you. The violence escalates. The lies become more brazen. And the normalization accelerates.

Why Noem Won’t Resign

Yesterday, I demanded Noem’s resignation. Today, it’s clear she has no intention of resigning. She’s not apologizing. She’s not acknowledging any problems. She’s not promising reforms. She’s doubling down, attacking critics, and promising more of the same.

This makes sense when you understand what’s actually happening. Noem isn’t failing at her job. She’s succeeding at her actual job, which is not to enforce immigration law but to demonstrate federal power, punish sanctuary cities, and terrorize immigrant communities and their allies into submission.

The violence is not a bug. It’s a feature.

The lies are not mistakes. They’re strategy.

The attacks on critics are not defensive reactions. They’re warnings.

Noem won’t resign because from her perspective—and from Trump’s perspective—she’s doing exactly what she was hired to do. She’s deploying overwhelming force to cities that dare to resist federal immigration enforcement. She’s creating chaos and fear. She’s defending her agents no matter what they do. And she’s attacking anyone who questions the narrative.

The fact that American citizens are dying, that video evidence proves officials are lying, that local law enforcement officials are calling the federal accounts “bullshit” and “propaganda”—none of that matters. Because accountability was never the point. Compliance is the point. Fear is the point. Demonstrating that there are no limits to what the federal government will do to sanctuary cities—that’s the point.

From Resignation Demand to Removal Demand

Since Noem clearly won’t resign voluntarily, and since Trump obviously won’t fire her, what recourse do we have?

Impeachment. It’s not just appropriate—it’s necessary.

A Cabinet official who lies about fatal shootings by her agents, who doubles down on those lies even after forensic video analysis proves them false, who attacks critics and promises to continue “unabated” operations that are resulting in escalating violence—that official cannot be allowed to remain in office simply because the President won’t fire her.

The House of Representatives has a constitutional duty to impeach officials who have committed high crimes and misdemeanors. Lying to the American people about why a federal agent killed an American citizen—and continuing those lies even after video evidence proves them false—meets that standard.

But impeachment takes time, and people are dying now. So in the interim:

Congress must immediately:

  • Subpoena all video evidence from the Minneapolis and Portland shootings

  • Subpoena Jonathan Ross and require his testimony under oath

  • Subpoena all ICE agents involved in the Portland incidents

  • Demand a full accounting of all uses of force during these federal operations

  • Cut funding for these operations until accountability mechanisms are in place

Local officials must:

  • Continue documenting federal agents’ actions

  • Continue providing video evidence to media outlets

  • Continue speaking out against the federal narrative regardless of attacks from Noem

  • Coordinate legal challenges across sanctuary cities

  • Prepare for the violence to escalate further

Media must:

  • Continue forensic analysis of all available video

  • Demand answers about the Portland shootings immediately

  • Document every lie told by Noem and Trump

  • Refuse to “both sides” situations where video evidence clearly contradicts official narratives

  • Make Jonathan Ross’s name and history public information

Americans must:

  • Recognize this as a test of whether accountability still exists

  • Contact representatives and demand action

  • Share video evidence widely

  • Refuse to accept official narratives that contradict documented evidence

  • Prepare for more violence, because it’s coming

The Portland Question

As I write this, details about the Portland shootings are still emerging. But here’s what we know:

U.S. Border Patrol agents were conducting a “targeted vehicle stop” Thursday afternoon in the Hazelwood neighborhood, about eight miles from Portland’s city center. According to federal officials, agents opened fire after the driver “tried to run over agents”—the standardized justification deployed yet again.

The driver drove off after agents fired—and drove more than two miles before being found by police. Think about that: if the agent was truly in imminent danger of being run over, how did the vehicle drive away for over two miles? Two people—a man and a woman—were eventually found with gunshot wounds. The injured man had to call 911 himself because federal agents had left the scene entirely. They weren't there when local police arrived.

Federal officials immediately identified the driver as “an undocumented immigrant and member of Tren de Aragua,” a Venezuelan gang that Trump has frequently targeted. They provided no evidence of gang membership. Just the claim, ready to go, designed to make the shooting seem justified regardless of the actual circumstances.

Portland Police Chief Bob Day made clear at an evening press conference that local officials knew very little about the incident: “We do not know if this is an immigration-related event. We do not know which federal agencies were involved.” The FBI is leading the investigation—which means, once again, federal agencies investigating themselves with no state or local oversight.

The information blackout is complete. Local officials know nothing because federal agents vanished after shooting two people. Witnesses described seeing bloody footprints on the sidewalk, officials providing medical aid. Oregon State Senate Majority Leader Kayse Jama, who lives near where the shooting occurred, was blunt: 'We do not need you. You are not welcome. You need to get the hell out of our community.

Oregon Governor Tina Kotek demanded transparency: “Trust is essential to maintain community safety and uphold the law. Federal agents at the direction of the Department of Homeland Security are shattering trust. They are destroying day by day what we hold dear.”

Portland Mayor Keith Wilson called for an end to federal enforcement operations in his city: “The administration is trying to divide us, to pit communities against one another, to make us fear one another. Portland, this is a moment to hold each other close.”

But here’s the critical question: Will there be video evidence from Portland? Will there be multiple camera angles? Will the New York Times conduct forensic analysis? Or will these shootings become numbers ten and eleven in a pattern of violence that gets justified with standardized narratives and never independently verified?

We can predict with confidence what will happen next:

Federal officials will claim their agents acted in self-defense. They will characterize those shot as threats, criminals, gang members, maybe even terrorists. They will release carefully selected details that support their narrative while withholding evidence that contradicts it. Noem will defend the agents. Trump will defend the agents. Critics will be attacked as soft on crime or pro-illegal immigration.

And unless there’s video evidence—clear, multi-angle, forensically analyzable video evidence—the shootings will be ruled justified. The standardized narrative will be accepted. The pattern will continue.

Because that’s what happened nine times before Minneapolis. The standardized justification was deployed. It was accepted. The cases were closed. And only now, with forensic video evidence proving the narrative was a lie, do we understand that this has been happening all along.

Conclusion: The Pattern Exposed

Twenty-four hours. That’s how long it took from Renee Nicole Good’s killing for two more shooting incidents in Portland.

But the real story isn’t about 24 hours. It’s about three and a half months. It’s about nine previous shootings that used the exact same justification. It’s about a systematic pattern of violence that has been operating in plain sight, hidden only because we accepted the standardized narrative without question.

The Minneapolis shooting matters not because it was unusual, but because it was usual—and this time, there was video evidence to prove the official narrative was a lie.

Think about what this means. For months, ICE agents have been shooting people in or near vehicles and justifying those shootings by claiming they feared being struck. In case after case, that justification was accepted. The shootings were ruled justified. The pattern continued.

Then came Minneapolis. Multiple camera angles. Forensic video analysis by the New York Times. Proof that the standardized justification—”feared being struck by vehicle”—was false. The agent was not in the path of the vehicle. The woman was turning away from him. The entire narrative was a lie.

And what happened after that lie was exposed? Two more shootings in Portland, less than 24 hours later. Both involving people in vehicles. Both immediately justified with the same standardized narrative that was just proven to be a lie in Minneapolis.

This reveals something crucial: the Minneapolis shooting didn’t cause the Portland shootings. The pattern was already established. The systematic violence was already happening. The standardized justification was already in place. Minneapolis simply exposed it.

Renee Nicole Good died because Jonathan Ross shot her without justification and his superiors lied to protect him. But the reason the lies were so ready, so polished, so quickly deployed, is because they’ve been using those same lies for months. Nine times before Minneapolis. And now twice more in Portland.

Every shooting from the past three and a half months must now be reexamined. Every claim that an agent “feared being struck by vehicle” must be independently verified. Every case where that standardized justification was used must be reopened with the understanding that federal officials have been systematically lying.

Because we now have proof they lied about Minneapolis. Which means every other time they used that same justification is suspect.

This is bigger than Kristi Noem. This is bigger than Jonathan Ross. This is bigger even than Donald Trump’s immigration crackdown.

This is about a systematic pattern of state violence being concealed behind a standardized justification that officials know will be accepted without scrutiny. This is about federal agents learning that they can shoot people in vehicles, say they feared being struck, and face no accountability. This is about a government that has normalized killing as a routine tool of immigration enforcement.

Twelve shootings in three and a half months. All involving vehicles. All justified the same way. One proven to be a lie.

How many of the other eleven are also lies?

We don’t know. But we know this: without accountability for Minneapolis, without consequences for the lies, without a systematic review of every shooting that used the same justification, the pattern will continue.

The bodies are already piling up. The lies are already standardized. And Secretary Noem promises the operations will continue “unabated.”

The question is not whether we can stop this. The question is whether we will demand accountability for a systematic pattern of violence that has been operating in plain sight for months.

Renee Nicole Good’s death exposed the pattern. How many more people will die before we dismantle it?
