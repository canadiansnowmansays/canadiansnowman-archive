The Hearing Where the Attorney General Wouldn’t Turn Around

Part 21: When the Survivors Asked Her to Just Look at Them

February 11, 2026

February 11, 2026

Attorney General Pam Bondi appeared before the House Judiciary Committee on Wednesday for her one-year oversight hearing. Behind her in the hearing room sat survivors of Jeffrey Epstein’s abuse, some wearing white T-shirts with blacked-out words representing the redactions in the Epstein files. The shirts read: “The truth is — Epstein survivors are still waiting.”

Before the day was over, those survivors would watch the nation’s chief law enforcement officer refuse to even turn around and look at them.

The Setup

The hearing came two weeks after the Justice Department’s January 30 release of 3.5 million pages of Epstein-related documents—a release that exposed victims’ names, faces in nude photographs, and banking information while heavily redacting the names of co-conspirators in the 2007 draft federal indictment.

At least eight survivors had submitted statements to the court about being re-traumatized by what they called the DOJ’s catastrophic failures. One woman received death threats after 51 entries included her private banking information. Another called the release “life threatening.”

Now those survivors sat in the hearing room, ready to hear what the Attorney General would say about what her department had done to them.

The Opening: Generic Apology

In her opening statement, Bondi offered a blanket expression of sympathy: “I am deeply sorry for what any victim, any victim, has been through, especially as a result of that monster.”

Note the phrasing: “that monster.” Not “my department’s failures.” Not “our mistakes that exposed your identities.” Just sympathy for what Epstein did to them—the crime everyone already condemned.

Nothing about what her Justice Department had just put them through two weeks ago.

Representative Raskin: “You’re Running a Massive Epstein Cover-Up”

Before any questions were asked, before any exchanges devolved into shouting matches, Ranking Member Jamie Raskin used his opening statement to systematically lay out the case against Attorney General Bondi.

It took ten minutes.

Raskin began by introducing the survivors sitting behind Bondi: Teresa Helm, Jess Michaels, Laura Blume McGee, Danny Benski, Liz Stein, Marina Lera, Sky and Amanda Roberts (family of the late Virginia Giuffre), Charlene Rashard, and Lisa Phillips.

“You still haven’t met with these survivors,” Raskin said. “You’re not showing a lot of interest in the victims, Madam Attorney General, whether it’s Epstein’s human trafficking ring or the homicidal governmental violence against citizens in Minneapolis. As attorney general, you’re siding with the perpetrators and you’re ignoring the victims. That will be your legacy unless you act quickly to change course.”

Then he began documenting the failures.

On the document release:

“You’re running a massive Epstein cover-up right out of the Department of Justice. You’ve been ordered by subpoena and by Congress to turn over 6 million documents, photographs, and videos in the Epstein files, but you’ve turned over only three million. You say you’re not turning over the other three million because they’re somehow duplicative, but we know that there are actual memos of victim statements in there.”

“In the half you did produce, you redacted the names of abusers, enablers, accomplices, and co-conspirators, apparently to spare them embarrassment and disgrace, which is the exact opposite of what the law ordered you to do.”

“Even worse, you shockingly failed to redact many of the victims’ names, which is what you were ordered to do by Congress. Some of the victims had come forward publicly, but many had not. Many had kept their torment private, even from family and friends. But you published their names, their identities, their images on thousands of pages for the world to see.”

“So you ignored the law. And even with over 100,000 employees at your disposal, you acted with some mixture of staggering incompetence, cold indifference, and jaded cruelty towards more than 1,000 victims raped, abused, and trafficked.”

“This performance screams cover-up.”

On Ghislaine Maxwell:

“Convicted sex trafficker and groomer Ghislaine Maxwell opened the gates of hell to Virginia Giuffre and hundreds of other victims. But when Maxwell was subpoenaed to come testify before Congress, you and Todd Blanche quickly moved her from a higher security prison to a minimum security camp in Texas, where she’s enjoyed five-star treatment, including catered meals, private gym time, and access to a therapy puppy.”

“All because Todd Blanche, who’s utterly failed to investigate the monstrous crimes of Epstein and Maxwell’s co-conspirators, spent nine hours with Maxwell and satisfied himself that she would have nothing untoward to say about Donald Trump, which is your only real interest in the matter based on institutional performance.”

On Minneapolis and the ICE killings:

“Abandoning victims and coddling perpetrators is what you do best. When the FBI opened a criminal investigation into the brutal killing in Minneapolis of Renee Good, a poet and 37-year-old mother of three, by Trump’s masked paramilitary ICE agents, you shut it down.”

“You claim you’re investigating the cold-blooded murder of Alex Pretti, an ICU nurse at the VA. But how can we trust the administration when the president and Kristi Noem call Pretti a domestic terrorist and Stephen Miller called him a would-be assassin? Not only do you refuse to share evidence with the state and local investigators and prosecutors in Minnesota, you have blocked their access to the crime scene and the evidence.”

“You even launched a criminal investigation into Renee Good’s grieving widow. How sick is that?”

On weaponized prosecutions:

“But it’s even worse. You’ve turned the People’s Department of Justice into Trump’s instrument of revenge. Trump orders up prosecutions like pizza and you deliver every time. He tells you to go after James Comey, Letitia James, Lisa Cook, and Jerome Powell, the head of the Federal Reserve Board, and members of Congress like Adam Schiff, Mark Kelly, Alyssa Slotkin, Chrissy Houlahan, Jason Crow, Chris D’Esposito, and Maggie Goodlander to name a few. And you snap to it.”

“You replace real prosecutors with counterfeit stooges who robotically do the president’s bidding. Nothing in American history comes close to this complete corruption of the justice function and contamination of federal law enforcement.”

On DOJ lawyers who refused:

“Danielle Sassoon, your original pick for acting U.S. attorney in Manhattan, resigned rather than follow your corrupt order to quash an indictment against Mayor Eric Adams as a political favor from Donald Trump. A Federalist Society member who clerked for Justice Scalia, U.S. Attorney Sassoon refused to participate in this blatantly corrupt scheme.”

“Her top assistant, Hagan Scotten, an Iraq war vet and two-time Bronze Star recipient who clerked for Chief Justice Roberts and then-Judge Kavanaugh, promptly resigned too, writing to your office: ‘I expect you will eventually find someone who is enough of a fool or enough of a coward to file your motion, but it was never going to be me.’”

On grand juries rejecting DOJ:

“Grand juries of American citizens have repeatedly rejected your vendettas and baseless indictments brought by the hacks left at DOJ. Two different grand juries in Virginia voted down indictments against Letitia James in a single week. And just yesterday, another grand jury shut down your vendetta factory by rejecting indictments against the six members of Congress who had spoken out to remind all service members that they have a duty to refuse illegal orders.”

“You tried to get a grand jury to indict six members of Congress who were veterans of our armed forces on charges of seditious conspiracy simply for exercising their First Amendment rights.”

On DOJ lawyers lying in court:

“Your new lawyers keep lying in court. In dozens of cases they’ve been excoriated for lying to federal judges. Chief Judge Boasberg right here in D.C. suggested your Department of Justice perpetrated a fraud on the court. Other judges found your statements to be ‘inexplicably misleading,’ ‘patently incredible,’ ‘totally inconsistent,’ and ‘so disingenuous that the court is left with little confidence that the government can be trusted to tell the truth about anything.’”

The warning:

Raskin ended with a warning about Bondi’s “burn book”—a binder of personal attacks she’d brought to her Senate hearing to use against lawmakers.

“Please set the burn book aside and answer our questions. And when you hear us reclaim our time, that means it’s time for you to stop speaking. We only have five minutes. So when we reclaim our time, that means you stop.”

“The quality of justice in America depends on the character of our government. Please do your job and bring the Department of Justice back from the brink. The survivors seated behind you and the American people watching everywhere deserve a Department of Justice worthy of its name.”

Ten minutes. A systematic indictment of failures, lies, cover-ups, and weaponized prosecutions. Documentation of career prosecutors resigning rather than follow her orders. Grand juries refusing to indict. Judges accusing DOJ of fraud on the court. Survivors sitting three feet behind her that she’d never met with. Victims exposed while co-conspirators protected.

And when Raskin finished and sat down, cameras captured Attorney General Pam Bondi smiling.

AG Bondi's response after Ranking Member Raskin's ten-minute opening statement detailing the Epstein cover-up, weaponized prosecutions, and victims she'd never met with. February 11, 2026. (C-SPAN)

Not a grimace. Not the tight-lipped expression of someone enduring criticism. A genuine smile—the kind you might wear at a pleasant social event.

No one else found the subject matter amusing.

That smile told you everything you needed to know about how the rest of the hearing would go.

From “This Review is Over” to “I Won’t Look at You”

Part 20 of this series documented how Deputy Attorney General Todd Blanche declared “this review is over” on January 30, 2026, after releasing 3.5 million pages while withholding 2.5 million more. It showed how the DOJ exposed victims’ faces in nude photos while redacting co-conspirators’ names from the 2007 draft indictment. It revealed the pattern of strategic incompetence so consistent it could only be deliberate.

What Part 20 couldn’t show was what would happen when the person responsible for all of that sat in a room with the victims she’d harmed and was asked to simply turn around and acknowledge them.

On February 11, 2026, we found out.

Representative Jayapal: “Will You Turn Around?”

Representative Pramila Jayapal, a Washington Democrat, appearing on a screen behind Ms. Bondi during the hearing on Wednesday.Credit...Eric Lee for The New York Times

Democratic Representative Pramila Jayapal of Washington began by asking the survivors in the room to stand. They did.

“The survivors in the room, if you are willing, please stand, and if you are willing, please raise your hands if you have still not been able to meet with this Department of Justice,” Jayapal said.

Every single survivor raised their hand.

“Please know for the record that every single survivor has raised their hand,” Jayapal said for the record.

Then she turned back to Bondi:

“Attorney General Bondi, you apologized to the survivors in your opening statement for what they went through at the hands of Jeffrey Epstein. Will you turn to them now and apologize for what your Department of Justice has put them through—with the absolutely unacceptable release of the Epstein files and their information?”

Bondi refused.

Instead, she immediately pivoted to attacking the Biden administration: “Congresswoman, you sat before—Merrick Garland sat in this chair twice—”

Jayapal tried to reclaim her time. Chairman Jim Jordan intervened to say “members get to ask the questions, and the witness gets to answer in the way they want to answer.”

Jayapal pushed back: “That’s not accurate, Mr. Chairman.”

As the exchange grew heated, Bondi said: “I won’t get into the gutter for her theatrics.”

More shouting followed. Jayapal tried again: “I wish that you would turn around to the survivors who are standing right behind you and on a human level apologize to them for what you have done.”

Bondi accused Jayapal of orchestrating a “circus” and dismissed the request as political theater.

She never turned around. She never looked at the victims. She never apologized for what her department did to them.

Cameras captured her smiling.

Representative Moskowitz: The Questions

Democratic Representative Jared Moskowitz of Florida addressed the victims directly, asking them a series of yes-or-no questions and having them respond by raising their hands.

Had they met with the Justice Department and provided testimony and evidence?

No hands went up.

Had they reached out “individually, or through a lawyer or representative to offer to provide testimony and evidence”?

Every hand went up.

Had they consequently been “denied or ignored” by the Justice Department?

Every hand went up.

Were they still willing to speak to the department?

Every hand went up.

The message was clear: The Justice Department hadn’t just failed to protect these women in the document release. It had actively refused to even meet with them or accept their testimony.

Representative Nadler: “You Just Lied Under Oath”

Representative Jerrold Nadler of New York pressed Bondi on the lack of indictments against any Epstein co-conspirators despite the 3.5 million pages of documents released.

When Bondi tried to deflect, Nadler interrupted. Bondi lashed out: “I’m gonna answer the question the way I want to answer the question. Your theatrics are ridiculous.”

The interaction devolved into shouts, with Ranking Member Jamie Raskin eventually calling for Nadler to receive additional time, accusing Bondi of trying to “filibuster” through the hearing.

Representative Lieu: “If You Had Any Decency, You Would Resign”

Democratic Representative Ted Lieu of California took the resignation call even further. When Bondi claimed there was “no evidence that Donald Trump has committed a crime,” Lieu pointed to evidence in the Epstein files showing Trump, like Prince Andrew, attended parties with Epstein.

Bondi called the question “ridiculous” and said Democrats were trying to “deflect from all the great things Donald Trump has done.”

Lieu reclaimed his time and accused her of lying under oath.

“You said there’s no evidence of a crime,” Lieu said. Then he delivered what may be the defining statement of the entire hearing:

“Epstein should rot in hell, so should the men who patronize his operation. And as we speak here today, there are over 1,000 sex trafficking victims, and you have not held a single man accountable. Shame on you. If you had any decency, you would resign right after this hearing.”

Representative Jesus Garcia of Illinois also brought up the prospect of impeachment and told Bondi she should resign.

Representative Becca Balint of Vermont tried to ask whether the Justice Department had questioned different Trump administration officials about their ties to Epstein.

“This is pathetic. I am not asking trick questions,” Balint said after Bondi refused to answer. “The American people deserve to know.”

Representative Massie: The Republican Who Broke Ranks

Notably, Republican Representative Thomas Massie of Kentucky—one of the architects of the Epstein Files Transparency Act—was one of the few GOP members to press Bondi on the Epstein files.

Speaking to reporters after the hearing, Massie said: “My question to Pam Bondi, that she wouldn’t answer, is: Do you have any structure in your organization? Do you know who the individual was who put the black ink over the one most incriminating document there is on Les Wexner? That’s the name I want. That’s the person I want to come in front of oversight.”

Asked about having survivors in the room, Massie replied: “I think it’s helpful. I mean, they’re the reason this bill got passed. People are saying, ‘When will you be satisfied?’ I’ll be satisfied when those victims are satisfied, the survivors who are here today. And until then, I’m going to, you know, work my butt off trying to get the DOJ to comply with this law.”

Republican Representative Nancy Mace told CNN: “I personally have lost all faith in our Justice Department. It’s a system of injustice. There is evidence, there are co-conspirators, you can’t have thousands of victims and have no other accomplices other than one.”

Bondi’s Defense Strategy: Deflection and Attack

Throughout the hearing, Bondi employed a consistent strategy when faced with questions about Epstein:

Deflect to Merrick Garland: “Why didn’t she ask Merrick Garland this twice when he sat in my chair?”

Deflect to Obama: “This has been around since the Obama administration. None of them asked Merrick Garland over the last four years one word about Jeffrey Epstein.”

Deflect to the stock market: When given time by a Republican member, Bondi launched into praise of Trump: “He is the most transparent president in the nation’s history.” She then pivoted to talking about the Dow and the economy. At one point she ominously told two Democrats: “You’re a great stock trader.”

Deflect to border security: “When he was elected overwhelmingly by the American people, the majority of the American people wanted Donald Trump, one of the main reasons was border security and keeping Americans safe. That’s why today, the other side sits here, they yell, cut me off. They want to yell. They want to ask a question, and don’t want answers, because they want to distract from all the great things that this president and this administration working hand in hand.”

Attack Democrats personally: “Theatrics,” “ridiculous,” “circus,” “washed-up loser lawyer,” “getting in the gutter.”

What she never did: Turn around and look at the victims sitting behind her. Answer specific questions about who redacted co-conspirators’ names while exposing victims. Explain why her department hasn’t met with survivors who offered testimony. Take responsibility for the failures.

The Moment Captured

The photograph from the hearing tells the entire story: Epstein survivors stand in the rows behind the witness table, hands raised high, wearing their white T-shirts with blacked-out words representing the redactions. In the foreground, blurred but unmistakable, sits Attorney General Pam Bondi at the witness table, facing forward, her back to the women standing behind her.

They are visible. She won’t look at them.

Every hand is raised—answering Representative Jayapal’s question about whether they’ve been able to meet with the Department of Justice. Every single one.

And three feet in front of them sits the Attorney General of the United States, the person responsible for that failure, refusing the simple human act of turning around to acknowledge their existence.

Not a grimace of discomfort at a difficult situation. Not an apologetic glance. Just... nothing. Facing forward. Refusing to turn.

It’s the physical manifestation of what this entire hearing demonstrated: The Attorney General of the United States has more contempt for Epstein’s victims than she has accountability for what her department did to them.

What This Hearing Revealed

The February 11 hearing wasn’t just about Bondi’s refusal to apologize. It revealed the complete breakdown of any pretense that the Justice Department is treating Epstein survivors as victims deserving protection rather than obstacles to be managed.

Every survivor raised their hand: They haven’t met with DOJ.

Every survivor raised their hand: They’ve offered to provide testimony and evidence.

Every survivor raised their hand: They’ve been denied or ignored.

Every survivor raised their hand: They’re still willing to speak to the department.

And when given the opportunity to even acknowledge them, to turn around and look at them, to apologize for exposing their identities while protecting powerful co-conspirators—the Attorney General refused.

She called it “theatrics.” She called it a “circus.” She smiled.

Representative Ted Lieu summarized what that refusal meant: “There are over 1,000 sex trafficking victims, and you have not held a single man accountable. Shame on you. If you had any decency, you would resign right after this hearing.”

She didn’t resign. She didn’t apologize. She didn’t turn around.

Representative Lucy McBath, a Georgia Democrat whose son was murdered, spoke to this directly. She praised Bondi for how prosecutors under her leadership as Florida Attorney General had protected her son’s name from his murderer’s attacks.

“The compassion and respect that my family received throughout that ordeal were critical in helping us keep faith in the rule of law and justice,” McBath said.

Then she asked Bondi to show that same compassion to Epstein survivors.

Bondi didn’t respond to that request either.

She doesn’t just refuse to take responsibility for her department’s failures. She refuses to even acknowledge their existence as human beings deserving of eye contact.

The smile isn’t a nervous reaction. It’s contempt made visible. It’s the physical manifestation of a system that has spent a year protecting powerful men while treating victims as collateral damage in a political strategy.

When you accommodate abuse of power long enough, when you make excuses for “technical errors” that only go one direction, when you declare investigations “over” while withholding evidence, when you call accountability “theatrics”—eventually you reach a point where you can sit three feet from the people you’ve harmed and smile when asked to acknowledge them.

That’s where we are now.

The Broader Context

This hearing took place exactly two weeks after the Justice Department’s January 30 release of 3.5 million pages. It came one week after the Clintons finally agreed to depositions following a contempt vote. It came two days before Ghislaine Maxwell’s scheduled February 13 deposition before the House Oversight Committee.

The timing matters. Bondi knew she would face questions about the redaction failures. Her staff had two weeks to prepare. The victims’ statements to the court were public. The criticism from both Democratic and Republican members of Congress was on the record.

She came prepared with a strategy: Don’t apologize. Don’t take responsibility. Attack Democrats. Praise Trump. Deflect to previous administrations. Call it all political theater.

And whatever you do, don’t turn around and look at the victims.

What Happens Next

The survivors are still waiting.

They’re waiting for the Justice Department to release the 2.5 million pages still being withheld.

They’re waiting for the co-conspirators’ names to be unredacted from the 2007 draft indictment.

They’re waiting for someone to be held accountable for two decades of crimes beyond Jeffrey Epstein and Ghislaine Maxwell.

They’re waiting for the Attorney General to turn around.

On February 11, 2026, they learned that waiting might last forever.

Because the person who runs the Justice Department won’t even look at them.
