What I Heard Outside The Situation Room

A Canadian journalist, a thin door, and the pre-game briefing before tonight's address to the nation

April 1, 2026

By CanadianSnowmanSays | April 1, 2026

Publishing ninety minutes before the President addresses the nation.

I should not have been there. I am a retired Canadian businessman and investigative journalist, and I have no security clearance, no press credential, and no business whatsoever being in the West Wing of the White House on a Wednesday afternoon.

But the spirit of my great uncle, the distinguished Canadian diplomat Hugh Keenleyside — a man who spent his career navigating the most consequential rooms of the twentieth century with quiet purpose and absolute discretion — was upon me this morning. He urged me on. I could feel it. Uncle Hugh would have used proper channels, of course. Letters of introduction. Careful protocol. A firmly pressed suit.

I flew to Washington on the early flight and gained entry to the White House through an open door on the east side. Or rather — and I want to be precise here — a demolished door on the east side. Nobody has explained to me why it was demolished. Nobody appeared to be asking. There was exposed drywall, a hard hat someone had abandoned on a folding chair, and absolutely nobody checking credentials. Uncle Hugh, I suspect, would have disapproved of the entry method. He would also have had pointed questions about the structural integrity of the east wing. I pressed on regardless.

I was, if I am being honest, also there to use the bathroom. The one near the Roosevelt Room has excellent hand soap. Whether it survives the renovations is unclear.

What I heard through the wall, however, was worth the flight.

It was the pre-game briefing. The preparation session. The forty minutes before the motorcade, before the teleprompter, before Karoline Leavitt adjusts her cross and tells the cameras that the President will now provide “an important update on Iran.”

I pressed my ear to the plaster. I am not proud of this. I am also not sorry.

What follows is a transcript, reconstructed from memory, rendered in the spirit of the great Downfall meme tradition — that glorious internet institution in which Hitler’s bunker becomes a vehicle for whatever catastrophe the present moment requires. The original film has never been more apt. A leader of catastrophic military overconfidence, surrounded by terrified aides, in a room from which there is no good exit. The subtitles have simply been updated.

The inspiration for the format below comes from a brilliant Downfall parody currently circulating on Substack, created by Hitler Rants Parodies (YouTube). In the grand tradition of the Downfall meme — in which actor Bruno Ganz’s shattering portrayal of a delusional leader facing total collapse becomes a vessel for whatever catastrophe the present moment requires — someone has done the Lord’s work and updated the subtitles for 2026. I have merely eavesdropped on the sequel.

The President was seated at the head of the table. The generals were arranged around him in descending order of visible despair.

AIDE: Sir, we’ve prepared your talking points for tonight. We’ve taken out Supreme Leader Khamenei, ensured Israel is our only remaining ally, made cool videos of blowing things up, broken eighty-five percent of our campaign promises, and somehow managed to keep Hegseth mostly sober for the cameras.

TRUMP: The Mullahs are going to call any second begging for peace. We are WINNING. Get Hegseth to airdrop MAGA hats over Tehran.

GENERAL: (quietly) Mr. President... Iran...

TRUMP: I know where Iran is. I’ve bombed most of it.

GENERAL: Iran simply replaced the old Ayatollah with his gay son — known in the seedier side of Tehran as Mullah Mama — who now, through the Strait of Hormuz, effectively controls world energy markets. Senator Graham, upon learning this, has apparently had second thoughts about further bombing.

[Silence. The kind that precedes weather.]

GENERAL: We now expect runaway inflation followed by a global recession. Gas is trending toward eight dollars. The Strait closure has cut off one-fifth of the planet’s oil supply. Twenty thousand seafarers are trapped in an active warzone. Thirteen of our service members are dead. Twenty more were injured in Saudi Arabia on Friday. Iran is striking Gulf bases. The Houthis are firing on Israel. Hezbollah has re-engaged in Lebanon. And sir — with respect — the allies are not coming.

TRUMP: Everyone leave except Vance, Lil Marco, Hegseth’s designated driver, and the Vampire Miller.

[The room empties. What remains is the inner circle. The last men on the elevator going down.]

TRUMP: (to no one in particular) Why the FUCK did you let me do this.

You told me I could do a badass quickie on the Mullahs and get my peace prize. Nobel, people. I was going to get the Nobel. Now I’ve got to send in ground troops after claiming victory seventeen times, and somehow pretend this shitshow is going as planned.

Will Fox viewers really believe that more inflation and a stock market crash is owning the libs? How long can Fox and Friends keep saying that going broke is patriotic, and that this illegal war is a “special military operation”? Do you people have any idea what the impeachment orgy looks like if the Democrats take the Senate? It’ll be like Epstein and Watergate had a love child, and Monica Lewinsky was the midwife.

And could Don Jr. and the donors be a little less obvious while insider trading? They make Pelosi and Hunter Biden look like Mother Teresa and Mr. fucking Rogers.

AIDE: But sir — Lindsey Graham still believes this war will —

TRUMP: Lindsey jerks off to war footage, you fucking idiot. Although I’m told he’s reconsidering since he heard about the gay son. He’s been begging to meet the new Supreme Leader instead of bombing him.

(Senator Graham confirmed this in a statement this morning, calling on the President to “wind down” the war and pursue a peace deal. You cannot make this up.)

AIDE: But Sir, Steve Bannon now says conquering the globe is “America First.”

TRUMP: Bannon would say Epstein was the Easter Bunny if I told him to.

AIDE: (very quietly) But is eight-dollar gas “Trump Winning”?

TRUMP: (standing, beginning to pace) We had the Christian Nationalists, some of the Jews, the Holocaust deniers, the podcast bros, the anti-vax hippies, and even some brothers all buying MAGA merch. Even those poor Tejano-loving bastards we just deported said “Trump won’t start wars.” A ground invasion only plays with the neocons, the Israelis, and the most brain-dead Fox News tube-feeders.

It’s my own damn fault for hiring you bunch of soul-less boot-lickers. You endlessly degrade yourselves trying to worship me in front of the cameras.

But now you’re probably wondering how to rehab your image after this dumpster fire.

[He stops. Looks at the room. Looks at the map. Looks at the ceiling.]

TRUMP: Someone draft a statement calling this “Biden’s War.”

(beat)

At least sweet lips Karoline still looks hot talking this war up with that cross around her neck.

AIDE: What promises have we actually kept, really?

TRUMP: (a long pause, the deflation visible, the orange dimming slightly)

At least we closed the southern border. And people can say “gay” and “retarded” now. Football coaches don’t have to give their preferred pronouns.

(another pause)

Get me a Diet Coke and Laura Loomer.

And so, at nine o’clock tonight, the motorcade will roll. The cameras will find their angle. The flag will be positioned at the correct patriotic distance from the left shoulder. Karoline’s cross will catch the light. The teleprompter will scroll.

And the President of the United States will look into the lens and tell you that Iran asked for a ceasefire, that we’ll be leaving in two or three weeks, that gas prices are coming down, that the Strait of Hormuz is somebody else’s problem now, and that America — battered, exhausted, $8-a-gallon America — is winning bigly.

He is interrupting The Masked Singer to do this. The irony is not lost on anyone with a functioning cortex. One masked performer, replacing others.

I have slipped out of the West Wing. I washed my hands. The soap was, indeed, excellent.

You are on your own for the next twenty minutes.
