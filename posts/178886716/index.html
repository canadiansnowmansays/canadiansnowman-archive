When Democracy Fights Back

The Hypocrisy Exposed

Part 4: How Trump's DOJ Weaponized Justice

November 14, 2025

A four-month timeline reveals stunning double standards: Texas gets legal cover, California gets sued

On Thursday, November 13, 2025, Attorney General Pam Bondi announced that the Department of Justice would join a lawsuit to block California’s new congressional map, claiming it constituted “a brazen power grab that tramples on civil rights and mocks the democratic process.”

The statement would be laughable if it weren’t so dangerous.

Because just four months earlier, the same DOJ gave Texas Republicans a convenient legal justification to gerrymander five Democratic seats—then did absolutely nothing when Texas used that justification to implement maps that civil rights advocates say “illegally disadvantage minority communities.”

The DOJ joined a lawsuit filed by the California Republican Party challenging Proposition 50, which was approved by nearly 65% of California voters in the November 4 election. CNNNBC News The Trump administration claims California engaged in racial gerrymandering by using race as a factor to favor Hispanic voters, violating the 14th and 15th Amendments. CNNNBC News

But here’s what makes this lawsuit extraordinary: This marks the first time the Justice Department has sued over the wave of mid-decade redistricting across the country. KGNS Three Republican-led states—Texas, Missouri, and North Carolina—have not faced federal legal action after revamping district lines following Trump’s call for new maps to expand GOP numbers in the House. KGNS

This isn’t about protecting voting rights. It’s about protecting power.

And the receipts prove it.

The Smoking Gun: DOJ’s Texas Letter

To understand the stunning hypocrisy of this lawsuit, you need to know about a letter sent in early July 2025.

Assistant Attorney General Harmeet Dhillon sent a letter to Texas Governor Greg Abbott and Attorney General Ken Paxton warning that four of Texas’s congressional districts were unconstitutional “coalition districts” where Black and Hispanic voters combine to form a majority. The Texas TribuneElection Law Blog The letter claimed it was “well-established that so-called ‘coalition districts’ run afoul of the Voting Rights Act and the Fourteenth Amendment.” The Texas Tribune

Governor Abbott, days after receiving Dhillon’s letter, included redistricting on his special session agenda, citing “constitutional concerns raised by the U.S. Department of Justice.” The Texas TribuneThe Texas Tribune Before Abbott called lawmakers back to Austin, he was initially resistant to the redistricting plan pushed by Trump’s political team, according to two people who spoke with Abbott and the White House. Then Trump placed a personal phone call to Abbott during which they discussed redistricting, and Abbott subsequently agreed to put it on his agenda. Chiggerfest

The four districts Dhillon targeted? All represented by Democrats of color: Reps. Al Green, Sylvia Garcia, and Marc Veasey, plus a vacant seat previously held by Sylvester Turner. Votebeat

Texas Democrats immediately called bullshit. They demanded Dhillon come testify about her legal reasoning. State Rep. Cody Vasut, the Texas House redistricting chair, told his committee he was still waiting to hear back from Dhillon about the invitation he sent, adding with some laughter: “I received an out of office notification.” Democracy Docket

Texas Senate Republicans voted 6-3 against issuing a subpoena to compel Dhillon’s testimony. Sen. Borris Miles, D-Houston, was blunt: “We couldn’t garner enough respect from our colleagues to just question the person who wrote the letter attacking our districts, attacking our communities.” The Dallas Morning News

Dhillon never testified. She never defended her legal analysis. And she confirmed the DOJ has not sent a letter to any other state warning that its coalition districts violate federal law. Democracy Docket

Texas went ahead with redistricting anyway, dismantling those four districts and implementing a map designed to flip five Democratic seats to Republican control.

And the DOJ? Radio silence. No lawsuit. No enforcement action. Nothing.

Texas Throws DOJ Under the Bus

Then it got worse.

In September court filings, Texas Republicans officially renounced the DOJ’s legal rationale, calling Dhillon’s letter a “mistake” and acknowledging that Governor Abbott had used it as “political cover” to hide the partisan goal of the redistricting effort. Democracy Docket

Let me repeat that: Texas admitted the DOJ letter was legally wrong and that they used it as political cover for a partisan gerrymander.

“A legislative act based on [a] mistake – or that cite a Washington official’s mistake as political cover for lawful political redistricting – remains a valid legislative act,” the Texas filing stated. Democracy Docket

The filing also suggested that when Abbott cited DOJ’s letter, he was seeking to hide partisan motive: “Governor Abbott’s statements plainly reflect no desire to redraw districts because of their racial makeup, but instead an effort to transform the State’s most heavily Democratic districts regardless of their racial makeup and to cite a legal necessity (rather than political desire) as the goal.” Democracy Docket

So to be absolutely clear:

  1. DOJ gave Texas a dubious legal justification for redistricting

  2. Texas used it to gerrymander five Democratic seats

  3. Texas later admitted the DOJ analysis was wrong

  4. Texas explicitly stated they used DOJ as political cover

  5. DOJ took no enforcement action against Texas

And NOW—four months later—the same DOJ is suing California for racial gerrymandering?

The Harmeet Dhillon Problem

Here’s where it gets truly grotesque.

The California Republican Party lawsuit challenging Prop 50 was filed by Dhillon Law Group, the firm founded by Harmeet Dhillon—the same Harmeet Dhillon who is Assistant Attorney General in the Justice Department. Orange County Register

Read that again. The Trump administration’s top civil rights official at DOJ runs a private law firm that filed the original lawsuit. Then her own department joined that lawsuit.

This isn’t a conflict of interest. It’s a farce.

Dhillon wrote the Texas letter that even Texas admits was legally “mistaken.” She refused to testify about it. Her private firm sued California. Then her government department joined her private firm’s lawsuit.

At what point do we stop pretending this is about constitutional law and acknowledge it’s about raw partisan power?

When “Civil Rights” Becomes Code for Partisan Warfare

Attorney General Bondi’s statement made the partisan intent crystal clear: “Governor Newsom’s attempt to entrench one-party rule and silence millions of Californians will not stand.” NBC News

“One-party rule”? California is responding to Texas’s one-party gerrymander. If Bondi is concerned about partisan entrenchment, where was her statement about Texas? About Missouri? About North Carolina?

Bondi also claimed: “Newsom should be concerned about keeping Californians safe and shutting down Antifa violence, not rigging his state for political gain.” NBC News

What does “shutting down Antifa violence” have to do with redistricting? This is pure culture war signaling, not legal analysis. It reveals that DOJ views this as political combat, not constitutional law enforcement.

The timeline speaks for itself:

Early July 2025: Dhillon sends letter giving Texas legal cover for partisan gerrymander

July-August 2025: Texas uses letter to justify dismantling four minority-held Democratic districts

August 2025: Texas implements +5R map

September 2025: Texas admits DOJ letter was “mistaken” and used for “political cover”

September-November 2025: DOJ takes no action against Texas, Missouri, or North Carolina

November 4, 2025: California voters approve Prop 50 by 65%

November 6, 2025: California Republicans (via Dhillon’s private firm) sue

November 13, 2025: DOJ immediately joins lawsuit against California

This isn’t law enforcement. It’s selective prosecution designed to help one party and harm the other.

The Selective Enforcement Scandal

Let’s be absolutely clear about what the Justice Department is supposed to do.

DOJ is meant to defend the Constitution and protect voting rights for all Americans. It’s supposed to ensure that laws are applied consistently, without regard to which party benefits.

Under this administration, DOJ has become a partisan enforcement arm:

  • ✓ Gives legal cover to Texas (with a letter Texas admits was wrong)

  • ✓ Takes no action when Texas implements partisan gerrymander

  • ✓ Refuses to investigate Missouri, North Carolina, Ohio redistricting

  • ✓ Immediately sues California within days of Prop 50 passing

  • ✓ Does so via Assistant AG whose private firm filed the original suit

When one party gets immunity and the other gets prosecution, you don’t have rule of law. You have rule by party.

This is the very definition of weaponizing the justice system—using federal law enforcement not to uphold the law, but to advantage your political allies and punish your opponents.

What Comes Next

California will fight this lawsuit. And they have powerful defenses.

But the damage is already done. The Trump DOJ has revealed that in this administration, the Justice Department doesn’t defend justice—it defends power.

The question now is whether the courts will let them get away with it.

In Part 5, we’ll examine California’s legal defense, the three possible outcomes of this lawsuit, and why the Trump DOJ might have accidentally given opponents a weapon to challenge every Republican gerrymander in the country.

The selective enforcement might be the scandal. But the precedent it creates could be the solution.
