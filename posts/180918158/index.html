Trump's Foreign Policy

Civilizational Warfare Against Democratic Allies

Part 13: How the National Security Strategy Codifies Europe as the Enemy

December 6, 2025

The 33-page document that declares war on democratic allies

December 6, 2025

The Document That Explains Everything

Two days after Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner spent five hours in the Kremlin learning why America should offer more territorial concessions to Vladimir Putin, the Trump administration released its National Security Strategy. The timing was not coincidental. The Moscow meeting was tactical theater; the NSS is the strategic blueprint that makes such theater inevitable.

My Part 12 analysis documented how the five-hour Moscow meeting produced no specific compromises, no agreement on territorial control, and no scheduled Putin-Trump summit—but it did provide something far more valuable to Putin: American validation of Russian positions without European interference. The NSS explains why: Europe isn’t America’s ally in Trump’s worldview. Europe is the problem Russia is helping solve.

The 33-page document, released late Thursday night, represents what the Council on Foreign Relations called “a sharp change from the previous 2022 NSS that outlined the United States’ role in strengthening democracy and preserving peace” Council on Foreign Relations. But “change” understates the magnitude. This is architectural demolition of seventy-five years of American strategic doctrine, replaced with a framework that treats democratic allies as civilizational failures while courting accommodation with authoritarian powers.

Aaron Parnas, whose analysis I reviewed today, captured the stakes precisely: “This is not simply another policy paper. It is an urgent warning that the architecture of American foreign policy is shifting beneath our feet.”

The NSS does what my TACO analysis predicted: it transforms accommodation from observable pattern into official policy. And it does so through a civilizational critique of Europe so extreme that it validates Russian propaganda while echoing white nationalist conspiracy theories.

Europe: The Civilizational Threat

The NSS devotes two full pages to what it calls Europe’s existential crisis. The language is stunning in its hostility to America’s closest allies:

“Economic decline is eclipsed by the real and more stark prospect of civilizational erasure. The larger issues facing Europe include activities of the European Union and other transnational bodies that undermine political liberty and sovereignty, migration policies that are transforming the continent and creating strife, censorship of free speech and suppression of political opposition, cratering birthrates, and loss of national identities and self-confidence.” The White House

The document continues: “Should present trends continue, the continent will be unrecognizable in 20 years or less. As such, it is far from obvious whether certain European countries will have economies and militaries strong enough to remain reliable allies.” The White House

And most tellingly: “Over the long term, it is more than plausible that within a few decades at the latest, certain NATO members will become majority non-European. As such, it is an open question whether they will view their place in the world, or their alliance with the United States, in the same way as those who signed the NATO charter.” The White House

Political experts immediately recognized this language. NBC News reported that the language regarding “civilization erasure” and immigration echoes “great replacement theory,” a debunked white nationalist conspiracy theory that white populations are being replaced by immigrants from majority nonwhite nations NBC News. Time Magazine noted that the paper adopts the racist conspiracy theory known as the “Great Replacement Theory,” arguing that many NATO countries will soon be “non-European” due to mass immigration. As the theory has left the confines of internet forums and white nationalist sites and entered into the mainstream of prime-time cable news and political speeches, it has fueled racially based violence across the globe Time.

This is not diplomatic criticism of allied policies. This is ideological warfare against allied societies, wrapped in demographic fear-mongering that questions whether racially diverse NATO members will remain “aligned” with American values.

European Reactions: From Shock to Clarity

European officials responded with unusual directness to what they recognized as a fundamental betrayal.

Former Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt wrote on X: “It’s language that one otherwise only finds coming out of some bizarre minds of the Kremlin,” saying the document places the U.S. “to the right of the extreme right in Europe.” Time

Gérard Araud, former French ambassador to the United States, observed: “The stunning section devoted to Europe reads like a far-right pamphlet,” noting that the document “largely confirms” perceptions that Trump is an “enemy of Europe.” Time

German Foreign Minister Johann Wadephul, while acknowledging the U.S. as “our most important ally” in NATO, stated that questions about freedom of expression or “the organization of our free societies” are not part of alliance discussions. “We also don’t think that anyone needs to give us any advice on this.” NPR

Even more striking was the U.S. ambassador to NATO’s subsequent commentary. Matthew G. Whitaker asked at the Doha Forum: “Is Europe a dynamic economy that can grow, or is it just a museum that is a relic of the past and we just go to see the people’s lovely wines and cheeses and beers and waffles in the case of Brussels?” Newsweek

This is how America’s NATO ambassador describes the alliance: as potentially nothing more than a culinary tourism destination.

Russia: The Reasonable Partner

While Europe receives civilizational condemnation, Russia gets respectful engagement. The contrast is not subtle.

The NSS states: “European allies enjoy a significant hard power advantage over Russia by almost every measure, save nuclear weapons. As a result of Russia’s war in Ukraine, European relations with Russia are now deeply attenuated, and many Europeans regard Russia as an existential threat. Managing European relations with Russia will require significant U.S. diplomatic engagement, both to reestablish conditions of strategic stability across the Eurasian landmass, and to mitigate the risk of conflict between Russia and European states.” The White House

Note the framing: America’s role is not to help Europe defend against Russian aggression. America’s role is to “mitigate the risk of conflict between Russia and European states”—as if they were morally equivalent parties whose dispute requires American mediation.

The document calls for “reestablishing conditions of stability within Europe and strategic stability with Russia” The White House and prioritizes “ending the perception, and preventing the reality, of NATO as a perpetually expanding alliance” CNN—effectively granting Russia veto power over NATO membership decisions.

CNN’s analysis captured the dynamic: Russia is “spared, unsurprisingly, similar criticism, and deemed a relationship worthy of ‘strategic stability.’ The US is familiarly cast not as a NATO ally to Europe, but a mediator ‘to mitigate the risk of conflict between Russia and European states.’” CNN

Ukraine: The European Problem

On Ukraine, the NSS transforms American policy from supporting a democracy defending itself against invasion to pressuring Europe to accept Russian demands:

“It is a core interest of the United States to negotiate an expeditious cessation of hostilities in Ukraine, in order to stabilize European economies, prevent unintended escalation or expansion of the war, and reestablish strategic stability with Russia, as well as to enable the post-hostilities reconstruction of Ukraine to enable its survival as a viable state.” The White House

Note the priorities: stabilize European economies, prevent escalation, reestablish strategic stability with Russia. Ukraine’s “survival as a viable state” comes last—and the word “viable” suggests something considerably less than sovereign independence.

The document accuses European governments of subverting democracy: “The Trump Administration finds itself at odds with European officials who hold unrealistic expectations for the war perched in unstable minority governments, many of which trample on basic principles of democracy to suppress opposition. A large European majority wants peace, yet that desire is not translated into policy, in large measure because of those governments’ subversion of democratic processes.” CNN

This language mirrors Russian propaganda precisely. Carl Bildt noted that “the only part of the world where the new security strategy sees any threat to democracy seems to be Europe” NBC News—not Russia, which actually suppresses opposition; not China, which operates concentration camps; but Europe, whose democratic governments Trump accuses of antidemocratic practices.

The Authoritarian Preference Revealed

The most damning evidence of Trump’s authoritarian preference comes not from what the NSS says about adversaries, but from how it treats different kinds of allies.

Democratic Europe gets civilizational condemnation and demands to “correct its current trajectory.” The NSS explicitly calls for “cultivating resistance to Europe’s current trajectory within European nations” CNN—openly endorsing interference in allied democracies’ internal politics.

But Gulf monarchies? The NSS states America should abandon “America’s misguided experiment with hectoring” nations in the Middle East, especially monarchies in the Gulf, about their traditions and forms of government NPR. These nations, the document says, are “emerging as a place of partnership, friendship, and investment.” NPR

The message is clear: Democratic allies who question Trump’s Russia policy face ideological attack. Authoritarian monarchies who don’t challenge American accommodation get respectful partnership.

Time Magazine captured the double standard: “The document lambasts European leaders while simultaneously promising to stop ‘hectoring’ Gulf monarchies like Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates (UAE) into ‘abandoning their traditions.’ As the document claims to view these nations as ‘a source and destination of international investment’—and as Trump seemingly prioritizes the broader U.S.-Saudi relationship—the language shows a clear difference in how the Trump Administration views these leaders compared with Europeans.” DNYUZ

The Democracy Abandonment

Perhaps most fundamentally, the NSS formally abandons democracy promotion as an American objective. CSIS analysis stated bluntly: “The democracy agenda is clearly over. Foreign policy choices will be made based on what makes the United States more powerful and prosperous.” Center for Strategic and International Studies

The NSS itself declares: “We seek good relations and peaceful commercial relations with the nations of the world without imposing on them democratic or other social change that differs widely from their traditions and histories.” The White House

CFR expert analysis noted this represents “a major evolution in how Washington frames its competition with Beijing. This is the first time since the 1988 NSS—published during a period of optimism toward China’s reform and opening to the world—that the NSS has neither condemned China’s governance system nor expressed an intent to promote democratic reform in China.” Atlantic Council

Al Jazeera summarized the principle: “The US will pursue its own interests in dealing with other countries, the document says, suggesting that Washington will not push for the spread of democracy and human rights.” Al Jazeera

This is not pragmatic realism. This is ideological alignment with authoritarian governance models, combined with hostility toward democratic allies whose policies don’t align with MAGA ideology.

Conclusion: The Blueprint Revealed

The December 5th National Security Strategy provides documentary evidence of what my previous analyses identified through behavioral observation: Trump’s foreign policy treats democratic allies as problems while treating authoritarian accommodation as strategy.

The document establishes several definitive principles:

  • Europe faces “civilizational erasure” and may become unreliable as allies become “non-European”

  • Russia deserves “strategic stability” while Europe’s security concerns are “unrealistic”

  • America should interfere in European democracies’ internal politics to support “patriotic” (far-right) parties

  • Democracy promotion is abandoned as an American objective

  • NATO expansion must end—granting Russia effective veto power

  • Authoritarian regimes receive respectful partnership while democratic allies face ideological condemnation

These aren’t policy adjustments. They’re the systematic replacement of alliance solidarity with authoritarian accommodation, documented in America’s foundational strategic text.

Part 14 will examine how this strategic framework connects to the Moscow meeting, expert assessments of the implications, and what this means for American allies—including Canada—who thought shared democratic values mattered more than demographic composition and ideological alignment with MAGA preferences.

The NSS proves they were wrong.
