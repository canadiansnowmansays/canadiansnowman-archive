And the Envelope Please...

The First Annual State of the Union Awards — Hosted by Jimmy Kimmel

March 1, 2026

by CanadianSnowmanSays — March 1, 2026

The First Annual State of the Union Awards

Presented by the Academy of Political Performance Arts

Hosted by Jimmy Kimmel

A note on our host:

In September 2025, Jimmy Kimmel made a joke about the MAGA movement’s reaction to the murder of Charlie Kirk. The FCC chairman — a Trump appointee who authored the agency’s chapter of Project 2025 — called the comments “truly sick” and threatened ABC’s broadcast license. Disney suspended the show. Nexstar and Sinclair pulled it from over sixty affiliates. The president celebrated, posting: “Great News for America.”

Six days later, after public backlash wiped nearly $5 billion from Disney’s stock and prompted protests outside corporate headquarters, Kimmel returned to thunderous applause. His comeback monologue drew 21 million views on YouTube alone. The president responded: “I can’t believe ABC Fake News gave Jimmy Kimmel his job back... Let Jimmy Kimmel rot in his bad Ratings.” In December, ABC extended Kimmel’s contract through 2027.

Tuesday night, while the president delivered the longest State of the Union in American history, Kimmel waited as long as he could, then gave up and started taping his show while Trump was still talking. He then delivered his own State of the Union in under sixty seconds. It was more accurate than the president’s. It was also one hundred and seven minutes shorter.

There is no one more qualified to host an awards show where the facts don’t match the envelope.

[Kimmel walks to the podium in a tuxedo, picks up the first envelope, looks at the camera]

“Good evening. Welcome to the First Annual State of the Union Awards. I’m Jimmy Kimmel — and yes, I’m still on television. I know the President was told my show was cancelled — he said so on Truth Social. Someone should really update his briefings. But I figured: who better to host an awards show where the facts don’t match the envelope than the guy who survived Envelopegate? I was literally standing on stage at the Oscars the night Faye Dunaway announced La La Land as Best Picture when the winner was actually Moonlight. That was an honest mistake by two accountants from PricewaterhouseCoopers. Tonight, the wrong envelope is the format.

“I should mention: the President did not want me to host. He wanted me suspended, cancelled, investigated by the FCC, sued by ABC, and — I believe his exact words were — left to ‘rot in my bad Ratings.’ Last September his FCC chairman threatened to revoke our broadcast license over a joke. Over a joke. Meanwhile, the president made a crack Tuesday night about ‘damaging the fishing industry’ after his military strikes killed 115 people in the Caribbean, and the House chamber laughed. So we’ve established the rules: my jokes get you suspended; his jokes about dead people get applause.

“Anyway. Thank you, Mr. President. Once again, I couldn’t have done it without you. Twenty-one million views on the comeback monologue. Contract extended through 2027. You’re the best publicist I’ve ever had.

“Now — I was told the speech last night ran 108 minutes. The speech went on so long, Kristi Noem’s dog shot itself. They really do need to hire an orchestra to play him off like the Oscars. At the stroke of 90 minutes, go straight into YMCA and end it.

“But we’re not here to review the show — our Canadian colleague already did that, and he gave it two and a half stars, which frankly is generous. We’re here to hand out the awards. So — shall we begin?”

🏆 BEST ORIGINAL FICTION

Winner: “Gasoline is now below $2.30 a gallon in most states”

The judges were particularly impressed by the boldness of this claim, delivered to a national audience on a night when AAA data showed no state averaging below $2.37 and the national average sat at $2.95. Extra points for the Iowa visit where a member of the president’s own crowd shouted “No, $2.63!” — an unrehearsed moment of audience participation that the writers surely did not intend.

[Kimmel opens the envelope, squints, holds it up to the light]

“This says $2.30 a gallon. I’m being told that’s... not what it says at AAA. Or any gas station in the continental United States. We’re going to need Warren Beatty to verify this one — and even he’d have the good sense to double-check the card.”

Runners-up:

  • “$18 trillion in investment commitments“ (the White House website says $9.7 trillion — creative rounding)

  • “The largest tax cuts in American history“ (sixth largest since 1940, per the Tax Foundation — but who’s counting?)

  • “Zero illegal aliens admitted“ (dramatic reduction presented as absolute elimination — the statistical equivalent of claiming you’ve stopped eating because you skipped breakfast)

[Kimmel, reading the “zero illegal aliens” card]

“Zero. Not one. Complete and total elimination. Also, the door is always open to those who come in legally to be his next wife. So that’s something.”

🏆 BEST SUPPORTING PERFORMANCE BY A STATISTIC

Winner: “Core inflation down to 1.7%”

This cherry-picked three-month annualized rate — presented as though it were the standard year-over-year measure of 2.4% — delivered a virtuoso performance in the art of selective framing. The judges noted that choosing your favorite three months and presenting them as the whole year is a technique more commonly associated with dating profiles than economic policy.

[Kimmel, reading the card]

“The winner for Best Supporting Performance by a Statistic is... 1.7%. I’m told this number was plucked from a very specific three-month window, like choosing your best angle on a dating app and calling it a full-body shot. I tried this once. My wife was not impressed. The Bureau of Labor Statistics was also not impressed.”

Runner-up: The unemployment rate, which quietly rose from 4.0% to 4.3% but did not receive an invitation to the ceremony.

🏆 BEST DRAMATIC REINTERPRETATION OF A LEGAL DEFEAT

Winner: The Supreme Court tariff ruling, reimagined as “an unfortunate ruling”

A 6-3 Supreme Court decision — including two of the president’s own appointees — declaring his signature economic policy illegal, with $160 billion in tariffs collected unlawfully, was recast as a minor procedural inconvenience. The judges compared this to describing the iceberg as “an unfortunate piece of ice that the Titanic encountered.”

The president’s assurance that “alternative legal statutes” would preserve the tariffs was especially admired, given that his own lawyers argued during the case that those same statutes were inadequate.

[Kimmel, to camera]

“Now, I know a thing or two about being told my show is over when it isn’t. But when six Supreme Court justices — including two you personally appointed — say your tariffs are illegal, that’s not ‘an unfortunate ruling.’ That’s the judicial equivalent of your own kids changing the locks. And then telling your lawyers that the spare key you’re holding doesn’t work either.”

🏆 LIFETIME ACHIEVEMENT IN CREDIT-TAKING

Winner: President Donald J. Trump

For sustained excellence across two seasons in the art of claiming ownership of trends he inherited, including but not limited to: the murder rate decline (began 2023 under Biden), record oil production (trajectory established before 2025), and the economic recovery (GDP growth actually slowed from 2.8% to 2.2% under his stewardship).

The Academy notes that this award was previously held by the rooster who takes credit for the sunrise.

[Kimmel, presenting]

“This is a Lifetime Achievement Award, which is fitting because the President has been taking credit for other people’s work for a lifetime. He also took credit for getting my show cancelled last September — even though ABC brought me back six days later and just extended my contract through 2027. He took credit for ending eight wars, some of which he started. He took credit for bringing down inflation, which his tariffs made worse. He took credit for the economy, which grew faster before he touched it.

“At this point, I fully expect him to take credit for this awards show. ‘Without me, there would be no material.’ And honestly? He’s not wrong about that one.”

🏆 BEST DISAPPEARING ACT

Winner: Elon Musk / DOGE

For the complete and seamless vanishing of the administration’s most prominent initiative and its most famous champion from a two-hour address. The judges were unable to locate any mention of DOGE, Musk, or the 317,000 federal workers who departed under their watch. This is the governmental equivalent of removing your ex from every photo on Instagram — impressive in its thoroughness, transparent in its motivation.

[Kimmel looks offstage, shields his eyes]

“Is Elon here tonight? No? Has anyone checked the rafters? Under the seats? He was running the entire government six months ago — you’d think someone would have noticed him leave. I speak from experience: when someone disappears from a network’s programming, there’s usually an FCC chairman involved. But in Elon’s case, it appears the president just... moved on. Two hours. Not one mention. That’s the most thorough ghosting since my first marriage.”

🏆 MOST CREATIVE STAGING OF AN AWKWARD MOMENT

Winner: “Stand up if you agree” — the Democrat dare

The host’s attempt to force the opposition to physically stand in agreement or be filmed sitting in apparent opposition to protecting American citizens produced 15 seconds of the most exquisitely uncomfortable television since the Scott’s Tots episode of The Office. The Democrats’ collective decision to simply sit and stare was, in its own way, a masterclass in counter-programming.

[Kimmel]

“I’ve hosted the Oscars four times. I once had to keep a live audience entertained while accountants sorted out whether La La Land or Moonlight won Best Picture. I have never — never — experienced fifteen seconds of silence more painful than watching the president dare people to stand up and agree with him while carefully not mentioning the Americans his agents shot. That’s like asking someone to applaud your driving while the car is still on fire.

“He said, ‘You should be ashamed of yourself, not standing up!’ To which I’d say: you should be ashamed of yourself not mentioning Renee Good and Alex Pretti. But shame requires self-awareness, and we’re 108 minutes past that.”

🏆 BEST PERFORMANCE BY A NUMBER IN A ROLE IT WAS NOT QUALIFIED FOR

Winner: “Eight wars ended”

For the extraordinary achievement of inflating one fragile ceasefire, several pre-existing negotiations, and at least two active military operations the president himself ordered into an unprecedented record of peacemaking. Special recognition for including Iran on the list of “wars ended” despite the president having ordered airstrikes on Iranian soil seven months earlier.

[Kimmel holds up the envelope, pauses, then slowly pulls out a second card]

“Correction... one fragile ceasefire, some pre-existing talks, and a couple we actually started.”

Jimmy Kimmel hosting the SOTU awards: because someone has to call out the mix-up when reality doesnt match the card

[He turns to camera, deadpan]

“Not again.”

[Beat]

“You know, at the Oscars when we read the wrong card, we fixed it in under two minutes. This administration has been reading the wrong card for a year and they just keep going. At least Faye Dunaway had the decency to look embarrassed.”

🏆 THE “YOU HAD TO BE THERE” AWARD FOR UNINTENTIONAL COMEDY

Winner: The fishing joke

After the administration’s extrajudicial boat strikes killed at least 115 people in the Caribbean, the president joked about “damaging the fishing industry.” The judges determined this was not, technically, a joke — but noted that the audience laughed anyway, which may be worse.

[Kimmel, quietly]

“I got suspended for six days for a joke about how the MAGA gang reacted to Charlie Kirk’s murder. The FCC chairman called it ‘truly sick.’ The President celebrated it. He said it was ‘Great News for America’ that I was off the air.

“Last night, the President of the United States joked about 115 people killed by his own military strikes, and the United States Congress laughed.

“I just want to make sure we all understand the rules: my joke about a political reaction gets me pulled off television. His joke about actual dead people gets applause in the House chamber.

“Noted.”

🏆 BEST HISTORICAL IRONY IN A DRAMATIC PERFORMANCE

Winner: The internment camp tribute

For the achievement of applauding a World War II veteran who helped liberate an internment camp — during a speech defending the administration’s own detention facilities. The judges noted that the camera lingered on the veteran’s face during the standing ovation. He did not appear to notice the irony. The host certainly did not. History, watching from the gallery, took notes.

[Kimmel]

“He applauded the efforts of a World War II vet who liberated an internment camp, at the same time he is building new ones here in the United States. And the audience cheered. They cheered for the liberation and the construction, simultaneously, without any apparent awareness that these are opposite things.

“That’s not a political observation. That’s a diagnostic one.”

🏆 BEST SHORT FILM (ABSENCE CATEGORY)

Winner (tie):

  • The USAID elimination and its 600,000 estimated deaths

  • The Epstein files

  • The names Renee Good and Alex Pretti

  • The word “DOGE”

  • Any acknowledgment that the president’s party controls both chambers of Congress during a government shutdown

  • The U.S. women’s Olympic hockey team, who declined the invitation

The judges noted that what a two-hour speech chooses not to mention is its own form of cinema. The women’s hockey team receives special recognition for understanding that sometimes the most powerful statement is the one you make by not showing up.

🏆 THE CHRISTMAS MESSAGE FROM THE GRINCH AWARD

Winner: The food stamps boast

For the singular achievement of bragging — during a cost-of-living crisis worsened by your own tariff policies — about removing two million Americans from food assistance programs, and receiving applause for it.

[Kimmel]

“He bragged about kicking two million people off food stamps. While grocery prices are up. While his tariffs are making everything more expensive. It was like a Christmas message from the Grinch. And in fairness, the Grinch eventually felt bad about it. We’re still waiting.”

[Kimmel gathers the scattered envelopes from the podium]

“And that concludes the First Annual State of the Union Awards. I want to thank the Academy of Political Performance Arts for having me tonight. I’ve now hosted the Oscars and the SOTU Awards, and I can tell you — the Oscars had fewer fictional categories. At least when we announced the wrong Best Picture, we corrected it in under two minutes. This administration has been reading from the wrong card for a year and shows no signs of stopping.

“I’d also like to thank the President personally. Last September, you tried to have me fired. You celebrated when my show was pulled. You told the world my talent ‘was never there.’ You told America it was ‘Great News’ that I was silenced. Your FCC chairman threatened our broadcast license. Nexstar and Sinclair pulled us from sixty stations.

“And here I am — still on television, contract extended through 2027, hosting an awards show in your honor, while you delivered a 108-minute speech so long I had to start taping my show before you finished.

“In your speech, you forgot to mention the Americans your agents killed, the Supreme Court that struck down your tariffs, the efficiency czar who vanished, the women’s hockey team that said no, and the 600,000 people who died when you eliminated USAID. But you did remember to make a joke about dead fishermen.

“I believe that is what they call a Lifetime Achievement in Irony.

“Goodnight, everybody. I’ll see you tomorrow — whether you like it or not.”

[Applause. Cut to commercial. The President posts about it on Truth Social before the credits roll.]
