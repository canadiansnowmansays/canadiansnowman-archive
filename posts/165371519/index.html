The Artifice of Power

Part 1: How Trump's Administration Rejects Evidence

June 9, 2025

When polling, economic research, and your grocery bill all become "fake news"

Stephen Lawton
June 9, 2025

Thanks for reading! Subscribe for free to receive new posts and support my work.

We're living through something unprecedented in American politics: an administration that doesn't just spin the truth—it systematically rejects the very idea that facts should guide government decisions.

When multiple economic analyses show Trump's tariffs will cost families $1,200-$3,800 annually, the administration doesn't adjust policy. It attacks economic science itself. When federal judges unanimously block executive orders as "blatantly unconstitutional," the response isn't legal compliance but judicial intimidation. When polling consistently shows net disapproval, allies like Marjorie Taylor Greene dismiss it all as "fake news."

This isn't normal political theater. It's governance through constructed reality rather than empirical evidence.

Over the next three parts, I'll show you exactly how the Trump administration has built and maintains this alternative universe—and why it threatens the foundation of democratic governance itself.

Breaking: The LA Immigration Protests and Federal Power Overreach

This weekend's deployment of 2,000 National Guard troops to Los Angeles provides a real-time example of the administration's reality construction in action. Following ICE immigration raids that arrested 118 immigrants, protests erupted across LA—and Trump's response reveals how the administration frames civil unrest to justify unprecedented federal intervention.

Members of law enforcement during a standoff with protesters in Paramount on Saturday.

National Guard members are deployed around downtown Los Angeles on Sunday.

What Actually Happened

The Real Sequence:

  • ICE conducted raids in heavily Latino areas of LA on Friday as part of Trump's immigration crackdown

  • Protests erupted over the weekend against the immigration enforcement

  • Some confrontations occurred between demonstrators and federal agents

  • Trump federalized 2,000 California National Guard troops against the objections of Governor Newsom and Mayor Bass

The Local Response:

  • California Governor Gavin Newsom called the deployment "unlawful" and "purposefully inflammatory"

  • LA Mayor Karen Bass said the National Guard was not needed, praising local law enforcement's handling

  • Local officials emphasized they had adequate law enforcement resources (17,000 LAPD personnel)

What the Administration Claims

Trump's Justification:

  • Claims protests constitute "a form of rebellion against the authority of the Government of the United States"

  • Says state and local authorities are "unable to handle the task" of controlling protests

  • Frames deployment as necessary to protect federal agents and restore "very strong law and order"

The Federal Framing:

  • Labels protesters as "violent, insurrectionist mobs" attacking federal agents

  • Claims emergency authority under rarely-used federal laws typically reserved for actual rebellions

  • Demands federal control over state National Guard against Governor's wishes

The Constitutional Reality vs. Administrative Claims

Legal Experts' Assessment:

  • Georgetown's Steve Vladeck warns this raises risks of troops using force in "protection" role

  • Bernie Sanders calls it evidence of America's "slide into authoritarianism"

  • First time since 1992 Rodney King riots that a president has federalized Guard against state objections

The Constitutional Issue:

  • Trump used authority claiming protests constitute "rebellion"—a designation typically reserved for actual insurrections

  • Governor Newsom called it "a serious breach of state sovereignty"

  • Legal precedent shows federal military deployment usually requires state consent or genuine emergency

The Pattern: Civil Unrest as Pretext for Federal Control

This response exemplifies the administration's broader approach to governance through reality construction:

Step 1: Frame civil protest as existential threat ("rebellion against federal authority")

Step 2: Claim local authorities are inadequate despite evidence to contrary

Step 3: Deploy federal military force to project strength and establish precedent

Step 4: Use emergency powers to override normal constitutional limitations

The Reality: Immigration protests, while heated, don't constitute "rebellion" requiring military intervention. Local law enforcement was managing the situation, but the administration prioritized federal control and military spectacle over collaborative governance.

Governor Newsom's Assessment: "The federal government is taking over the California National Guard and deploying 2,000 soldiers in Los Angeles – not because there is a shortage of law enforcement, but because they want a spectacle."

The Hypocrisy: When Real Rebellion Wasn't "Rebellion"

The administration's framing of LA immigration protests as "rebellion" requiring military intervention reveals stunning hypocrisy when compared to its response to actual violent insurrection at the U.S. Capitol.

January 6, 2021: Actual Rebellion

  • Armed rioters stormed the U.S. Capitol building

  • Attacked police officers, injured 140 law enforcement personnel

  • Threatened to hang the Vice President of the United States

  • Attempted to stop the constitutional transfer of power

  • Chanted "hang Mike Pence" while hunting for lawmakers

  • Actual rebellion against the constitutional order of government

Trump's Response to Real Rebellion:

  • No National Guard deployment despite pleas from Capitol Police

  • Delayed response for hours while violence continued

  • Called rioters "very special people" and told them "we love you"

  • Never characterized the actual insurrection as "rebellion"

  • Pardoned over 1,500 participants after taking office

LA 2025: Immigration Protests

  • Peaceful protesters opposing immigration raids

  • No weapons, no storming of government buildings

  • No threats to constitutional order or elected officials

  • Standard civil demonstration against federal policy

  • Local law enforcement managing situation effectively

Trump's Response to Civil Protest:

  • Immediately deployed 2,000 National Guard troops

  • Characterized protests as "rebellion against federal authority"

  • Federalized state troops against governor's objections

  • Used emergency powers typically reserved for actual insurrections

  • Threatened Marine deployment for additional force

The Reality Construction Exposed

The pattern reveals the administration's true priorities:

  • Actual violent rebellion targeting democracy = "very special people" deserving pardons

  • Civil protests against immigration policy = "rebellion" requiring military response

  • Armed insurrection against constitutional government = no emergency powers needed

  • Peaceful demonstration in Latino neighborhoods = federal troops deployed

What this tells us: The administration's use of "rebellion" has nothing to do with actual threats to government and everything to do with which groups are protesting and which policies they're opposing.

The constitutional perversion: Trump pardoned those who committed actual rebellion while deploying military force against those exercising constitutional rights to protest government policy.

This comparison perfectly illustrates how the administration constructs reality to serve political ends rather than respond to actual threats to democratic order.

The Systematic Pattern: How Reality Gets Reconstructed

The LA National Guard deployment reveals the administration's approach in real-time, but it's part of a much broader pattern of reality construction that extends across every aspect of governance. When facts don't support preferred narratives, the administration doesn't adjust policies—it reconstructs the facts themselves.

This systematic approach operates across all policy domains, from emergency response to public opinion. The same techniques used to transform immigration protests into "rebellion" get applied to polling data, economic research, and democratic feedback. Let's examine how this reality construction machine operates across the board.

The Polling Mirage: Manufacturing Universal Support from Mixed Data

Take, for example, Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene's recent claim that Americans "love" Trump and his MAGA policies, dismissing all unfavorable polls as fabricated media lies. This represents the same reality construction technique we just saw with the LA protests—when evidence contradicts the preferred narrative, attack the evidence rather than acknowledge the reality.

Here's what the comprehensive polling data actually reveals:

The Real Numbers Right Now

Current Polling Averages:

  • Newsweek tracker: 47% approval, 49% disapproval

  • Silver Bulletin average: Net approval of -3.7%

  • YouGov/Economist: 45% approval, 49% disapproval

  • Reuters/Ipsos: 42% approval (matching lowest level of new term)

Where Trump Really Struggles:

  • Economic stewardship: 36% approval, 56% disapproval (lowest of either term)

  • Tariff handling: 34% approval, 58% disapproval

  • Foreign policy: 39% approval, 53% disapproval

The Marist Poll's "report card" on Trump's first 100 days tells the real story: while 68% of Republicans approve, 84% of Democrats rate his performance as "poor or terrible," and a majority of Americans describe his direction for the nation as "change for the worse."

The Cherry-Picking Strategy

Here's how the administration manufactures its "universal support" narrative:

Step 1: Find polls from Republican-leaning firms with "strong house effects"
Step 2: Amplify those outliers as representative of public opinion
Step 3: Dismiss comprehensive polling as "fake news"
Step 4: Create an echo chamber where 47% approval becomes "universal love"

Silver Bulletin notes that Trump's recent improvement comes largely from "polls from firms with strong Republican house effects." When your best polls only reach 47% approval and you're claiming universal support, you're operating in constructed reality.

The administration's refusal to acknowledge polling nuances—like the fact that improvement from 41% to 47% still means more Americans disapprove than approve—exemplifies its fundamental dishonesty about public sentiment.

The pattern is clear: any data that contradicts the preferred narrative gets dismissed as fabricated rather than addressed as democratic feedback.

The Kitchen Table Betrayal: How MAGA Voters Pay the Price

Here's where Trump's reality construction becomes truly cruel: the families who voted for him based on promises to lower everyday prices are bearing the heaviest financial burden from his policies.

The Grocery Store Reality Check

Trump campaigned relentlessly on high grocery prices, specifically citing egg costs as symbols of Biden-era inflation. Remember the constant refrain about expensive eggs?

Here's the devastating irony: Trump's policies are systematically making eggs more expensive.

How Trump's Policies Increase Egg Prices:

  • Tariffs on imported feed ingredients → higher chicken feed costs

  • Steel tariffs → more expensive chicken houses and processing equipment

  • Energy tariffs → higher heating and transportation costs

  • Result: National Chicken Council estimates 15-20% egg price increases

The broader food system impact:

  • Agricultural equipment faces 10-25% tariffs

  • Fertilizer imports hit with additional levies

  • Food packaging materials see substantial tariff burdens

  • Transportation costs rise due to steel and energy tariffs

Yale Budget Lab's bottom line: Food prices rising 2.8% from tariffs alone—equivalent to an entire year's worth of grocery inflation compressed into immediate price increases.

Gas Prices: The "Drill, Baby, Drill" Contradiction

Despite campaign promises that expanded drilling would bring cheap energy, Trump's tariff policies are systematically raising costs across the energy sector:

How "America First" Energy Becomes "America Pays More":

  • 25% tariffs on steel and aluminum increase pipeline construction costs

  • Refinery equipment becomes significantly more expensive

  • Energy infrastructure projects face higher material costs

American Petroleum Institute estimates: These tariffs add $2-4 billion annually to energy infrastructure costs—expenses passed directly to consumers at the pump.

So much for "drill, baby, drill" bringing down gas prices.

The MAGA Voter Mathematics

Here's the cruel reality: Trump's core supporters bear the heaviest burden from his policies.

The income distribution problem:

  • Rural/working-class families (Trump's base) spend 25-30% of income on necessities hit by tariffs

  • Wealthy urban households (who get tax cuts) spend just 15-20% on these items

Real family budget impact for a working-class Pennsylvania family:

  • Monthly grocery bill: $400 → faces $11-15 additional monthly costs

  • Energy costs: +$8-12 monthly

  • Clothing: +$6-10 monthly

  • Auto repairs: +$4-8 monthly (steel tariffs)

  • Annual impact: $360-540 in additional expenses

The Systematic Gaslighting

Rather than acknowledge these costs, the administration employs sophisticated narrative inversion.

When Walmart announced price increases citing tariffs, Trump's response? Tell them to "eat the tariffs"—as if presidential commands override economic laws.

When grocery prices rise? Blame "corporate greed" rather than tariff policies.

The gaslighting effect: The administration systematically tells working families that their lived experiences of higher costs are imaginary. Rising grocery bills become prosperity indicators. Increased energy costs become energy independence benefits.

This represents perhaps the most cynical aspect of Trump's reality construction—convincing his own supporters to disbelieve their monthly budgets while providing tax benefits to wealthy households who can afford the increased costs.

The Economic Reality Inversion: When Research Becomes "Fake News"

The disconnect between economic research and administration claims reveals the core of Trump's constructed reality. Multiple authoritative institutions—Penn Wharton, Yale Budget Lab, OECD—all project significant economic damage from Trump's policies. Yet the administration operates in a parallel universe where these same policies generate prosperity.

What Multiple Research Institutions Actually Show

Penn Wharton Budget Model:

  • Trump's tariffs will reduce GDP by 6%

  • Wages will decline by 5%

  • Middle-income households face $22,000 lifetime losses

  • Economic damage twice as large as raising corporate tax from 21% to 36%

Yale Budget Lab:

  • Consumer prices up 2.3% from tariffs

  • Average household cost: $3,800 annually

  • Bottom-income households face $1,700 annual losses

  • Apparel prices rising 17%

OECD Warning:

  • U.S. GDP growth slowing to 1.6% (down from 2.8% last year)

  • Inflation expected to spike to 3.9% by year-end

  • Global growth decelerating due to trade uncertainty

What the Trump Administration Claims

Peter Navarro dismisses economic research as "accounting gimmicks" and projects 2.7% growth rates that would create budget surpluses.

Stephen Miller claims tariffs will generate $2.3-3.3 trillion in revenue over ten years.

White House economists say tariffs "will not meaningfully contribute to inflation," claiming "foreign suppliers will have to eat the economic burden."

The Alchemy of Economic Spin

This isn't policy disagreement—it's reality construction through narrative inversion.

The administration doesn't argue that tariff costs are worth the benefits; it denies the costs exist. It doesn't acknowledge trade-offs; it claims only benefits. Economic research becomes "fake news" rather than input for policy refinement.

The Economic Policy Uncertainty Index has doubled since January, reaching levels not seen since COVID-19. Yet the administration presents this uncertainty as confidence. International warnings become foreign interference rather than economic expertise.

When the Federal Reserve expresses inflation concerns, the administration attacks central bank independence rather than acknowledging policy trade-offs. When major retailers cite tariffs for price increases, Trump suggests they "eat the tariffs"—as if presidential declarations can override economic laws.

The Pattern: Systematic Reality Rejection

What we're seeing isn't isolated spin on individual issues. It's a comprehensive system for dismissing any empirical evidence that contradicts preferred narratives:

  • Economic research → "accounting gimmicks"

  • Polling data → "fake news"

  • Consumer price increases → "corporate greed"

  • International warnings → "foreign interference"

  • Emergency management expertise → "federal control necessity"

The administration has developed sophisticated methods for transforming policy failures into narrative victories. This represents governance through alternative facts rather than evidence-based policy.

The broader pattern is consistent:

  1. Make empirically false claims about policy success

  2. Dismiss contradictory evidence as politically motivated

  3. Attack institutional sources rather than engage with substance

  4. Double down on alternative narratives regardless of mounting evidence

  5. Frame all criticism as persecution rather than democratic feedback

Why This Matters: The Democracy Stakes

This systematic rejection of expertise threatens democratic governance itself. Democracy requires shared factual foundations for meaningful policy debate. When empirical evidence becomes partisan opinion, informed citizen participation becomes impossible.

Citizens cannot evaluate trade-offs when the administration denies costs exist. Voters cannot assess policy effectiveness when statistics get manipulated for political narrative. Democratic accountability requires honest acknowledgment of policy outcomes—which this administration systematically refuses to provide.

The implications extend far beyond politics:

  • Families facing higher costs are told their experiences are imaginary

  • Economic expertise gets dismissed as partisan bias

  • Constitutional constraints become political attacks

  • Emergency management becomes military theater

  • Shared reality—the foundation of democratic debate—crumbles

When an administration systematically tells its own supporters to disbelieve their grocery bills while attacking the economic research that explains why those bills are rising, we've moved beyond political spin into something far more dangerous.

The Bottom Line

We're witnessing the systematic construction of alternative reality as a governing philosophy. When polling, economic research, emergency management expertise, and your grocery bill all become "fake news" to be dismissed rather than addressed, we've crossed a line that threatens the foundation of democratic governance itself.

The Trump administration has built a comprehensive system for rejecting empirical evidence in favor of constructed narratives. This isn't political theater—it's a direct assault on the idea that facts should guide government decisions.

From LA's protest-filled streets to your local grocery store, from polling data to emergency response, the pattern is consistent: when reality conflicts with preferred narratives, abandon reality.
