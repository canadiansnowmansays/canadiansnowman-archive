The Race for Full Self-Driving

Episode 9: Investment Strategies & Comparative Analysis

November 27, 2025

CanadianSnowmanSays November 27, 2025

Episode 8 established the risk landscape for Tesla’s manufacturing moonshot: extreme macro risk from potential AI bubble burst, severe manufacturing execution risk across unprecedented production ramps, and multiple additional risks spanning technology, finance, regulation, competition, and execution bandwidth.

The valuation models showed dramatic divergence—from $2.7-7T if everything works to $200-400B if execution fails during a macro collapse. The risk framework revealed that even perfect execution might not prevent 50-70% losses if Michael Burry’s AI bubble thesis proves correct.

But understanding risks isn’t enough. Investors need actionable frameworks: How should different risk tolerances approach this opportunity? What strategies make sense given the triple threat of manufacturing, technology, and macro risks?

This episode provides concrete investment strategies and comparative analysis of different strategic approaches. Episode 10 will provide scenario planning, decision frameworks, and series conclusion.

The Venture Capital Lesson: Show Me Traction or Show Me the Door

Before discussing strategies, let’s understand what sophisticated capital is doing.

Total AV sector funding in 2024: ~$4.5 billion—lowest since 2020.

But the story isn’t just about total dollars—it’s about where that capital is going.

Funding Concentration:

  • Number of funding rounds dropped 30% versus 2023

  • Average round size surged 75% to $78 million, the highest ever

Translation: Capital is concentrating in fewer, later-stage companies with proven commercial traction. The speculative phase is over.

Major 2024-2025 Funding Rounds:

Waymo: $5.6 billion (October 2024)

  • Led by Alphabet with T. Rowe Price, Silver Lake, Fidelity, a16z, Tiger Global

  • Largest AV funding round in history

  • Total raised: $11.1 billion, valuation $45+ billion

Wayve (UK): $1.05 billion (May 2025)

  • From SoftBank

  • One of Britain’s largest startup deals ever

GM → Cruise: $850 million (May 2025)

  • Restart funding for Phoenix, Dallas, Houston operations

  • After $10B+ in write-offs

Uber → WeRide: $100 million (May 2025)

  • Strategic partnership for 15 additional cities globally

Nuro: $203 million (August 2025)

  • Series E extension with new investor Nvidia

  • Company pivoting from fleet operator to technology licensing

Strategic Insight: Corporate strategic investors—automakers, tech giants, transportation platforms—are replacing venture capitalists as primary funding sources. This reflects risk-off mentality where only players with strategic synergies or balance sheet strength participate.

The Lesson for Retail Investors:

If venture capitalists with information advantages won’t fund speculative AV plays, why should retail investors? The smart money demands:

  • Proven commercial operations

  • Sustainable unit economics

  • Clear path to profitability

  • Strategic corporate backing

Any investment strategy that ignores this reality risks repeating the mistakes that created the $25 billion graveyard.

Investment Strategies: How to Play the Manufacturing Moonshot

Given the risk framework from Episode 8, here are three distinct approaches matched to different risk tolerances.

Conservative Approach (For Risk-Averse Investors)

Core Principle: Demand proof before committing capital. Avoid pure-play exposure until validation milestones achieved.

Avoid Tesla direct exposure until proof points:

  • Wait for April 2026 Cybercab production validation

  • Require FSD v14.3 unsupervised approval evidence

  • Confirm Q2-Q3 2026 production and margin data

  • Monitor macro: Avoid entry if Nasdaq enters confirmed bear market or AI sector shows bubble characteristics

Alternative exposures with lower risk:

  • Alphabet (40-50% of AV allocation): Waymo exposure with Google Cloud and Search diversification, lower beta to AI bubble

  • Baidu (30-40%): Proven AV profitability with search business stability, emerging market discount

  • Component suppliers (10-20%): Nvidia (AI chips), Mobileye (vision systems), Qualcomm (connectivity)

If taking small Tesla position (<5% of portfolio):

  • Dollar-cost average across 2026 execution milestones

  • Set stop-losses at 30-40% decline

  • Consider macro hedges: Put options, inverse Nasdaq ETFs, or sector rotation to value/defensive stocks

  • Accept this is speculation, not core holding

  • Exit entirely if both execution slips AND macro deteriorates

Key Monitoring for Conservative Investors:

  • Quarterly production reports vs. targets

  • FSD approval timeline and regulatory signals

  • Free cash flow trends

  • Nasdaq/AI sector technical indicators

  • Guidance credibility

Moderate Growth Approach (Balanced Risk/Reward)

Core Principle: Diversified exposure with dynamic rebalancing based on execution milestones and macro conditions.

Portfolio Allocation:

  • 40% Alphabet: Lowest risk, proven technology, patient capital, lower AI bubble exposure

  • 30% Tesla: Highest upside if execution succeeds AND macro cooperates

  • 20% Baidu: International expansion, proven profitability, emerging market discount

  • 10% Component suppliers or Pony.ai: Optionality and diversification

Timeline expectations aligned with realistic schedules:

  • Safety driver removal: 2026-2028

  • Sustainable profitability: 2027-2030

  • Significant market penetration: 2030+

Dynamic Risk Management:

Rebalancing Triggers - Increase Tesla Exposure:

  • April 2026 Cybercab production starts on time

  • FSD v14.3 achieves unsupervised approval Q1-Q2 2026

  • Q2-Q3 2026 production validates 2.7M run rate trajectory

  • Nasdaq stabilizes/recovers, AI bubble fears subside

Rebalancing Triggers - Reduce Tesla Exposure:

  • Cybercab delayed beyond Q2 2026

  • FSD approval pushed to late 2026 or beyond

  • Guidance cuts or cash flow deterioration

  • Nasdaq confirms bear market (20%+ decline from highs)

  • AI sector multiple compression accelerates

Macro Overlay Management:

  • Reduce overall AV allocation if Nasdaq enters bear market

  • Use options collars on Tesla position if AI bubble fears intensify

  • Shift from Tesla to Alphabet if macro deteriorates (lower beta to same theme)

  • Monitor AI sector leading indicators: Palantir, C3.ai multiples

Key Monitoring for Moderate Investors:

  • Monthly: Macro trends, Nasdaq direction, AI sector sentiment

  • Quarterly: Production data, financial results, guidance updates

  • Annual: Competitive positioning, regulatory progress, strategic execution

Aggressive Growth Approach (High Risk Tolerance)

Core Principle: Concentrated bet on Tesla’s vertical integration thesis with explicit acceptance of binary outcomes and macro risk.

Position Structure:

  • Core thesis: Vertical integration creates winner-take-most dynamics

  • Belief that Musk executes on aggressive timelines

  • Acceptance of 50-80% downside risk for 3-7x upside potential

  • Macro awareness: Explicitly betting that either (a) AI bubble fears overblown OR (b) Tesla differentiates from AI sector during selloff

Implementation Options:

Direct Equity (50-70% of allocation):

  • Concentrated position accepting extreme volatility

  • 3-5 year time horizon minimum

  • Conviction-weighted entries on weakness

  • Mental preparation for 40%+ quarterly swings

Long-Dated Options (30-50% of allocation):

  • January 2027-2028 expiration

  • Strike prices $450-600 capturing bull case

  • Risk defined: Premium paid vs. unlimited equity downside

  • Consider spreading: Buy $450 calls, sell $650 calls to reduce premium if worried about macro capping upside

Monitoring Triggers - Increase Position:

  • FSD v14.3 unsupervised approval achieved

  • April 2026 Cybercab production starts

  • Q2-Q3 2026 production validates trajectory

  • AND Nasdaq stabilizes/recovers from current weakness

Monitoring Triggers - Reduce Position:

  • Cybercab delayed to late 2026 or 2027

  • FSD approval pushed beyond Q2 2026

  • Guidance cuts or margin pressure

  • Cash flow deterioration requiring capital raise

  • OR Nasdaq enters confirmed bear market

  • OR AI sector shows bubble characteristics accelerating

Monitoring Triggers - Exit Entirely:

  • Multiple delays across manufacturing/FSD/regulatory

  • Capital raise required, diluting equity

  • Competitive losses to Waymo or Chinese players

  • OR Michael Burry-style macro indicators suggest imminent AI bubble burst

Macro Hedge Considerations:

  • If concentrated long Tesla, consider small Nasdaq put options as insurance

  • Monitor AI sector multiples (Palantir, C3.ai, etc.) as leading indicators

  • Watch for short interest increases across AI sector

  • Follow prominent hedge fund positioning (Burry, Chanos, Einhorn, etc.)

Critical Reassessment Point:

If execution succeeds but stock flat/down due to macro = thesis invalidated, consider exit. This scenario (good execution, bad returns) proves macro override, suggesting capital better deployed elsewhere.

Key Monitoring for Aggressive Investors:

  • Weekly: Stock price action, options flow, short interest

  • Monthly: Macro trends, Fed policy, AI sector sentiment, technical analysis

  • Quarterly: All KPIs from conservative/moderate strategies

  • Continuous: News flow, analyst updates, competitor developments

The $5 Trillion Waymo Question vs. The $7 Trillion Tesla Question

Understanding the alternative strategic bet helps frame Tesla’s risk/reward.

Waymo Bull Case: The Partnership Model

Some analysts argue Waymo alone could ultimately be worth $5 trillion, potentially driving Alphabet stock to $500+.

The bull case logic:

  • Dominant market position in safest, most validated technology

  • Network effects from safety data and geographic coverage

  • Platform potential beyond robotaxis (delivery, logistics, licensing)

  • Winner-take-most dynamics in transportation platform markets

  • No catastrophic failure risk—patient Alphabet capital sustains through setbacks

The post-November 6 reality:

  • Waymo pursues slow, safe, expensive approach

  • Partnership model limits capital requirements but also limits scaling speed

  • No vertical integration into manufacturing—depends on OEM partnerships

  • Patient capital advantage but also patience requirement (2030+ for material profitability)

  • Lower risk, lower reward profile

Investment implication: Waymo through Alphabet offers lower risk/lower return exposure. If you believe manufacturing is the decisive advantage, Waymo loses. If you believe safety validation and patient scaling wins, Waymo prevails.

Tesla Bull Case: The Vertical Integration Bet

CERN Basher’s framework provides a simple rule: Assuming $25,000 annual profit per robot with a PE multiple of 40, every 1 million robots deployed creates $1 trillion in market value.

This means:

  • 1M robots = $3T total market cap ($1T Optimus + $2T auto/energy)

  • 2M robots = $6T market cap

  • 10M robots = $10T market cap

For robotaxis specifically: “$3 trillion for robotaxi with a million vehicles. If we double that, we’re close to six trillion. If we triple it, we’re close to 9 trillion just on robotaxi.”

This framework assumes conservative 25% profit margins and modest PE multiples—both likely low given platform business characteristics and winner-take-most dynamics.

The bull case logic:

  • Manufacturing scale creates insurmountable cost advantages

  • Chip independence provides structural profit margin superiority

  • Vertical integration from silicon to service creates platform moat

  • First-mover advantage at scale captures winner-take-most dynamics

  • Speed advantage: 2-2.5M robotaxis by 2028 vs. Waymo’s slower scaling

The post-November 6 reality:

  • Requires near-perfect execution across manufacturing, technology, regulatory

  • Aggressive timelines create binary outcomes (massive success or significant failure)

  • Capital requirements strain cash flows during ramp

  • Execution risk concentrated in 18-36 month period

  • Higher risk, higher reward profile

Investment implication: Tesla offers concentrated risk/return. If vertical integration + manufacturing scale proves decisive, returns could be generational (3-7x). If execution falters, losses could be severe (50-80%).

The Choice:

Waymo (via Alphabet): Tortoise strategy—slow, safe, possibly wins through attrition and patient capital

Tesla: Hare strategy—fast, risky, possibly wins through scale and vertical integration

For investors: The question isn’t which technology is better. It’s which risk profile matches your portfolio objectives, time horizon, and conviction level.

The Public Market Reality: IPO Graveyard vs. Vertical Integration Premium

The 14 AV-related companies that went public are down average 80% from debut prices:

  • Embark: Down 95%+

  • Velodyne: Down 95%+

  • Quanergy: Down 95%+

  • Pony.ai: Down 50-53% from private peak (though up 70% September 2025 on expansion news)

Only companies with diversified business models maintaining market confidence:

  • Alphabet: Google revenue funds Waymo development

  • Baidu: Search and AI cloud revenue funds Apollo Go

  • Tesla: Vehicle sales fund robotaxi development (though margins compressing)

Message from public markets: Show profitability or get crushed. Pure-play AV bets without alternative revenue face severe skepticism.

Post-November 6 Twist: Tesla is attempting to go the opposite direction—increasing risk and capital requirements with manufacturing moonshot. Public markets will either reward this with multiple expansion (if execution succeeds) or punish with severe multiple compression (if execution falters).

The vertical integration premium or discount depends entirely on execution over next 18-36 months.

Investor Takeaway: Avoid pure-play AV exposure without diversified business models. The IPO graveyard proves public markets won’t sustain speculative valuations without proof of profitability.
