The Pentangeli Gambit

Part 5 of The Enforcers Series

April 2, 2026

There is a scene in The Godfather Part II that every student of power should be required to watch before they are permitted to vote, hold office, or operate a gavel.

Frank Pentangeli — former Corleone capo, protected government witness, man who has already told Senate investigators everything — sits at the hearing table ready to bring down the family. He has the facts. He has the immunity deal. He has the microphone.

Then he looks across the room.

Sitting quietly in the gallery, saying absolutely nothing, doing absolutely nothing, is his brother. Vincenzo. Brought over from Sicily for precisely this occasion. Not to testify. Not to threaten. Simply to be there.

Frank Pentangeli clears his throat, leans into the microphone, and tells the United States Senate that he made the whole thing up.

Michael Corleone walks out a free man. The family survives. Democracy, as usual, picks up the dry cleaning bill.

On Wednesday, April 1st — and the calendar date, dear reader, is doing its own satirical work here — Donald J. Trump became the first sitting president in American history to attend oral arguments at the United States Supreme Court.

He did not testify. He did not argue. He was not, technically, a party to the proceedings, though the case was named, with some economy of irony, Trump v. Barbara.

He simply sat there.

Vincenzo, freshly arrived from Sicily, nodding pleasantly at the family’s business.

The Don had come to watch the witnesses.

Mr. Trump in January 2025 signed an executive order to limit birthright citizenship.Credit...Doug Mills/The New York Times

The case in question concerned the President’s executive order to end birthright citizenship — a right enshrined in the 14th Amendment since 1868, upheld in every court that has examined it, and inconvenient to the current administration’s preferred demographic arithmetic. Every lower court in the land had struck the order down as plainly unconstitutional. The Supreme Court, stacked as it is with three of the Don’s own personal appointments, was understood to be the last best hope for the Family.

So the Don came to court. Not to explain himself. Not to make the case. His consigliere, Solicitor General D. John Sauer — a man who has perfected the art of saying preposterous things with a straight face — was there to handle the talking.

The Don was there to watch. And to be seen watching.

The case argued on Wednesday focused on the constitutionality of an executive order seeking to end citizenship for babies born on U.S. soil to undocumented immigrants and temporary foreign visitors.Credit...Anna Rose Layden for The New York Times

Here is where the Pentangeli Gambit, deployed with such elegant brutality in Godfather II, met its first operational difficulty.

The nine witnesses did not fold.

Chief Justice John Roberts — a man appointed by a Republican president, confirmed by a Republican Senate, and not, it should be noted, in possession of a brother available for transport from Sicily — looked at Sauer’s argument that the world has changed since 1868 and said, with the patience of a man explaining basic arithmetic to a persistent toddler: “It’s the same Constitution.”

Justice Neil Gorsuch — Trump Appointment No. 1 — noted that the administration’s key legal concept appeared nowhere in the actual debates around the 14th Amendment. Precious little, he suggested, with the tone of a man who has read the documents.

Justice Amy Coney Barrett — Trump Appointment No. 2 — questioned the administration’s central arguments with what legal observers described as “skepticism” and what the rest of us would describe as barely suppressed disbelief.

Even Justice Samuel Alito, reliably the Court’s most creative interpreter of constitutional provisions that say the opposite of what he decides they say, could only manage to press questions about the framers’ intent, which is not quite the same as siding with the Don.

Vincenzo sat in the gallery. The witnesses kept talking.

The Don departed after approximately ninety minutes — conveniently, just as the other side’s lawyer rose to speak. He had heard his own lawyer’s arguments. He had no interest in the counterarguments. This is, in fairness, a consistent governing philosophy.

On his way out, he paused to share his constitutional analysis with the waiting press corps.

“We are the only Country in the World STUPID enough to allow ‘Birthright’ Citizenship!” he explained, on Truth Social, deploying his trademark capitalization to signal which words deserved the solemnity usually reserved for the names of God and himself.

The ACLU’s executive director, who had spent the morning arguing the law at some length, noted that if the President wished to attend the proceedings to watch the Constitution explained to him, they would be delighted to have him in the room.

Now, in a properly run Family, the Pentangeli Gambit works because the witnesses understand what is at stake. Vincenzo doesn’t need to say a word. His presence says everything: We know where you live. We know where your brother lives. The family has a long memory and a longer reach.

The problem with deploying this tactic against a Supreme Court Justice is that Supreme Court Justices have lifetime tenure, live in federal housing, and cannot be fired by executive order, no matter how many executive orders are drafted on the subject.

They are, in the technical language of the mob, untouchable.

This has not, it should be said, prevented the administration from trying. The Don has previously suggested that judges who rule against him should be “impeached,” that courts are “weaponized,” and that the entire judicial system represents a conspiracy against the interests of a man who has, by sheer coincidence, faced rather a lot of legal proceedings.

But suggestions are not omertà. And the Justices, to a one, kept asking questions.

The decision in Trump v. Barbara is expected by early summer.

Every lower court has found the order illegal. The text of the 14th Amendment says what it says. The precedents are a century old and unambiguous. The Supreme Court’s conservative wing spent Wednesday morning dismantling the administration’s own arguments in real time while the Don sat in the gallery and the American press corps attempted to process the historically unprecedented spectacle of a sitting president attending his own case like a nervous defendant who doesn’t quite trust his lawyer.

Frank Pentangeli, confronted with his brother’s presence, made the rational choice. You fold. You protect the family. You take whatever they give you and you go live out your days in a government safehouse in Nowheresville, drinking bad wine and pretending the last twenty years didn’t happen.

But the Supreme Court of the United States is not Frank Pentangeli.

And John Roberts, one suspects, does not have a brother in Sicily.

President Trump departed the Supreme Court oral arguments at the end of Solicitor General Sauer’s arguments and is now back at the White House. Jonathan Ernst/Reuters

The Enforcers Series series documents, with all appropriate sardonic gravity, the ongoing transformation of American governance into something that would have seemed familiar to the Corleone family and deeply alarming to the Founders. The series will continue for as long as the material keeps presenting itself, which, current trajectories suggest, will be for some time.



A BRIEF FACTUAL ADDENDUM

(Because the Don, on his way to the motorcade, could not resist)

Departing the Court, the President posted the following on Truth Social:

“We are the only Country in the World STUPID enough to allow ‘Birthright’ Citizenship!”

For the record — and the record, unlike the Don, does not embellish — approximately 33 countries offer unconditional birthright citizenship, among them Canada, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Jamaica, Cuba, Chile, and most of Central and South America. The United States is not a lonely eccentric on this question. It is, in fact, surrounded by neighbours who feel exactly the same way about it.

Where the claim contains a kernel of something approaching accuracy: the US and Canada are the only two countries the IMF classifies as “developed” that grant unrestricted birthright citizenship. Europe and Asia do not, as a general matter, follow this principle.

This is, one notes, a rather more modest and defensible argument than “the only Country in the World.” It is also considerably less useful for all-caps posting purposes, which may explain why it went undeployed.

The Supreme Court heard two hours of constitutional argument on Wednesday. The Don spent ninety minutes of it in the room, left when the other side began speaking, and was factually incorrect before reaching his car.

The witnesses, it bears repeating, kept talking
