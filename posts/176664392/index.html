The Charlie Kirk Debates

#7: "Empathy is a Made-Up Concept and Damaging to Society" - Part I: The Case

November 4, 2025

Charlie Kirk’s “Prove Me Wrong” wasn’t rhetoric—it was a radical call to evidence, a dare to dismantle weak claims with stronger ones. That’s the spirit guiding these debates: Direct engagement with his words, as if he’s here to rebut, because silencing the dead dishonors the discourse he ignited. No gloating over gaps; just the grind of applying his standard back at him, from constitutional ink to the scales of sanctity and safety. If the address feels forward, it’s by design—honoring a provocateur by provoking thought. With that, let’s turn the mirror:

Charlie Kirk, let’s apply your “Prove Me Wrong” standard to your claim that empathy is a “made-up, New Age term” that “does a lot of damage.” You argued it blurs judgment and manipulates politics, preferring sympathy instead. Prove me wrong.

Kirk’s Statements on Empathy

Charlie Kirk’s critique of empathy emerged in his broader attacks on what he saw as emotional manipulation in progressive politics, particularly during discussions on immigration, criminal justice, and “victimhood culture.” During a September 2023 episode of The Charlie Kirk Show, responding to audience questions about “toxic compassion,” Kirk delivered one of his most direct broadsides against the concept itself.

The “Made-Up Concept” Claim

Kirk’s core assertion was blunt and unqualified:

”I can’t stand the word empathy, actually. I think empathy is a made-up, New Age term that—it does a lot of damage, but it is very effective when it comes to politics. Sympathy I prefer more than empathy.”

He elaborated that empathy “blurs judgment” and “clouds truth,” positioning it as a modern invention designed to erode rational discourse:

”Empathy blurs judgment, it clouds truth. Sympathy allows compassion with clarity. We need more sympathy, not empathy.”

Empathy as Political Weapon

Kirk frequently tied his disdain to cultural and political battles, echoing guest Allie Beth Stuckey’s warnings about “toxic empathy.” In a June 2025 X post sharing Stuckey’s clip from a Turning Point USA event, Kirk highlighted:

”Toxic empathy is the primary tool of persuasion used by progressives to manipulate well-meaning Christians. They use toxic empathy by employing our language, our Bible verses, our concepts, and then pervert them to morally extort us into adopting their position.”

In the same November 2023 episode of The Charlie Kirk Show, he applied this to immigration debates:

”Empathy for the so-called ‘migrants’ is being weaponized to destroy our borders. It’s not real compassion—it’s a fake emotion that ignores the harm to American families.

”Kirk illustrated the distinction by arguing that sympathy maintains boundaries while empathy leads to exploitation, as seen in debates over immigration where unchecked emotional immersion could compromise national security.

The Broader Attack on “Feelings”

This fit Kirk’s larger worldview, where emotions were suspect and facts paramount—a riff on Ben Shapiro’s “facts don’t care about your feelings”:

  • Empathy enables “woke” policies like open borders and DEI initiatives

  • It undermines Christian discernment by prioritizing “victimhood” over truth

  • True morality requires rational sympathy, not immersive emotional surrender

In an October 2022 X post, he contrasted selective empathy, noting: “The same people who lecture you about ‘empathy’ have none for the soldiers discharged for the jab, the children mutilated by Big Medicine, or the lives devastated by fentanyl pouring over the border.”

Kirk’s Consistency on the Issue

Kirk reiterated this stance across platforms, often in response to progressive policies:

  • In a 2024 CPAC speech critiquing criminal justice reform: “The left’s empathy scam is why we have drag queen story hours instead of secure schools.”

  • On X in August 2024, quoting Musk and Trump: “There’s a lack of empathy for the victims of criminals and too much empathy for the criminals...there has to be real, deep empathy” for police and citizens.

  • In May 2023 X exchanges on immigration: “Where’s your empathy for America and its citizens? Where’s your empathy for the overrun schools, hospitals, and cities?”

He saw rejecting empathy as intellectual courage, aligning with his “Prove Me Wrong” ethos: Challenge feelings with facts.

The Theological Component

Speaking to faith audiences, Kirk framed this as biblically sound:

  • Empathy confuses mercy with enabling sin (e.g., “turning the other cheek doesn’t mean inviting thieves”)

  • Sympathy echoes Proverbs’ call for wisdom over unchecked compassion

  • Christians must “love from a distance” to avoid the “New Age” trap of emotional idolatry

Public Reaction

Kirk’s views drew immediate backlash:

  • Progressives called it “sociopathic,” especially amid his hardline stances on immigration and LGBTQ+ issues

  • Even some conservatives, like David French, critiqued: “Dismissing empathy guts the Gospel’s heart.”

His stance would prove tragically ironic given later events.

Kirk’s Framework

Kirk established key premises:

  • Invented Term: Empathy as a 20th-century “New Age” psyop, not timeless virtue

  • Damaging Effect: Blurs truth, enables manipulation, erodes societal strength

  • Preferred Alternative: Sympathy as clear-eyed compassion without emotional contagion

  • Political Utility: Effective leftist tool, but rejectable through rational faith

  • Moral Calculus: Facts and principles over feelings preserve civilization

This stance encapsulated Kirk’s blend of anti-woke fervor and self-proclaimed Christian realism: Empathy wasn’t just wrong—it was a threat to the America he championed.

Part II: The Challenge will systematically examine this position using Kirk’s own standards of evidence, Christian theology, constitutional principles, and logical consistency—while drawing on neuroscience, evolutionary biology, and moral philosophy to reveal empathy’s indispensable role in human flourishing.
