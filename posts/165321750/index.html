The Trump Grift

Part 5 Taxpayer-Funded Advertising: The Golf Course Grift

July 4, 2025

CanadianSnowmanSays
July 4, 2025

The Most Expensive Marketing Campaign in History

While Trump's cryptocurrency schemes and foreign business deals grab headlines, the most brazen example of his grift is hiding in plain sight: his golf trips. Every weekend, taxpayers fund what amounts to the world's most expensive advertising campaign for Trump-branded properties.

Most presidents vacation at Camp David (free) or their personal homes. Trump has turned presidential travel into a systematic wealth extraction scheme that costs taxpayers hundreds of millions while generating revenue for his businesses.

The scale is breathtaking: if Trump maintains his current pace, he'll spend roughly $272 million of taxpayer money on golf trips over four years. That's more than the annual GDP of some small countries, all so the president can play golf at properties he owns and profits from.

This isn't presidential recreation—it's a taxpayer-funded marketing campaign that happens every single weekend.

Chapter 1: The Numbers Don't Lie

Donald Trump's insistence on playing golf at his Florida courses has already cost American taxpayers more than $18 million since he regained the presidency, setting him on a pace to exceed the $151.5 million he spent in his first term.

Let's break down the staggering costs:

Per-Trip Breakdown

Each trip to Mar-a-Lago costs taxpayers approximately $3.4 million, according to a 2019 Government Accountability Office report. That figure, based on 2017 dollars, would be about $4.4 million in today's money when adjusted for inflation.

These costs include:

  • Air Force One operations: Nearly $200,000 per hour in flight

  • Military transport: C-17 cargo planes to transport the presidential motorcade

  • Coast Guard operations: Vessels stationed in the Atlantic

  • Local law enforcement: Police boats with mounted machine guns patrolling waterways

  • Secret Service: Agents, accommodation, and overtime costs

  • Explosive detection: K-9 units and bomb disposal teams

The Current Pace: Golf Every 4.9 Days

Trump has played golf on 13 of his first 48 days in office—more than 27% of his time as president. That's golf every 4.92 days, compared to Obama's pace of once every 8.77 days.

If Trump maintains this pace, he'll play golf roughly 296 times over four years, costing taxpayers approximately $272 million. That's $68 million per year, or $1.3 million per week, just for presidential golf.

Chapter 2: The Obama Comparison - Hypocrisy on Steroids

Before becoming president, Trump relentlessly attacked Obama for golfing, posting dozens of tweets criticizing the cost and frequency of presidential golf. The hypocrisy is now off the charts.

Trump's Anti-Golf Twitter Archive

Here are some choice tweets from Trump's pre-presidency days:

"I say we cannot continue to let Obama fly around on Air Force 1, at a cost of millions of dollars a day, for the purpose of politics & play!" - July 25, 2014

"While our wonderful president was out playing golf all day, the TSA is falling apart, just like our government! Airports a total disaster!" - May 2016

"Everything's executive order because he doesn't have enough time because he's playing so much golf. He doesn't have enough time to convince Congress to do it. This guy plays more golf than people on the PGA Tour." - October 2016

The Reality Check

Obama's eight-year totals:

  • 333 rounds of golf over 8 years

  • $96.9 million in total travel costs (all travel, not just golf)

  • Usually played at military bases near D.C. for security and cost reasons

Trump's record:

  • Playing golf every 4.92 days vs. Obama's 8.77 days

  • $151.5 million on golf trips alone in his first term

  • Exclusively plays at Trump-owned properties, generating revenue for his businesses

Trump spent more on golf in four years than Obama spent on all travel in eight years, while putting money directly into his own pocket.

The Promises vs. Reality

During the 2016 campaign, Trump made specific promises about his golf plans:

"I would rarely leave the White House because there's so much work to be done. I would not be a president who took vacations. I would not be a president that takes time off."

"I'm going to be working for you. I'm not going to have time to go play golf."

When reporters asked about golfing during his first trip to Florida as president, Trump replied: "No. I don't think so. I'm busy." Two days later, he was photographed on the golf course.

It took just two weeks for President Trump to break his promise and head to the golf course.

Chapter 3: The Double-Dip Scheme

Here's how Trump's golf grift works as a wealth extraction mechanism:

Step 1: Taxpayers Pay for Transportation

Every Mar-a-Lago trip costs $3.4 million in federal transportation and security costs. Air Force One alone costs nearly $200,000 per hour to operate, while military cargo planes transport the presidential limousines and other equipment.

Step 2: Government Pays Trump Properties

Once at Mar-a-Lago, the federal government pays Trump's properties for:

  • Secret Service accommodation

  • Staff meals and lodging

  • Meeting rooms and facilities

  • Golf cart rentals

  • Communications equipment setup

The Washington Post reported that the government had been billed about $2.5 million by Trump properties during his first term alone. That money goes directly into Trump Organization coffers.

Step 3: Invaluable Marketing Value

Every presidential visit provides massive free advertising for Trump properties. Photos of Air Force One at Palm Beach International Airport, news coverage of the "Winter White House," and international attention all promote the Trump brand globally.

The marketing value of having the President of the United States regularly visit your resort is literally priceless. No amount of paid advertising could generate the exposure Trump properties receive from presidential visits.

Chapter 4: The Security Theater Costs

The logistical requirements for protecting Trump at Mar-a-Lago are mind-boggling and expensive:

Maritime Security

Because Mar-a-Lago sits on a barrier island, every golf trip requires:

  • Police boats with machine guns patrolling the Intracoastal Waterway

  • Coast Guard vessels stationed in the Atlantic Ocean

  • Complete waterway closure disrupting local boat traffic

  • Underwater security sweeps before each visit

Aviation Security

  • Complete airspace restrictions around Palm Beach International Airport

  • Fighter jet patrols during presidential visits

  • Helicopter security perimeter over the resort

  • Coordination with multiple air traffic control centers

Ground Security

  • Explosive-sniffing dogs sweeping the entire property

  • Secret Service advance teams arriving days before Trump

  • Local law enforcement overtime costing millions annually

  • Road closures disrupting local traffic

The Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office requested $45 million just to cover security expenses through November 2025. By 2018, they had already spent $3.3 million in overtime costs reimbursed by the federal government.

Chapter 5: The Property Revenue Stream

Trump doesn't just benefit from free advertising—his properties generate direct revenue from presidential visits:

Secret Service Payments

The Secret Service pays for:

  • Agent accommodations at Trump properties

  • Command center space rental

  • Communication equipment installation

  • Golf cart rentals for security details

  • Meeting room bookings for briefings

Staff and Press Accommodations

White House staff, reporters, and security personnel all require:

  • Hotel rooms at Trump properties

  • Meals at Trump restaurants

  • Transportation arranged through Trump vendors

  • Event space for official functions

The $60,000 Mar-a-Lago Tab

The GAO found that $60,000 was paid directly to Mar-a-Lago for just four early trips, mostly for Secret Service rooms. That figure doesn't include the broader economic impact or the value of services provided by other Trump vendors.

Over hundreds of visits, these direct payments add up to millions flowing from taxpayers to Trump businesses.

Chapter 6: Breaking Records in All the Wrong Ways

Trump's golf obsession has shattered every precedent for presidential travel:

Visit Frequency

During his first term, Trump visited Trump Organization properties on 428 days of his presidency—one visit every 3.4 days. That's nearly 30% of his time in office spent at his own businesses.

Family Travel Explosion

The Trump family took 3,249 Secret Service-protected trips in just two fiscal years, compared to 933 trips for the Obama family over seven years. Much of this travel was to promote Trump Organization businesses worldwide.

Mar-a-Lago: The "Winter White House"

Trump rebranded Mar-a-Lago as the "Winter White House," hosting foreign leaders and conducting official business at his private club. This created the unprecedented situation where:

  • Foreign governments pay Trump to stay at his property during official visits

  • Diplomatic negotiations occur at his private business

  • Club members witness sensitive government discussions

  • National security briefings happen in public dining rooms

Chapter 7: The Opportunity Cost

The money Trump spends on golf could fund critical government services:

$272 million over four years could pay for:

  • Medicaid coverage for 47,500 recipients annually

  • 2,500 federal workers' salaries for an entire year

  • 280 teachers' salaries in underserved communities

  • 50,000 children's school lunches every day for four years

Instead, it goes into Trump's bank account every weekend while he plays golf.

As Rep. Jasmine Crockett pointed out: "If we are gonna talk about efficiency… let's talk about the fact that, as of March 30, Trump's golfing has cost us approximately $26 million. The last time I checked, we're not getting anything in return for that."

Chapter 8: The International Embarrassment

Trump's golf obsession has created diplomatic incidents and international embarrassment:

Missing Military Honors

Trump skipped the dignified transfer of four U.S. Army soldiers killed in Lithuania because he was playing golf at Mar-a-Lago. Military families watched their president choose golf over honoring fallen soldiers.

Foreign Leader Golf Diplomacy

Trump regularly hosts foreign leaders at his golf courses, creating situations where:

  • Diplomatic discussions occur at Trump businesses

  • Foreign governments pay Trump properties for accommodations

  • National security information is discussed in unsecured locations

  • Club members overhear sensitive conversations

Scotland Vandalism Response

When pro-Palestinian activists vandalized Trump's Turnberry golf course in Scotland, criticizing his "genocidal rhetoric" on Gaza, Trump's response was to go golfing at Mar-a-Lago the same day.

Chapter 9: The False Justifications

Trump and his defenders have offered various justifications for the excessive golf spending:

"Working Vacations"

The White House claims Trump conducts business during golf trips, signing executive orders and holding meetings. But every president works during travel—that doesn't justify the massive costs and conflicts of interest.

"Security Requirements"

Defenders argue that presidential security is expensive regardless of location. But Camp David is specifically designed for presidential use and costs a fraction of Mar-a-Lago trips.

"Personal Residence"

Trump claims Mar-a-Lago as his legal residence, suggesting he has a right to go home. But no previous president has claimed a commercial resort as their residence or charged the government for visits.

Chapter 10: The Systematic Nature

Trump's golf grift isn't accidental—it's systematic wealth extraction:

Pattern Recognition

  • Every weekend possible: Trump maintains a near-constant schedule of golf trips

  • Exclusive Trump properties: He only plays at courses he owns and profits from

  • Maximum extraction: Each trip generates maximum revenue for Trump businesses

  • Zero alternative consideration: Never considers cost-saving alternatives like Camp David

The Infrastructure Investment

Trump has invested millions in upgrading his golf courses specifically to accommodate presidential visits:

  • Enhanced security features at Mar-a-Lago

  • Communication equipment installations

  • Expanded Secret Service facilities at multiple properties

  • Helicopter landing areas at golf courses

These investments turn his properties into presidential-ready facilities that can command premium rates for government use.

The Ultimate Grift

Trump's golf trips represent the perfect embodiment of his family's three-generation wealth extraction template:

  • Access other people's money (taxpayers fund the trips)

  • Extract maximum value (properties receive payments and priceless advertising)

  • Limit personal exposure (government bears all costs and risks)

  • Let others absorb losses (taxpayers pay while Trump profits)

It's the grift hiding in plain sight, happening every single weekend with the blessing of Air Force One and the protection of the Secret Service.

While Trump golfs, taxpayers foot the bill for the world's most expensive advertising campaign. It's not presidential recreation—it's systematic wealth extraction with a 9-iron.

The bankruptcy king found the ultimate licensing deal: leasing the presidency to promote his golf courses, one taxpayer-funded weekend at a time.
