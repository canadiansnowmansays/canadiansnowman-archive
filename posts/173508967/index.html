The Hypocrisy of Donald Trump and His Administration

The Hypocrisy of Donald Trump and His Administration

Part 3: Institutionalizing Christian Nationalism

September 13, 2025

"If I get in, you're going to be using that power at a level that you've never used before. I really believe it's the biggest thing missing from this country, the biggest thing missing. We have to bring back our religion. We have to bring back Christianity in this country." — Donald Trump to National Religious Broadcasters

Trump didn't just make promises to Christian nationalist leaders—he delivered. Since taking office, the administration has systematically weaponized federal agencies to establish religious favoritism, abandon church-state separation, and institutionalize a corrupted version of Christianity that serves political rather than spiritual ends.

This isn't religious freedom. It's the establishment of Christian nationalism through the machinery of government, creating exactly the religious test for public office that the Constitution explicitly prohibits.

The Task Force to Eradicate Anti-Christian Bias: Weaponizing Justice

In February 2025, Trump signed an Executive Order establishing a "Task Force to Eradicate Anti-Christian Bias" within the Department of Justice. The order claimed to address the "egregious pattern of targeting peaceful Christians" by the previous administration.

But examine the specifics: the task force was created specifically to investigate prosecutions of "nearly two dozen peaceful pro-life Christians for praying and demonstrating outside abortion facilities." These weren't cases of religious persecution—they were lawful prosecutions of individuals who violated federal laws protecting access to healthcare facilities.

The creation of this task force sends a clear message: if you claim Christian motivation for lawbreaking, this administration will treat your prosecution as religious persecution. This isn't protecting religious freedom—it's creating religious exemptions from the rule of law.

The Religious Liberty Commission: Institutionalizing Favoritism

In May 2025, Trump established the "Religious Liberty Commission," ostensibly to "restore and safeguard religious liberty for all Americans." But the commission's actual function reveals its true purpose: not protecting religious freedom broadly, but advancing a specific Christian nationalist agenda.

The commission doesn't defend religious liberty for Muslims facing discrimination, Jews confronting antisemitism, or Buddhists encountering prejudice. It exists to advance conservative Christian positions on issues like LGBTQ+ rights, reproductive healthcare, and education policy.

Real religious liberty protection would be religiously neutral. This commission is anything but—it's a government agency dedicated to advancing one particular interpretation of one particular faith tradition.

The White House Faith Office: Forgetting Separation

Perhaps most revealing is Trump's instruction to his White House Faith Office to "forget about" the separation of church and state. This wasn't a casual comment—it was a direct order to abandon one of the fundamental principles of American democracy.

The Faith Office, led by prosperity gospel figures like Paula White, doesn't represent diverse faith traditions. It promotes a narrow theological interpretation that directly contradicts mainstream Christian teaching. When government agencies promote specific theological positions, they violate the First Amendment's prohibition on establishing religion.

The office's influence extends far beyond ceremonial functions. It shapes policy decisions, influences appointments, and creates a religious litmus test for access to government power.

Cabinet Secretaries as Preachers: When Policy Becomes Sermon

The June 2025 Faith and Freedom Coalition conference revealed how thoroughly Christian nationalism has penetrated the administration. Multiple Cabinet secretaries didn't just attend—they preached.

Veterans Affairs Secretary Doug Collins defended controversial staffing cuts at his agency while calling his job a "spiritual calling." He dismissed gender-affirming care for transgender veterans as a "social experiment" in the same breath that he claimed "taking care of our veterans" was his only concern.

This is government by theology: policy decisions justified not by evidence or constitutional principles, but by religious doctrine.

Housing and Urban Development Secretary Scott Turner defended cuts to his agency and new work requirements for benefits during what he explicitly called a "sermon." He told the audience he wants his "team" to promote "faith in God and hard work."

When Cabinet secretaries preach policy and justify cuts to social programs through religious language, they transform governance into ministry. This violates both the Constitution and the basic principle that government should serve all citizens, regardless of their religious beliefs.

Defense Secretary Hegseth: Militarizing Christianity

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth's Pentagon prayer services represent perhaps the most dangerous merger of Christian nationalism with government power. Having his pastor declare Trump "sovereignly appointed" by God doesn't just violate church-state separation—it transforms military leadership into religious authority.

When defense officials claim divine appointment, they place military decisions beyond democratic accountability. If God has chosen your commander-in-chief, then questioning military strategy becomes questioning divine will.

This transformation of the Pentagon into a prayer meeting represents the militarization of Christian nationalism and the Christianization of military power—a combination that has historically led to holy wars and religious persecution.

Project 2025: The Blueprint Implemented

During the campaign, Trump repeatedly denied knowledge of Project 2025, the Heritage Foundation's comprehensive plan for Christian nationalist governance. "I know nothing about Project 2025," he claimed. "I have no idea who is behind it."

Yet since taking office, Trump has implemented dozens of Project 2025 recommendations through executive orders and agency directives. The plan explicitly calls for:

  • Dismantling the administrative state to remove secular expertise from government

  • Installing ideologically vetted candidates based on religious and political loyalty

  • Eliminating the Department of Education to remove secular oversight of schools

  • Restructuring Health and Human Services to prioritize "biblical principles" over medical science

  • Establishing government "imbued with biblical principles" rather than constitutional ones

The systematic implementation of these recommendations reveals that Trump's campaign denials were themselves violations of the Ninth Commandment against bearing false witness.

The Charlie Kirk Test: When Performance Christianity Meets Real Tragedy

The administration's response to Charlie Kirk's assassination on September 10, 2025, revealed the systematic establishment of Christian nationalism in its starkest form. When faced with the murder of a political ally, Trump abandoned all pretense of Christian leadership—no calls for prayer, no appeals to forgiveness, no spiritual guidance for a grieving nation.

Instead, within six hours, Trump delivered a divisive Oval Office address focused entirely on political retaliation. The contrast with the administration's elaborate prayer performances for other tragedies couldn't be more revealing: when children die in schools, we get staged prayer circles; when conservative activists are murdered, we get calls for political warfare.

This selective deployment of religious imagery exposes the administration's true relationship with faith: Christianity as political tool rather than spiritual guide, deployed when convenient and abandoned when revenge seems more profitable.

The Purge of Secular Expertise

One of Project 2025's central goals is replacing secular expertise with religious ideology throughout the federal government. This isn't just about policy preferences—it's about fundamentally changing how government operates.

At the Department of Health and Human Services: The administration has systematically removed scientists and medical professionals who base recommendations on evidence rather than religious doctrine. Vaccine science, reproductive health research, and LGBTQ+ medical care have all been subordinated to religious ideology.

At the Department of Education: The push to eliminate the department entirely represents the Christian nationalist goal of removing secular oversight from education. School voucher programs don't just provide "choice"—they redirect public funds to religious institutions that can discriminate based on faith.

At the Environmental Protection Agency: Climate science has been subordinated to prosperity gospel theology that prioritizes short-term corporate profits over long-term environmental stewardship. When the Genesis mandate to care for creation conflicts with corporate interests, the administration chooses Mammon over ministry.

The Congressional Prayer Spectacle: Blessing Harm in God's Name

Perhaps no image better captures the administration's religious hypocrisy than recent photographs of Speaker Mike Johnson and the MAGA caucus gathered in prayer over legislation that actively harms the vulnerable.

These scenes—dozens of politicians laying hands on bills that cut food assistance for hungry children, strip healthcare from millions, and accelerate environmental destruction—represent a profound perversion of Christian prayer.

The social media response to these images posed the essential question: "What god were they praying to?" This isn't rhetorical flourish but theological accuracy. The God of Christianity explicitly commands believers to feed the hungry (Matthew 25:35), heal the sick (Matthew 25:36), and care for creation (Genesis 2:15).

When politicians invoke this same God while blessing legislation that does the opposite, they reveal their true allegiance. This prayer gathering represents religious theater at its most obscene—the ritualistic blessing of policies that Jesus would have explicitly condemned.

The Corruption of Religious Language

The administration has systematically corrupted religious language to serve political ends. Terms like "religious liberty," "faith-based," and "biblical principles" have been weaponized to justify discrimination, inequality, and cruelty.

"Religious Liberty" now means the right of conservative Christians to discriminate against others, not the protection of all faiths from government interference.

"Faith-Based" now means channeling public funds to conservative Christian organizations that can exclude those who don't share their beliefs.

"Biblical Principles" now means whatever policy positions the administration wants to implement, regardless of their relationship to actual biblical teaching.

This corruption of religious language damages both faith and democracy by making it impossible to distinguish between authentic religious conviction and political manipulation.

The International Implications

The administration's Christian nationalism hasn't gone unnoticed internationally. When American leaders claim divine appointment while implementing policies that violate basic human rights, they undermine America's moral authority worldwide.

European allies watch with concern as America abandons the secular governance principles that made it a beacon of religious freedom. When American officials claim God's blessing for policies that European Christian leaders condemn, it damages transatlantic relationships.

Middle Eastern partners question America's commitment to religious tolerance when American leaders explicitly promote Christianity over other faiths. This complicates diplomatic relationships and undermines efforts to promote religious freedom globally.

Global Christian leaders increasingly distance themselves from American Christian nationalism, recognizing that it corrupts the Gospel message and damages Christianity's witness worldwide.

The Constitutional Crisis

The administration's systematic establishment of Christian nationalism creates a constitutional crisis that extends beyond normal political disagreements. When government agencies explicitly promote specific religious doctrines, they violate the First Amendment's prohibition on establishing religion.

The Establishment Clause forbids government from establishing religion, but the administration has created government offices specifically designed to advance conservative Christian positions.

The Free Exercise Clause protects religious practice, but the administration has used it to justify religious exemptions from generally applicable laws.

The Equal Protection Clause requires equal treatment regardless of religion, but the administration has created explicit preferences for conservative Christians.

This isn't about legitimate policy disagreements—it's about fundamental violations of constitutional principles that have protected religious freedom for over two centuries.

The Damage to Authentic Christianity

Real Christian leaders across denominations have condemned the administration's corruption of their faith for political ends. The systematic weaponization of Christianity damages the Church's moral authority and spiritual mission.

Catholic leaders have criticized immigration policies that violate Catholic social teaching about welcoming strangers.

Mainline Protestant leaders have condemned environmental policies that violate biblical mandates for creation care.

Evangelical leaders increasingly recognize that Christian nationalism corrupts the Gospel message and damages Christian witness.

When government claims divine authority while violating divine commandments, it forces authentic believers to choose between their faith and their government—a choice that the First Amendment was designed to prevent.

The End Game: Theocracy Through Democracy

The administration's systematic institutionalization of Christian nationalism reveals its ultimate goal: establishing a theocracy through democratic institutions. By capturing government agencies, corrupting religious language, and claiming divine authority, they seek to make Christian nationalism the unofficial state religion.

This isn't hyperbole—it's the logical conclusion of their stated goals. When Heritage Foundation president Kevin Roberts declares "We are in the process of the second American Revolution," he's describing the transformation of secular democracy into religious authoritarianism.

When Trump tells religious broadcasters they'll have power "at a level you've never used before," he's promising to subordinate democratic governance to religious authority.

When Cabinet secretaries preach policy and Pentagon officials claim divine appointment, they're implementing theocracy through bureaucracy.

The Resistance from Within

Not all government employees have accepted the administration's Christian nationalist transformation. Career civil servants, military officers, and agency professionals have resisted efforts to subordinate expertise to ideology.

But the administration's response has been systematic purges disguised as "efficiency" measures. The real goal isn't efficiency—it's ideological purity. They don't want government employees who serve the Constitution; they want government employees who serve their interpretation of Christianity.

This purge of secular expertise and replacement with religious ideology represents the methodical destruction of professional governance in favor of amateur theocracy.

What's at Stake

The institutionalization of Christian nationalism threatens both democracy and authentic Christianity. When government claims divine authority, it corrupts both faith and politics.

For Democracy: Religious tests for public office, government promotion of specific theological positions, and claims of divine political authority all violate constitutional principles that have protected American freedom for centuries.

For Christianity: The weaponization of faith for political power damages the Church's moral authority, corrupts the Gospel message, and drives people away from authentic spiritual seeking.

For Religious Freedom: The establishment of Christian nationalism as unofficial state religion threatens the religious liberty of all Americans, including Christians who reject this corrupted version of their faith.

The Choice Before Us

Americans face a fundamental choice: accept the institutionalization of Christian nationalism or defend the constitutional principles that protect religious freedom for all.

This isn't about hostility to Christianity—it's about protecting authentic Christianity from political corruption. Real religious freedom requires government neutrality on matters of faith, allowing all traditions to flourish without official favoritism or hostility.

The administration's systematic establishment of Christian nationalism forces this choice upon us. We can allow them to continue corrupting both faith and democracy, or we can insist that government serve all Americans regardless of their religious beliefs.

The institutionalization is already well advanced. The question now is whether Americans will recognize the threat and act to preserve both constitutional democracy and authentic religious freedom before it's too late.

The founders understood that religious freedom requires keeping faith and government in their proper spheres. The current administration has deliberately collapsed those boundaries, creating exactly the religious establishment that the First Amendment was designed to prevent.

History will judge whether Americans in this moment chose to defend the principles that made religious freedom possible, or whether they allowed those principles to be sacrificed on the altar of political power wrapped in religious rhetoric.

The choice is ours. The time is now.
