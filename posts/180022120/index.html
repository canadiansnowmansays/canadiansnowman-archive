The Enemies List

From Courthouse to Court-Martial

When Reminding Troops to Follow the Law Becomes Sedition

November 26, 2025

Less than 24 hours after the Pentagon announced its investigation, Mark Kelly appeared on Jimmy Kimmel—because when a war hero faces court-martial for protected speech, even late-night TV can’t look away.

Monday, federal judges threw out two political prosecutions because Trump’s handpicked prosecutor was illegally appointed.

That same day, the Pentagon announced it’s investigating a sitting United States senator for possible court-martial.

The alleged crime? Mark Kelly—combat pilot, astronaut, husband of Gabby Giffords—appeared in a video reminding service members they can refuse illegal orders.

Yes, you read that correctly. A war hero who flew 39 combat missions in Desert Storm is now facing military prosecution for stating what every boot camp recruit learns on day one: you must refuse unlawful orders.

When the Justice Department stumbles, the Pentagon steps in. The enemies list has found a new home.

The Timeline That Tells Everything

November 18, 2025, 10:47 AM: Six Democratic lawmakers—all military or intelligence veterans—post a 90-second video on X. “Our laws are clear,” they state. “You can refuse illegal orders.” They remind service members their oath is to the Constitution, not to any individual.

November 20, 2025, 7:23 PM: Trump posts on Truth Social: “These six radical left Democrats just committed SEDITION—encouraging a military coup against their Commander-in-Chief! In the old days that was punishable by death. Mark Kelly and his band of insurrectionists should be investigated immediately!”

November 24, 2025, 11:35 AM: The Pentagon releases a statement: “The Department has received serious allegations of misconduct against Captain Mark Kelly, USN (Ret.).” They cite 10 U.S.C. § 688 and threaten “recall to active duty for court-martial proceedings.”

November 24, 2025, 2:14 PM: Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth posts on X: “The video made by the ‘Seditious Six’ was despicable, reckless, and false... However, Mark Kelly (retired Navy Commander) is still subject to UCMJ—and he knows that.”

November 24, 2025, 4:27 PM: Kelly responds: “Secretary Hegseth’s tweet is the first I heard of this. I also saw the President’s posts saying I should be arrested, hanged, and put to death.”

Six days. From video to investigation. And notice who’s being investigated: not all six lawmakers. Just the one they can still court-martial.

The Video That Started It All

Let’s be clear about what triggered this investigation. Here’s the complete transcript of what six combat veterans told the troops:

“We want to speak directly to members of the military and intelligence community. Like us, you all swore an oath to protect and defend this Constitution. Right now, the threats to our Constitution aren’t just coming from abroad but right here at home. This administration is pitting our uniformed military and intelligence community professionals against American citizens. Our laws are clear: You can refuse illegal orders. The trust that citizens have in you is at risk. Don’t give up the ship.”

That’s it. That’s the “seditious” content. Ninety seconds reminding service members of their legal obligations under the Uniform Code of Military Justice.

No call for mutiny. No encouragement to disobey lawful orders. Just a reminder that illegal orders exist and must be refused—a principle established at Nuremberg and codified in the UCMJ itself.

Why Only Mark Kelly?

The video featured six lawmakers:

  • Sen. Mark Kelly (D-AZ) – Retired Navy captain (O-6), combat pilot, astronaut

  • Sen. Jack Reed (D-RI) – Former Army officer (O-4)

  • Sen. Tammy Duckworth (D-IL) – Retired Army lieutenant colonel (O-5)

  • Rep. Jason Crow (D-CO) – Former Army Ranger (enlisted)

  • Rep. Mikie Sherrill (D-NJ) – Former Navy pilot (O-4)

  • Rep. Chrissy Houlahan (D-PA) – Former Air Force officer (O-3)

As Hegseth gleefully noted: “Five of the six individuals in that video do not fall under @DeptofWar jurisdiction.”

Only Kelly retired at a rank (Navy captain, O-6) and under regulations (having served 20+ years) that make him subject to recall under 10 U.S.C. § 688. The others either separated before retirement eligibility or retired at lower ranks not subject to recall authority.

In other words: the Pentagon went shopping for the one person they could prosecute and found Mark Kelly.

The “Charges” They’re Floating

No formal charges have been filed, but NBC News and Task & Purpose report the likely articles:

Article 133, UCMJ – Conduct unbecoming an officer and gentleman Maximum punishment: Dismissal, forfeiture of all pay and allowances, confinement for 1 year

Article 134, UCMJ – Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline Maximum punishment: Bad-conduct discharge, forfeiture of all pay and allowances, confinement for 6 months

18 U.S.C. § 2387 – Activities affecting armed forces during war Maximum punishment: 20 years imprisonment (though we’re not at war)

Legal experts across the spectrum are calling this absurd. Kevin Carroll, a retired Army JAG colonel who served in Trump’s first administration, told NPR the only comparable case was Billy Mitchell in 1925—and Mitchell was active duty, publicly attacking his superiors in newspapers.

Greg Lukianoff, CEO of the Foundation for Individual Rights and Expression, was blunt: “The Pentagon’s actions are clear retaliation for something Sen. Kelly is entirely within his rights to say.”

The Context They Don’t Want You to See

The lawmakers’ video didn’t appear in a vacuum. Since September, questions have swirled about the legality of several military operations:

Operation Caribbean Shield: U.S. Navy vessels have conducted strikes on suspected drug boats in international waters, sinking at least 17 vessels. Colombia and Panama have filed protests with the UN.

Domestic Deployments: Federal judges in California and Texas have issued restraining orders blocking National Guard deployments that appear to violate the Posse Comitatus Act.

The JAG Purge: When military lawyers raised concerns about Rules of Engagement, Hegseth fired or reassigned nine senior Judge Advocates in one week.

Service members were asking their commanders: “What do we do if we get an order that the lawyers say is illegal?”

The video was answering that question with established law. Now answering that question can get you court-martialed.

The Disparity Is the Point

Let’s review what gets you prosecuted in Trump’s America:

Won’t Get You Prosecuted:

  • Storing classified nuclear documents in your bathroom (Trump)

  • Showing military plans to unauthorized people at your golf club (Trump)

  • Directing staff to delete security footage of moving classified documents (Trump)

  • Refusing to return classified materials when subpoenaed (Trump)

Will Get You Prosecuted:

  • Allegedly lying about a conversation from five years ago (Comey)

  • Paying off a mortgage early 20 years ago (James)

  • Sharing your diary with your wife and daughter (Bolton)

  • Reminding troops they can refuse illegal orders (Kelly)

See the pattern?

The Political Hit Job

Kelly isn’t a random target. Consider what recalling him to active duty accomplishes:

  1. He loses his Senate seat immediately - Federal law prohibits simultaneous service as a senator and active military member

  2. Arizona’s Republican governor appoints his replacement - Flipping a crucial Senate seat

  3. He’s gagged under Article 88, UCMJ - No more criticizing the commander-in-chief

  4. His 2028 presidential prospects die - Hard to campaign while facing court-martial

  5. Every veteran in public life gets the message - Cross Trump, lose your civilian status

As Paul Rieckhoff of Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America told CNN: “This is crossing the Rubicon. The idea you can drive him back onto active duty and threaten a court-martial... is breaking glass that has never been broken before in the modern military.”

Historical Parallel: When Mitchell Dared to Speak

The Pentagon keeps citing the 1925 court-martial of Billy Mitchell as precedent. Let’s examine that:

Mitchell was an active-duty Army colonel who gave newspaper interviews accusing military leadership of “incompetency, criminal negligence, and almost treasonable administration of national defense.” He was court-martialed, convicted, and suspended from duty.

History vindicated Mitchell—his advocacy for air power proved correct in World War II. But here’s the key difference: Mitchell was active duty, publicly insubordinate, and attacking his chain of command in newspapers for months.

Kelly is a retired senator who spent 90 seconds on video reminding troops of their existing legal obligations.

If that’s equivalent, then words have lost all meaning.

The Constitutional Crisis No One’s Naming

Here’s what should terrify you: The President of the United States is using the military justice system to remove a sitting senator.

Think about that. The executive branch is using its control over military prosecutions to potentially remove a member of the legislative branch.

This isn’t just an attack on Mark Kelly. It’s an attack on the separation of powers itself.

If they can recall a senator to active duty and prosecute him for speech made as a civilian legislator, then congressional oversight of the military is dead. Every veteran in Congress now serves at the pleasure of the commander-in-chief.

What Happens Next

The Pentagon has initiated what they call a “thorough review.” Here’s the process:

  1. Investigation (30-90 days): Military investigators will review the video and Trump’s orders

  2. Article 32 Hearing: If charges are preferred, a preliminary hearing like a grand jury

  3. Referral Decision: A convening authority (likely Hegseth himself) decides whether to court-martial

  4. Trial: If referred, Kelly would be tried by military judges and a panel of officers

Legal experts believe any charges would be dismissed at the Article 32 hearing. Steve Vladeck, a military law expert at Georgetown, notes that while three appellate courts have upheld recall authority, “they’ve never upheld it for pure speech, let alone speech made as a sitting member of Congress.”

But again: the process is the punishment.

The Message Being Sent

Every veteran watching understands what’s happening. The administration is establishing new rules:

Old Rule: Your military service ends when you retire. You become a civilian with full First Amendment rights.

New Rule: Your military status follows you forever. Criticize the president, and we’ll reactivate you and prosecute you under military law where he’s the ultimate authority.

Old Rule: Veterans in Congress provide crucial oversight of the military.

New Rule: Veteran legislators who question military operations can be recalled and silenced.

Old Rule: Reminding troops to follow the law is patriotic.

New Rule: Reminding troops to follow the law is sedition.

The Escalation Pattern

Look at the progression over nine weeks:

Week 1: Prosecute a former FBI director for testimony Week 3: Prosecute a state attorney general for a mortgage Week 6: Prosecute a former NSA for diary entries Week 9: Court-martial a sitting senator for protected speech

Each step normalizes the next. Each prosecution makes the subsequent one seem less shocking.

By Week 12, will they be court-martialing retired generals who sign open letters? By Week 15, will they recall any veteran who attends a protest?

The slope isn’t slippery. It’s a cliff.

Conclusion: The Rubicon Has Been Crossed

Nine weeks ago, I began documenting the construction of an enemies list. Critics said I was alarmist. They said our institutions would hold.

Yesterday, two judges proved that some institutions still function. They threw out prosecutions brought by an illegally appointed prosecutor.

Today, we learned that when one institution pushes back, the administration simply routes persecution through another. When civilian courts assert independence, military courts—where the president is the ultimate authority—become the new venue.

Mark Kelly flew combat missions over Iraq when Trump was getting his fifth draft deferment. Kelly went to space four times serving America while Trump was hosting reality TV shows. Kelly’s wife took a bullet to the head while serving her constituents.

Now Trump wants Kelly court-martialed for reminding troops to follow the law.

If they can come for Mark Kelly—war hero, astronaut, senator—they can come for anyone who ever wore the uniform.

The enemies list has metastasized from DOJ to DoD. From civilian courts to military tribunals. From prosecuting lies to prosecuting truth.

Unless we stop this now, there will be a Week 10, and a Week 20, and a Week 50.

And by then, it will be too late to remember that we once lived in a country where veterans could speak freely after their service ended.

Where senators couldn’t be removed by military prosecution.

Where reminding troops to follow the law wasn’t sedition.

That country is dying. Whether it survives depends on what we do next.
