Bette Davis Eyes

A CSIS Intelligence Assessment of the American FBI Director

April 26, 2026

Subject: Bette Davis. The Letter, 1940. Director: William Wyler. Status of gentleman on the ground: assessed as no longer a concern.

A Sunday Satire from the desk of Stephen Gordon for CanadianSnowmanSays

🎵 Bette Davis Eyes — Kim Carnes (1981)

CANADIAN SECURITY INTELLIGENCE SERVICE DIRECTORATE OF INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENTS CLASSIFICATION: CONFIDENTIAL — CANADIAN (WIDE) EYES ONLY SUBJECT: Assessment of Current Leadership, United States Federal Bureau of Investigation PREPARED FOR: Director, CSIS; National Security and Intelligence Advisor to the Prime Minister; Five Eyes Liaison Desk DATE: April 2026 DISTRIBUTION: Limited

1.0 PURPOSE This assessment provides an evaluation of the current leadership posture of the United States Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) under Director Kashyap “Kash” Patel¹, with particular attention to implications for Canadian intelligence cooperation, Five Eyes operational security, and shared counterterrorism interests in the context of the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict. This assessment draws exclusively on open-source reporting, which — in a development that is itself analytically significant — has been more than sufficient.

¹ Subject is known professionally as “Kash,” a contraction of his given name Kashyap. Analysts have observed, with no editorial comment whatsoever, that Subject appears to be less about the Kash and considerably more about the yap. It is further noted, again without editorial comment, that he appears to have shortened his first name with the wrong syllable.

2.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY The FBI director presents an unprecedented constellation of institutional risk factors. Subject has demonstrated patterns of personal incapacitation, impulsive public communications, misuse of government resources, and aggressive internal surveillance of his own workforce. His tenure has been marked by the systematic removal of senior personnel with expertise in counterintelligence, counterterrorism, and allied liaison — functions of direct relevance to Canadian national security. Assessment: The FBI’s capacity to serve as a reliable Five Eyes partner has been materially degraded. Information-sharing protocols should be reviewed. The probability that Subject will remain in his current position through the 2026 U.S. midterm elections is assessed as LOW to MODERATE. Confidence Level: HIGH. The source base for this assessment is unusually robust, as approximately 45 current and former FBI employees have spoken to The New York Times alone, suggesting an institutional consensus regarding Subject’s fitness that transcends normal bureaucratic grumbling.

3.0 PERSONAL CONDUCT — RISK ASSESSMENT 3.1 Alcohol Consumption Subject is known to consume alcohol to the point of incapacitation at establishments in Washington, D.C. (Ned’s, a private club) and Las Vegas (the Poodle Room). On multiple occasions, Subject’s security detail has been unable to wake him due to apparent intoxication. On at least one occasion, Subject’s protective detail considered requesting breaching equipment — tactical entry tools normally reserved for hostage-rescue and counterterrorism operations — because Subject was unreachable behind locked doors. Analytical Note: The deployment of breaching equipment to reach a sleeping bureau director would represent, to our knowledge, a first in the history of the Five Eyes alliance. CSIS has consulted informally with counterpart services and can confirm that at no point in the post-war period has any allied intelligence chief required forcible entry by his own protection detail. 3.2 Impulsive Communications Subject has a pattern of releasing unverified investigative information on social media platform X (formerly Twitter). Notable incidents include the premature announcement of a suspect in the Charlie Kirk assassination (subject released, no charges), a “person of interest” in the Brown University shooting (released, no charges), and a Michigan terrorism case (announced before Department of Justice lawyers had filed charges, causing internal conflict). During at least one operational conference call, Subject and his former deputy director were observed scripting their social media strategy rather than discussing investigative coordination. Analytical Note: Subject has stated publicly that “the most important thing in any crisis is controlling the narrative.” This represents a fundamental misalignment with the evidentiary standards expected of law enforcement leadership in Five Eyes partner nations. 3.3 Travel and Resource Utilisation Subject has used government aircraft for trips to: a wrestling event in Pennsylvania, a hunting resort in Texas owned by a Republican political donor, a golf resort in Inverness (Scotland), and the Winter Olympics in Milan (Italy), where his official schedule showed one 20-minute security briefing and approximately four and a half hours of “personal time/cultural activities.” Subject has assigned a four-agent SWAT team to provide full-time protection to his romantic partner, a country music performer, in Nashville, Tennessee. This detail has escorted the partner to hair appointments, fund-raising events, senior centres, and a dinner at Windsor Castle. Analytical Note: The Nashville SWAT team is no longer performing its primary tactical function and must be replaced by other units for arrest operations. From a resource allocation perspective, this represents the conversion of a counterterrorism asset into a concierge service.

4.0 IMPLICATIONS FOR FIVE EYES COOPERATION 4.1 The London Position At the May 2025 Five Eyes conference in the United Kingdom, Subject personally assured MI5 Director Ken McCallum that an FBI liaison position in London — one engaged in sensitive work including embassy penetrations and technology — would be maintained. Subject reversed this commitment within two weeks. The position was removed. Assessment: Subject’s commitments at the allied leadership level cannot be treated as reliable. All future undertakings by the FBI director should be confirmed in writing through institutional channels before being acted upon by Canadian or allied services. 4.2 The Windsor Castle Photograph At the same conference, a group photograph was taken at Windsor Castle that included nondisclosed intelligence officials — individuals whose affiliation with allied intelligence services is not public. The photograph was distributed privately with explicit instructions that it not be shared. Subject attempted to post it on social media and was prevented from doing so only by the physical intervention of his staff. Assessment: This incident represents a near-breach of operational security at the highest level of the Five Eyes alliance. The fact that it was prevented by staff intervention rather than institutional safeguards suggests that the risk of inadvertent disclosure remains ongoing. Subject appears not to understand — or not to care — that the identities of nondisclosed allied intelligence personnel are protected for reasons that have to do with their physical safety, not with social media engagement metrics. 4.3 The Compromised Email Account In March 2026, hackers claiming affiliation with Iran’s Handala Team posted more than 300 emails from Subject’s personal Gmail account. The website hosting the materials was located on a Russian server. Indications suggest that additional compromising material is being withheld, possibly for future release or leverage. Assessment: A foreign adversary — one with which Subject’s country is currently at war — has demonstrated the capacity to compromise Subject’s personal digital communications. Combined with Subject’s documented patterns of personal vulnerability (see Section 3.1), this creates a counterintelligence risk profile that would be flagged in any standard allied vetting process. Recommendation: CSIS should conduct a review of information recently shared with the FBI through bilateral and Five Eyes channels to assess potential exposure. Pending the outcome of this review, sharing of operationally sensitive intelligence with the FBI director’s office should be routed through the Deputy Director (career) or designated institutional liaisons rather than through Subject directly.

5.0 INSTITUTIONAL ASSESSMENT 5.1 Personnel Subject has dismissed or forced out the FBI’s top six executives, the heads of field offices in Washington and Miami, the head of the intelligence directorate, members of counterintelligence squads devoted to Iran, the supervisory special agent and agents of the public corruption squad (CR-15), and numerous additional personnel — totalling, by open-source count, dozens of senior officials with cumulative centuries of experience. More than 20 percent of the FBI’s workforce has been reassigned to immigration enforcement, reducing capacity in counterterrorism, counterintelligence, cybersecurity, and public corruption. 5.2 The Polygraph Programme Subject has expanded the use of polygraph examinations to include questions about employees’ personal opinions of the director. Agents have been asked whether they have ever said anything negative about Subject or the president. Analytical Note: This represents the use of a physiological stress-measurement tool as a loyalty-enforcement mechanism. It is not counterintelligence. It is not internal affairs. It is, in the clinical sense, an authoritarian control behaviour with significant parallels to practices documented in the intelligence services of non-allied states, including the Russian FSB and the former Soviet KGB. 5.3 Morale Bureau morale is assessed as CRITICALLY LOW. Current and former employees have spoken to major news organisations in numbers that are, by the bureau’s historically secretive standards, unprecedented. One senior executive told reporters that he can “no longer recommend that people go work for the FBI.” Agents report spending time on LinkedIn rather than on investigations.

6.0 FORECAST Subject is aware that his position is precarious. He has told associates he intends to remain through the 2026 midterm elections while acknowledging the president could change his mind at any time. Senior administration officials are already discussing potential replacements. Subject’s survival to date is attributed primarily to his compliance with directives from Stephen Miller, the president’s top domestic policy adviser, and to the political cost of replacing him during an active military conflict. Probability of Subject’s removal before November 2026 midterms: MODERATE to HIGH. Probability of meaningful institutional recovery under a successor, should one be appointed: LOW in the near term. The expertise pipeline that Subject has destroyed cannot be reconstituted in fewer than five to ten years, assuming a successor with the institutional commitment to attempt it.

7.0 RECOMMENDATIONS 7.1 Downgrade direct intelligence sharing with the FBI director’s office to ROUTINE. Operationally sensitive material should be routed through institutional channels with career FBI personnel. 7.2 Increase bilateral liaison with FBI field offices that maintain experienced counterterrorism and counterintelligence personnel, bypassing headquarters where possible. 7.3 Review all material shared through Five Eyes channels since February 2025 for potential exposure, given Subject’s demonstrated pattern of impulsive social media posting and his compromised personal communications. 7.4 Maintain current cooperative posture with FBI career staff, who continue to operate professionally despite leadership dysfunction. The institutional FBI remains a critical partner. Its current director does not. 7.5 Prepare contingency briefing for the Prime Minister on implications of Subject’s removal, including potential disruption to ongoing joint operations during transition period.

8.0 SUPPLEMENTARY NOTES 8.1 The Challenge Coin Subject has created a personal challenge coin featuring a skull motif associated with the Punisher (a fictional Marvel Comics vigilante), the designation “K$H,” spider-like imagery in the eye sockets, flanking pistols, and a tommy gun on the reverse. Analytical Note: This item has no intelligence significance. It is noted here because it was the subject of an extended discussion at the Liaison Desk during which a staff member suggested it would “make a good bottle opener.” The item is assessed as representative of Subject’s broader approach to institutional leadership: performative, aesthetically adolescent, and fundamentally unserious. 8.2 The Yearbook Quote Subject’s high school yearbook quote, from the Jewish theologian Abraham Joshua Heschel, reads: “Racism is man’s gravest threat — the maximum of hatred for a minimum reason.” Analytical Note: No further analysis required. The quote speaks for itself. As does its distance from Subject’s current conduct.

This document is a work of satire. CSIS has not, to the author’s knowledge, produced such an assessment — though one suspects they might find it useful if they did. — CanadianSnowmanSays, Toronto
