The 60 Minutes Syndrome

When 18 Lies Walk Into a TV Studio: A Real-Time Chronicle of Nasal Catastrophe

November 4, 2025

Previously on The Pinocchio Presidency: Our hero’s nose had reached 47 feet from investment claims alone. Now, live from 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue...

Pre-Interview: The Makeup Chair Incident

4:47 PM, The White House, Washington D.C.

“We’re going to need more powder,” whispered the makeup artist, staring at the appendage that had required its own freight elevator.

The nose, already a monument to monetary mythology, seemed to sense what was coming. It quivered slightly, like a seismometer before an earthquake. The investment lies had built the infrastructure—now the 60 Minutes interview would add the decorations.

Donald sat in the reinforced chair (standard ones had proven inadequate), reviewing his talking points. “Grocery prices—down. Inflation—gone. Wars—ended. Simple stuff, really.”

The nose twitched. The makeup artist ducked.

Minute 1-5: The Grocery Gambit

5:03 PM - On Air

“Let’s start with the economy,” Norah O’Donnell began, maintaining professional eye contact despite the elephant—er, nose—in the room. “Are grocery prices down?”

“Way down,” Donald declared confidently. “Biggest drop in history, probably.”

The nose didn’t just grow—it blushed. A deep crimson spread from base to tip, the color of a grocery receipt showing 22% food inflation since 2020.

“Mr. President, grocery prices are actually up—”

“Down!” he insisted. “So far down they’re practically underground!”

The crimson deepened to burgundy. CBS’s color correction software crashed. The nose had developed its own heat signature, showing up on infrared cameras like a massive thermometer of untruth.

Dale Note #1: Grocery prices up 22% since 2020, currently rising 2.3% annually

Minute 6-10: The Inflation Inflammation

5:08 PM

“What about inflation overall?” O’Donnell pressed on, her notes trembling from the nose’s gravitational pull.

“No inflation! None! Less than 2%—actually, probably negative inflation. Deflation? That’s probably what we have.”

The nose developed texture. Not just any texture—scales, like a dragon hoarding mathematical impossibilities. Each scale seemed to represent a percentage point of actual inflation (3%), glinting under the studio lights.

“The Bureau of Labor Statistics shows inflation at 3%—”

“Their computers are from the Biden era,” Donald explained. “Probably still infected with socialist algorithms.”

A small puff of smoke emerged from the left nostril. The fire alarm system tensed.

Dale Note #2: Inflation at 3%, identical to when Trump took office

Minute 11-15: The AI Nodes

5:13 PM

“Let’s talk about your AI infrastructure initiative—”

“My idea!” Donald brightened. “Nobody thought of having AI facilities generate their own power. Nobody! I invented it, really.”

The nose sprouted nodes—small, blinking protrusions that looked suspiciously like tiny server racks. They hummed with the frequency of falsehood, creating a harmony that sounded like dial-up internet crossed with a cat in distress.

Backstage, a Google engineer whispered to a colleague: “We’ve been doing on-site generation since 2019.”

“Shh,” the colleague replied. “The nose might hear you.”

Dale Note #3: Tech companies started on-site power generation during Biden administration

Minute 16-20: The Seventeen Trillion Dollar Tumor

5:18 PM

“You’ve claimed $17 trillion in investments—”

“Twenty trillion now, actually. Maybe twenty-five by the time this airs.”

The nose achieved what physicians would later term “geometric impossibility.” It grew not just in length but in dimensions, creating a Klein bottle of fabrication that existed partially in our reality and partially in a universe where math worked differently.

CBS’s insurance adjusters began calculating premiums for “Acts of Nose.”

Dale Note #4: White House’s own inflated figure only $8.8 trillion

Minute 21-25: The Boat Float

5:23 PM

“These drug boats you mention—”

“Each one kills 25,000 Americans!”

The nose developed barnacles. Actual barnacles, as if it had spent years underwater in a sea of statistical impossibility. They whispered accusations in frequencies only fact-checkers could hear.

“Sir, that would mean just four boats account for all US overdose deaths—”

“Probably more than 25,000 per boat, actually. Could be 30,000. The boats are getting bigger.”

A small anchor materialized at the tip, dragging the conversation toward new depths.

Dale Note #5: Total US overdose deaths ~100,000 annually; math suggests impossibility

Minute 26-30: The Insurrection Eruption

5:28 PM

“You’ve invoked the Insurrection Act—”

“Other presidents used it 28 times! I’m very restrained, actually. Very legal, very cool.”

The nose developed what could only be described as historical hives—welts that spelled out “GRANT: 6” in raised letters. Historians watching at home reached for their emergency bourbon.

“The record is actually six times—”

“Fake history!”

The hives rearranged themselves to spell “STILL 6.” The nose had developed fact-checking capabilities, betraying its host.

Dale Note #6: Ulysses Grant holds record with six invocations

Minute 31-35: The War Sores

5:33 PM

“Let’s discuss the eight wars you claim to have ended—”

“Beautiful endings! Nobody ends wars like me. They’re so ended they’re double-ended. Triple-ended!”

Open sores appeared along the bridge—one for each ongoing conflict he claimed was over. The Gaza sore wept openly. The DRC lesion pulsed with distant gunfire. Two spots marked “not actually wars” simply looked confused.

Norah O’Donnell, professional to the end, continued: “But Gaza and the Democratic Republic of Congo—”

“So ended they’ve started being ended in the past tense!”

The sores formed a connect-the-dots puzzle that spelled “STILL HAPPENING.”

Dale Note #7: List includes non-wars and very much ongoing conflicts

Minute 36-40: The Timeline Tangle

5:38 PM

“Your lawsuit about the Harris interview—”

“Aired two days before the election! Blatant interference!”

The nose began to age backward at the tip while aging forward at the base, creating a temporal paradox that made the studio clocks run in circles. The October 7 actual air date fought with the November 3 claimed date in a wrestling match of chronology.

“It was October 7th, actually—”

“Time is fake news!”

Einstein’s ghost briefly materialized, shrugged, and departed.

Dale Note #8: Interview aired October 7, 2025, four weeks before election

Minute 41-45: The Ukraine Ulcer

5:43 PM

“About Ukraine aid—”

“Biden gave them $350 billion! Probably $400 billion! Could be half a trillion!”

A massive ulcer formed near the left nostril, oozing the actual figures: $94 billion disbursed, $93 billion more appropriated. The ulcer seemed to be doing long division, showing its work.

“Inspector General reports show—”

“Inspector Generals are optional! Like facts!”

Dale Note #9: Actual total $187 billion, much spent in US

Minute 46-50: The Tariff Tumors

5:48 PM

“Who pays your tariffs?”

“China! Other countries! They write big, beautiful checks!”

Tumors sprouted like mushrooms after rain, each one labeled with an American company name—the actual payers of import duties. They formed a ticker-tape parade of domestic corporations, scrolling their quarterly losses.

“Economists universally agree that importers pay—”

“Economists don’t understand business! I understand business! The nose understands business!”

He’d said it out loud. The nose, now sentient, nodded in agreement.

Dale Note #10: US importers pay tariffs, pass costs to consumers

Minute 51-55: The Inflation Infection (Part 2)

5:53 PM

“You inherited 3% inflation, the same as now—”

“Worst inflation in history! Probably universe history! Other planets called to sympathize!”

The nose developed a severe infection that caused it to flash different inflation rates throughout history: Germany 1923 (322%), Hungary 1946 (13,600,000,000,000,000%), Zimbabwe 2008 (79,600,000,000%). The current 3% looked like a rounding error.

“The actual peak was 9.1% under—”

“WORST! IN! HISTORY!”

The infection spread, creating a rash that spelled out “SAME AS NOW: 3%”

Dale Note #11: Inherited 3% inflation, identical to current rate

Minute 56-60: The Election Lesions

5:58 PM

“The 2020 election—”

“Rigged and stolen!”

The nose developed lesions shaped like swing states, each one containing a tiny courtroom where judges perpetually ruled against election fraud claims. The lesions performed a small theatrical production of democracy functioning normally.

“Every court found—”

“Rigged courts! Stolen judges! Fraudulent facts!”

The lesions took a bow. Tiny legal briefs fell like confetti.

Dale Note #12: No evidence of rigged election despite 60+ court cases

Minute 61-65: The Documents Dermatitis

6:03 PM

“The Presidential Records Act—”

“Says I can have documents! Beautiful documents! They’re mine!”

A severe rash spread across the nose spelling out the actual text of the Presidential Records Act in tiny, litigious font. Legal scholars would later need magnifying glasses to confirm it said exactly the opposite of his claims.

“The Act specifically states all records must be in government custody—”

“Optional custody! Like a suggestion! Very flexible law!”

Dale Note #13: Act mandates government custody immediately upon leaving office

Minute 66-70: The Comey Cyst

6:08 PM

“Did you instruct the DOJ to go after Comey?”

“Never! Would never! Probably the opposite!”

A cyst formed at the exact spot where his Truth Social post had explicitly pressured the Attorney General to do just that. The cyst had WiFi, broadcasting the post on repeat to anyone with a smartphone.

“Your social media post directly—”

“That was my evil twin! Digital deepfake! AI hallucination!”

The cyst grew a small screen displaying the post in real-time. It had better resolution than the studio monitors.

Dale Note #14: Public post explicitly pressured DOJ to investigate Comey

Minute 71-75: The Immigration Inflammation

6:13 PM

“Democrats want to give $1.5 trillion to illegal immigrants?”

The nose developed a severe inflammation that swelled in proportion to the exaggeration. It grew 10x its size, then 20x, accurately representing how he’d inflated the disputed figure.

“And 25 million came in—”

The inflammation developed its own immigration system, showing actual numbers trying to correct the record. Fact-checkers manned the bridge like border patrol.

Dale Note #15: Actual figure a fraction of $1.5T; migrant numbers under 12 million including expelled

Minute 76-80: The Prison Polyps

6:18 PM

“Foreign leaders are emptying their prisons—”

Polyps shaped like international prisons sprouted, each one displaying its actual population (spoiler: still full). The polyps performed a small documentary about how prisoner transfers actually work.

“There’s no evidence—”

“Evidence is optional! Like gravity! Or math!”

The polyps formed a choir, singing “We’re Still Here” in 17 languages.

Dale Note #16: No evidence for prison-emptying claim per experts and Trump’s own team

The Final Five: The Transcript Tumor

6:23 PM

“Nancy Pelosi flipped out when she found out the call was taped!”

A massive tumor formed at the very tip, pulsing with the knowledge that there was no tape, only a rough transcript that actually supported impeachment. The tumor seemed embarrassed for everyone involved.

“Actually, the transcript reinforced—”

“Beautiful transcript! Perfect call! The tumor agrees!”

The tumor, in fact, did not agree. It spelled out “NO TAPE” in Morse code through its pulsing.

Dale Note #17: No known recording; rough transcript bolstered Pelosi’s case

The nose had become a medical textbook of mendacity. 47 feet long and now decorated with:

  • Grocery Glow (crimson, 22% inflamed)

  • Dragon-Scale Deflation Denial (3% reality, zero admission)

  • AI Node Networks (humming with hubris)

  • Investment Impossibilities (Klein bottle configuration)

  • War-Wound Weeping (ongoing and oozing)

  • Ukrainian Ulceration ($187B reality check)

  • Document Dermatitis (Acts don’t act that way)

  • Comey Cysts (WiFi-enabled evidence)

  • Transcript Tumor (no tape, no escape)

CBS’s medical team issued a statement: “We’ve never seen anything like it. It’s like every lie targeted a different system. The nose isn’t just growing—it’s evolving.”

Donald, helped to his feet by a team of structural engineers, seemed unbothered. “Great interview! The best! The nose performed beautifully!”

The nose, now requiring its own zip code, had indeed performed. It had become a living fact-check, a monument to the 60 Minutes Syndrome—where 18 lies in 80 minutes created a new form of life.

As they wheeled him out (walking had become impractical), he called back: “Same time next week?”

Norah O’Donnell, still maintaining her professionalism, simply said: “We’ll need a bigger studio.”

The nose, in what witnesses swear was deliberate, knocked over the “APPLAUSE” sign on its way out.

It now simply read “APPALL.”
