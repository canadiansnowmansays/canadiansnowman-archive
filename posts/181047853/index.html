INTRODUCTION: WHEN SATIRE BECOMES OFFICIAL POLICY

In Parts One and Two of this field guide, we employed feudal terminology—vassals, tribute, fealty—as satirical device to expose modern power dynamics.

Protocol Update, December 5, 2025: The Trump administration’s newly released National Security Strategy (NSS) employs actual feudal and imperial terminology to describe America’s relationship with its allies. The document uses the precise language that the Kremlin employs for its subordinate states.

Critical Note: This memorandum documents official U.S. government language from the 2025 NSS. No satirical exaggeration is required.

PART I: THE OFFICIAL FEUDAL LEXICON

When the Emperor Speaks Russian

Protocol 1.1: The “Sphere of Responsibility” Designation

The 2025 National Security Strategy states that Canada exists “within the United States’ strategic sphere of responsibility.” This phrase appears multiple times throughout the document.

The Problem: This is not American diplomatic language. This is not NATO alliance language. This is Kremlin language.

“Sphere of responsibility” (сфера ответственности) is the exact terminology Russia uses for Belarus (fully subordinate), Moldova (contested subordination), Georgia (claimed subordination, resisted), Kazakhstan (economic subordination), and Ukraine (attempted subordination, resisting).

What It Means in Practice:

When Russia says a nation is in its “sphere of responsibility,” it means:

  1. That nation’s sovereignty is conditional, not absolute

  2. That nation’s policies require Russian approval

  3. That nation’s military alignment is predetermined

  4. That nation’s deviation will be corrected by force if necessary

The Trump administration has adopted Russian imperial terminology to describe Canada. This is not diplomatic carelessness. This is doctrinal choice.

Historical Context: The United States traditionally described Canada as “longest undefended border in the world,” “closest allies and partners,” and “special relationship.” The 2025 NSS strips all partnership language and replaces it with subordination language.

Protocol 1.2: Canada’s Sovereignty as Threat

The NSS explicitly frames Canada’s sovereignty itself as destabilizing to United States security.

Any Canadian policy not aligned with Trump’s agenda is described as destabilizing, obstructive, problematic, or contradictory to U.S. interests.

Examples of “Threatening” Canadian Policies:

  • Independent climate targets

  • Digital platform regulation not aligned with U.S. tech interests

  • EU-aligned standards and regulations

  • Independent Arctic governance frameworks

  • Canadian-EU defense cooperation

  • Alternative weapons procurement (not buying American)

  • Autonomous foreign policy decisions

This is identical to Russia’s framing of Ukrainian EU membership or Georgian NATO aspirations as “threats to Russian security.” A neighboring state exercising sovereignty in ways that reduce imperial control constitutes a security threat.

The NSS characterizes “Canada’s potential and sovereignty” as requiring U.S. management to prevent destabilization. In other words: Canada being Canada is a problem that America must solve.

Protocol 1.3: The “Synchronization” Demand

The NSS states that Canada should “synchronize“ its defense posture with the United States.

Not “coordinate” (equals working together). Not “align” (suggests mutual adjustment). “Synchronize” (one party matches the other’s rhythm). This is the language of subordination.

Canada’s Response: Prime Minister Mark Carney synchronized Canada’s defense posture—with Europe, not America. Strategic partnerships with Sweden (Gripen fighters) and Germany (industrial cross-integration) represent the biggest shift in Canadian defense policy since World War II.

Assessment: This is the first successful vassal rebellion in the Trump feudal system.

PART II: THE TRUMP COROLLARY TO THE MONROE DOCTRINE

When 1823 Becomes 2025

Protocol 2.1: Official Revival of Hemispheric Imperialism

The 2025 NSS formally announces the “Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine“ to “restore American preeminence in the Western Hemisphere.”

The Monroe Doctrine (1823) originally opposed European colonization in the Americas. It was subsequently weaponized to justify U.S. military interventions throughout Latin America for 150 years: multiple occupations of Haiti, Panama Canal Zone seizure, countless Central American interventions, support for dictators, and CIA coups against democratically elected governments.

The Trump Corollary explicitly seeks to:

  1. Restore absolute American dominance in the hemisphere

  2. Treat all of Latin America as U.S. “sphere of responsibility”

  3. Use military force to enforce compliance

  4. Exclude China and Russia from any Western Hemisphere influence

  5. Treat regional resources as “shared North American assets”

Note the phrase “shared North American assets” in reference to Canadian resources. This implies Canadian natural resources are not solely Canadian—that U.S. has claim to Canadian oil, minerals, water, and timber. This is identical to Soviet language about Ukrainian grain or Russian language about Kazakh oil.

Protocol 2.2: Military Enforcement Mechanisms

Current Operations:

  • Lethal military strikes on alleged drug boats in Caribbean and eastern Pacific

  • “Targeted deployments” to the border with authorization for lethal force against cartels

  • Threats of military action in Venezuela

The NSS states these actions will “replace the failed law enforcement-only strategy.” Law enforcement (police, courts, legal process) has failed. Therefore, military force (shooting, bombing, invading) is the solution.

This mirrors exactly how empires have always treated their spheres of influence: declare the region as “area of responsibility,” identify threats within the region, use military force to eliminate threats, justify force as necessary for regional stability, and present empire as benevolent protector.

Protocol 2.3: The 19th Century Vision

Trump has repeatedly stated that America was “wealthiest and most prosperous” in the late 1800s and early 1900s. This is the “again” in “Make America Great Again.”

What that era included: High protective tariffs, territorial expansion and acquisition, sphere of influence imperialism, and minimal business regulation.

Trump’s Inaugural Address (January 2025): “We will once again consider itself a growing nation—one that increases our wealth, expands our territory.”

This is not metaphorical. Trump sees independent Canada as an obstacle to American territorial expansion, exactly as 19th century American leaders did when the U.S. had multiple territorial disputes with Canada throughout the 1800s.

PART III: THE HIERARCHY OF KINGDOMS

Democratic Allies as Vassals, Autocrats as Peers

Protocol 3.1: Treatment of Democratic Allies

Nations with free elections, free press, and democratic accountability receive public criticism, threats, demands for tribute, questions about reliability as allies, and pressure to abandon independent policies.

Canada: “Sphere of responsibility,” sovereignty framed as threat, told to “synchronize” policies, tariff threats, “51st state” annexation jokes (that aren’t entirely jokes).

Europe: Accused of “civilizational erasure,” described as unreliable allies, criticized for free speech policies, told birthrates and immigration are threats, questioned whether they’ll be strong enough to remain allies.

NATO Members: Constant complaints about defense spending, threats to withdraw from alliance, public attacks on leaders.

Protocol 3.2: Treatment of Authoritarian Regimes

Nations with no free elections, controlled press, and authoritarian rule receive public praise, diplomatic deference, no pressure for reform, respect for sovereignty, and partnership without conditions.

Middle East Monarchies - NSS Quote: “Partnering with countries in the region will require dropping America’s misguided experiment with hectoring these nations—especially the Gulf monarchies—into abandoning their traditions and historic forms of government.”

Stop criticizing Saudi Arabia for not being democratic. Stop mentioning Khashoggi’s murder. Stop talking about women’s rights. Accept absolute monarchy as legitimate governance.

The Contrast: Trump threatens Canada’s sovereignty while explicitly protecting Gulf monarchies’ “traditions” (no elections, no women’s rights, no free press, religious police, public beheadings).

Russia: U.S. seeks to end Ukraine war on terms favorable to Putin. NSS criticizes Europeans who “hold unrealistic expectations for the war.” No mention of Russian war crimes, civilian attacks, or territorial theft.

China: Seeks “balanced” trade deal, respectful language about competition, no criticism of Hong Kong crackdown, Uyghur genocide, or Taiwan threats.

Protocol 3.3: The Civilizational Erasure Language

The NSS states that Europe faces “the stark prospect of civilizational erasure“ because of immigration policies and “declining birthrates.”

The Specific Claims:

  • “Certain NATO members will become majority non-European”

  • Europe is being “enfeebled” by immigration

  • “Loss of national identities and self-confidence”

  • “Should present trends continue, the continent will be unrecognizable in 20 years or less”

The Explicit Goal: “Our goal should be to help Europe correct its current trajectory.“

The Endorsement: The NSS explicitly supports far-right parties: “America encourages its political allies in Europe to promote this revival of spirit, and the growing influence of patriotic European parties indeed gives cause for great optimism.”

Assessment: This is white supremacist language in an official U.S. National Security Strategy. The concern is not about European security or economic stability. The concern is about demographics—specifically, about Europe becoming “non-European” (less white).

The Irony: The same administration that describes Europe’s demographic change as existential threat runs the most diverse nation on Earth, relies on immigration for economic growth, and has no European ethnic majority to “preserve.”

This language reflects ideological alignment. The NSS endorses the same ethno-nationalist worldview that Putin promotes, that Orbán champions, that Le Pen advocates.

Democratic allies who embrace diversity = unreliable, threatened with “civilizational erasure.”
Autocratic regimes that suppress minorities = partners whose “traditions” must be respected.

PART IV: THE CANADIAN REBELLION

When a Vassal Refuses the Designation

Protocol 4.1: Prime Minister Carney’s Pivot

Mark Carney became Prime Minister knowing exactly what was coming. Trump’s first term provided the preview. The 2025 NSS made it official: Canada was designated a subordinate state.

Carney’s Response: Canada systematically began building independence from U.S. dominance through military, economic, and diplomatic pivots.

The Military Pivot:

  • Sweden: Gripen fighter jets (not American F-35s), industrial technology transfer, joint production, Arctic defense cooperation

  • Germany: Defense industrial integration, technology sharing, joint R&D, European supply chain integration

  • Significance: The most significant shift in Canadian defense policy since WWII. For 80 years, Canadian military procurement meant buying American.

The Economic Pivot: Increased trade with EU, Asia-Pacific partnerships, alternative supply chains, reduced reliance on U.S. markets.

The Diplomatic Pivot: Strengthening ties with European Union, Commonwealth nations, Pacific allies (Japan, South Korea, Australia), and international institutions Trump rejects.

Protocol 4.2: The Message

Carney’s Implicit Statement: “You designated us a vassal state within your sphere of responsibility. We reject that designation. We will build our own defense, diversify our economy, and align with partners who treat us as equals.”

This is the first successful resistance to Trump’s feudal system at the national level. Individuals who resist (Liz Cheney, MTG, the six lawmakers) face destruction. Canada is demonstrating that nations can resist.

The Risk: Trump’s response options include tariffs (already deployed), trade restrictions, border harassment, intelligence sharing cutoffs, military pressure, and economic warfare.

The Historical Question: When nations have tried to escape imperial “spheres of responsibility”—Hungary 1956, Czechoslovakia 1968, Georgia 2008, Ukraine 2014-present—invasions followed. Will the United States allow Canada to leave its sphere, or will Trump escalate?

PART V: HISTORICAL PRECEDENTS

Every Empire Uses the Same Playbook

Protocol 5.1: The Soviet Sphere

The Brezhnev Doctrine:

After crushing the Prague Spring (1968), Soviet leader Leonid Brezhnev announced: any threat to socialism in one country was a threat to all socialist countries, justifying Soviet intervention.

Once you’re in our sphere, you don’t leave. Your sovereignty is conditional on accepting our system. Independent choices constitute threats.

The Soviet Language:

Soviet documents described Eastern European nations as “fraternal socialist states,” “within the socialist commonwealth,” “strategic defense zone,” and “area of responsibility.”

The Practice:

  • Hungary 1956: Invasion

  • Czechoslovakia 1968: Invasion

  • Poland 1981: Martial law under threat of invasion

  • Baltic states: Occupied until 1991

The Parallel: Trump’s NSS uses the exact same logic—Canada is in our sphere, Canadian sovereignty is conditional, Canadian independent choices are threats.

Protocol 5.2: The Russian Federation

Putin’s “Near Abroad”:

Since the USSR collapsed, Russia has maintained that former Soviet states remain in Russia’s “sphere of privileged interests” or “sphere of responsibility” (сфера ответственности).

The Practice:

  • Georgia 2008: Invasion, occupation of 20% of territory

  • Ukraine 2014: Invasion, annexation of Crimea

  • Ukraine 2022-present: Full-scale invasion

The Justification: Russia claims these nations moving toward the West constitutes a security threat, exactly as Trump’s NSS claims Canadian independent policies are destabilizing.

The Parallel: The 2025 U.S. National Security Strategy has adopted Russian imperial logic and Russian imperial vocabulary to describe Canada’s relationship to the United States.

When Satire Cannot Keep Up with Reality

Protocol 6.1: The Transformation is Complete

Parts One and Two: Used feudal language satirically, drew parallels to historical monarchs, employed exaggeration to expose power dynamics.

Part Three: No exaggeration is possible. The Trump administration has made the feudal system explicit.

The Evidence:

  1. Official government document uses imperial terminology for Canada

  2. Democratic allies designated as subordinate states

  3. Autocratic regimes treated as sovereign equals

  4. White supremacist language about “civilizational erasure”

  5. Explicit revival of 19th century sphere-of-influence imperialism

  6. Canada officially described using Kremlin vocabulary

Protocol 6.2: What This Means

For Canada: Every Canadian policy decision is now viewed through the lens of compliance or defiance. The U.S. government officially considers Canadian sovereignty conditional on American approval.

For Europe: The United States has declared that European demographics, immigration policies, and political choices are U.S. security concerns requiring American intervention.

For Latin America: The entire Western Hemisphere is declared America’s “sphere of responsibility” subject to military enforcement.

For Democratic Allies Everywhere: Partnership has been replaced with hierarchy. Alliances have become subordination. Sovereignty is conditional on compliance.

For Autocratic Regimes: Democracy pressure is gone. Human rights criticism is “hectoring.” Authoritarian governance is recognized as legitimate “tradition.”

Protocol 6.3: The Choice Before Us

Do we accept that the United States has officially adopted imperial language and hierarchical worldviews traditionally associated with Russia’s approach to its neighbors?

The Canadian Answer: No. Canada is building independent defense capability, diversifying economically, and strengthening ties with partners who treat it as equals.

The European Answer: Uncertain. Some leaders object to the NSS language. Others welcome Trump’s support for far-right parties. The continent is divided.

The American Answer: That depends on whether Americans recognize what has been done in their name.

This is not conservatism. This is imperialism.
This is not America First. This is Empire First.
This is not partnership. This is vassalage.
