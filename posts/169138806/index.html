How Gabbard's Debunked Obama Claims Killed the Epstein Story

Part 2 of "The Russia Files" series - analyzing the political aftermath of a masterful distraction

July 24, 2025

Nothing up my sleeve! While everyone watches Gabbard pull the 'Obama conspiracy' rabbit out of her hat, $1.1 billion in Epstein sex trafficking wire transfers remain scattered and forgotten on the floor.

Stephen Lawton
July 24, 2025

Five days ago, I wrote about what seemed like suspiciously convenient timing—a classic magician's misdirection. Just as Trump's base was revolting over broken promises about Jeffrey Epstein files, suddenly Tulsi Gabbard pulled a shiny new rabbit out of her hat: "bombshell" documents claiming Barack Obama led a "treasonous conspiracy" against Trump.

I called it political theater designed for distraction. I was right—but even I underestimated how thoroughly the magic trick would work.

Like any good illusion, the key was getting everyone to look at the wrong thing. While the audience gasped at Gabbard's Obama rabbit, the real evidence—$1.1 billion in documented sex trafficking wire transfers—remained scattered on the floor, ignored and forgotten.

Since July 19th, this story has evolved into a perfect case study of how modern political distraction operates. Not only have Gabbard's claims been systematically dismantled by fact-checkers, but the Epstein story has completely vanished from public discourse. The magic trick worked flawlessly.

The Fact-Check Avalanche

What's happened since my initial analysis is remarkable: virtually every major news organization has taken apart Gabbard's claims piece by piece.

FactCheck.org called her allegations "misleading" and noted they "rely heavily on an alleged contradiction" between intelligence assessments that simply doesn't exist. The contradiction she claims—between pre-election assessments that Russia wasn't hacking voting infrastructure and post-election findings about Russian influence campaigns—is fabricated. These were always different types of operations.

The Washington Post delivered perhaps the most devastating analysis, noting that Gabbard claims to have found new evidence that "somehow eluded four previous investigations" totaling "about 2,500 pages of dense prose, compared with the thin gruel of emails and meeting agendas released by Gabbard."

CNN found that her allegations "conflate and misrepresent what the intelligence community actually concluded" and that "multiple congressional sources familiar with the Senate report said that Gabbard is trying to lean on intelligence assessments that no voting systems were breached to falsely make a broader leap that a Russian influence and cyber campaign did not occur."

This isn't partisan disagreement—this is comprehensive debunking by professional fact-checkers who actually read the documents.

The Marco Rubio Problem

But here's the detail that completely destroys Gabbard's narrative: Trump's own Secretary of State Marco Rubio already investigated these exact claims.

As a Republican senator serving on the Intelligence Committee, Rubio "signed off on reports that scrutinized the creation of the Intelligence Community Assessment" that Gabbard now claims was fabricated. The committee found the assessment "presents a coherent and well-constructed intelligence basis for the case of unprecedented Russian interference in the 2016 U.S. presidential election."

Think about this for a moment. If Obama officials really manufactured a "treasonous conspiracy" using fabricated intelligence, how did a Republican-led Senate investigation—with full access to classified materials and witness testimony—reach the same conclusions?

More importantly, if Gabbard is right, then Trump's own Secretary of State was either:

  1. Part of the conspiracy against Trump, or

  2. Incompetent at detecting an obvious fabrication

Neither seems likely. The simpler explanation is that Gabbard is wrong.

Rubio himself stated in 2020: "We found irrefutable evidence of Russian meddling... Let there be no doubt, the Russians did meddle, and they continue to meddle, along with Iran and China."

So Trump's current Secretary of State contradicts his current DNI. Awkward.

The Escalation Into Dangerous Territory

What's particularly concerning is how quickly this evolved from political theater into something more dangerous.

Trump has now directly accused Obama of "treason"—a crime punishable by death in the United States. He posted an AI-generated video showing Obama being handcuffed by FBI agents. In a press conference, Trump said "Obama's been caught directly... Look, he's guilty... This was treason."

This isn't normal political rhetoric. This is a sitting president accusing his predecessor of a capital crime based on documents that have been thoroughly debunked by fact-checkers and contradict findings from his own administration officials.

Obama's office responded that the allegations are "bizarre" and "ridiculous," noting that "nothing in the document issued undercuts the widely accepted conclusion that Russia worked to influence the 2016 presidential election but did not successfully manipulate any votes."

What the Distraction is Designed to Bury

But here's the most important part: while everyone argues about Obama and 2016, genuinely explosive information about Jeffrey Epstein has been systematically buried. Let me show you exactly what Trump's administration is hiding:

The $1.1 Billion Treasury File: Senator Ron Wyden has revealed that "Treasury's Epstein file details 4,725 wire transfers adding up to nearly $1.1 billion flowing in and out of just ONE of Epstein's bank accounts. If you ask me, that's 4,725 potential lines of investigation right there. Hundreds of millions more flowed through other accounts".

International Sex Trafficking Networks: The file shows "Epstein used multiple Russian banks, which are now under sanctions, to process payments related to sex trafficking. A lot of the women and girls he targeted came from Russia, Belarus, Turkey and Turkmenistan".

Wall Street Connections: Wyden documented how "billionaire Leon Black paid Epstein $170 million for purported tax and estate planning advice, far more than other professional attorneys and advisors involved in Black's estate planning. This is an abnormal amount to pay for tax advice, yet no satisfactory explanation has been provided".

Banking Cover-ups: Major banks including "Bank of America, JP Morgan and Deutsche Bank, processed billions in suspicious transactions that flowed through Epstein's accounts" while potentially failing to conduct proper due diligence.

Seven Lines of Investigation: Wyden laid out specific investigative roadmaps for the DOJ, including subpoenaing bank records, investigating ultra-wealthy financiers who paid Epstein hundreds of millions, and examining the role of sanctioned Russian banks.

This isn't theoretical—it's actionable intelligence about an international sex trafficking operation with clear financial trails and documented connections to major financial institutions and foreign banks.

The Perfect Distraction

And what happened to all this explosive information? It completely disappeared from public discourse the moment Gabbard released her Obama documents.

The timing is beyond suspicious. On July 7, the Justice Department announced it wouldn't release more Epstein files, breaking explicit campaign promises. Trump's base revolted, with conservatives at the Turning Point USA conference demanding Attorney General Pam Bondi be fired. Trump called his own supporters "weaklings" who had "bought into this 'bullshit,' hook, line, and sinker" and declared the entire Epstein case a "hoax".

Then—perfectly timed—came the Obama conspiracy documents on July 18.

Instead of Trump supporters demanding answers about $1.1 billion in sex trafficking wire transfers, they're now focused on prosecuting Obama. Conservative media moved from criticizing Trump over broken Epstein promises to celebrating Gabbard's "bombshell revelations". The revolt from Trump's base—where he was calling them "weaklings" for demanding transparency—has been completely neutralized.

When did you last hear about those 4,725 wire transfers? The Russian banks processing sex trafficking payments? The $170 million Leon Black paid Epstein without explanation? The Treasury Department's refusal to release actionable intelligence about international trafficking networks?

Exactly. Those stories are dead, buried under weeks of arguing about Obama and 2016 Russian interference—an issue that's been exhaustively investigated by four separate inquiries totaling 2,500 pages.

From a pure political strategy perspective, it's brilliant. Morally and factually, it's appalling.

The Intelligence Community Under Attack

Perhaps most troubling is what this reveals about the weaponization of intelligence for political purposes.

Senator Mark Warner noted it's "sadly not surprising that DNI Gabbard, who promised to depoliticize the intelligence community, is once again weaponizing her position to amplify the president's election conspiracy theories."

Think about the irony here: Gabbard accuses Obama officials of politicizing intelligence while potentially doing exactly that herself—releasing documents not based on national security considerations, but to help Trump manage a political crisis.

As DNI, Gabbard oversees 18 intelligence agencies. If she's timing declassifications to distract from controversies rather than serve national security, that represents a fundamental corruption of the intelligence community's mission.

The Bigger Pattern

This episode fits a clear pattern we've seen throughout Trump's presidency:

  1. Crisis emerges (broken Epstein promises)

  2. Base begins to revolt (rare criticism from loyal supporters)

  3. Massive distraction deployed (Obama conspiracy documents)

  4. Media coverage shifts completely (Epstein story dies)

  5. Base rallies around new enemy (prosecute Obama instead)

It's the political equivalent of a magician's misdirection, and it works because people want to believe in grand conspiracies more than they want to focus on complicated policy questions or uncomfortable truths.

What We Should Actually Be Asking

Instead of relitigating 2016 Russian interference—which has been exhaustively investigated by Republicans and Democrats alike—here are the questions that actually matter:

On Russia: We have extensive bipartisan evidence that Russia interfered in 2016. Some FBI officials made mistakes or showed bias, but that doesn't erase underlying facts documented by multiple independent sources, including Trump's own officials.

On Epstein: Why won't the Trump administration release Treasury files documenting $1.1 billion in suspicious wire transfers through just one Epstein account? Why are they hiding evidence of Russian banks processing sex trafficking payments? What about the $170 million Leon Black paid Epstein without explanation? Why did major Wall Street banks process billions in suspicious transactions without proper due diligence? These are active investigations into international sex trafficking with clear financial trails—not decades-old political grievances.

On Intelligence: Should the DNI make declassification decisions based on political needs rather than national security? How do we maintain intelligence community independence when documents become tools for narrative management?

On Accountability: When intelligence officials make false or misleading claims—whether under Obama, Trump, or any president—how do we ensure accountability without destroying institutional credibility?

The Uncomfortable Truth

Here's what's most uncomfortable about this entire episode: it reveals how easily public attention can be manipulated, even when the manipulation is obvious.

I predicted this was a distraction on July 19th. Major news organizations systematically debunked the claims. Trump's own officials contradict the narrative. The timing was transparently suspicious.

None of it mattered. The distraction worked anyway.

That should terrify anyone who cares about informed democratic discourse. If political theater can consistently override factual analysis, even when the theater is this obvious, what hope do we have for addressing real problems?

Looking Forward

The most important lesson from this episode isn't about Obama or Trump or Russian interference. It's about how political distraction works in 2025—and how easily we fall for it, even when the setup is obvious.

This wasn't a coincidence. This was a calculated move, executed with precision timing, that successfully buried a major story about international sex trafficking in favor of relitigating eight-year-old political grievances.

When you see sudden "bombshell" revelations that perfectly coincide with political crises—especially when they involve old grievances rather than current issues—ask yourself: what story is this designed to bury?

In this case, we know exactly what story was buried. While everyone argued about Obama and 2016, the Jeffrey Epstein Treasury files story—with $1.1 billion in documented wire transfers, connections to Russian banks processing sex trafficking payments, and clear investigative leads into international trafficking networks—quietly disappeared from public discourse.

The distraction didn't just work—it successfully buried evidence of what Senator Wyden called "years and years of international sex trafficking" with actionable financial intelligence that could lead to prosecutions.

And that's the most disturbing revelation of all.
