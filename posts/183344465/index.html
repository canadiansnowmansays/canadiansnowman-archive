THE MAR-A-LAGO THEATER

Trump announced he would hold a press conference at 11 AM at Mar-a-Lago—his private club in Palm Beach, Florida.

Not the White House.
Not the State Department.
Not the Pentagon.

His private resort.

This connects directly to the pattern I documented in “This is NOT Another Chapter of Mr. Smith Goes to Washington“—Trump conducting foreign policy from his private club rather than democratic institutions.

Timeline this week:

  • Sunday, Dec 29: Zelensky travels to Mar-a-Lago for accommodation meeting

  • Monday, Dec 30: Netanyahu arrives for Mar-a-Lago meeting

  • Saturday, Jan 3: Trump announces Venezuela invasion, schedules press conference at Mar-a-Lago

Foreign leaders must travel to Trump’s private club to court his favor.
American military operations are announced from Trump’s private club.
Constitutional crises are addressed via press conference at Trump’s private club.

The shift from Washington to Mar-a-Lago symbolizes the transformation of American power from public trust to personal property.

Trump told The New York Times the operation was “a brilliant operation, actually” with “a lot of good planning and lot of great, great troops.”

When asked about Congressional authorization: “We’ll discuss that. We’re going to have a news conference.”

The call lasted 50 seconds before Trump hung up.

THE CHRISTMAS DAY TIMING

ABC News reported that Trump gave the green light to capture Maduro “several days ago” and troops were ready to strike on Christmas Day, but were delayed due to military airstrikes in Nigeria against ISIS.

Let that sink in.

Trump planned to:

  • Invade Venezuela on Christmas Day

  • While simultaneously conducting strikes in Nigeria

  • Both operations framed around protecting Christians/fighting terrorism

  • Both operations actually about something else entirely

The Nigeria strikes, as I documented in “When Tomahawks Become Christmas Cards,” were conducted despite:

  • Nigerian officials saying the targeted area had NO “problem with persecution” of Christians

  • Local bishop (Catholic, in Sokoto) confirming the region is Muslim-majority and faces no Christian persecution

  • Strikes hitting areas with no known terrorist presence

  • Timing designed to appeal to evangelical base

Trump wanted to invade Venezuela on the same day. The pattern of instrumentalizing Christianity for military theater while ignoring actual Christian leaders on the ground would have been doubled.

Weather and logistics delayed Venezuela until January 3. But the intent was clear: maximum symbolic impact, minimum regard for either Christian theology or constitutional process.

Iran’s Foreign Ministry: “A flagrant violation of the national sovereignty and territorial integrity of Venezuela... a clear violation of the basic principles of the United Nations Charter.”

Colombia’s President Gustavo Petro: “Alert the world: Venezuela has been attacked!” (Deployed forces to border anticipating refugee crisis)

Support:

The authoritarian leaders of the world condemned it.
The libertarian populist and Florida Republicans praised it.
The democracies urged restraint and respect for international law.

Draw your own conclusions about which side of that divide America now occupies.

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT: THE CONSTITUTIONAL CRISIS

The Succession Question

If Trump’s claim is true and Maduro has been captured, Venezuela’s constitution states power passes to Vice President Delcy Rodríguez, who runs economic policy.

But we’re in uncharted territory:

  • The U.S. doesn’t recognize Maduro as legitimate president

  • Venezuelan opposition says rightful president is exiled politician Edmundo González

  • Venezuelan military has not publicly responded or resisted

  • Rodríguez is demanding “proof of life” for Maduro and his wife

The Legal Question

Trump faces potential challenges on multiple fronts:

Domestic:

  • War Powers Resolution violations

  • Potentially impeachable offense (war without Congressional authorization)

  • Congressional Republicans have shown zero appetite to constrain him

International:

  • UN Charter violations (prohibition on use of force)

  • Geneva Conventions violations (extrajudicial killings in boat strikes)

  • Potential International Criminal Court jurisdiction

Practical:

  • What happens to Maduro? Trial in Southern District of New York?

  • Who governs Venezuela?

  • What about the oil? (The actual objective)

  • Will U.S. occupy? Install opposition? Withdraw?

Trump’s 11 AM press conference may address some of these questions. Or it may be another performance focused on “brilliance” and “great troops” while dodging constitutional issues.

THE THREAT MATRIX VINDICATED

When I wrote “The Threat Matrix” on December 10, I identified a pattern:

  1. Identify target with massive natural resources

  2. Fabricate or exaggerate threat to American security

  3. Designate as terrorist/existential danger

  4. Build military presence

  5. Strike when politically convenient

  6. Present as law enforcement/humanitarian intervention

  7. Seize resources

  8. Deal with consequences later (or never)

Every element has now manifested:

✓ Venezuela identified (303 billion barrels oil - largest reserves on Earth)
✓ Threat fabricated (drugs that mostly come from China/Mexico, “Cartel de los Soles” that doesn’t exist)
✓ Terrorist designation (November 2025 - Maduro labeled head of foreign terrorist organization)
✓ Military buildup (15,000 troops, carrier strike group, largest Caribbean deployment in decades)
✓ Strikes launched (January 3, 2026 - “politically convenient” after holiday recess)
✓ Framed as law enforcement (”Executing arrest warrant” per Mike Lee/Marco Rubio)
✓ Oil seizure next (Trump already demanded “return” of Venezuelan oil)
✓ Consequences TBD (Regional instability, refugee crisis, international condemnation)

The only surprise is how quickly it happened.

And how little Congressional or institutional resistance materialized.

THE PERFECT TIMING: WHEN WAR BURIES SCANDAL

Friday, January 3, 2026: Federal court unseals Epstein files containing damaging testimony about Trump’s connections to Jeffrey Epstein. Major scandal brewing. Congressional hearings expected when Congress reconvenes.

Saturday, January 3, 2026, 6:47 AM: Trump announces U.S. forces have captured Nicolás Maduro. Venezuela invasion dominates every news cycle. Biggest story in the world.

Monday, January 6, 2026: Congress reconvenes facing constitutional crisis over presidential war powers, not questions about Epstein connections.

The effect: Whether the timing was coincidental or calculated, the result is clear. The biggest domestic scandal of Trump’s presidency—credible testimony about his relationship with a convicted sex trafficker—has been completely buried under the biggest foreign policy crisis since the Iraq invasion.

War always drowns out scandal. Constitutional crises displace personal ones. And when Congress returns Monday, they’ll debate War Powers Resolutions and military authorizations—not Epstein depositions.

The Epstein files will sit on page 10, if they’re mentioned at all. Not because the story isn’t important. Because Trump just invaded a country.

THE BASELINE PROBLEM MEETS FOREIGN POLICY

As I documented this morning in Baseline Part 7, Trump’s judgment is demonstrably impaired. His own explanations reveal:

  • Magical thinking about basic biology (”nice, thin blood”)

  • Ignoring medical expertise in favor of personal preference

  • Inability to accurately describe recent medical procedures

  • Prioritizing appearance over health outcomes

  • Sleep deprivation affecting public functioning

Today, that impaired judgment invaded a sovereign nation.

The same pattern visible in Trump’s medical decision-making—”I know better than the experts”—now applies to war powers:

  • His doctors say take less aspirin. He takes more.

  • The Constitution says get Congressional approval for war. He invades anyway.

  • Cardiologists say his aspirin dose increases bleeding risk without benefit. He explains it gives him “nice, thin blood.”

  • Constitutional scholars say his Venezuela invasion violates war powers. He says it’s “law enforcement.”

In both cases, Trump substitutes his preferred narrative for expert consensus and legal requirements.

The difference is that self-medicating with excessive aspirin only harms Trump.

Invading Venezuela without Congressional authorization harms Venezuela, regional stability, international law, American democratic institutions, and potentially triggers consequences we can’t yet predict.

But it also accomplishes something else: It makes everyone forget about Epstein.

WHAT THE PATTERN PREDICTS NEXT

If the Threat Matrix playbook continues:

Short term:

  • Trump will claim “brilliant success”

  • Media will debate Maduro’s legitimacy rather than invasion’s legality

  • Congressional Republicans will defend or stay silent

  • Opposition will fragment between those who wanted Maduro gone and those opposing illegal war

  • Trump will move to secure Venezuelan oil infrastructure

Medium term:

  • Venezuelan military/political factions will compete for power

  • Regional instability will increase (Colombia already deploying to border)

  • Refugee crisis will intensify

  • Russia/Iran will increase regional presence as countermeasure

  • Trump will use chaos to justify continued presence

Long term:

  • U.S. companies will receive favorable oil contracts

  • Cost of occupation/stabilization will exceed any resource benefit

  • Another generation of Latin Americans will view U.S. as imperial aggressor

  • Precedent established: President can invade sovereign nations without Congressional approval by framing as “law enforcement”

That last point is the most dangerous.

If Trump succeeds in framing the Venezuela invasion as legitimate “law enforcement” rather than illegal war, every future president inherits that precedent.

Designate foreign leader as terrorist → Issue arrest warrant → Invade to execute warrant → Bypass Congress entirely.

This is the imperial presidency the Founders feared and the Constitution was designed to prevent.

Trump just demonstrated it works—if Congress refuses to exercise its constitutional authority.

THE QUESTION FOR CONGRESS

Three War Powers Resolutions have failed.

Republicans have voted consistently to give Trump unlimited war-making authority.

Democrats lack the numbers to override.

Even if they passed a resolution, Trump would veto it.

Overriding requires 2/3 majority—mathematically impossible.

So the question becomes: Is this an impeachable offense?

The Constitutional Case:

  • Article I, Section 8: Only Congress can declare war

  • Trump invaded sovereign nation without Congressional authorization

  • He explicitly stated he would “discuss” authorization later

  • This represents usurpation of Congressional war powers

The Political Reality:

  • Republicans control Congress

  • No appetite to constrain Trump

  • “Maduro is a bad guy” will be the defense

  • “President has inherent Article II authority” will be the legal claim

  • Impeachment requires Republican votes that won’t materialize

The Precedent: If Congress does nothing, Trump establishes that presidents can:

  • Designate foreign leaders as terrorists unilaterally

  • Invade sovereign nations to execute warrants

  • Frame wars as “law enforcement” to bypass Congressional approval

  • Conduct military operations without any legislative check

This precedent will outlast Trump’s presidency.

Just as “I want nice, thin blood” revealed medical judgment impairment that couldn’t be sanewashed, “We’ll discuss that later” reveals contempt for constitutional process that can’t be normalized.
