Part 5 - The Affordability Capitulation

How Democrats Surrendered on the Very Issue That Just Won Them Elections

November 10, 2025

By CanadianSnowmanSays

Five senators who normally vote with the Democratic Party spoke at a news conference at Capitol Hill on Sunday night to explain why they voted with Republicans.Credit...Tierney L. Cross/The New York Times

In Essay 4, I warned that every American had become a pawn in Trump’s shutdown game. Last night, eight Democratic senators proved me right by handing Trump his “victory” without securing the one thing that mattered - preventing 24 million Americans from facing 114% premium increases.

The capitulation came Sunday night, November 9th - four days after Democrats swept elections on affordability platforms. Senators King, Shaheen, Hassan, Kaine, Durbin, Fetterman, Cortez Masto, and Rosen voted with Republicans to advance legislation that funds government through January but provides zero guarantee on ACA subsidies. What did they get? A promise - a promise - from John Thune of a December vote on healthcare. No commitment it will pass. No leverage to ensure it does.

Angus King’s quote to The New York Times captures the surrender perfectly: “We’re not going to get what we want, although we still have a chance.”

That’s not negotiation. That’s capitulation on affordability - the very issue that helped deliver Trump his second term and just delivered Democrats their victories.

The Affordability Panic

The Times’ Erica Green documented Trump’s meltdown. After Democrats won on November 5th running on affordability, Trump spent the week ranting about the word - calling it “new,” then a “con job,” finally declaring “I don’t want to hear about the affordability.”

Why the panic? In one week, he mentioned “affordability” as much as in the previous nine months. Polls show only 30% believe he’s tackled cost of living. Even Marjorie Taylor Greene admits: “Grocery prices remain high. Energy prices are high.” A Washington Post-ABC-Ipsos poll found most Americans say they’re spending more on groceries than a year ago.

Trump’s response? Lie. Claim there’s “no inflation.” Say gas is almost $2 (it’s $3). Insist grocery prices are “way down” (they’re up). As Green wrote, affordability had become “existential” for Trump - “his one strength that has now turned into a pretty considerable weakness.”

President Trump has lost ground on an issue that helped deliver him a second term.Credit...Michael A. McCoy for The New York Times

The Walmart Thanksgiving Lie

Even as Democrats were capitulating, Trump was already deploying his next affordability lie. On November 5th and 6th - as he faced electoral losses on the affordability issue - he found his “irrefutable proof” that he’s winning: a Walmart Thanksgiving promotion.

“Grocery prices are way down, and Walmart just announced that the cost of their standard Thanksgiving meal — this is the greatest, their greatest. It is 25% lower than one year ago. That’s a big deal,” Trump declared Wednesday.

On Truth Social, he went further: “2025 Thanksgiving dinner under Trump is 25% lower than 2024 Thanksgiving dinner under Biden, according to Walmart. My cost are lower than the Democrats on everything, especially oil and gas! So the Democrats ‘affordability’ issue is DEAD! STOP LYING!!!”

He called it “irrefutable.” He said it was “better than a poll.” He claimed “they’ve got everything included. From the trimmings. To the turkey. A lot of different items.”

Here’s what he didn’t mention:

Walmart’s 2024 bundle: 29 items, fed 8 people, cost $56 ($7/person)

Walmart’s 2025 bundle: 15-23 items (depending on how you count), feeds 10 people, costs $39 ($4/person)

Missing this year: onions, celery, sweet potatoes, chicken broth, poultry seasoning, muffin mix, marshmallows, whipped topping, and pecan pie

Downgrades this year:

  • Sweet Hawaiian rolls → generic dinner rolls

  • Cranberry sauce → fresh cranberries (cheaper, requires preparation)

  • Fried onions: 6oz → 4.5oz

  • Turkey: 10-16 lb range → fixed 13.5 lb

  • Cream of mushroom soup: 2 cans → 1 can

  • French’s fried onions → Kinder’s brand

Walmart didn’t lower prices on the same basket. They created a cheaper promotional bundle by cutting items and downgrading ingredients. It’s the retail equivalent of shrinkflation - make the package smaller and cheaper, then claim victory on prices.

When NBC’s reporter asked Trump about the bundle containing less food, he responded: “Well, I haven’t heard that. Who are you with?”

When told NBC: “You’re fake news. NBC’s gone down the tubes with most of the rest of them.”

He called documented facts “fake news.” Just like he calls documented economic data “fake” when it shows prices rising.

Meanwhile, the actual data tells the real story: food prices rose 3.2% between August 2024 and August 2025 according to USDA data. Grocery prices are 2.7% higher than they were in 2024. Wholesale turkey prices are up 75% since October 2024; retail turkey prices up 25% year-over-year. Inflation remains at 3% - the same rate as when Biden left office.

But Trump stood before reporters and declared the Walmart promotion was “better than anything there is. That’s better than a poll.”

A marketing promotion with fewer items became his economic indicator. A misleading comparison became “irrefutable.” And anyone who pointed out the truth became “fake news.”

This is what Democrats surrendered to.

The Math That Democrats Ignored

The Congressional Budget Office was clear: without the ACA subsidies, 24 million Americans face premium increases averaging 114%. For families currently paying $60/month, that means $128/month - an additional $816 annually at a time when affordability is already crushing Americans.

This wasn’t theoretical. The increase was scheduled for January 1st, 2026. Democrats had Trump cornered. His polls tanking. His credibility on affordability collapsing. His administration unable to fund itself past October 1st without Democratic votes.

They had every card.

And they folded.

What Democrats Got vs. What They Gave

What Democrats Secured:

  • A promise of a December vote

  • No commitment it will pass

  • No leverage to ensure it does

What Democrats Surrendered:

  • Actual leverage over someone who needs Democratic votes

  • The ability to prevent 24 million Americans from facing massive premium increases

  • Their credibility on the affordability issue that just won them elections

  • Any moral authority to criticize Trump’s next crisis

John Thune can now bring a healthcare bill for a vote in December, watch it fail, and say “we tried.” Democrats will have zero leverage to force anything different.

The Shutdown’s Affordability Toll

The shutdown itself was an affordability crisis that Democrats just validated:

Travel chaos: 2,000+ flights canceled Sunday alone. Families paid premium prices for rebooking or missed Thanksgiving entirely. Transportation Secretary Duffy warned the holiday could become “a trickle.”

Federal workers: Dipping into savings, borrowing money to buy food, missing mortgage payments while Trump posted about his golden ballroom.

Military families: Food pantry usage up 300% in Kansas alone. The people who defend our country standing in line for donated groceries while Trump attended football games.

SNAP recipients: Like Tyrone Green in New York, skipping meals and borrowing money just to eat. 42 million Americans facing hunger while Trump claimed he was defending against funding “illegals.”

Small contractors: Losing $800 million daily in back pay they’ll never see.

For 40 days, Trump weaponized American suffering. And Democrats just rewarded it.

Trump’s Affordability Hypocrisy: Two Screens

Sunday morning: Trump proposes $2,000 dividends from tariffs - “(not including high income people),” desperately trying to seem populist about affordability.

Sunday afternoon: He’s posting pictures of his newly renovated White House ballroom with “magnificent high end carpeting” and gold fixtures.

The Times captured it perfectly: He attended a “Great Gatsby” themed party at Mar-a-Lago while federal workers missed paychecks. He flew to football games while families rationed groceries. He posted about golden faucets while claiming to care about grocery prices.

That’s Trump’s “affordability” agenda: Lie about Walmart promotions. Claim gas prices that don’t exist. Insist grocery prices are down when they’re up. Post pictures of gold ballrooms. Blame Democrats for trying to prevent the healthcare affordability catastrophe he’s enabling.

The Party in Revolt

The fury within the Democratic caucus is palpable. Ro Khanna called for Schumer’s replacement: “If you can’t lead the fight to stop healthcare premiums from skyrocketing for Americans, what will you fight for?”

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez: “Working people want leaders whose word means something.”

California Governor Gavin Newsom called it “pathetic.”

Bernie Sanders warned it would be “a policy and political disaster.”

They’re right. After 40 days of manufactured suffering, after Trump’s approval cratered to 31%, after Democratic victories built on affordability just days earlier - this was the moment to hold firm on making healthcare affordable.

The Strategic Cowardice

Look who broke ranks:

  • Two retiring senators (Durbin, Shaheen)

  • Three not up until 2028 (Fetterman, Cortez Masto, Hassan)

  • Three safe until 2030 (Kaine, Rosen, King)

Every single Democrat running in 2026 held firm. Because as Schumer admitted, healthcare costs “made a major impact on the 2025 election, and they will certainly have an even greater impact on the 2026 election.”

The eight who capitulated could afford the political hit. They traded 24 million Americans’ healthcare affordability for their own political comfort. They chose personal safety over protecting constituents who just voted for Democrats promising to tackle affordability.

The December Delusion

Thune promises a vote on ACA subsidies “by mid-December.” Democrats choose the bill. Republicans make no commitment to support it.

Mark Warner, who opposed the deal, saw it clearly: millions will still wonder “how they are going to pay for their health care or whether they will be able to afford to get sick.”

This is Lucy with the football, and eight Democrats just played Charlie Brown. They know Republicans will block it. They know premiums will explode. They know Trump will blame them anyway while claiming he’s made things affordable.

Yet they agreed to these terms after 40 days of suffering.

What Victory Looks Like

The Times laid it out: “Trump stayed on the sidelines” during the entire shutdown. No meetings with Democrats. No trips to Capitol. Just social media rants, football games, and lies about Walmart promotions.

The White House was scrambling on affordability all week:

  • Friday: Trump suddenly announces investigating meat prices

  • Saturday: Proposes redirecting health subsidies to savings accounts

  • Sunday morning: The $2,000 dividend idea

  • Sunday afternoon: Back to posting about golden ballrooms

Treasury Secretary Bessent claimed “gasoline prices are way down” and they’re “on a very good path to bringing prices down.” Both lies - gas hovers at $3 and inflation just ticked up.

Speaker Johnson? Sent his chamber home for weeks, telling them there was “no reason to negotiate.”

They let eight moderate Democrats do their work for them. And it worked.

The Ultimate Irony

Democrats won on November 5th because they ran on affordability. Trump panicked because he was losing the affordability debate. His own team admitted prices remain high. Polls showed only 30% of Americans believe he’s tackled cost of living.

The White House Deputy Chief of Staff admitted to Politico that Trump would need to focus “very, very focused on prices and cost of living” going forward.

Four days later, Democrats handed him a “victory” that will enable one of the biggest affordability catastrophes of his presidency.

While Trump posts pictures of his newly renovated ballroom with gold fixtures. While he claims grocery prices are “way down” using a misleading Walmart promotion with fewer items. While he calls anyone who fact-checks him “fake news.”

The Times’ Carl Hulse asked the essential question on November 6th: “Will Mr. Trump’s tax break for the wealthy and his attempts to slash the social safety net catch up with him?”

Democrats just ensured Americans will never find out, because they’ll be too busy trying to afford health insurance.

The Surrender Timeline

November 5th: Democrats sweep elections on affordability platforms
November 6th: Trump panics, starts ranting about “affordability” being a con job, posts misleading Walmart claims
November 7th: Trump calls NBC “fake news” for fact-checking his Walmart lie
November 8th: NBC poll shows only 34% believe Trump has lived up to economic expectations
November 9th: Eight Democratic senators vote to advance shutdown resolution with no healthcare guarantee

From electoral victory on affordability to surrender on affordability in four days.

Why This Matters

In Essay 4, I wrote about how Trump turned every American into a pawn. Today’s surrender proves it. But there’s something deeper happening here.

The eight senators who voted yes - King, Shaheen, Hassan, Kaine, Durbin, Fetterman, Cortez Masto, Rosen - represent states full of people who will face those 114% increases. Their constituents just voted for Democrats promising to tackle affordability. Now those same constituents will open their January insurance bills and wonder what happened.

Trump will blame Democrats for voting against his “beautiful deal.” He’ll claim he tried to help but Democrats refused. He’ll post more lies about Walmart Thanksgiving meals and claim grocery prices are down.

And too many people, hearing the lie repeated enough times, will believe him.

Just like some people believe his Walmart story - despite the missing items, despite the downgrades, despite the actual economic data showing prices rising.

The Pattern Completes

This is what The Hypocrisy Chronicles has been documenting:

A president who posts pictures of golden ballrooms while claiming the economy is fine. Who cites misleading promotional bundles as economic indicators. Who calls facts “fake news.” Who promised “We will rapidly drive prices down and make America affordable again,” then enables a 114% premium increase while claiming victory.

And Democrats who won on affordability, then surrendered on affordability, giving Trump exactly what he wanted when they had him at his weakest.

Throughout this series, we’ve documented:

  • Part 1: How Trump found billions for everything except feeding Americans

  • Part 2: How he abandoned every principle of presidential leadership

  • Part 3: How he deployed his admitted propaganda strategy

  • Part 4: How he turned every American into a pawn

Now Part 5: How Democrats validated his entire strategy by capitulating without achieving their core demand - preventing an affordability catastrophe.

The shutdown lasted 40 days - the longest in history. Democrats had leverage: Trump’s tanking polls, his panic over losing the affordability debate, governors defying him, courts ruling against him, even MAGA admitting prices are high.

They traded it all for a meaningless promise while Trump claims victory over fictional demands for “illegals” funding.

The hypocrisy isn’t just Trump’s anymore. It’s a Democratic Party that campaigns on protecting Americans from affordability crises, then accepts a meaningless promise rather than using their leverage to actually prevent one.

The Affordability Catastrophe Ahead

January 1st, 2026: 24 million Americans see premiums jump 114%

What that means:

  • Rural hospitals close (80% in red states rely on ACA subsidies)

  • Diabetics face tripled out-of-pocket costs

  • Families choose between healthcare and groceries

  • The inflation Trump claims doesn’t exist explodes

  • Premium increases that will cost families an average of $816 more per year

And Trump will tweet from his golden ballroom that he never gave money to “illegals” while claiming his Walmart promotion proves he’s made America affordable.

The Truth Amid the Lies

Trump claims he stood strong against funding for criminals and immigrants.
The truth: Democrats asked to prevent a healthcare affordability crisis for working Americans.

Trump claims vindication on affordability with his Walmart story.
The truth: The Walmart bundle has fewer items and downgraded ingredients. Actual grocery prices are up 2.7%. Only 30% believe he’s tackled cost of living.

Trump claims gas prices are almost $2.
The truth: Gas is $3.

Trump claims grocery prices are “way down.”
The truth: They’re up 2.7% year-over-year.

Trump promised to “rapidly drive prices down and make America affordable again.”
The truth: He just enabled the biggest affordability crisis of his presidency.

But truth doesn’t matter when repetition rules. As Trump told Stephanie Grisham: “If you say something enough, people will believe you.”

The Question No One’s Asking

But here’s what troubles me most as I finish this essay: We’re keeping score by wins and losses, by who got the better deal, by which party can claim victory. Meanwhile, 24 million Americans are about to face 114% premium increases. Families who are already spending more on groceries, utilities, and basic necessities now face healthcare costs that could devastate them.

When Trump posts his victory tweet, millions lose. When Democrats claim they tried their best, millions still lose. When Trump posts lies about Walmart promotions, millions still can’t afford groceries. The shutdown ends, both sides declare various forms of victory or noble defeat, and America collectively suffers.

That’s not a Republican loss or a Democratic loss. That’s all of America losing.

The question shouldn’t be “who won the shutdown?” The question should be “why does it feel like America has lost?”

Maybe because when politicians treat governance as a game of winners and losers, the American people always end up paying the price. Maybe because “affordability” has become a talking point to win elections rather than an actual crisis to solve. Maybe because both parties have gotten so good at declaring victory that they’ve forgotten what winning should actually look like.

Winning should look like 24 million Americans not facing 114% premium increases. Winning should look like grocery prices actually coming down instead of Trump lying about Walmart promotions. Winning should look like a government that functions and a healthcare system that remains affordable and politicians who use their leverage to protect people rather than preserve their ability to claim they tried.

The shutdown is over. The suffering continues. And until we stop keeping score by party and start measuring by whether American families can afford to live, America will lose.

The Hypocrisy Chronicles continue because the hypocrisy isn’t just Trump’s or the Democrats’. It’s a system that declares victory while America collectively suffers, that claims wins and losses while families - all families - pay the price.

Update - November 10, 2025, Morning: The shutdown ends. Trump claims victory while posting more lies about Walmart Thanksgiving meals. Democrats claim they tried. America wonders why, after 40 days of shared suffering, everyone still loses. Grocery prices continue rising. Healthcare premiums will explode in January. And Trump calls it all “irrefutable” proof of his success.
