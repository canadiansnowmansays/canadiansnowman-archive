The Baseline Problem

PART 3: The Double Standard: Why Media Treats Trump's Lapses Differently

June 25, 2025

Media Normalization and Democratic Consequences

Imagine for a moment that on June 18, 2025, President Joe Biden had walked into the Oval Office and, when asked about Federal Reserve policy, responded: "Maybe I should go to the Fed. Am I allowed to appoint myself at the Fed? I'd do a much better job than these people."

The media response would have been immediate and overwhelming:

  • CNN: Breaking news banners about presidential confusion

  • New York Times: Front-page analysis of constitutional misunderstanding

  • Washington Post: Editorial calling for cognitive evaluation

  • Fox News: 24/7 coverage questioning fitness for office

  • MSNBC: Panel discussions with neurologists and constitutional scholars

  • Social Media: Trending hashtags about the 25th Amendment

The story would dominate news cycles for weeks. Medical experts would be interviewed. Constitutional scholars would explain the separation of powers. Political commentators would debate whether such confusion disqualifies someone from office.

Instead, when Donald Trump said exactly these words, the story barely registered as news.

This is the media double standard that Lawrence O'Donnell warned about — and it's not hypothetical anymore. We're living through it in real time.

O'Donnell's Media Warning

O'Donnell was explicit about this disparity: "We must not grow weary of reminding ourselves what the White House press corps would have done if Joe Biden ever said anything like that. And how big the headlines would be in the next day's print editions of The New York Times and The Washington Post, and every newspaper in the country."

But his warning went deeper than just pointing out unfairness. O'Donnell identified a process that threatens democracy itself: "If we grow weary of making the point of how singularly stupid and possibly clinically demented Donald Trump's statements are, then we will become part of the normalization process of those statements, which most of the American news media has unwittingly participated in."

The consequence? "14-year-olds in this country will think it's perfectly normal for a president to say those things."

The Normalization Process in Action

Consider how differently the media has treated similar incidents:

Biden's Treatment: Maximum Scrutiny

When Joe Biden showed signs of cognitive decline, the media response was swift and comprehensive:

  • Verbal stumbles received extensive analysis

  • Name confusion generated immediate medical speculation

  • Debate performance triggered calls for withdrawal

  • Physical mobility was scrutinized and questioned

  • Medical transparency was demanded and delivered

Result: Biden ultimately withdrew from the 2024 race after sustained media pressure about his cognitive fitness.

Trump's Treatment: Systematic Minimization

Meanwhile, Trump's far more severe cognitive lapses receive dramatically different treatment:

  • Constitutional confusion is barely covered

  • Historical ignorance is dismissed as typical

  • Temporal confusion is ignored entirely

  • Fictional obsessions are treated as entertainment

  • Medical transparency is not demanded

Result: Trump continues in office with minimal scrutiny of documented cognitive decline.

Case Study: The Fed Chair Coverage Gap

Trump's June 18, 2025 Fed Chair self-appointment suggestion provides a perfect example of this double standard in action.

What Should Have Been News:

  • President doesn't understand basic constitutional principles

  • Commander-in-Chief confused about separation of powers

  • Leader of free world unaware of Senate confirmation requirements

  • 79-year-old showing signs of cognitive confusion

Actual Coverage: Minimal mentions, quickly forgotten, no follow-up analysis.

O'Donnell noted that this statement was "more indicative of dementia than any other president" had ever made, yet it generated a fraction of the coverage that Biden's lesser verbal stumbles received.

The Fox News Factor: Media as Policy Driver

One of the most concerning aspects of Trump's cognitive vulnerability is how easily his positions are influenced by television coverage. O'Donnell documented this process in real-time regarding Iran policy:

The Influence Pattern

Day 1: Trump seeks diplomatic solution with Iran Day 2: Israel launches strikes, Fox News provides "wall to wall imagery" celebrating Israeli "military genius"
Day 3: Trump changes position, begins claiming credit, hints at escalation

As O'Donnell reported: "His favorite TV channel, Fox News, was broadcasting wall to wall imagery of what it was portraying as Israel's military genius, and Mr. Trump could not resist claiming some credit for himself."

The Elderly Viewer Problem

O'Donnell highlighted Tucker Carlson's own explanation of Fox's strategy: "What they're doing is what they always do, which is just turning up the propaganda hose to full blast and just trying to, you know, knock elderly Fox viewers off their feet and make them submit."

Critical Point: "The elderly 79-year-old Fox viewer living in the White House has a mind that is at least as vulnerable to Rupert Murdoch's persuasion as anyone else."

This represents a national security crisis: foreign policy decisions being made by someone whose cognitive vulnerability makes them susceptible to television manipulation.

The Interpersonal Dysfunction Pattern

O'Donnell also analyzed Trump's bizarre approach to influencing Fed Chair Jerome Powell, which reveals deeper cognitive problems with cause-and-effect reasoning:

Trump's Self-Described Strategy (verbatim):

"I call him every name in the book trying to get him to do something? I've been nice to him. I do it always. I don't know how to sell. I've been nice. I've been so nice to him. Fellas, you wouldn't believe. Let's have dinner. Too late. I call him too late. Come on, too late. Let's have dinner. I do it every way in the book. I'm nasty, I'm nice. Nothing works. He's like a stupid person."

Clinical Analysis

This represents what O'Donnell called "a flow of interpersonal madness that anyone on the other end of that madness would have to ignore, just for their own sanity."

The cognitive problems evident in this statement include:

  • Contradictory behavioral patterns within single sentences

  • No understanding of cause and effect in relationships

  • Inability to recognize that insults undermine persuasion

  • Executive functioning failure in social situations

  • Lack of self-awareness about his own behavior

Any business executive displaying this level of interpersonal dysfunction would face immediate intervention. For a president, such patterns raise serious questions about diplomatic and leadership capacity.

Historical Coverage Comparison

The media's treatment of presidential cognitive concerns has historically been more consistent — until Trump.

Previous Standards:

Ronald Reagan (1980s): Media extensively covered concerns about his age and occasional confusion, leading to detailed medical evaluations and transparency.

George W. Bush (2000s): Verbal mistakes ("misunderestimate," "nucular") received significant coverage and comedic attention.

Joe Biden (2020-2024): Every verbal stumble analyzed, medical experts consulted, cognitive fitness questioned extensively.

Trump Exception:

Despite exhibiting far more severe symptoms than any previous president, Trump receives minimal coverage of cognitive concerns. The media has created what amounts to a "Trump exception" to normal standards of presidential fitness evaluation.

The Sanewashing Phenomenon

Coverage of Trump has generated discussion of whether the media has been "sanewashing" Trump by "selecting more coherent clips or quotes from his speeches that give a false impression of mental acuity without balancing that coverage by also focusing on the parts of his speeches that might raise concerns about his mental fitness to be president."

How Sanewashing Works:

  1. Selective Editing: Using only coherent portions of Trump's statements

  2. Context Removal: Presenting bizarre comments without full rambling context

  3. Normalization Language: Describing clearly abnormal behavior as "typical Trump"

  4. False Equivalence: Treating major cognitive lapses as equivalent to minor verbal stumbles

  5. Entertainment Framing: Presenting serious cognitive concerns as amusing quirks

The "Trump Being Trump" Defense

Perhaps the most damaging aspect of media normalization is the reflexive explanation that concerning behavior is just "Trump being Trump." This phrase has become a catch-all excuse that prevents serious analysis of genuine cognitive decline.

How the Defense Works:

Bizarre Statement Occurs → Media Response: "That's just Trump being Trump" → No Further Analysis → Public Acceptance → Normalization Complete

This pattern has allowed increasingly severe cognitive lapses to pass without scrutiny. As clinical psychologist Dr. Ben Michaelis noted: "The fact of the matter is that he is an entertaining — he's an entertainer fundamentally, and people — he's entertaining to watch for people. So if you're not trained in this, you may be focused only on — and he has a lot of vigor."

But entertainment value shouldn't override medical concern, especially for the person controlling nuclear weapons.

The Professional Journalism Failure

O'Donnell's analysis reveals a fundamental failure in professional journalism standards. The press corps has abandoned its watchdog function when it comes to Trump's cognitive fitness, creating different standards for different politicians.

Standard Journalistic Practice Should Include:

  1. Consistent application of cognitive fitness scrutiny across all candidates

  2. Expert consultation with neuropsychologists and medical professionals

  3. Historical comparison to document changes over time

  4. Public interest prioritization over entertainment value

  5. Follow-up analysis of concerning statements

What's Actually Happening:

  1. Inconsistent standards based on political figure

  2. Entertainment prioritization over medical concern

  3. Normalization language that dismisses serious symptoms

  4. Lack of expert consultation on cognitive issues

  5. Minimal follow-up on concerning behaviors

The Measurement Problem

The media's failure becomes even more stark when we consider objective measurements. While extensively covering Biden's verbal hesitations, major outlets largely ignored:

  • Statistical decline in Trump's speech complexity

  • Expert assessments from neuropsychologists

  • Documented temporal confusion spanning multiple years

  • Constitutional knowledge deficits below elementary school level

  • Behavioral changes indicating cognitive decline

This selective attention creates what amounts to informational malpractice — the media is failing to provide citizens with essential information about their leaders' cognitive capacity.

International Perspective

The normalization problem extends beyond domestic coverage. International observers have noted the stark contrast between American media treatment of Trump's cognitive issues versus how such behavior would be covered in other democracies.

European Analysis: Foreign correspondents have noted that Trump's cognitive lapses would trigger immediate calls for medical evaluation and potential succession planning in most parliamentary systems.

Comparative Context: The American media's tolerance for cognitive dysfunction in leadership is unusual among developed democracies, where such symptoms typically receive immediate, serious coverage.

The Voice Dulling Effect

O'Donnell made a crucial observation about how Trump's communication style itself contributes to the normalization problem:

"Because I know Donald Trump's voice dulls the brain of any healthy person trying to make sense of the English language, I am going to read Donald Trump's words exactly as he spoke them, without submitting you to the mind dulling, mind fogging experience of the sound of those words coming from Donald Trump's voice."

This "voice dulling effect" may partially explain why media coverage is inadequate. The sheer volume and incoherence of Trump's statements may overwhelm journalists' ability to analyze individual concerning elements.

Result: "All of the words coming from Donald Trump's voice all the time, all sound the same and are just a jumble to our ears so often that we don't really take in each one of them."

The Consequence: Democratic Information Failure

This normalization and double standard creates a fundamental problem for democratic governance: voters cannot make informed decisions about cognitive fitness when the media fails to provide consistent, professional coverage of concerning symptoms.

What Voters Need:

  • Consistent standards for evaluating all candidates' cognitive fitness

  • Expert medical analysis of concerning behaviors

  • Historical context showing changes over time

  • Serious treatment of constitutional knowledge deficits

  • Objective assessment free from entertainment considerations

What Voters Get:

  • Wildly inconsistent standards based on political figure

  • Entertainment framing of serious medical concerns

  • Normalization language that dismisses symptoms

  • Minimal expert consultation or analysis

  • Coverage that prioritizes ratings over public service

The Stakes: War and Peace Decisions

O'Donnell connected the media failure directly to national security concerns: "On a day when the president of the United States is reportedly in a meeting in the Situation Room where he is trying to decide how much to support or fully join a war that he opposed a week ago," the person making those decisions "doesn't know if he can appoint himself chair of the Federal Reserve."

The cognitive confusion that media treats as entertainment becomes genuinely dangerous when applied to:

  • Nuclear weapons decisions

  • Military deployment choices

  • International diplomatic negotiations

  • Economic policy implementation

  • Constitutional crisis management

Breaking the Normalization Cycle

O'Donnell's warning about normalization comes with an implicit call to action. He emphasized that "we must not grow weary" of pointing out cognitive dysfunction, because "if we grow weary... then we will become part of the normalization process."

What's Required:

  1. Consistent Standards: Apply the same cognitive fitness scrutiny to all political figures

  2. Professional Coverage: Treat cognitive symptoms as medical issues, not entertainment

  3. Expert Consultation: Include neuropsychologists in political health coverage

  4. Historical Context: Document and analyze changes over time

  5. Public Education: Help citizens understand cognitive decline markers

  6. Democratic Accountability: Demand transparency and professional evaluation

What's at Stake:

Without breaking this normalization cycle, we risk establishing new precedents where:

  • Cognitive dysfunction becomes acceptable in leadership

  • Media abandons watchdog functions for entertainment value

  • Democratic accountability weakens due to information failure

  • International credibility suffers due to leadership concerns

  • Constitutional knowledge becomes optional for constitutional officers

The International Credibility Crisis

Beyond domestic concerns, the normalization of Trump's cognitive issues creates international problems. Foreign leaders and diplomats must now navigate relationships with someone they may privately consider cognitively compromised, but whom American media treats as normally functioning.

This creates:

  • Diplomatic confusion about how to interact with American leadership

  • Alliance strain due to unpredictable decision-making

  • Economic uncertainty from erratic policy positions

  • Security concerns about American reliability

The Next Generation Problem

O'Donnell's concern about "14-year-olds" thinking cognitive dysfunction is normal represents a generational threat. If media normalization continues, we risk:

  • Lowered expectations for political leadership competency

  • Reduced civic knowledge about constitutional requirements

  • Weakened democratic institutions due to acceptance of dysfunction

  • Diminished international standing as leadership standards decline

The Path Forward

The solution requires acknowledging that media coverage of cognitive fitness has become inconsistent and inadequate. Professional journalism standards must be restored and applied equally to all political figures.

As O'Donnell noted: "We must never lose our capacity to be shocked by these sheer stupidity and rank ignorance." The moment we accept cognitive dysfunction as normal in leadership is the moment democratic accountability begins to fail.

In Part 4, we'll examine what this media failure means for democratic governance, national security, and the constitutional system designed to protect America from exactly these kinds of leadership crises.
