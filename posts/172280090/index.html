How Trump's Mob Mentality Infected His Entire Administration

When the Don's Lieutenants Learn the Family Business

August 29, 2025

Bottom Line Up Front: Trump's mob-style governance has metastasized beyond his personal vendettas into a government-wide enforcement operation, where cabinet members now independently wield federal agencies as weapons against anyone who challenges the administration's narratives or refuses to show proper loyalty.

Last month, I wrote about how Trump had turned the White House into a protection racket - demanding tribute from tech companies, wielding tariffs like a don collecting debts, and performing power for the cameras like a reality TV mobster. But that was just the beginning.

What we're witnessing now isn't just Trump being vindictive - it's his entire administration learning the family business. His cabinet members have internalized the mob playbook so thoroughly that they're now running independent enforcement operations across every corner of government. The don doesn't need to give direct orders anymore; his lieutenants know exactly what loyalty looks like.

The Protection Racket Goes Nuclear

The security protection withdrawals started with Trump himself, but they've become the signature move of systematic intimidation. In just the past week, Trump stripped Secret Service protection from former Vice President Kamala Harris - conveniently timed as she prepares for a nationwide book tour starting September 23.

Harris isn't alone on Trump's hit list. Since January, he's systematically removed security from anyone who crossed him:

The Iran Hawks: Former Secretary of State Mike Pompeo and former National Security Advisor John Bolton, both facing credible Iranian assassination threats, lost their protection after criticizing Trump. A former intelligence official told CNN: "Somebody is going to get killed, there is a credible and serious Iranian threat against Pompeo and Bolton"

The COVID Critic: Dr. Anthony Fauci, who faced regular death threats during the pandemic, was stripped of federal protection after growing critical of Trump's attempts to undermine public health guidance

When asked if he'd feel responsible if harm came to these officials, Trump's response was pure mob boss: "Certainly, I would not take responsibility... They all made a lot of money. They can hire their own security, too."

This isn't fiscal responsibility - it's protection racket enforcement. Cross the don, lose protection. The message couldn't be clearer.

Pete Hegseth: The Military Muscle

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has become Trump's chief enforcer in the military, systematically purging anyone who delivers inconvenient intelligence or fails loyalty tests.

Last week, Hegseth fired Lt. Gen. Jeffrey Kruse, head of the Defense Intelligence Agency, after his preliminary assessment contradicted Trump's claims about totally destroying Iranian nuclear sites. The same day, he also fired Vice Adm. Nancy Lacore (Navy Reserve chief) and Rear Adm. Milton Sands (Navy SEAL commander), with no public explanation.

This wasn't isolated housekeeping. Hegseth has systematically fired "Air Force Gen. CQ Brown Jr. as chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, the Navy's top officer, the Air Force's second highest-ranking officer, and the top lawyers for three military service branches". He even removed portraits of former Joint Chiefs Chairman Mark Milley from the Pentagon and ordered an investigation that could result in the retired general's demotion.

The pattern is unmistakable: deliver intelligence that contradicts Trump's preferred narrative, and you're gone. As Sen. Mark Warner noted: "The firing of yet another senior national security official underscores the Trump administration's dangerous habit of treating intelligence as a loyalty test rather than a safeguard for our country."

RFK Jr.: The Health Enforcer

Health and Human Services Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. has turned public health agencies into loyalty enforcement mechanisms, demanding officials "rubber-stamp" unscientific directives or face termination.

This week's CDC implosion perfectly demonstrates the new normal. Kennedy summoned CDC Director Susan Monarez to Washington and demanded she fire three top scientists: Dr. Debra Houry (chief medical officer), Dr. Demetre Daskalakis (immunization director), and Dr. Dan Jernigan (infectious diseases director). When Monarez refused, Kennedy had Trump fire her.

Monarez's lawyers captured the broader significance: "When CDC Director Susan Monarez refused to rubber-stamp unscientific, reckless directives and fire dedicated health experts, she chose protecting the public over serving a political agenda. This is not about one official. It is about the systematic dismantling of public health institutions."

Multiple senior CDC officials resigned in protest, with resignation letters describing "the weaponization of public health" and "communication failures". The nation's top public health agency is now leaderless at a time when expertise matters most.

Tulsi Gabbard: The Intelligence Purger

Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard has systematically weaponized security clearances against perceived enemies, particularly those with expertise in Russian interference.

This week, Gabbard revoked security clearances for 37 current and former intelligence officials, accusing them of "politicization or weaponization of intelligence" without providing evidence. The targeting pattern is telling: many officials had "knowledge or involvement in monitoring Russian, Putin directed interference in American elections".

But Gabbard herself has become a liability. Trump now views her as "off message" on Middle East policy and doesn't want her "in the room" when she contradicts his preferred narratives about Iran. Even his enforcers face loyalty tests.

Marco Rubio: The Diplomatic Decimator

Secretary of State Marco Rubio has conducted the most sweeping personnel purge, attempting to fire nearly 2,000 State Department employees under the guise of "reorganization."

Rubio sent reduction in force notices to about 1,100 civil servants and 250 foreign service officers, calling the agency a "bloated bureaucracy" pursuing "radical political ideology". A draft memo estimates that dismantling USAID alone will cost over $6 billion in lawsuits from illegally fired contractors and employees.

The timing exposed the administration's contempt for expertise: while planning mass firings, leadership asked employees to volunteer for extra duty during Middle East crises. As one State Department employee noted: "Doing extra shifts while this anxiety is hanging over us is just devastating to morale."

A federal judge temporarily halted the firings, but the damage to institutional knowledge and diplomatic capacity may be irreversible.

The Systematic Expansion: Everyone's a Target

Beyond cabinet-level enforcement, the administration has weaponized every tool of government against critics:

The Inspector General Massacre: Trump fired at least 17 inspectors general in a single night, violating federal law requiring 30-day notice to Congress. These independent watchdogs, designed to prevent waste and corruption, were eliminated for being too independent.

Law Firm Intimidation: Trump issued executive orders targeting major law firms - Paul Weiss, Covington & Burling, Perkins Coie, and Jenner & Block - stripping security clearances, barring access to federal buildings, and threatening government contracts. Paul Weiss capitulated, agreeing to pay $40 million in pro bono work for Trump-approved causes to get the executive order rescinded.

The Fed Attack: Trump attempted to fire Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook over unproven mortgage fraud allegations - the first president to ever try firing a Fed governor in 111 years. Cook is fighting back in court, with the Fed itself noting that presidents can only remove governors "for cause".

The New Normal: Fear as Governance

What makes this uniquely dangerous is how normalized it's become. Each violation of democratic norms gets performed as political theater, making authoritarianism feel like entertainment rather than emergency.

The protection withdrawals enforce the broader protection racket: show loyalty to get protection, cross the don and face consequences. As Steve Benen noted: "There is no modern precedent for an American president effectively wielding security details as a political tool, stripping protection from those he deems unworthy."

The cabinet enforcers have learned this lesson well. They don't need direct orders - they understand that expertise without loyalty is worthless, that contradicting preferred narratives means career death, that institutional independence is disloyalty to the family.

What's Really at Stake

When government becomes enforcement operation, democracy dies by a thousand cuts. Each firing normalizes the next, each loyalty test weakens institutional independence, each security withdrawal sends the message that crossing power brings consequences.

Trump's enforcers aren't just implementing policy - they're systematically dismantling the idea that government serves the public rather than the president. Career officials self-censor, agencies prioritize loyalty over expertise, and democratic institutions become personal weapons.

The scariest part? This creates the template for future authoritarians. Every norm Trump's enforcers break, every institution they capture, every precedent they set becomes the new baseline. We're not just dealing with Trump's mob government - we're creating the playbook for American autocracy.

The Don's Success Story

The original mob boss framework I described last month has succeeded beyond Trump's wildest dreams. He doesn't need to personally threaten every critic or fire every expert - his enforcers have internalized the loyalty-over-law mentality so completely that they're running independent operations.

Hegseth purges military leadership, RFK weaponizes public health, Gabbard targets intelligence professionals, Rubio decimates diplomacy. Each enforcer knows their role in the family business: eliminate opposition, reward loyalty, send messages through fear.

This isn't just vindictiveness anymore - it's systematic institutional capture performed as political theater. The don's lieutenants have learned the business, and business is booming.
