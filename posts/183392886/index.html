THE PERMISSION STRUCTURE: HOW NORMS COLLAPSE

International relations scholars have a term for this: “permissive precedent.”

When a powerful nation violates an international norm and faces no meaningful consequences, other nations calculate that the norm no longer constrains behavior.

The process:

STAGE 1: Norm exists

  • Don’t invade sovereign nations

  • Respect territorial integrity

  • Follow international law

  • Get UN approval or defensive justification

STAGE 2: Powerful nation violates norm

  • Russia invades Ukraine (2022)

  • Claims: protecting Russians, preventing NATO expansion, removing Nazi government

  • Response: Sanctions, condemnation, military aid to victim, but Russia not stopped

STAGE 3: Another powerful nation violates same norm

  • United States invades Venezuela (2026)

  • Claims: executing arrest warrants, protecting security, reclaiming stolen resources

  • Response: Condemnation from some, support from others, no consequences for U.S.

STAGE 4: Permission structure completes

  • If U.S. faces no consequences, norm is dead

  • Other nations calculate: invasions are acceptable for powerful countries

  • Regional powers test whether they can use same framework

  • International system transitions from rules-based to power-based

We’re in Stage 4.
