When Cruelty Becomes the Point

Part 4: Trump Wants to Take Food From Children’s Mouths—Literally

November 9, 2025

Previously in The Hypocrisy Trilogy (Now The Hypocrisy Chronicles): We’ve seen how Trump found billions for everything except feeding Americans (Part 1), how he violated every principle he proclaimed about presidential responsibility (Part 2), and how he deployed the exact propaganda strategy he’d bragged about (Part 3). Now, as the shutdown enters its 40th day, we witness the final descent: from manufactured crisis to pure cruelty.

The cruelest revelation of Trump’s 40-day shutdown isn’t just that he’s willing to starve Americans. It’s that he’s turned every hungry child, every unpaid worker, every stranded traveler into a weapon against Democrats. Republican voters, Democratic voters, independents—all reduced to pawns in Trump’s game of political chicken. Their suffering isn’t collateral damage; it’s the ammunition.

On Friday, November 7, Democrats made an extraordinary concession. After 38 days of shutdown, they offered to accept just a one-year extension of healthcare subsidies—down from their original demand for permanent extension. Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer called it “a very simple compromise.”

Senate Republicans killed it in seconds.

“That’s a nonstarter,” Majority Leader John Thune declared. Lindsey Graham went further, calling the proposal “political terrorism.”

Political terrorism. That’s what Graham calls preventing 24 million Americans from seeing their insurance premiums double. Without the extension Democrats are fighting for, the average American on ACA will see premiums jump from $888 to $1,904 annually—a 114% increase. Republicans call preventing this catastrophic hit to family budgets “terrorism.”

But Trump wasn’t satisfied with just rejecting compromise. Faced with federal judges ordering him to release food assistance, faced with governors defying his directives, faced with his own supporters turning against him, Donald Trump made a choice that will define his presidency:

He ordered states to literally take food back from families who’d already received it.

Free food distribution for SNAP recipients, in Daytona Beach, Fla., today.Credit...Miguel J. Rodriguez Carrillo/Agence France-Presse — Getty Images

The Agriculture Department’s late-night Saturday memo, obtained by The New York Times, wasn’t just another bureaucratic directive. It was an order to steal food from children:

“To the extent states sent full SNAP payment files for November 2025, this was unauthorized. Accordingly, states must immediately undo any steps taken to issue full SNAP benefits for November 2025.”

Read that again. The Trump administration wants states to claw back food assistance that hungry families have already received. Not just withhold future benefits—actually take back food people already have.

Wisconsin Governor Tony Evers’ response was perfect in its simplicity: “No.”

When your policy requires taking bread from children’s mouths, you’re not projecting strength. You’re advertising desperation.

The Numbers Don’t Lie: Even MAGA Is Hungry

Trump’s cruelty might have worked when it was abstract—when he could blame “illegal aliens” and his base could imagine the suffering belonged to others. But hunger doesn’t check party registration. Cancer doesn’t care who you voted for. Unpaid bills don’t discriminate between red and blue.

The latest YouGov/Economist poll tells the story:

  • Only 31% approve of Trump’s shutdown handling (58% disapprove)

  • His net approval on the shutdown has crashed to -27

  • It’s his worst shutdown rating yet

But here’s the number that should terrify Trump: 19% of his own supporters receive SNAP benefits.

One in five Trump voters. One in five MAGA hat wearers. One in five of the people who cheered “hurt the right people” are now discovering they were the people all along.

This isn’t about partisan suffering anymore. This is about Trump weaponizing ALL American suffering—using Republican hunger to attack Democrats, using independent anxiety to score points, using universal misery as a political cudgel.

Even more damning: 54% of Trump supporters think SNAP benefits should be paid during the shutdown. His approval among his own voters has dropped from +74 in August to +70 now—and falling.

As one Democratic strategist told Newsweek: “Voters are frustrated—and for good reason. Energy prices heading into winter are skyrocketing. Grocery prices are up. Inflation has been made worse by Trump’s own tariffs. And Trump’s telling voters he doesn’t want to talk about it.”

Trump’s response? Pure delusion. On November 7—Day 38 of the shutdown—he posted that Thanksgiving dinner was “25% lower” under him and that “costs are lower than the Democrats on everything.” That same day, his own supporter Marjorie Taylor Greene went on CNN and contradicted him: “No, I go to the grocery store myself. Grocery prices remain high. Energy prices are high.”

When even MTG won’t back your lies about grocery prices, you’ve lost touch with reality.

The Art of the Desperate Deal

Faced with eroding support, what does the master dealmaker do? He panics.

On Sunday, Trump suddenly announced he had a new healthcare plan. Not the “concepts of a plan” he mentioned in the debates. An actual plan! He would send money directly to people instead of insurance companies!

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent admitted on ABC there was no actual proposal. Lindsey Graham immediately called it “simply brilliant” anyway.

Because nothing says “serious policy proposal” like making it up on Twitter while your approval ratings crater.

Even more pathetic: Trump’s call to “TERMINATE THE FILIBUSTER” for “EVERYTHING ELSE.” He called Democrats “CRAZED LUNATICS” who will never compromise.

The Democrats had literally just offered a compromise. He rejected it.

When Reagan Cosplay Meets Reality: Weaponizing Your Vacation

Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy’s weekend announcement that he might use military air traffic controllers was the perfect metaphor for this administration: desperately trying to cosplay Reagan while missing the point entirely.

Reagan used military controllers to break a strike and show strength. Trump needs them because his shutdown has created such chaos that 1,600 flights were cancelled on Sunday alone.

But here’s what makes this particularly insidious: The major airports bearing the brunt of cancellations are in Democratic strongholds. LaGuardia, JFK, Newark. O’Hare. LAX. SFO. Reagan National right in D.C.

This isn’t an accident. This is strategy.

Think about it: Who flies through these hubs? Everyone. Republicans flying to see family. Democrats heading to business meetings. Independents trying to take vacations. But where are the delays? Where are the cancellations making headlines? Blue cities. Democratic districts.

The White House can point to the chaos and say, “Look at these Democrat-run cities failing you.” Never mind that it’s federal air traffic controllers—whom Trump refuses to pay—causing the problem. Never mind that the Trump administration controls the FAA. The optics are what matter: Democratic cities in chaos.

Duffy admitted the military controllers aren’t even trained for the airspace they’d be managing. “But if I can, I’m going to use them,” he said on CNN. Translation: We’ll risk passenger safety for political theater.

By Thanksgiving, Duffy warned, air travel would be “reduced to a trickle.”

Thanksgiving. The one holiday when even politically disengaged Americans travel. When grandparents fly to see grandchildren. When families reunite across the country. Trump is literally planning to weaponize Thanksgiving travel—to trap millions of Americans in airports in Democratic cities, then blame Democrats for their misery.

Your conservative uncle stuck at O’Hare? That’s Pritzker’s fault, Trump will say. Your apolitical cousin stranded at LAX? Blame Newsom. Your MAGA-hat-wearing neighbor missing Thanksgiving dinner because of delays at Newark? Obviously, it’s those New Jersey Democrats.

The administration has even started seeding this narrative. The official White House website blamed “Democrat-driven chaos” for the air travel crisis, specifically naming Democratic mayors and governors. They’re creating the crisis, then pre-blaming Democrats for the suffering they’re about to inflict on travelers of every political stripe.

Strong leadership, folks. The best leadership.

Using your family’s Thanksgiving as a weapon to own the libs.

The Wisconsin Mirror: When States Say No

Perhaps nothing illustrates Trump’s weakness more than Wisconsin’s open defiance. When the federal government ordered states to “immediately undo” food assistance already distributed, Wisconsin didn’t just refuse—they went public with their refusal.

“My message to America is your government is failing you right now—we have failed you,” Agriculture Secretary Brooke Rollins accidentally admitted.

Mike Johnson rushed to clarify: “When she says ‘we have failed you,’ she means ‘we, the Democrats,’ okay?”

The desperation is palpable. They can’t even acknowledge failure without immediately rewriting reality.

Wisconsin is now bringing the administration’s obstruction to the attention of federal judges. Other states are watching. When you’ve lost the ability to make states follow federal directives, you’ve lost the fundamental power of the presidency.

The Tuesday Night Lesson

Trump’s doubling down might have made sense before Tuesday’s elections. But Democrats swept races across the country. In deep-red Kentucky, a Democrat won the governorship by running on healthcare.

The message was clear: Americans are sick of the chaos. They’re sick of the cruelty. They’re sick of being told their suffering is someone else’s fault while Trump builds golden ballrooms.

Senate Democrats understood the moment. As Senator Chris Murphy tweeted: “The Tuesday election was a message to Democrats to keep fighting. People are scared at what Trump is doing to their lives and our democracy.”

That’s why Democrats could drop their demands from permanent to one-year extension. They know they’re winning. Trump knows it too.

That’s why he’s escalating. It’s not strength—it’s the flailing of a weak man who’s running out of moves.

The Ultimate Tell

Want to know how weak Trump really is? Look at what he tweeted on November 2:

“SNAP BENEFITS... will be given only when the Radical Left Democrats open up government, which they can easily do, and not before!”

There it is. In writing. He’s holding food hostage.

But here’s what that admission reveals: He knows he could fund SNAP. He knows the judges are right. He knows the $6 billion is there.

He’s choosing not to.

And while he withholds food, he demonizes Democrats for trying to prevent 24 million Americans from facing insurance premium increases that would add $12,000+ annually to family budgets. He calls their effort to prevent this inflation bomb “holding America hostage.” The projection is breathtaking.

When you’re reduced to explicitly admitting you’re using hunger as a weapon, you’ve already lost the moral argument. When you’re demanding states steal back food from families, you’ve lost the practical argument. When your own supporters are turning against you, you’ve lost the political argument.

The Big Lie Behind the Demonization

Strip away the rhetoric and here’s what Trump calls “Democrat obstruction”:

  • Preventing premiums from jumping from $888 to $1,904 per year

  • Protecting 24 million Americans from a collective $288 billion inflation bomb

  • Keeping rural hospitals open (they rely on ACA subsidies to survive)

  • Ensuring that 3 out of 4 ACA recipients in Trump-won states don’t lose coverage

This is what Republicans label “political terrorism.” Democrats trying to prevent the very inflation Trump claims doesn’t exist.

Trump’s Katrina Moment

Every failed presidency has its Katrina moment—the point where even supporters can’t deny the incompetence and cruelty anymore. For Trump, it’s this: ordering states to take food back from hungry families while his approval crashes even among his base.

The man who said shutdowns mean “the president is weak” has become the weakest president in modern history. The man who demanded Obama negotiate refuses to even meet with Democrats. The man who found creative solutions for everything else claims helplessness when children go hungry.

But the cruelest irony? Trump’s base—the people who cheered when he promised to “hurt the right people”—are discovering they were the targets all along.

19% of them need SNAP to eat. 54% of them want it funded. And 100% of them are learning what Democrats have known all along:

When Trump runs out of other people to hurt, he’ll hurt you too.

Every American a Hostage

The most insidious aspect of Trump’s strategy isn’t just the cruelty—it’s the calculation behind it. He’s not just hurting Democrats. He’s hurting everyone, then blaming Democrats for the pain.

That federal worker missing her third paycheck? She might be Republican. Trump’s using her suffering to attack Schumer.

That diabetic worried about insulin costs when his ACA subsidies expire? He might have voted Trump. Trump’s using his fear to demonize Pelosi. Pelosi, who’s trying to prevent his monthly insulin costs from tripling. But Trump calls her efforts “radical obstruction.”

That family at the food bank? They might be independents. Trump’s using their hunger to paint Democrats as villains.

Every American—regardless of how they voted—has become a human shield in Trump’s war against Democratic leadership. He’s taken the entire country hostage and is beating the hostages to make Democrats look bad for not paying the ransom.

This is beyond politics. This is psychological warfare using American citizens as ammunition.

The Choice Ahead

John Thune spent the weekend in “bipartisan talks” that he now claims are “coming together.” On Saturday afternoon, he told reporters he hoped to hold a test vote as early as Sunday evening. The moderate senators keep huddling over a package that includes three spending bills—for agriculture, military construction, and legislative agencies.

But notice what’s missing? Any guarantee on healthcare subsidies. Any commitment to prevent 24 million Americans from seeing their premiums double. Democrats’ simple ask—prevent a 114% premium increase that would turbocharge inflation—still gets labeled “radical Left demands” and “political terrorism.”

42 million Americans of all political stripes wonder where their next meal will come from. Republican federal workers, Democratic federal workers, independent federal workers—all facing another missed paycheck. Trump doesn’t care about their party registration; he only cares that their suffering can be weaponized against Democrats.

And Trump’s response? Take back the food. Destroy the filibuster. Call everyone crazy. Double down on the cruelty.

This is what happens when a president sees citizens not as people to serve but as pawns to sacrifice. When your own voters’ hunger becomes a tool to attack your opponents. When American suffering becomes politically useful.

Democrats offered a compromise. Governors are defying federal orders. Judges are ruling against the administration. Even Trump’s own supporters are abandoning him.

The question isn’t whether Trump will back down. It’s whether Americans will realize that regardless of party, they’re all just casualties in Trump’s war against Democratic leadership.

Because at this point, the cruelty isn’t just the point—it’s the weapon. And every American, regardless of how they vote, is in the crosshairs.

Update - November 9, 2025, 3:45 PM ET: As this essay publishes, the shutdown continues into its 40th day. Majority Leader Thune claims a deal is “coming together” with a possible test vote tonight, but offers no healthcare guarantees. Trump continues tweeting in all caps. And 42 million Americans continue to suffer while politicians claim progress without addressing the core issue: preventing a massive healthcare inflation bomb.

What’s your take? When does political strategy cross the line into pure cruelty? And what does it say about our politics when taking food from hungry children becomes a negotiating tactic?
