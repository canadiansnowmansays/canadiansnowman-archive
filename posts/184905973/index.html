The Guardian: Khamenei's Iran

Khamenei’s Iran

Part 6: Operation Rising Lion

February 5, 2026

In the early hours of June 13, 2025, Israeli F-35 stealth fighters screamed toward Iranian airspace. Over 200 aircraft carried 330+ precision-guided munitions. Their targets: nuclear enrichment facilities, ballistic missile sites, IRGC command centers, and the men who ran them.

Operation Rising Lion had begun.

An Israeli air force F-35 fighter lands during the “Blue Flag” multinational air defence exercise at the Ovda air force base, north of the Israeli city of Eilat, on October 24, 2021. (Photo by JACK GUEZ/AFP via Getty Images).

By 06:30 Israel Daylight Time, five waves of airstrikes had hit dozens of locations across Iran. Nuclear scientists died in their homes—some in residential buildings where neighbors also perished. IRGC generals died at headquarters, at bases, in vehicles. Iran’s most sophisticated air defense systems failed to stop the onslaught.

Among the dead, according to Prime Minister Netanyahu’s June 24 address: three chiefs-of-staff of Iran’s armed forces and dozens of senior IRGC commanders. The entire upper command structure was decapitated in the opening strikes.

And somewhere beneath his compound in central Tehran, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei—Supreme Leader, Commander-in-Chief, Guardian of the Revolution—retreated to a bunker where, according to Western intelligence assessments, he would remain for twelve days, avoiding all electronic communication, making no public appearances.

For a man who spent 37 years proclaiming Iran’s strength and invincibility, who promised to protect the Islamic Republic from arrogant powers, who declared that enemies would be crushed—the war exposed the hollowness of every claim.

By his own standards of leadership, protection, and resistance, June 2025 stands as definitive proof of failure.

I. THE OPENING BLOW

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced Operation Rising Lion in a televised address as the strikes began: “For decades, Iran’s leaders have openly called for Israel’s destruction. Iran’s nuclear program represents a clear and present danger to Israel’s very survival. We are acting to eliminate that threat.”

The targets revealed meticulous intelligence:

  • Natanz enrichment facility

  • Fordow underground facility

  • Isfahan Nuclear Technology Center

  • Parchin military complex

  • IRGC command centers across Tehran

  • Quds Force headquarters

  • Ballistic missile production sites

  • Senior military commanders’ locations

The Assassinations

Israel didn’t just strike facilities—they hunted people. Nine nuclear scientists died, some killed with their families in residential apartment strikes. The targeting suggested years of surveillance, penetration of Iran’s most sensitive programs, real-time intelligence on personnel locations.

One strike hit a meeting of the Supreme National Security Council in Tehran on June 15. Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian sustained leg injuries but escaped through an emergency exit. An official later initiated inquiry into potential spies—acknowledging that Israel knew the meeting’s exact location and timing.

Iran International reported that 120 surface-to-surface missile launchers were destroyed. The IRGC Ansar al-Mahdi Corps confirmed commanders killed in Zanjan province. Initial reports suggested over 1,000 Iranians died in the first days—military, scientists, civilians caught in strikes.

The Command Decapitation

In his June 24 victory speech, Netanyahu revealed the scale of leadership losses: “In one strike, in one blow, we eliminated Iran’s senior command, including three chiefs-of-staff and many other senior officers.”

The three chiefs-of-staff killed represented Iran’s highest military leadership across the armed forces. Dozens of other senior IRGC commanders died alongside them—a systematic elimination of Iran’s military brain trust in a single night.

The message was unmistakable: Israeli intelligence knew where Iran’s senior commanders would be, when they would be there, and could strike them all simultaneously.

The Air Defense Failure

For decades, Iran claimed sophisticated air defense networks made the country impregnable. Russian-supplied S-300 systems. Domestically produced Bavar-373 systems. Layered radar coverage.

In five waves of strikes, Israel destroyed 80+ air defense batteries. Not a single Israeli aircraft was shot down. The vaunted defense systems either failed, were overwhelmed, or had been compromised.

Iran’s military invincibility—proclaimed in countless Khamenei speeches—evaporated in hours.

II. THE BUNKER

The secret bunker

As bombs fell, Khamenei disappeared.

No live television appearances. No public speeches. No visible leadership. For twelve days—June 13-24—Iran’s Supreme Commander was invisible.

Iranian state media released no contemporaneous footage. When he finally “appeared” on June 26, it was via pre-recorded video message. No proof he’d emerged. No interaction with media or public.

The Reports

Multiple intelligence assessments and media reports suggested:

Western intelligence sources told Israeli media Khamenei had suffered mental breakdown under stress—specifically the shock of senior commanders’ assassinations and nuclear facilities’ destruction.

Other reports indicated IRGC commanders had sidelined Khamenei entirely, withholding information about the war’s progress while making decisions without him. According to National Interest analysis published post-war, an “IRGC military junta” had effectively replaced Khamenei during the crisis.

Iranian Minister of Islamic Culture and Tourism later suggested Majlis speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, a former IRGC commander, had taken charge during the war.

The Implications

Whether breakdown or sidelining, the message was identical: when Iran faced its greatest crisis, the Supreme Leader was irrelevant.

For 37 years, Khamenei positioned himself as indispensable—the only authority capable of guiding Iran through external threats. He accumulated absolute power precisely for moments like this.

When the moment arrived, he hid underground while others made decisions.

His Standard: Leadership Under Fire

In countless speeches, Khamenei invoked wartime leadership examples. He praised Ayatollah Khomeini’s steadfastness during the Iran-Iraq War. He emphasized leadership requiring courage, presence, visible command.

In a 2023 speech: “True leaders don’t hide when enemies threaten. They stand with their people, guide them through crisis, demonstrate strength and faith. This is Islamic leadership.”

Test that against June 2025: Khamenei spent the war in a bunker, incommunicado, either mentally incapacitated or deemed too unreliable for command by his own military.

By the standard he established—visible leadership during crisis—he failed absolutely.

III. IRAN’S RESPONSE: QUANTITY WITHOUT QUALITY

On June 14, Iran launched its retaliation: the largest ballistic missile barrage in history.

Over twelve days, Iran fired:

  • Approximately 525-550 ballistic missiles

  • Over 1,000 armed drones

The numbers were staggering. The results were not.

The Interception Rate

Israeli and American air defense systems—Iron Dome, David’s Sling, Arrow, Patriot, THAAD—intercepted approximately 86-90% of incoming missiles and drones.

Of the 10-14% that penetrated defenses, most struck non-critical targets or open areas. Some hit residential neighborhoods in Tel Aviv, Haifa, and Beersheba. One hospital suffered damage. Several apartment buildings were destroyed.

By June 24 ceasefire, Israeli casualties: 28 civilians killed, approximately 1,472 wounded (15 serious, 58 moderate, 1,399 light injuries).

Compare Iranian casualties: Reports ranged from 657 killed (HRANA documentation by June 20) to over 2,000+ killed according to Iran’s Health Ministry and NBC News reporting of Iranian government statements.

The asymmetry was brutal. Iran threw everything it had—525+ missiles, the product of decades of ballistic missile development—and achieved minimal impact.

What Went Wrong

Iran’s missile force had been considered its primary deterrent. If Israel attacked, the theory went, Iran would rain missiles on Israeli cities, inflicting unacceptable casualties.

The deterrent failed because:

  1. Interception technology advanced: Israeli-American systems proved far more capable than anticipated

  2. Intelligence advantage: Israel and U.S. knew launch locations, intercepted many missiles in boost phase

  3. Quantity over quality: Iranian missiles lacked sophisticated penetration aids or evasive capabilities

  4. Limited precision: Most missiles couldn’t reliably hit specific targets even when not intercepted

Iran’s decades-long investment in ballistic missiles—which consumed resources that could have fed hungry Iranians—proved largely ineffective against modern air defenses.

IV. JUNE 22: AMERICA ENTER

NZZ / jum.

On June 22, ten days into the war, the United States joined Israel with devastating effect.

Multiple B-2 Spirit stealth bombers struck three Iranian nuclear sites in Operation Midnight Hammer:

  • Fordow Uranium Enrichment Plant (deep underground)

  • Natanz Nuclear Facility

  • Isfahan Nuclear Technology Center

The weapons: fourteen GBU-57A/B Massive Ordnance Penetrator “bunker buster” bombs, each weighing 30,000 pounds, specifically designed to destroy deep underground facilities.

Additionally, Tomahawk cruise missiles fired from submarines hit supporting infrastructure.

According to President Trump, U.S. F-35 and F-22 fighters entered Iranian airspace to bait air defenses, but Iran didn’t launch surface-to-air missiles—either because systems were destroyed, crews were dead, or commanders feared revealing remaining positions.

The Damage Assessment

Isfahan: Debate over the precise scope of damage associated with U.S. airstrikes on Iran’s nuclear program has consumed Washington.

The satellite image of Natanz from March 10, 2003, shows construction work on what is now an underground facility. Sources: Maxar Technologies / Reuters

Trump immediately claimed the strikes “completely and totally obliterated” Iran’s nuclear enrichment facilities. The statement was characteristically hyperbolic, but damage was extensive.

A July 2025 Pentagon assessment suggested Iran’s nuclear program was set back 1-2 years—possibly more depending on damage to underground facilities and loss of specialized personnel.

CIA Director later stated new intelligence indicated facilities were “severely damaged.”

IAEA Director General said on June 23: “No one is in a position to have fully assessed the underground damage at Fordow, but very significant damage is expected.”

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi admitted: “Nuclear sites sustained severe damage.”

Hundreds of Billions Destroyed

Iran had spent an estimated $100-200 billion developing its nuclear program over decades. Hundreds of billions more on ballistic missiles and air defenses.

In twelve days, much of that investment was destroyed. Enrichment centrifuges shattered. Underground facilities collapsed. Production equipment buried under rubble. Irreplaceable scientists dead.

Iran’s Response

Iran fired approximately 15 ballistic missiles at Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar, the largest U.S. military installation in the Middle East.

American casualties: Zero.

The missiles either missed, were intercepted, or caused minimal damage. It was symbolic retaliation—showing Iran could reach U.S. bases but couldn’t meaningfully damage them.

V. JUNE 24: THE CEASEFIRE

Hours after Iran’s Qatar strike, President Trump announced a ceasefire effective June 24. Both Israel and Iran indicated willingness to comply while reserving right to self-defense.

Two hours into the ceasefire, Iran launched two final ballistic missiles toward northern Israel—perhaps military hardliners refusing to accept defeat. Both were intercepted. Israel responded by striking an Iranian radar installation north of Tehran.

Then silence. The Twelve-Day War was over.

Both Sides Claimed Victory

Netanyahu: Israel had set Iran’s nuclear program back years, destroyed military infrastructure, eliminated senior commanders, demonstrated air superiority. Mission accomplished.

In his June 24 victory speech, Netanyahu declared: “We removed two existential threats: The threat of annihilation by nuclear weapons and the threat of annihilation by 20,000 ballistic missiles. In the 12 days of Operation Rising Lion, we achieved a historic victory, which will stand for generations.”

Khamenei (in his June 26 recorded message): Iran had “stood firm against arrogant powers,” demonstrated resistance, survived coordinated attack. The Islamic Republic endured—therefore, victory.

The Objective Reality

Measure outcomes:

By any objective military standard, Iran suffered devastating defeat.

VI. THE AFTERMATH: ECLIPSE OF AUTHORITY

The war’s true impact wasn’t just military—it was political.

The Succession Question Accelerates

Reports emerged immediately after the war that the Assembly of Experts had been directed by Khamenei before the conflict to prepare succession planning. After the war, with Khamenei rarely seen publicly, succession discussions intensified.

In November 2025, President Pezeshkian warned in parliament: “Harm to Khamenei could cause internal factions to turn on each other, leading to regime collapse without need for external intervention.”

Translation: Elite unity depends on Khamenei’s presence, but that presence is increasingly phantom-like.

The IRGC Ascendant

If Khamenei was sidelined during the war—as reports suggested—the IRGC demonstrated it could function without him. The Supreme Leader, supposedly indispensable, proved dispensable during crisis.

National Interest analysis concluded: “An IRGC military junta had already replaced Khamenei.”

Whether accurate or exaggerated, the perception mattered: Khamenei’s authority had been eclipsed. The system could operate without him. He had become figurehead.

The Propaganda Failure

For decades, Khamenei’s speeches proclaimed Iranian invincibility. Enemies fear us. Our defenses are impregnable. Our missiles deter aggression. We can inflict unacceptable costs.

June 2025 destroyed those claims permanently. Videos of Israeli strikes circulated globally. Images of destroyed facilities appeared on social media. The body count couldn’t be hidden.

Iranians saw their “invincible” military decimated, their “impregnable” defenses penetrated, their Supreme Leader hiding in a bunker, and their vaunted missile force achieving minimal results.

The propaganda of strength collapsed when tested.

VII. HIS OWN STANDARDS CONDEMN HIM

Return to Khamenei’s decades of speeches about Iranian military capability and resistance.

On Deterrence (2023 speech): “Iran’s military power deters enemies. They know attacking us means unacceptable costs. Our missile force guarantees our security.”

Test: Israel attacked. Iran fired 525+ missiles. Israel suffered 28 deaths, Iran suffered 657-2,000+ deaths. Iran’s deterrent failed.

On Defense (2024 speech): “Our air defenses protect Iranian soil. Enemies cannot penetrate our airspace without severe consequences.”

Test: Israel conducted 1,000+ sorties over twelve days. Destroyed 80+ air defense systems. Lost zero aircraft.

On Leadership (2019 speech): “Islamic leadership means presence during crisis, guiding people through difficulty, demonstrating faith and courage.”

Test: Spent twelve days in bunker, invisible, either mentally broken or deemed irrelevant by commanders.

On Resistance (2024 speech): “The Iranian nation has proven it can stand firm against arrogant powers and convey Islamic values more loudly than ever.”

Test: 657-2,000+ killed vs. 28. Nuclear program set back years. Senior commanders dead. Infrastructure devastated. Resistance produced catastrophic defeat.

His December 2024 Post-War Speech

In December, Khamenei addressed the war outcome: “It has been proven that the Iranian nation, by relying on its own capabilities and under the shadow of faith and righteous action, can stand firm against corrupt and oppressive arrogant powers.”

Translate from propaganda to reality:

“Stand firm” = hide in bunker for twelve days

“Relying on own capabilities” = capabilities proved inadequate

“Faith and righteous action” = didn’t prevent three chiefs-of-staff from dying

“Proven” = proved the opposite

By his own metrics—deterrence, defense, leadership, resistance—June 2025 was comprehensive failure.

VIII. THE STRATEGIC DISASTER

Beyond immediate military defeat, the war exposed strategic vulnerabilities:

Intelligence Penetration: Israel knew meeting locations, commanders’ whereabouts, nuclear facilities’ details, defense systems’ locations. Either Iranian security was compromised entirely, or Israeli intelligence capabilities far exceed Iranian countermeasures.

Technological Gap: Iranian missiles, drones, air defenses proved generation behind Israeli-American systems. Decades of investment couldn’t overcome technological superiority.

Regional Isolation: Russia and China provided only symbolic diplomatic support. Iran’s “Look East” strategy—pivoting away from West toward Moscow and Beijing—produced nothing when tested.

Domestic Impact: Iranians watched their government spend hundreds of billions on nuclear program and military while they suffered economic crisis. Then watched those investments destroyed in days. The waste became undeniable.

Nuclear Ambitions: Whether Iran wanted nuclear weapons or not, the path there now extended significantly. Facilities destroyed. Scientists dead. Equipment buried. Timeline reset.

CLOSING: THE BUNKER REVEALS THE TRUTH

For 37 years, Ayatollah Khamenei built his legitimacy on strength. Iran would be strong. Islam would be strong. Resistance would be strong. Enemies would fear Iranian power.

The bunker beneath his compound revealed the truth: when enemies stopped fearing and started attacking, the strength was illusion.

The vaunted military—decades of investment, revolutionary rhetoric, displays of missiles—crumbled against modern technology and superior intelligence.

The invincible Supreme Leader—37 years of accumulated absolute power—hid underground, silent, irrelevant or incapacitated.

The nuclear program—hundreds of billions spent while Iranians went hungry—was bombed into rubble.

The deterrent—525+ missiles representing Iran’s ultimate protection—achieved nothing but proving its inadequacy.

By Khamenei’s own standards of leadership, protection, and resistance, June 2025 stands as final proof the Guardian failed to guard anything.

The revolution promised strength and dignity. Khamenei delivered bunkers and humiliation.

Iranians watched. They learned. When protests erupted six months later, they knew the system’s weakness had been exposed for the world to see.

The emperor had no clothes. And the bunker couldn’t hide that truth forever.
