The Charlie Kirk Debates

#13: “Nuremberg Trials for Gender-Affirming Care” - Part II: The Challenge

December 17, 2025

Charlie Kirk challenged others to “prove me wrong” while calling for Nazi-style tribunals against doctors providing evidence-based medical care. Medical consensus, constitutional rights, Holocaust memory, and basic human decency all prove his eliminationist rhetoric catastrophically wrong.

I. Medical Consensus: Gender-Affirming Care Saves Lives

Every Major Medical Organization Supports Gender-Affirming Care

Kirk claimed doctors provide gender-affirming care “without any thought whatsoever.” The medical consensus completely contradicts him:

Organizations Endorsing Gender-Affirming Care:

  • American Medical Association

  • American Psychiatric Association

  • American Academy of Pediatrics

  • American Academy of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry

  • World Medical Association

  • World Health Organization

  • Endocrine Society

  • American Psychological Association

These aren’t fringe groups—they represent millions of medical professionals and decades of research.

The Evidence Base

Depression and Anxiety Reduction: Multiple studies demonstrate that depression and anxiety are significantly alleviated in youth when they socially transition and receive treatment for gender dysphoria.

Quality of Life Improvement: Longitudinal studies consistently show that gender-affirming care vastly improves quality of life for people who identify as transgender.

Peer-Reviewed Research: Out of 55 peer-reviewed studies on gender transition, not a single one found negative outcomes.

Regret Rates: Gender-affirming surgeries have among the lowest regret rates of any major surgery:

  • 0.3% for transgender men

  • 0.6% for transgender women

For comparison:

  • 7% of people regret having children

  • 16.2% regret getting tattoos

  • Higher regret rates for knee replacements, cosmetic surgery

The majority who detransition do so due to lack of social or financial support—not because the care itself was wrong.

The Medical Process Kirk Misrepresented

Kirk portrayed gender-affirming care as rushed (”Here’s the script”). The reality involves:

  1. Mental health assessment by qualified professionals

  2. Informed consent process explaining risks and benefits

  3. Medical evaluation of physical health and readiness

  4. Hormone therapy (reversible in many effects) before any surgical intervention

  5. Social transition period to confirm identity

  6. Continuous monitoring by medical team

  7. Surgical options (if desired) only after extensive preparation

This isn’t an “assembly line”—it’s careful, evidence-based medicine following established protocols.

II. Constitutional Rights: Medical Care Is Not a Crime

The Right to Medical Treatment

The Constitution protects individuals’ rights to make medical decisions with their doctors. Meyer v. Nebraska (1923) and subsequent cases establish that individuals have fundamental liberty interests in bodily autonomy and medical care.

Gender-affirming care falls within this protected sphere. Criminalizing doctors for providing evidence-based treatment—as Kirk advocated—would violate:

  • Doctors’ First Amendment rights (medical speech)

  • Patients’ Fourteenth Amendment rights (due process, equal protection)

  • The fundamental right to medical privacy (established in Griswold and subsequent cases)

Equal Protection

Singling out one category of medical care for criminalization based on the identity of patients receiving it violates equal protection principles. Kirk’s advocacy specifically targeted care for transgender people, not medical care generally.

Professional Medical Judgment

Courts consistently protect physicians’ ability to provide care according to medical standards and professional judgment. Kirk’s call to “put in prison” doctors following medical consensus and professional protocols would criminalize medicine itself.

III. Holocaust Memory: The Obscenity of the Comparison

What the Nuremberg Trials Actually Prosecuted

Kirk invoked Nuremberg to describe doctors providing hormone therapy. Here’s what Nuremberg actually prosecuted:

The Holocaust:

  • Systematic murder of six million Jews

  • Gas chambers at Auschwitz, Treblinka, Sobibor

  • Mass shootings by Einsatzgruppen

  • Death marches and starvation

Medical Experiments:

  • Josef Mengele’s torture of prisoners

  • Forced sterilization programs

  • Hypothermia experiments

  • Poison testing on prisoners

Crimes Against Humanity:

  • Genocide

  • Enslavement

  • Deportation

  • Systematic torture

Kirk compared doctors helping transgender youth access puberty blockers to Nazi officials who orchestrated the murder of millions.

The Insult to Holocaust Memory

Holocaust survivors and Jewish organizations have repeatedly condemned using Holocaust comparisons for political rhetoric. Kirk’s Nuremberg invocation:

  • Trivializes actual genocide

  • Insults Holocaust victims’ memory

  • Appropriates Jewish tragedy for anti-trans politics

  • Normalizes Nazi comparisons in political discourse

When everything becomes “like the Holocaust,” nothing is.

The Actual Nazi Treatment of Transgender People

Nazis actually persecuted transgender people and destroyed the world’s first gender clinic:

The Institut für Sexualwissenschaft (Institute for Sexual Science):

  • Founded by Magnus Hirschfeld in Berlin (1919)

  • Pioneered transgender medicine and research

  • Performed some of the first gender-affirming surgeries

  • Nazis burned its library in 1933

  • Hirschfeld fled to exile

Nazi Persecution:

  • Transgender people were imprisoned in concentration camps

  • Forced to wear pink triangles or other designations

  • Subjected to medical experiments

  • Many were murdered

Kirk invoked Nuremberg to attack the very people Nazis actually persecuted and the very medicine Nazis actually destroyed.

IV. How Kirk Failed His Own Standards

The Evidence-Based Reasoning Test

Kirk’s claim: Doctors provide gender-affirming care “without any thought whatsoever.”

The evidence: Every major medical organization endorses gender-affirming care based on decades of research showing it improves outcomes and saves lives.

Kirk ignored medical consensus while claiming to value evidence.

The Constitutional Values Test

Kirk’s advocacy: Criminalize doctors, imprison healthcare providers, ban medical treatment

Constitutional principles: Medical freedom, bodily autonomy, equal protection, professional judgment

Kirk claimed to defend freedom while advocating for criminalizing medicine.

The Christian Values Test

Jesus’s teaching: “Whatever you did for the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me” (Matthew 25:40)

Kirk’s rhetoric: Called transgender people “groomers,” “abominations,” “a throbbing middle finger to God”

Kirk claimed Christian authority while dehumanizing vulnerable people.

The Holocaust Memory Test

Appropriate Holocaust references: Actual genocide, systematic murder, crimes against humanity

Kirk’s usage: Doctors providing evidence-based care to willing patients

Kirk trivialized the Holocaust to score political points.

V. The Eliminationist Pattern

What Eliminationist Rhetoric Looks Like

Kirk’s campaign against transgender people followed textbook eliminationist rhetoric:

Stage 1 - Dehumanization:

  • “Alphabet mafia”

  • “Groomers”

  • “Throbbing middle finger to God”

  • “Abomination”

Stage 2 - Threat Construction:

  • Social contagion

  • Corrupting children

  • Destroying civilization

  • Defying natural law

Stage 3 - Violence Normalization:

  • “Take care of things the 1950s way”

  • Physical confrontation advocacy

  • Invoking torture era as model

Stage 4 - Criminalization Advocacy:

  • Imprison doctors

  • Ban medical care

  • Nuremberg-style trials

  • Federal prohibition

Stage 5 - Elimination: Kirk stopped short of explicit calls for genocide, but his rhetoric pointed in that direction—removing transgender people from public life, criminalizing their healthcare, and invoking era when they were lobotomized and institutionalized.

Historical Parallels

This pattern has appeared before:

Nazi Germany:

  • Jews dehumanized as parasites → threat to German purity → violence normalized → legal persecution → genocide

Rwanda:

  • Tutsis dehumanized as cockroaches → threat to Hutus → violence normalized → systematic murder

Kirk’s Rhetoric:

  • Trans people dehumanized as groomers → threat to children → violence normalized (”1950s way”) → criminalize care (Nuremberg trials) → ?

The pattern is recognizable because it’s been used before.

VI. The Real-World Consequences

Violence Against Transgender People

Kirk’s rhetoric had consequences. Transgender people face:

  • Higher rates of violence and murder

  • Discrimination in healthcare, employment, housing

  • Elevated suicide risk (especially when rejected by family or denied care)

  • Harassment and assault

When influential figures like Kirk dehumanize transgender people and call for criminalizing their healthcare, violence increases.

The Targeting of Healthcare Providers

Kirk’s Nuremberg call put actual targets on doctors’ backs. Healthcare providers offering gender-affirming care have faced:

  • Death threats

  • Harassment campaigns

  • Bomb threats to clinics

  • Doxxing and intimidation

  • Actual violence

Kirk’s rhetoric encouraged this targeting by framing doctors as criminals deserving Nazi-style prosecution.

The Chilling Effect on Medicine

When political figures threaten to criminalize evidence-based care, doctors withdraw from providing it—not because it’s bad medicine, but because they fear legal persecution.

Kirk’s advocacy threatened to eliminate gender-affirming care not through evidence or medical judgment, but through political intimidation.

VII. What Medical Ethics Actually Requires

Primum Non Nocere - First, Do No Harm

Medical ethics requires doctors to act in patients’ best interests based on evidence and professional judgment.

For transgender patients, evidence consistently shows that gender-affirming care:

  • Reduces depression and anxiety

  • Lowers suicide risk

  • Improves quality of life

  • Has extremely low regret rates

Withholding this care—as Kirk advocated—would violate “first, do no harm.”

Patient Autonomy

Medical ethics respects patients’ right to make informed decisions about their own care. Gender-affirming care follows this principle through extensive informed consent processes.

Kirk’s advocacy would override patient autonomy, replacing individual medical decisions with political prohibition.

Professional Integrity

Doctors must follow medical evidence and professional standards, not political pressure. Kirk’s Nuremberg threat attempted to replace medical judgment with political coercion.

VIII. The Comparison Kirk Should Have Made

If Kirk wanted a historical comparison for something affecting children, he might have looked at:

Conversion Therapy:

  • Attempted to change sexual orientation or gender identity

  • Caused significant psychological harm

  • Higher suicide rates among those subjected to it

  • Now banned or restricted in many jurisdictions

  • Condemned by every major medical organization

Gender-affirming care is the opposite—it accepts people as they are and provides evidence-based support. Conversion therapy tried to force change and caused harm.

Kirk got it exactly backward—he advocated for the approach that medical consensus condemns while attacking the approach medical consensus supports.

Conclusion: Healthcare Is Not a War Crime

Charlie Kirk challenged others to “prove me wrong” while calling for Nazi-style tribunals against doctors providing evidence-based medical care. The evidence proves him catastrophically wrong:

  1. Medical consensus: Every major organization endorses gender-affirming care

  2. Evidence base: 55 peer-reviewed studies with zero negative outcomes

  3. Constitutional rights: Medical freedom is protected

  4. Holocaust memory: Kirk’s comparison trivializes actual genocide

  5. Medical ethics: Care follows established principles

  6. Real-world harm: Kirk’s rhetoric encourages violence

  7. Historical pattern: Eliminationist rhetoric has preceded atrocities before

Kirk didn’t just oppose gender-affirming care—he called for criminalizing doctors, imprisoning healthcare providers, and conducting show trials modeled on Nazi war crime tribunals.

This wasn’t policy debate. It was eliminationist rhetoric designed to remove transgender people from public life and criminalize the medicine that helps them survive and thrive.

When Kirk invoked Nuremberg, he invoked the prosecution of people who committed genocide. He compared doctors following medical consensus to Nazis who murdered millions.

The obscenity of this comparison should be obvious. The danger of this rhetoric should be clear. The violation of medical ethics, constitutional rights, and basic human decency should be undeniable.

Charlie Kirk challenged others to “prove me wrong.” On gender-affirming care, medical science, constitutional law, Holocaust memory, and basic morality all prove him devastatingly wrong.

Healthcare is not a war crime. Doctors following evidence-based protocols are not Nazis. Transgender people seeking medical care are not victims of genocide—but Kirk’s rhetoric pointed toward making them victims of something else.
