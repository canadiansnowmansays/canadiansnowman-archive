The Uncomfortable Truth Fascism Exists in America Today

Part 7 – How Institutions Fight Back

Reinforcing Courts and Reclaiming Congress: The Institutional Firewall

October 14, 2025

Part of the series: The Uncomfortable Truth, Fascism Exists in America Today

In Part 5, we saw both the threat and the response. Miller escalated to calling Democrats “domestic terrorists” requiring state “dismantling.” Trump openly admitted “We’re only cutting Democrat programs.” Vought’s scythe keeps swinging.

But Judge April Perry blocked troops in Chicago and protected journalists. Judge Immergut blocked Portland deployment. Judge Sullivan ruled against Vought’s violations. Union lawsuits were filed. Senate Democrats held seven votes.

The institutions are fighting back.

This is Part 7: How you reinforce the institutional firewall. How courts and Congress—when backed by citizen action—can blunt the Reaper’s scythe.

Part 8 will show how individual citizens organize, vote, and build community resistance. But first: The institutional levers that create space for everything else.

Why Institutions Matter: The Authoritarian’s Worst Enemy

Authoritarians don’t fear protests. They fear institutions that enforce constraints.

Miller can call judges “insurrectionists,” but if judges keep ruling against him, his rhetoric loses power. Trump can embrace Project 2025, but if courts block implementation, the blueprint stays on paper. Vought can freeze funds, but if Congress investigates and GAO documents violations, legal exposure accumulates.

Every institutional check buys time. Every ruling creates precedent. Every investigation builds evidence.

The blueprint below has two pillars:

I. Reinforce the Courts – Legal firewalls against executive overreach
II. Reclaim Congress – Restoring the power of the purse

Both require citizen action. Courts need resources and protection. Congress needs constituent pressure. Neither fights alone.

I. REINFORCE THE COURTS: Legal Firewalls Against the Scythe

What’s at Stake

The judiciary is America’s primary check against executive overreach. Vought’s “post-constitutional” playbook relies on ignoring laws like the Impoundment Control Act. Miller’s attacks—calling rulings “legal insurrection”—aim to delegitimize judicial authority entirely.

But courts are proving they have teeth:

October 9, 2025: Judge April Perry blocked Trump from deploying troops into Chicago and issued extraordinary First Amendment protections for journalists, prohibiting federal agents from “dispersing, arresting, threatening to arrest, threatening or using physical force against any person whom they know or reasonably should know is a Journalist” without probable cause.

October 2025: Judge Karin Immergut (Trump appointee) blocked 200 National Guard troops from deploying to Portland, ruling “This is a nation of Constitutional law, not martial law” and finding Trump’s violence claims “untethered to facts.”

July 2025: Judge Emmet Sullivan ruled Vought violated federal law by removing transparency data, writing: “There is nothing unconstitutional about Congress requiring the Executive Branch to inform the public of how it is apportioning the public’s money. Defendants are therefore required to stop violating the law!“

This week: Federal judges extended temporary restraining orders blocking OMB’s funding freeze directives.

The Government Accountability Office has documented that the Trump administration illegally impounded funds seven times in nine months, with GAO conducting nearly 50 ongoing investigations.

Every ruling creates a decision point: Will the administration obey courts or openly defy them? Every defiance is evidence for contempt proceedings and further legal action.

Major Lawsuits Already Fighting

American Federation of Government Employees v. OMB (filed September 30, 2025)

Challenge: Vought’s memo directing agencies to use the shutdown for mass Reduction in Force (RIF) layoffs.

Legal grounds:

  • Antideficiency Act violations (can’t spend funds not appropriated by Congress)

  • Administrative Procedure Act violations (arbitrary and capricious)

  • Government Employees Fair Treatment Act (guarantees backpay)

  • Agencies’ statutory duties aren’t repealed by funding lapses

Key argument: Nothing in federal law authorizes RIFs during shutdowns. Conducting firings isn’t “essential” work. Vought incorrectly claims funding lapses eliminate statutory requirements.

AFGE President Everett Kelley: “Announcing plans to fire potentially tens of thousands of federal employees simply because Congress and the administration are at odds is not only illegal—it’s immoral and unconscionable.“

AFGE v. Department of Education (filed October 5, 2025)

Challenge: Education Department changed employees’ out-of-office emails without consent to include: “Unfortunately, Democrat Senators are blocking passage... which has led to a lapse in appropriations.”

Legal grounds:

  • First Amendment violation (compelled speech)

  • Hatch Act violations (political activity in official capacity)

Government ethics experts called this “unprecedented” weaponization of government infrastructure for political messaging.

How You Amplify the Legal Firewall

→ Donate to litigation funds

These lawsuits need resources: expert witnesses, legal research, amicus briefs, appeals. Your contribution directly funds the legal firewall.

  • AFGE Legal Defense: afge.org

  • ACLU: aclu.org/donate

  • Democracy Forward: democracyforward.org

  • American Oversight: americanoversight.org

Amount matters less than consistency. $10 monthly beats $100 once. Sustained funding enables long-term litigation.

→ Pressure state attorneys general

If you live in jurisdictions Vought targeted, your state AG has standing to sue for Equal Protection violations.

Targeted jurisdictions:

  • New York City: $18 billion frozen

  • Chicago: $2.1 billion frozen

  • 16 states (all voted Harris): $8 billion climate grants canceled

Action: Contact your state AG’s office.

Script: “I’m urging the Attorney General to join multi-state litigation challenging OMB Director Vought’s partisan targeting of federal funding. Over $28 billion frozen or canceled exclusively for Democratic jurisdictions while protecting Republican areas. This violates Equal Protection. Illinois Gov. Pritzker called it ‘hostage taking.’ Our state should file suit.”

Find your state AG: naag.org/find-my-ag

→ Protect judicial independence

Miller’s rhetoric—calling rulings “legal insurrection,” judges “shielding terrorism”—creates conditions for violence. After Judge Perry’s Chicago ruling, right-wing accounts immediately attacked her legitimacy, calling her a “Biden appointee” serving “Soros-funded” prosecutors.

Action: Call your congressional representatives.

Script: “I’m calling to support legislation protecting federal judges from threats and intimidation. Stephen Miller’s rhetoric calling court rulings ‘legal insurrection’ creates dangerous conditions. After Judge Perry blocked troop deployment, right-wing accounts attacked her legitimacy. Congress must protect judicial independence through increased security funding and enhanced penalties for judicial intimidation.”

Find representatives: congress.gov

→ Support pro-rule-of-law judicial nominees

When judicial vacancies arise, contact your senators (especially if Republican) to demand they question nominees about:

  • Presidential impoundment authority limits

  • Whether courts can review military deployment decisions

  • Commitment to judicial independence from political pressure

  • Views on executive power constraints

→ Encourage organizational amicus briefs

If you’re part of any organization—professional association, union, civic group, religious congregation—encourage them to file amicus curiae briefs in major cases.

Many organizations don’t realize they have standing to show how Vought’s policies harm their members. Contact your organization’s leadership and legal team.

Why courts matter: Legal actions directly counter characteristic #8 (rejection of democratic norms), characteristic #2 (violent suppression through bureaucratic means), and characteristic #3 (scapegoating and targeting of opponents).

Courts checked Nixon during Watergate. They can blunt Vought’s scythe—if Americans resource and defend them.

II. RECLAIM CONGRESS: The Purse Strings Strike Back

What’s at Stake

Article I, Section 9: “No Money shall be drawn from the Treasury, but in Consequence of Appropriations made by Law.”

Congress—not the president—controls federal spending. Vought’s entire strategy depends on treating congressional appropriations as optional. Every illegal impoundment violates the Constitution’s most fundamental check on executive power.

Vought’s vulnerability is legal exposure. GAO has documented seven illegal impoundments in nine months. Every violation is evidence for oversight, contempt proceedings, and potential criminal referral.

GOP Cracks Are Showing

With shutdown blame tilting toward Republicans (though narrowing), even allies worry:

  • Senate Majority Leader John Thune: warned of “risk”

  • Sen. Kevin Cramer: concerned backlash could “squander GOP gains”

  • Sen. Susan Collins: expressing “deep concerns”

  • Sen. Lisa Murkowski: questioning partisan targeting

Only 53-47 Republican Senate control means three defections flip votes. Moderate Republicans are vulnerable to constituent pressure.

Democratic Leadership Is Holding

House Democratic Leader Hakeem Jeffries to Vought: “Listen Russ, you are a malignant political hack. We will not be intimidated by your threat to engage in mass firings. Get lost.”

Senate Democratic Leader Chuck Schumer called Vought “evil” and his actions “an attempt at intimidation.”

Senate Democrats held the line through seven rounds of failed votes, refusing to give Trump a clean CR without protections. Only three Democrats broke ranks: Sens. Fetterman (PA), Cortez Masto (NV), and Independent King (ME).

Rep. Rosa DeLauro (ranking member, House Appropriations) released a comprehensive fact sheet documenting Vought’s “lawless upheaval.”

They’re fighting. They need to hear you support them.

How You Reclaim Congressional Power

→ Demand OMB oversight hearings NOW

House Oversight Committee and Senate Homeland Security Committee have jurisdiction over OMB. They must investigate Vought’s illegal impoundments, partisan targeting, and RIF preparation.

Action: Call your representative and senators.

Script: “I’m calling to demand immediate Oversight Committee hearings on OMB Director Russ Vought’s illegal funding impoundments. GAO documented seven violations in nine months. Vought froze $28 billion exclusively for Democratic jurisdictions. This violates the Constitution and Impoundment Control Act. Congress must investigate immediately.”

Find contact info: congress.gov

Current oversight leadership:

  • Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD), Ranking Member, House Oversight Committee

  • Rep. Rosa DeLauro (D-CT), Ranking Member, House Appropriations Committee

Ask your representative to support oversight resolutions and demand accountability for Vought’s illegal actions.

→ Pressure for clean CR with protections

Senate Democrats are demanding a continuing resolution that:

  • Extends ACA subsidies (24 million Americans affected, expiring December 31)

  • Includes binding language preventing OMB from rescinding appropriated funds

  • Restores funding already illegally defunded

Action: Call your senators and representatives.

Script: “I want you to support a clean continuing resolution extending ACA subsidies with binding language preventing OMB from unilaterally rescinding congressionally appropriated funds. Seventy-one percent of Americans want ACA subsidies extended. Vought’s partisan targeting must stop. Hold the line.”

If your member is Democratic: Thank them for holding seven votes. Tell them constituent support is strong. Encourage them to continue.

If your member is Republican: Use the polling. “Republicans are getting more blame for this shutdown. Vought’s partisan freezes make it worse. A clean CR ends this before it costs you the majority.”

If you live in states with moderate Republican senators or representatives, they’re most vulnerable to pressure.

Alaska’s climate grants were canceled while similar red-state programs were protected. This is unacceptable. I want you to demand accountability and vote against any CR allowing OMB to continue partisan targeting.”

→ Support standalone military pay legislation

Over 140 House members co-sponsored legislation ensuring service members get paid during shutdowns. Speaker Mike Johnson refuses a vote.

Next military paycheck: October 15, affecting 1.3 million service members.

Action: Call your representative.

Script: “Why are you holding military families hostage? Over 140 members co-sponsored standalone military pay legislation. Speaker Johnson refuses a vote. The next military paycheck is October 15. Bring this to the floor immediately. Service members shouldn’t be pawns.”

→ Push for Impoundment Control Act enforcement

The 1974 Impoundment Control Act—passed after Nixon’s abuses—lacks strong enforcement mechanisms.

Action: Call representatives to support legislation strengthening the Act with:

  • Automatic expedited judicial review of impoundments

  • Civil and criminal penalties for OMB directors who illegally withhold funds

  • Requirement that GAO findings trigger immediate congressional investigation

  • Mandatory reversal of illegal impoundments within 30 days

Script: “The Impoundment Control Act was passed after Nixon’s abuses. Vought is doing the same thing. We need enforcement with teeth: automatic judicial review, penalties for illegal withholding, mandatory reversal. Support legislation strengthening the Act.”

→ Support whistleblower protections

Federal employees witnessing illegal activity—impoundments, politically motivated firings, document destruction, Hatch Act violations—need protection to come forward.

Action: Call representatives to support the Secure Whistleblowers Act.

Script: “Federal employees are witnessing illegal activity but fear retaliation. Vought explicitly said he wants workers ‘traumatized.’ We need stronger whistleblower protections: anonymous reporting, expedited retaliation investigations, penalties for supervisors who retaliate. Support the Secure Whistleblowers Act.”

Why Congress matters: Congressional action counters characteristic #8 (rejection of norms by restoring separation of powers), characteristic #4 (fusion of power under one leader by checking executive), and characteristic #3 (scapegoating by stopping partisan targeting).

Congress holds the ultimate check: the power of the purse. Make them use it.

The Institutional Firewall: Stronger Than It Looks

Authoritarians want you to believe institutions are powerless. That courts can’t stop them. That Congress won’t act. That the machinery is unstoppable.

Every assumption is false.

Judge Perry blocked troops TODAY. Judge Immergut ruled against manufactured crisis. Judge Sullivan stopped Vought’s transparency violations. Union lawsuits are filed and active. GAO is documenting every illegal impoundment. Senate Democrats held seven votes refusing to capitulate.

The machinery is fragile. It depends on courts not ruling, Congress not investigating, citizens not supporting resistance. When institutions hold—when backed by citizen action—authoritarian machinery grinds slower.

Your role is simple: Resource and reinforce what’s already working.

  • Donate $10-25 to legal defense funds fighting Vought’s overreach

  • Call representatives demanding OMB oversight hearings

  • Pressure state AGs to file suit over partisan targeting

  • Support legislation protecting judges and strengthening whistleblower protections

  • Thank Democratic legislators holding the line; pressure Republican moderates

Two actions this week. That’s all it takes to start.
